# Positioning & Value Proposition Language

> This file is authoritative for the Terraplex competitive narrative and value proposition language used across all dealer sites. Dealer sites should weave this language naturally into hero copy, about sections, and product descriptions — not present it as bullet lists.

When writing copy for any dealer site, these are the key messages that sell drones. Use this language naturally — don't list them as bullet points on the page, weave them into hero copy, about sections, and product descriptions.

**The core narrative:** Revolution Drones builds software that responds to what American farmers actually need. A farmer asked a Chinese drone company for point-to-point spraying for 3 years — Revolution shipped it in 45 days. Variable rate spraying was delivered in ~60 days. This responsiveness is the #1 differentiator.

**Key value propositions (in order of persuasiveness):**
1. **Software built for American agriculture** — not adapted from foreign platforms. Features are built in weeks, not years, based on direct farmer feedback.
2. **Local dealer support** — the dealer IS the competitive advantage. The biggest competitor (DJI) has no local presence — no dealer names, no phone numbers, no faces. Every dealer site should make the contrast obvious: a real person answers the phone, parts are in stock locally, support is a call away.
3. **Transitioning to US manufacturing** — "American-designed and supported" is accurate now. "Made in USA" is the direction but not 100% yet. Don't overclaim.
4. **Warranty and parts availability** — aspiration is "John Deere of the 1970s": reliable, parts always in stock, built to last. "Industry-leading warranty" is on both official sell sheets.
5. **Total cost of ownership** — not the cheapest upfront, but factor in parts availability, local service, no-runaround warranty support, and responsive software updates.
6. **ROI for farmers** — lead with outcomes, not just specs. "Reduce input losses," "lower operating costs," "save on chemical waste." The sell sheets list these benefits but existing dealer sites bury them. Make them prominent — a farmer doesn't buy a 32-gallon tank, they buy fewer refill stops and more acres per day.

## Canonical brand language (from terraplexag.com)

Use these phrases verbatim where appropriate. Mixing them with original prose is fine; rewriting them is not. They're the parent brand's voice, and consistency across dealer sites reinforces network coherence.

**Primary tagline / call-to-action:**
- "Join the Revolution"

**Geographic / origin claim** (note "Americas" plural — matches GTEEX's manufacturing footprint better than "Made in USA"):
- "Built in the Americas, for the American Farmer"

**Company self-description** (use exactly when introducing Terraplex):
- "Farmer-owned agricultural drone reseller"

**The honest-perspective frame** (use when describing why a customer should trust dealer recommendations):
- "We use the drones we sell, so we'll give you our honest perspective."
- "Every farm is different. Every need is different. There's a right drone for you, and we'll help you find it."
- "We carefully research agricultural drones from each major manufacturer to offer our customers the best options for their operation."

**Compliance program framing** (when mentioning Sam Welton or Part 137 / 107):
- "Compliance Made Easy"
- "360 service"
- "Behind-the-scenes partner"

**The 5-benefit frame** (use as ROI roll-up; appears on both R-32 and I-19 sell sheets):
1. Reduce Input Losses
2. Lower Operating Costs
3. Tough Terrain Access *(R-32)* / Unmatched Terrain Access *(I-19)*
4. Multi-role Functionality
5. Sustainable Operations

## Brand structure (do not confuse on dealer sites)

- **Terraplex Ag** is a *distributor*. Headquartered in Odebolt, Iowa.
- **GTEEX** is the manufacturer of the **Revolution Drone series** (R-32, I-19).
- Terraplex is the **exclusive US distributor** of the Revolution Drone series.
- Dealer sites are part of the Terraplex network — extensions of, not alternatives to, the parent brand.

When a dealer site mentions the parent in copy, the canonical phrasing is "Terraplex" or "the Terraplex network," not "Revolution Drones" (which refers to the product line) and not "GTEEX" (which is the OEM, not customer-facing).
