# I-19 Sell-Sheet Body Copy

> Official I-19 sell-sheet body copy. Use verbatim on dealer sites. Do not paraphrase.

It uses RTK 4-inch precision to accurately spray 20 gallons of liquid or spread 26 gallons of solids, virtually eliminating chemical and seed waste. The I-19 can easily navigate and operate in wet, uneven, or inaccessible terrain where a ground sprayer would get stuck. This versatility allows you to switch between spraying, fertilizing, and seeding, all while drastically lowering fuel and labor costs and ensuring your operation is the most efficient and sustainable in the field.

## Customer-facing FAQ (from terraplexag.com/drones)

The I-19 shares the brand's customer FAQ with the R-32. See [products/r-32.md](r-32.md) for the full FAQ. I-19-specific answers:

- **Coverage in a day**: Many operators plan missions up to 600 acres within the optimal application window.

## Product detail page content (beyond the sell-sheet)

Beats surfaced by the 2026 operator + technical manual ingest (see [log/2026-05-24-i19-manual-ingest.md](../log/2026-05-24-i19-manual-ingest.md)). These belong on a dealer's **I-19 product detail page**, not the homepage overview — too detailed for a hero/feature block, but exactly what a buyer evaluating the I-19 expects to find when they click through.

### "Every mission produces a customer-ready Service Report"

Tech manual §6.11.6 — the I-19 auto-generates a post-mission Service Report with: total acreage flown, area actually sprayed, anti-collision/exclusion area, obstacle area, flight time, total product applied, per-unit usage (vs configured baseline). This is real documentation an operator can hand to a customer for billing, compliance, or trust-building. Most competitors don't lead with this.

**Suggested detail-page treatment:** a "What you get with every flight" block listing the Service Report fields, or fold into a services-list item ("Every application generates a customer-ready Service Report").

### Owner-maintainable / right-to-repair posture

Tech manual foreword (verbatim): *"This manual is intended to give the power back to the farmers and Custom applicators to maintain and repair their equipment to keep them covering acres and increasing their production."* Direct first-party endorsement of owner repair. This is a sharper differentiator vs DJI's locked-down ecosystem than anything currently on dealer sites — and it dovetails with Terraplex's dealer-supported model (Sam Welton's compliance program, dealer parts network).

**Suggested detail-page treatment:** a positioning beat in an "Ownership experience" or "Built for the long haul" block. Pair with the dealer's parts/service capability so the message lands as "You can fix it — and your local dealer is here when you can't." Don't overclaim: this is platform-level guidance, not a warranty term.

### IP65 / IP67 weather resistance

Operator manual §2.5 + §3.1 (per IEC 60529): overall **IP65** (dust-tight + protection against water jets), with the **IP67 core module** (immersion-resistant). Standard credibility marker for outdoor-ag equipment — currently missing from the headline spec table above. Note: liquid damage is **not** covered by warranty (§2.5), so position as resistance, not invincibility.

**Suggested detail-page treatment:** add "**IP65 weather-resistant** (IP67 core module)" as a spec row on the detail page. Do NOT add to the homepage spec block — it's a confidence builder for shoppers in the detail flow, not a hero-deck headline.

### "Scout" mission profile — verify before reusing

The sell-sheet language above lists "Spray • Spread • Scout" as the I-19's three mission profiles, and notes "Scout" as I-19-unique. **Neither 2026 manual documents a Scout mode.** Operator manual §7.4 enumerates exactly four operation modes: Route, AB Point, Manual, Enhanced Manual. The aircraft does have a front FPV camera (§3.1) that enables visual reconnaissance, which may be the basis for "Scout" as marketing language — but it's not a discrete branded mode.

**Until verified with the brand:** dealers should either treat Scout as "FPV scouting view" (a capability, not a mode) or omit it. See [NOTES.md](../NOTES.md) for the open verification question.
