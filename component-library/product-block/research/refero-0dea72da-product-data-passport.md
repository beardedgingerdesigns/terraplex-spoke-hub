## Why this is library-worthy
A single-page **product data passport** treatment: the entire product summary lives in one printable dashboard rather than scrolling through marketing sections. Hairline-rule grid divides the page into cells — identity block, layered exploded diagram with A/B/C/D callouts, composition breakdown, certifications row, provenance/origin world map with flag pins, lifecycle impact numbers + equivalences, comparison bar charts vs a reference, QR code + revision date corner. Distinct from the [spec-sheet product-block](./refero-27715bc5-spec-sheet-product-block.md), which is a long vertical scroll of label/value sections — this pattern is **one page, one snapshot**, designed to read like a printed certificate or data sheet. The credibility move is the same (documentation register beats marketing register), but the affordance is different: the passport is exportable / shareable as a single artifact a customer can hand to a regulator, an insurer, or a fleet manager. Closes a gap the existing 11 product-block patterns leave open: the [aerial-photo-spine](./refero-4c2ed506-aerial-photo-spine.md), [editorial-cream-bands](./refero-ecfaaa4f-editorial-cream-bands.md), [editorial-warm-feature-blocks](./refero-4b64522d-editorial-warm-feature-blocks.md), and [alternating-pastel-stripes](./refero-4bcd783f-alternating-pastel-stripes.md) are all multi-section scroll-narratives, and the [head-to-head comparison](./refero-a1142f19-head-to-head-comparison.md) and [spec-comparison-grid](./refero-713b66c0-spec-comparison-grid.md) need ≥2 products. The passport works for a single product but pulls together provenance and impact data the spec-sheet doesn't surface.

## What to take
- **Single-page dashboard layout.** Hairline-rule grid (1px charcoal or near-black) divides the page into 6–10 cells of varying widths and heights. The whole composition fits on one printable surface (~16:10 or 4:3 aspect at desktop width); no vertical scroll within the passport itself. Treat it as a single artifact, not a scroll narrative.
- **Identity cell** (top-left): bold product code or model name (~32–40px), 2–3-line plain-language description below. No marketing tagline. Optional small attribute table: A / B / C / D component breakdown rows that pair with the layered diagram's callouts.
- **Layered anatomy diagram** (top-center): exploded view of the product showing 3–6 stacked layers separated vertically with thin connector lines, each layer labeled A / B / C / D pulled out to a side-caption with leader lines. Stroke weight consistent (~1px); no shading; perspective is mild axonometric or pure top-down. The callout letters match the breakdown table's letters in the identity cell — the diagram and table cross-reference each other.
- **Composition breakdown** (top-right): bold percentage figures stacked vertically with material names (e.g., "34% Recycled Polyester / 30% Polyester / 22% Wool / 14% Recycled Nylon"). Use a small bullet glyph or tiny pictogram per material. The percentages are the visual hierarchy — they're set largest in the cell.
- **Applications / use-case chips** below composition: pill-style chips ("Apparel / Outer Layer / Upholstery / Art / Homeware / Accessories"). Outline-only chips, no fill. Single-line row.
- **Front / Back yarn cells** (or whatever the product's two-sides-of-the-build are): two equally-sized side-by-side text blocks, each with bold sub-heading + 3–4-line description + 1–2 attribute pills below ("Recycled Content / Durable / Hazardous Chemical Free"). Mirrors the diagram's two-side composition.
- **Certifications row**: 4–6 monochrome certifying-body logos in a single horizontal strip with thin rules above and below. No color, no embellishment — the logos themselves are the credibility chrome.
- **Provenance / origin world map** (right side): grayscale outline world map with small black flag pins or dots marking sourcing locations. Adjacent to the map: a small "where things happen" table — Raw Materials / Main Yarn / Sampling / Production rows, each row paired with a country name. The map is reference, the table is the data.
- **Impact band** (bottom-left): two stacked rows of numeric stat cells. Top row "Per [unit]" = primary numbers (Emissions in KgCO₂eq / Water usage in L / Energy usage in kWh). Bottom row "Equivalences" = same numbers translated into everyday units a layperson groks (Car km / Cups of water / Phone charges). The equivalences row is the credibility loaded — abstract numbers don't move people, "X car km" does.
- **Comparison bar chart** (bottom-right): "How is your [product] doing?" — 3 small horizontal bar charts side by side, each comparing this product vs the reference ("conventional [category]"). Two stacked bars per chart, percentage-difference label inline ("19.53%↓ KgCO₂eq / 37.87%↓ Liters of water / 13.31%↓ kWh"). Directional arrow (↓ = better) makes the comparison readable at a glance.
- **Document-artifact signals** (bottom corners): QR code in lower-left (links to the LCA / source document). Lower-right has "Updated on [Month YYYY]" timestamp + "LCA → Sources" link. These two corner anchors signal "this is a real reference document, not a marketing page" — the same move that makes the [spec-sheet](./refero-27715bc5-spec-sheet-product-block.md) credible, applied to a single-page artifact instead of a vertical document.
- **Color discipline**: monochrome black-and-white with one neutral background (off-white or pale gray). No saturated brand color anywhere on the passport. The composition's only "colors" are the percentages, the country flags, and the bar-chart fill — and even those use the neutral palette.

## What NOT to take
- **Don't add lifestyle photography.** A single drone-in-flight shot pulls the page back into marketing-product-page register and dilutes the dossier credibility move. The layered anatomy diagram replaces the hero photo deliberately.
- **Don't replace the layered anatomy diagram with a 3D render.** The line-art axonometric stack is what reads as documentation; a glossy 3D render reads as catalog. If the dealer needs a 3D moment, put it on a separate page (e.g. the [interactive product-demo hero](../../hero/research/refero-d6176d7e-interactive-product-demo-hero.md)) — not inside the passport.
- **Don't break the single-page constraint.** The pattern's affordance depends on "everything fits on one printable surface." If the content doesn't fit, drop cells (e.g. cut applications chips, simplify the certifications row) rather than pushing into vertical scroll. Long-scroll content belongs in the [spec-sheet pattern](./refero-27715bc5-spec-sheet-product-block.md).
- **Don't skip the equivalences row** in the impact band. Abstract per-unit numbers don't carry the credibility — the "X car km / Y phone charges" translation is the move that makes the numbers feel real to a non-engineer reader.
- **Don't put a big "Buy" CTA on the passport.** The page reads as a reference document; a sales loop on a passport breaks the register. Buy lives on a sibling page; the passport links out via the QR code or the LCA-Sources link in the corner.
- **Don't import BYBORRE's specific knit-textile vocabulary.** Translate "Front Yarn / Back Yarn" → "Boom Assembly / Spreader Assembly" or whatever the drone's two structural halves are. Keep the **structure** (two equally-sized side-by-side cells with mirrored attribute pills); replace the **vocabulary** entirely.

## Adaptation notes (cross-archetype)
- **CLEAN**: native fit. Pure white background, charcoal hairline rules, neutral-gray text. Single brand accent reserved for the QR-code corner outline only. Pairs naturally with the [spec-sheet product-block](./refero-27715bc5-spec-sheet-product-block.md) on a sibling tab — passport for the one-page export, spec-sheet for the long-form scroll. See [CLEAN archetype](../../../archetypes/clean.md).
- **TACTICAL**: native fit with tighter geometry. Pure black-on-white, hairlines at 1px sharp (no antialiasing softness). Use a precision-sans for the percentage figures and section labels. The bar-chart fills are pure black; the comparison arrows are crisp glyphs. The world map's outlines are slightly heavier (~1.5px) so it reads as a chart rather than decoration. Pairs with the [emblem program hero](../../hero/research/refero-c1c2abcf-emblem-program-hero.md) when the page is a credentialed-program landing. See [TACTICAL archetype](../../../archetypes/tactical.md).
- **TERRAIN**: works as the calm "data-band" moment in TERRAIN's alternating rhythm. Cream-beige background instead of pure white; hairline rules in deep terrain-green or warm charcoal. The world map's outlines pick up a subtle topographic-line treatment at micro-scale (contour-curve edges instead of straight political-boundary edges). The comparison-bar fills use TERRAIN's accent muted to ~70% saturation. See [TERRAIN archetype](../../../archetypes/terrain.md).
- **FORGE**: invert to a near-black background (#0E0E0E or similar). Hairline rules in pale-warm-gray. The layered anatomy diagram's lines are off-white; the certification logos invert to monochrome white. The bar chart's "this product" fill is ember-orange at low saturation; the "conventional" fill stays muted gray. The QR code becomes white-on-black. Pairs with the [hardware-lineup dark hero](../../hero/research/refero-0945a36c-hardware-lineup-dark-hero.md) for a full FORGE page rhythm — dark hero opens, dark passport closes. See [FORGE archetype](../../../archetypes/forge.md).
- **FIELD**: cream background (#F4F1EC or similar), warm-charcoal hairline rules, slightly looser stroke weight (~1.5px) on the layered anatomy diagram so the drawings read as hand-drawn rather than CAD-precise. Single warm-amber accent reserved for the comparison-bar's "this product" fill — every other cell stays monochrome. The provenance world-map flag pins use FIELD's amber for "where" markers, charcoal everywhere else. See [FIELD archetype](../../../archetypes/field.md).
- **PATRIOT**: cream off-white background, deep-navy hairline rules and text, single barn-red accent reserved for the comparison-bar's "this product" fill. Section sub-headings in deep navy. The applications-chip row uses outline-only navy chips. The provenance map's flag pins replace generic dots with proper US-state-flag glyphs at the assembly locations (NC, KS, NE, IL per [Revolution Drones reporting](../../../research/digests/2026-05-03.md)) — pairs with PATRIOT's "Built in the Americas" credibility move from [content/positioning.md](../../../content/positioning.md). See [PATRIOT archetype](../../../archetypes/patriot.md).

## Source brief
[BYBORRE](https://create.byborre.com/) — a Dutch textile-creation platform whose generated "AO3" textile sample passport surfaces this single-page printable dossier pattern. The textile-industry use case (composition, provenance, lifecycle impact) maps directly to ag-drone use cases: an [R-32](../../../products/r-32.md) or [I-19](../../../products/i-19.md) passport could surface component layers (battery / pump / spreader / arms / boom), provenance pins (NC components / NE components / IL components / KS-NE assembly per the [Revolution Drones reporting](../../../research/digests/2026-05-03.md)), per-flight impact numbers (acres covered / chemicals reduced / water saved), and compliance certifications (FAA, [Part 137](../../../content/compliance-program.md), Part 107) — all on one exportable artifact a fleet manager or insurer can save as PDF. Sibling notes: [spec-sheet product-block](./refero-27715bc5-spec-sheet-product-block.md) for the long-scroll documentation register; [head-to-head comparison](./refero-a1142f19-head-to-head-comparison.md) when the dealer needs to compare two passports side-by-side; [editorial-cream-bands](./refero-ecfaaa4f-editorial-cream-bands.md) for the warm-archetype editorial register that complements the documentation register.
