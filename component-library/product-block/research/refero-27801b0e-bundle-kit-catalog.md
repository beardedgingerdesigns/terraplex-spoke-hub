## Why this is library-worthy
A **bundle / starter-kit catalog** product-block — every card on the page is a pre-configured package (multiple SKUs grouped into one purchase) rather than a single product, category, or feature. Each card pairs a hero photograph of the entire bundle laid out together with the kit's name, a "Starting at $X" anchor price, and a small bullet checkmark list of what's in the box / what it's compatible with. This pattern fills a gap none of the existing 15 product-block notes covers: the [color-tile category row](./refero-dc38a5c1-color-tile-category-row.md) shows category browse (one photo per tile, no price or contents), the [compatibility ecosystem grid](./refero-70415597-compatibility-ecosystem-grid.md) surfaces third-party accessories, the [head-to-head comparison](./refero-a1142f19-head-to-head-comparison.md) and [spec-comparison grid](./refero-713b66c0-spec-comparison-grid.md) ask the customer to weigh specs between models, and the [spec sheet](./refero-27715bc5-spec-sheet-product-block.md) and [product passport](./refero-0dea72da-product-data-passport.md) document a single product in depth. None of those answer the customer's question "which bundle is right for me?" — this pattern does. For a Terraplex dealer the same scaffold expresses pre-configured kits like "[R-32](../../../products/r-32.md) Solo Kit / R-32 Pro Kit (R-32 + spreader + extra batteries) / R-32 Fleet Kit (2× R-32 + service plan) / [I-19](../../../products/i-19.md) Starter Kit (I-19 + [Part 107 prep enrollment](../../../content/compliance-program.md)) / I-19 Custom-Application Kit" without forcing the buyer to assemble a configuration themselves.

## What to take
- **Product sub-nav above the grid.** Above the kit catalog runs a single horizontal row of small icon+label tabs naming the dealer's product categories (Register / Terminal / Stand / Reader / Accessories / **Hardware Kits** / Compare). The active tab ("Hardware Kits") is bolded; the rest are muted. This sub-nav signals "kits live alongside individual products in the catalog, not as a separate marketing page" — the credibility move is treating bundles as first-class merchandise, not promotional one-offs.
- **Heading + result count + filter dropdown.** Page-level heading "Hardware Kits" (~24–28px, left-aligned) with "Displaying N results" muted-gray subtext inline. A single 1–2-line subhead beneath ("Replace your old POS cash register with a complete POS system kit." / for a dealer "Pre-configured drone systems for first-season operators, multi-rig fleets, and Part 137 commercial applicators."). Right-aligned "Filter by:" dropdown (default value "All") sits at the heading's right edge — minimal filter chrome, single dropdown only.
- **2-column kit grid, generous whitespace.** The kit cards sit in a flat 2-column grid (no borders, no cards-with-shadows — the photographs ARE the card). Each kit gets ~50% page width and 350–450px of vertical breathing room. The pattern's restraint is the whitespace: each kit needs room because the hero photograph carries the bundle's story.
- **Each cell, top-to-bottom:**
  1. **Hero photograph of the full bundle laid out together** — every component visible in one shot (drone + spreader + extra batteries + charger + carry case all positioned in a clean group on a white seamless). White background, soft drop shadow under the group. The photograph must show ALL items in the kit; not a hero shot of the marquee item only. This is the pattern's central move — the buyer sees what they get at a glance.
  2. **Bold kit name** (~18–20px, sentence-case sans-serif): "Restaurant Mobile POS Kit" / for a dealer "R-32 Pro Kit" or "Custom-Application Starter Kit."
  3. **"Starting at $XXX.00"** in muted-gray (~14–15px) — directly below the name. The anchor price is "starting at" because kit configurations may have add-ons; this is the floor.
  4. **"Shop now →"** text-link in saturated brand-blue (no button chrome, no pill — text-link only, with the trailing right-arrow glyph). The text-link signals "click to configure / see full kit details on a dedicated PDP" rather than "add directly to cart." Compose with the [pdp-product-detail hero](../../hero/research/refero-d4c69c08-pdp-product-detail-hero.md) for the destination page.
  5. **Checkmark "what's in / compatible with" bullets** (~14–15px regular weight, 2–4 items max per card). Each bullet leads with a small ✓ glyph in muted-gray. The bullet copy is constrained: compatibility one-liners ("Compatible with: Samsung Galaxy A32 5G" / "USB, Ethernet (router required)") or distinguishing features ("No tablet needed" / "Tableside payments"). NOT a feature list — that belongs on the PDP. These bullets exist to disambiguate this kit from its siblings on the same grid.
- **Bottom: legal-fine-print band on dark.** Below the kit grid, a black full-bleed band carries credit-sale plan disclaimers, financing rates, and any limited-time offer terms in small muted-gray text. For a dealer this is where MAP / MSRP-only disclaimers and [pricing-policy](../../../content/pricing-policy.md) language can sit without breaking the kit grid's clean register. The dark band also visually closes the catalog ("you've reached the end of the list").
- **No "Add to Cart" buttons on cards.** Critical structural decision: the cards link OUT to dedicated kit PDPs, not into cart. This is what differentiates the kit-catalog pattern from a generic e-commerce category listing — kits need configuration, so the catalog's job is presentation, not transaction.

## What NOT to take
- **Don't substitute a single product hero shot.** If the kit photograph shows only the marquee item (the drone) without the included spreader, batteries, charger, etc., the pattern collapses into a generic product-listing page. The bundle photograph IS the affordance — every included item must be visible.
- **Don't put "Add to Cart" buttons on the cards.** Forcing transaction directly on the catalog assumes the buyer doesn't need to configure. For kits with optional add-ons (battery count, service tier, training enrollment), the catalog must route to a PDP. If the dealer's kits are truly fixed-SKU with no configuration, consider the [feature-card mosaic](./refero-96bb6cc1-feature-card-mosaic.md) instead — its in-the-box strip handles that use case.
- **Don't replace the text-link CTA with a saturated button pill.** A button pill on every card creates ~7 simultaneous CTAs and the page reads as a checkout flow. The flat text-link reads as "directory entry routing to a deeper page" — same restraint as the [horizontal-row directory](../../contact/research/refero-c29ba321-horizontal-row-directory.md) for contact.
- **Don't go past 8 kits.** Past 8 kits the page becomes a sitemap and the buyer can no longer compare. At >8 kits, route by use-case first (Beginner / Fleet / Part 137) and present 3–5 kits per filter — that's the moment the filter dropdown stops being decoration and starts being load-bearing.
- **Don't expand each card's checkmark list beyond 4 bullets.** Long bullet lists turn the catalog into a comparison grid; for that use case the [spec-comparison grid](./refero-713b66c0-spec-comparison-grid.md) is the right pattern. The kit catalog's bullets exist to disambiguate, not to compare.
- **Don't import Square's specific blue link color (#55A1DF).** The transferable move is text-link CTA in brand-accent — the color comes from the archetype.

## Adaptation notes (cross-archetype)
- **CLEAN**: native fit. White page background, charcoal kit names, neutral-gray "Starting at" subtext, single brand-navy on "Shop now →" text-link. Checkmark glyphs in muted-gray. The legal band at the bottom drops to dark-gray with light-gray text — corporate, formal, the right register for institutional buyers configuring fleet purchases. See [CLEAN archetype](../../../archetypes/clean.md).
- **FIELD**: cream page background (~#F4EFE7), warm-charcoal kit names, single warm-amber on the text-link CTA. Kit photographs use FIELD's warm-interior light register (golden-hour bench shots of the bundle laid out on barnwood or canvas, not seamless white). The checkmark glyphs warm to terracotta. Legal band closes on dark cream with warm-amber rule above the disclaimer copy. Pairs naturally with the [hands-on craft hero](../../hero/research/refero-be842c1e-hands-on-craft-hero.md) on the parent landing page — hero shows the operator mid-use, kit catalog shows the bundles that get them there. See [FIELD archetype](../../../archetypes/field.md).
- **TERRAIN**: cream-beige page background, deep-charcoal kit names, single terrain-clay on the text-link CTA. Kit photographs use TERRAIN's environmental register — bundles laid out on slate, sandstone, or weathered wood with a single contour-line behind the group at low opacity. The filter dropdown's chrome picks up a 1px topographic-line motif at the dropdown's edge. Legal band closes on deep terrain-green. Pairs with the [topographic layered-bg hero](../../hero/research/refero-ae84b62a-topographic-layered-bg.md). See [TERRAIN archetype](../../../archetypes/terrain.md).
- **PATRIOT**: cream off-white page background, deep-navy kit names, single barn-red on the text-link CTA. Kit photographs stay on white seamless (PATRIOT's heritage register reads as catalog-formal, not editorial). Checkmark glyphs in deep-navy. The "starting at" anchor price reads with PATRIOT's MSRP-only restraint — no crossed-out original prices, no "save $X" pills (those would conflict with [content/pricing-policy.md](../../../content/pricing-policy.md)). Legal band closes on deep-navy with cream text. The kit names themselves can lean editorial-named ("Homesteader Starter Kit / Heartland Fleet Kit / Commercial Applicator Kit"). See [PATRIOT archetype](../../../archetypes/patriot.md).
- **TACTICAL**: white-on-near-black inversion — the page background goes near-black (~#0E0E0E), kit names in pure white at semibold weight in a precision-sans, single steel-cyan on the text-link CTA. Kit photographs gain a faint single-point top-down light (rather than the soft seamless of CLEAN) so the equipment reads as field-deployment-ready. Checkmark glyphs become corner-bracket glyphs (`⌐` / `⌐⌐` rather than ✓) to match the archetype's bracket motif. Legal band stays near-black with mid-gray fine print. See [TACTICAL archetype](../../../archetypes/tactical.md).
- **FORGE**: invert fully to a near-black background with ember-orange on the text-link CTAs and ember-orange checkmark glyphs. Kit photographs gain a faint ember-rim light on the bundles' metal edges — single accent moment, otherwise pure monochrome. The filter dropdown's chrome picks up an ember-glow underline. Legal band closes on pure black. Pairs with the [hardware-lineup dark hero](../../hero/research/refero-0945a36c-hardware-lineup-dark-hero.md) and the [emblem program hero](../../hero/research/refero-c1c2abcf-emblem-program-hero.md) for a full FORGE page rhythm. See [FORGE archetype](../../../archetypes/forge.md).

## Source brief
[Square's `/shop/hardware/us/en/pos-kits`](https://squareup.com/shop/hardware/us/en/pos-kits) — Square presents 7 pre-configured POS hardware kits in a 2-column grid above a dark legal-fine-print band, each kit anchored by a "Starting at $X" floor price and 2–4 checkmark bullets. The structural move (every card = one pre-configured bundle SKU, every photograph shows all included items, every CTA is a text-link routing to a configurable PDP) is reusable for a Terraplex dealer selling [R-32](../../../products/r-32.md) and [I-19](../../../products/i-19.md) as pre-configured kits aimed at distinct buyer-types (first-season operator / fleet manager / [Part 137 commercial applicator](../../../content/compliance-program.md)) — the same affordance the dealer's customer needs when their question is "which bundle do I start with?" rather than "which model do I pick?" or "which spec wins?"
