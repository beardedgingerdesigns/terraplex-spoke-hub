## Why this is library-worthy
A documentation-led product-block pattern: instead of marketing feature bands, the entire page reads as a technical spec sheet. Grayscale line-art illustrations of every component in an "In the box" grid → flat label/value rows for each capability category → annotated CAD-style dimensional drawing → support and buy CTAs at the bottom. The restraint IS the affordance — engineering documentation register reads as honest and verifiable in a way feature-grid marketing doesn't. Travels across every archetype because it has no decorative chrome to clash with archetype palettes. Closes the [product-block × field](../../../archetypes/field.md) and [product-block × patriot](../../../archetypes/patriot.md) gaps from a different angle than the existing [alternating-pastel-stripes](./refero-4bcd783f-alternating-pastel-stripes.md) (lifestyle-led) and [dark-feature-bands](./refero-eb12db31-dark-feature-bands.md) (cinematic-led) patterns.

## What to take
- Open with a simple two-line product header: bold product name (~32–40px) and a sentence-case "[Product] Technical Specifications" subhead in the same heavy sans. No hero photograph, no marketing tagline — the page IS the documentation.
- "In the box" grid: 6–10 grayscale line-art illustrations of every component shipped in the package (drone body, controller, batteries, charger, propellers, accessories, mounting hardware), each with a small caption beneath. Consistent stroke weight (~1.5px), flat 2D perspective, no shading or color. Items align on a uniform baseline grid.
- Stack the rest of the page as flat label/value rows — each section is a left-aligned heading (~18–20px bold) opposite a right-column content block. Sections in order: "Optional Cable" (or accessories callout), "Accepts" (compatibility / payment / connection types with small icon-and-label rows), "Processing fees" or "Operating costs" (numeric label/value pairs), "Dimensions", "Connectivity", "Software", "Color", "Materials", "Weight", "Power modes", "Countertop space" (or operating-clearance equivalent), "Mounting options", "Accessories", "Support".
- The "Dimensions" section is the only place a non-line-art illustration shows up: a CAD-style technical drawing of the product with measurement annotations in inches and millimeters, dimension lines pulled outside the silhouette with leader arrows. This is the credibility-loaded moment of the page — drawings replace photographs because the dealer is selling specifications, not vibes.
- A single sticky-or-not "Buy [Product]" CTA appears once near the bottom: filled pill button with crossed-out original price + sale price. Above it, a small line of muted gray for any limited-time qualifier ("$XXX off through April 24"). One CTA per page; the table-of-specs above does the conversion work by removing remaining unknowns.
- Vertical rhythm is generous (~64–80px between sections) and section breaks come from white space + the bold heading alone — no horizontal rules, no pastel section fills.

## What NOT to take
- Don't add lifestyle photography between sections. The pattern's restraint depends on the engineering-documentation register; a single golden-hour drone shot pulls the page back into marketing-product-page territory and dilutes the spec-sheet credibility move.
- Don't replace the line-art "In the box" grid with photographic component thumbnails. The grayscale line art IS the affordance — it reads as documentation, not catalog. Photographic thumbnails turn the section into a product gallery.
- Don't compress the flat label/value rows into a feature-card grid. The single-column section/heading rhythm is what makes the page scannable; switching to cards turns it into yet another generic product-detail layout.
- Don't drop the dimensional drawing in favor of a bullet list of measurements. The annotated drawing is what differentiates "spec sheet" from "feature list" — text dimensions are forgettable, drawings with leader lines are not.
- Don't put a CTA on every section. The pattern reads as a reference document; a sales loop on every scroll position breaks the register.
- Don't import Square's specific blue accent or yellow "$100 off!" pill — those are brand-specific. The transferable move is the spec-sheet structure, not the chrome.

## Adaptation notes (cross-archetype)
- FIELD: cream background (#F4F1EC or similar), warm-charcoal line-art (slightly looser stroke weight ~2px so the drawings read as hand-drawn rather than CAD-precise), single warm-amber accent on the Buy CTA only. The dimensional drawing stays precise — FIELD's hand-feel applies to component illustrations, not measurements. Pairs naturally with the [manifesto-creed hero](../../hero/research/refero-b86571f2-manifesto-creed-hero.md) (warm photographic hero) followed by this spec sheet for the body of the page. See [FIELD archetype](../../../archetypes/field.md).
- CLEAN: native fit. White background, neutral-gray line-art at 1.5px stroke, single brand accent on links and the Buy CTA. The dimensional drawing reads as engineering reference at face value. See [CLEAN archetype](../../../archetypes/clean.md).
- TERRAIN: works as the "light band" in the alternating rhythm — the spec sheet is naturally a long calm light-band moment, with a topographic line motif at micro-scale used as the dimension-leader-line style (subtle contour-curve instead of straight leader lines on the dimensional drawing). See [TERRAIN archetype](../../../archetypes/terrain.md).
- PATRIOT: cream background (warm off-white), deep-navy line-art at 1.5–2px stroke, single barn-red accent reserved for the Buy CTA only. Section headings in deep navy. The flat label/value rows naturally fit PATRIOT's chrome-restraint rule — no flag motifs needed because the documentation register carries the page. Add a thin navy top-rule above the "Technical Specifications" header if the rest of the dealer site uses flag-derived rules elsewhere. Pairs with the [manifesto-creed hero](../../hero/research/refero-b86571f2-manifesto-creed-hero.md) for a heritage-led page rhythm. See [PATRIOT archetype](../../../archetypes/patriot.md).
- TACTICAL: native fit with tighter geometry. Precision-sans for headings, sharper Buy CTA rectangle (~6px corner radius instead of pill), monochrome black/white only with a single steel or cyan accent on the CTA. The dimensional drawing gets dotted leader lines and adds a small grid behind the silhouette so the spec sheet reads as engineering-drawing-grade documentation. See [TACTICAL archetype](../../../archetypes/tactical.md).
- FORGE: invert to a near-black background (#0E0E0E) with white line-art and ember-orange accent on the Buy CTA only. Section headings white; body text muted gray. The dimensional drawing's leader lines pick up a faint ember rim where the dimension extends past the silhouette — single accent moment, otherwise monochrome. Pairs with the [hardware-lineup dark hero](../../hero/research/refero-0945a36c-hardware-lineup-dark-hero.md) for a full FORGE page rhythm. See [FORGE archetype](../../../archetypes/forge.md).

## Source brief
Square's [/hardware/register/specs](https://squareup.com/us/en/hardware/register/specs) page — the technical specifications page for the Square Register POS hardware. Surfaces because the structural pattern (line-art "In the box" grid → flat label/value sections → CAD-style dimensional drawing → single Buy CTA) is reusable for a Terraplex dealer's [R-32](../../../products/r-32.md) or [I-19](../../../products/i-19.md) spec page without dragging Square's payments-vendor copy or blue accent along for the ride. The R-32 and I-19 sell-sheet PDFs already document the same kind of content (in-the-box accessories, dimensions, connectivity, software, weight, materials) — this pattern is the natural web treatment of that information.
