## Why this is library-worthy
A product-block pattern that surfaces **the breadth of compatible / supported third-party products** as a dark cinematic tile grid — 8–12 cells in a 4-up grid, each cell a clean cutout product photograph (third-party headphones / earbuds / drones / tools / accessories) on dark with brand-name + product-name caption beneath. Solves the missing dark-archetype "ecosystem / compatibility" gap: existing dark-archetype product-block patterns ([dark-feature-bands](./refero-eb12db31-dark-feature-bands.md), [spec-sheet](./refero-27715bc5-spec-sheet-product-block.md), [head-to-head comparison](./refero-a1142f19-head-to-head-comparison.md), [spec-comparison-grid](./refero-713b66c0-spec-comparison-grid.md)) all handle the dealer's OWN products. This pattern handles "what works with our product" — adjacent / compatible / partner equipment that the dealer's customers also use. The light-archetype counterpart already exists as the [color-tile category row](./refero-dc38a5c1-color-tile-category-row.md), but that pattern is bright cream-base saturated tiles. This is the dark cinematic counterpart for FORGE / TACTICAL dealers who want to surface compatibility breadth without breaking the dark-dominant register.

## What to take
- Open with a tight contained dark hero band (~25vh, near-black background): centered or upper-left bold sentence-case heading ("Enabled devices" → for a dealer "Compatible accessories" / "Works with" / "Approved third-party equipment"), 2-line muted-gray subhead beneath ("Connect your personalized sound profile to SoundID enabled devices" → for a dealer "All accessories certified for use with R-32 / I-19 fleet operations"). No CTA in the band — the grid below IS the affordance.
- **Compatibility tile grid** — the pattern's distinguishing move. Below the hero, a 4-column × 2-or-3-row grid of equal-width cells (~8–12 cells total). Each cell is:
  1. A near-black background (~#0E0E0E or matching the page) with a thin hairline edge between cells (~1px charcoal at ~#1F1F1F, very subtle — gives the grid structure without competing with the products).
  2. A SINGLE product photograph centered in the upper ~70% of the cell — the third-party product cutout on transparent background, dropped onto the cell's dark color. Photographs are clean, no environmental context, no shadow on backdrop, no rim light from the product side. The product floats on dark.
  3. Brand-name caption in muted-gray sans-serif (~14px) at the bottom-left of the cell ("Corsair" / "1MORE" / "grell" / "Drop + THX" / "Monoprice" → for a dealer "DJI" / "Hylio" / "Bestway" / "GTEEX OEM"). Product-name in white below the brand-name (~16px sentence-case bold) ("HS65 SURROUND" / "EVO" / "PistonBuds" / "ColorBuds 2 Earbuds" → for a dealer "Ground Station Pro" / "Spreader Hopper 80L" / "Battery Quick-Charge Adapter").
  4. NO CTA on the cell. The cell is informational — it tells the customer "this works with our equipment." If clicking is needed, the entire cell is the click target (link to the third-party product page or to a dealer-side accessory listing).
- Cells are uniform width and aspect ratio (~4:5 or square). Generous internal padding (~32px on each side of the product cutout) so each product reads as its own object rather than crowding the cell edge.
- The grid stretches the full page width (~1280–1440px container) with consistent gutter — the visual breadth is the affordance.
- Below the grid, an OPTIONAL single muted-gray informational line ("Don't see your equipment? [Submit it →]" → for a dealer "Not seeing your equipment? [Submit a compatibility request →]") — the only secondary CTA on the page, kept tiny.
- Page footer is monochrome dark — single-line "Quick Links" + "Elsewhere" + "Worldwide site" navigation. The compatibility grid IS the entire page; the footer is the only chrome around it.

## What NOT to take
- Don't import this pattern without real third-party compatibility. The grid's whole credibility move depends on every cell being a real product the dealer has tested / approved / supported. Made-up partner names or aspirational compatibility ("would work with most generic batteries") collapse the trust register the entire grid is built on. If the dealer's actual compatibility list is short (3–4 products), use the light-archetype [color-tile category row](./refero-dc38a5c1-color-tile-category-row.md) on a 4-up tile band instead — that pattern works at smaller counts.
- Don't substitute icons for product cutouts. The photograph IS the credibility move — the customer sees "yes, that's the exact battery I own; it's on the list." Icons collapse the section into a generic capability grid that any SaaS landing carries.
- Don't add brand logos in place of product photographs. Logos read as "brands we've partnered with" (marketing register); product photographs read as "specific products we've tested" (engineering register). The pattern's affordance is the latter — the dealer is taking a position on individual SKUs, not on brand alliances.
- Don't put more than 12 cells in the grid. The pattern's affordance depends on each product being scannable at a glance — 13+ cells turn the grid into a catalog and the cellular legibility dies. If the dealer's compatibility list exceeds 12, group products into categories (Accessories / Batteries / Controllers / Spreaders) and surface the top 3 per category, with a "View all [N] compatible products →" link.
- Don't lighten the page background to gray or cream. The pattern's affordance depends on the dark backdrop — the cutout-on-dark composition lifts each product cleanly. On a light background the cutouts lose contrast and the grid reads as a generic e-commerce category page (the [color-tile category row](./refero-dc38a5c1-color-tile-category-row.md) is the light-archetype equivalent and uses saturated color tiles instead of monochrome dark cells; don't try to merge the two registers).
- Don't import Sonarworks's literal "Enabled devices" copy or the headphones/earbuds product family — those are audio-vertical-specific. The transferable move is the structural pattern (dark cinematic tile grid + cutout product photographs + brand + product caption + no CTA). For a Terraplex dealer the grid surfaces compatible base stations, batteries, controllers, spreader add-ons, RTK kits, weather-station integrations — equipment the dealer has tested but doesn't sell.
- Don't combine this with the [color-tile category row](./refero-dc38a5c1-color-tile-category-row.md) on the same page — both surface a row / grid of products with cutout photography. Pick one based on archetype: this dark version for FORGE / TACTICAL / dark-leaning TERRAIN; the bright color-tile row for FIELD / PATRIOT / CLEAN / light-leaning TERRAIN.
- Don't add hover-flip, tilt, or animation on the cells. The pattern's restraint is the affordance — motion adds noise and shifts the register from "engineering reference" to "marketing card."

## Adaptation notes (cross-archetype)
- FORGE: native fit. Page background near-black (~#0E0E0E), cells in matching near-black with thin hairline edges (~#1F1F1F). Brand-name caption in muted gray; product-name in white. ONE product per row carries an ember-orange "Featured" or "New" small chip in the upper-right corner — the only saturated chrome on the grid (and only if the dealer has actual featured / newly-added products to highlight). See [FORGE archetype](../../../archetypes/forge.md). Pairs with the [hardware-lineup dark hero](../../hero/research/refero-0945a36c-hardware-lineup-dark-hero.md) above (introduces the dealer's lineup; this grid shows what works with it).
- TACTICAL: native fit with tighter geometry. Page background near-black, cells with sharper rectangular edges (no soft hairline blur — the gridlines are precise 1px hairlines at ~#1F1F1F). Precision-sans for brand and product names in tracked all-caps for brand ("CORSAIR" / "DJI" / "HYLIO"). Add 1px corner brackets to the upper-left and lower-right of each cell (consistent with the archetype's bracket motif) — when the cell is hovered, the brackets brighten to steel-cyan. The "Submit your equipment" footer link is the only place steel-cyan accent shows up on the page. See [TACTICAL archetype](../../../archetypes/tactical.md). Pairs with the [topographic layered-bg hero](../../hero/research/refero-ae84b62a-topographic-layered-bg.md).
- TERRAIN: works as the page's main "dark band" — pair the grid with a single light insert above (a 3-line introductory paragraph on a cream / clay band) and a single light close below (a "View full compatibility matrix" outline-pill CTA on a cream band) so the grid is the dark band sandwiched in the alternating rhythm. Cells use near-black with very subtle topographic-line motif at micro-scale on the cell hairlines (a contour-curve trace at 1px and ~20% opacity replacing the straight hairline). Brand and product captions in clay or sage accent. See [TERRAIN archetype](../../../archetypes/terrain.md). Pairs with the [topographic layered-bg hero](../../hero/research/refero-ae84b62a-topographic-layered-bg.md).
- CLEAN: lighten the cells from near-black to brand-navy (~#1F2C44) on a pale-gray page background — the contrast moves from "dark-on-dark" to "navy-on-pale-gray" but the cutout-product-on-tonal-tile affordance survives. Cells have a thin white hairline border so they read as discrete tiles. Brand and product captions in cream / off-white on the navy cells. The "Submit your equipment" footer link in brand-navy. The pattern works in CLEAN when the dealer wants the engineering / compatibility register without a fully dark page. See [CLEAN archetype](../../../archetypes/clean.md). Pairs with the [centered-screenshot-trio hero](../../hero/research/refero-f619e700-centered-screenshot-trio.md).
- FIELD / PATRIOT: skip the dark version. The cinematic dark cells conflict with both archetypes' cream-default and warm-photographic rules. For warm-archetype compatibility/breadth use the [color-tile category row](./refero-dc38a5c1-color-tile-category-row.md) which uses cream-base saturated tiles instead of dark cells — same compatibility-breadth affordance, warm-archetype-native palette.
