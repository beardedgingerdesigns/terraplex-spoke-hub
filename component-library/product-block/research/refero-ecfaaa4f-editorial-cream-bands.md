## Why this is library-worthy
A light-dominant product-block cadence built from one cream base color with tonal shifts (cream → warm-amber → cream → dark-wood → cream) instead of a multi-color stripe rotation. Each band is either a wide editorial "narrative" moment (intro text + featured product photograph) or a 3-up product card row, and the two band types alternate to give rhythm. No accent-color saturation anywhere — the restraint is the affordance, the photography carries the warmth. Structurally distinct from the [alternating-pastel-stripes pattern](./refero-4bcd783f-alternating-pastel-stripes.md) (which rotates 3–5 different pastel hues) and the [dark-feature-bands pattern](./refero-eb12db31-dark-feature-bands.md) (which is dark-dominant). Closes the product-block × FIELD / CLEAN / TERRAIN / PATRIOT gaps at 1 with a structurally distinct light-dominant alternative.

## What to take
- Establish ONE base color (warm cream, ~#F4EFE7) for the page background. Don't introduce a second hue; instead, introduce tonal "darker bands" at ~30–40% page intervals — full-bleed warm-amber or dark-wood photographic moments that visually inhale before the cream resumes. The eye reads the tonal shift as a section break without needing a rule.
- Alternate two band types down the page:
  - **Editorial narrative band** (~70vh): text column ~40% on one side (kicker label + sentence-case headline at ~32–40px + 2–3 line paragraph + secondary text-link CTA "Discover X →"), photographic product hero ~60% on the other. Each narrative band uses a different product as its focus; the two halves swap left-right every other narrative band.
  - **3-up product card row** (~50vh): three equal-width cards in a single row, each card a square or 4:5 photograph of one product variant on a tonal photographic backdrop (warm-amber, neutral cream, or dark-wood per variant) with the bottle/equipment silhouetted in the foreground. Below the photograph, a tight monospaced or sentence-case caption: product name → 1-line descriptor → size/spec → price.
- Photography rules: every product photo is a still-life on a tonal solid background — no environmental scenes, no people, no motion. Lighting is warm and directional (one side-light source). Shadows are soft, not hard-edged. The camera angle is consistent across the page (slight overhead, not eye-level).
- Typography: a single weight pairing across the entire page — light-or-regular sans-serif body + medium sans-serif headlines. No display serif, no all-caps headlines (kickers may be all-caps but stay tiny). The kicker label above each headline is the only place tracking widens.
- CTA pattern: text-link with arrow ("Discover X →") inside narrative bands; outlined small pill ("Browse all", "Speak with a consultant") at section-end moments. NO solid colored buttons anywhere — the page's restraint depends on every CTA reading as quiet.
- Vertical rhythm is generous (~96–120px between bands) and section breaks come from tonal shifts in the photography, not from rules or gradients. The cadence: narrative → 3-up → narrative → tonal-dark moment → narrative → 3-up → close.

## What NOT to take
- Don't import Aesop's specific apothecary aesthetic (cream-labeled brown bottles, monochrome typography on the labels themselves) — that's brand chrome. The transferable move is the band cadence and tonal-shift palette discipline, not the bottle styling.
- Don't introduce a saturated accent color on top of this pattern (e.g. a barn-red CTA, an ember-orange chip). The cadence's whole credibility is "no accent fights the photography" — adding one collapses the editorial register into a generic SaaS feature page.
- Don't use this pattern with fewer than ~4 bands. The alternation rhythm needs at least 2 narrative bands + 2 product-card rows to read as deliberate; with fewer, it just looks like an unstyled e-commerce category page.
- Don't pair this with the [alternating-pastel-stripes pattern](./refero-4bcd783f-alternating-pastel-stripes.md) on the same page — they're competing rhythm systems (one-base-tonal-shift vs many-base-color-rotation). Pick one.
- Don't drop people / lifestyle photography into the 3-up grids. The pattern's affordance is "still-life product on a tonal backdrop"; lifestyle photos pull the band into "campaign hero" territory and break the catalog-with-narrative read.
- The dark-dominant requirement of FORGE/TACTICAL conflicts with this pattern's cream-base affordance. Forcing the alternation onto a black background loses the tonal-shift logic — there's nowhere for the photography to "darken into" because the base is already dark. For dark archetypes use the [dark-feature-bands pattern](./refero-eb12db31-dark-feature-bands.md) instead.

## Adaptation notes (cross-archetype)
- FIELD: native fit. Cream base (~#F4EFE7), tonal shifts to warm amber (~#C4876C) and dark wood (~#3A2B1F) for the photography moments. Headlines in deep charcoal sans-serif, body in slightly muted brown-grey. Product photography is drone hardware on warm-amber paper-sweep backdrops, controllers on cream cloth, accessories on weathered-wood. The 3-up card rows show R-32 + I-19 + accessories side by side; the narrative bands carry capability stories ("A trio of swappable battery packs", "Built for the field"). See [FIELD archetype](../../../archetypes/field.md). Pairs with the [manifesto-creed hero](../../hero/research/refero-b86571f2-manifesto-creed-hero.md) above.
- CLEAN: neutralize the palette — base goes to off-white (~#F8F8F6), tonal shifts to soft grey (~#E5E5E2) and a single deeper neutral (~#1A1A1A) for the dark photographic moment. Headlines in brand-charcoal, the only accent is a single brand color on the secondary text-link CTAs (brand-navy on "Discover →"). Photography stays still-life but on neutral grey backdrops, not warm amber. The 3-up card rows show product variants with technical-spec captions (model name → key spec → price). See [CLEAN archetype](../../../archetypes/clean.md). Pairs naturally with the [centered-screenshot-trio hero](../../hero/research/refero-f619e700-centered-screenshot-trio.md).
- TERRAIN: the cream / dark-tonal alternation IS the archetype's light/dark band rhythm at section scale. Add a thin topographic-line motif as the bottom rule of each tonal shift (where cream meets dark-amber) — at 1px and ~30% opacity, so it reads as a contour-line trace rather than a divider. The dark-tonal moments should pull toward clay/sage (TERRAIN's organic palette) rather than warm amber (FIELD's). See [TERRAIN archetype](../../../archetypes/terrain.md). Pairs with the [topographic layered-bg hero](../../hero/research/refero-ae84b62a-topographic-layered-bg.md) for an opening dark moment that the cream bands then alternate against.
- PATRIOT: cream base (~#F4EFE7), tonal-shift moments use deep navy (~#1F2C44) as the dark-band photographic backdrop, NOT warm amber. The cadence becomes cream → navy-photographic → cream → navy-photographic. Headlines in navy, body in charcoal. The only saturated color is a barn-red micro-accent on the kicker labels in the narrative bands (1 in 3 kickers carries red; the other 2 stay charcoal) — restraint is the move, not flag-stripe stripes between every band. The 3-up card row product photography is golden-hour American-farmland stills (drone on weathered-wood porch, controller on a tractor seat, accessories on a barn floor) — heritage, not corporate. See [PATRIOT archetype](../../../archetypes/patriot.md). Pairs with the [manifesto-creed hero](../../hero/research/refero-b86571f2-manifesto-creed-hero.md) above and the [photo-mosaic services pattern](../../services/research/refero-2fdf9ace-photo-mosaic-services.md) below.
- FORGE / TACTICAL: skip — light-dominant by definition. See the [dark-feature-bands pattern](./refero-eb12db31-dark-feature-bands.md) for the dark-archetype counterpart.

## Source brief
Aesop's [/fr/en/c/home/](https://www.aesop.com/fr/en/c/home/) main category page — a Nordic-apothecary brand whose product-page restraint is architecturally relevant. Most directly transferable to FIELD and PATRIOT dealers showing 3–6 product variants (R-32, I-19, controllers, batteries, spreader add-ons) without tipping into either SaaS feature-grid or e-commerce-catalog registers. The library-worthy move is the structural cadence (cream-base with tonal-shift photographic bands → narrative band ↔ 3-up card row alternation → no accent saturation), reusable for a dealer who wants product-block depth without per-feature CTAs and stripe-color rotation.
