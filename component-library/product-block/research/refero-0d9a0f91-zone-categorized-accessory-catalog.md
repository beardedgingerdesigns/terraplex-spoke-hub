## Why this is library-worthy
A **zone-categorized OEM accessory / parts catalog** product-block — every item on the page is a first-party accessory or replacement part for ONE marquee product, organized into spatial / functional category headers (FEATURED PRODUCTS → FLOOR MATS → INTERIOR → EXTERIOR → VAULT → WHEELS AND TIRES → PARTS) on a pure-black page with cutout product photography. Fills a real gap: the existing 16 product-block patterns answer "which model? / which spec? / which bundle?" but none answer **"which accessory / part fits my [product] in [this area]?"** The closest neighbors are different patterns — the [compatibility ecosystem grid](./refero-70415597-compatibility-ecosystem-grid.md) handles THIRD-PARTY compatible products (the dealer doesn't sell them, they just verify they work); the [bundle / starter-kit catalog](./refero-27801b0e-bundle-kit-catalog.md) handles pre-configured bundle SKUs (one card per multi-item bundle); the [color-tile category row](./refero-dc38a5c1-color-tile-category-row.md) is a single bright cream-base 4-up row of broad product categories. Zone-categorized accessory catalogs handle the post-purchase merchandising case — the customer already owns the [R-32](../../../products/r-32.md) and wants to find the right replacement battery, propeller, spreader hopper, or hardpoint mount without scrolling through hundreds of unrelated SKUs. Spatial / functional categorization (Power / Spray System / Frame / Cameras / Safety / Parts) is the wayfinding move that scales to 30–60 SKUs without collapsing into a flat scroll.

## What to take
- **Pure-black page background, no chrome between sections.** The entire scrollable region is `#000000` with white type and product cutouts. No gradient bands, no full-bleed photography between sections, no card chrome around products. The dark monochrome IS the visual unification — the only structural separator is the uppercase white category heading sitting alone at the top of each section.
- **One marquee-product nameplate at the top.** The page opens with a horizontal nav strip carrying the marquee product's name (`CYBERTRUCK`) in the brand wordmark followed by a small in-page nav (`CHARGING`, `VEHICLE ACCESSORIES`, `APPAREL`, `LIFESTYLE`) — top-aligned, no hero band, no headline. This signals "you are inside the marquee product's accessory tab, not browsing the dealer's full catalog" and prevents the customer from wondering whether items elsewhere also fit. For a dealer the equivalent is `R-32` / `I-19` nameplate followed by `BATTERIES` / `SPRAY SYSTEM` / `CAMERAS` / `SAFETY KITS` / `PARTS` in a slim secondary strip.
- **Spatial / functional category headers in flat uppercase white.** Each section is anchored by a single line of `~18–22px` uppercase white text, left-aligned (`FEATURED PRODUCTS`, `FLOOR MATS`, `INTERIOR`, `EXTERIOR`, `VAULT`, `WHEELS AND TIRES`, `PARTS`). The headers are non-clickable — they're labels, not filter chips. The categorization is the wayfinding affordance: a customer who knows they need a floor mat scrolls to FLOOR MATS without scanning the surrounding sections. For a Terraplex dealer the analogue is functional zones of the airframe: `POWER` (batteries, charge bricks, adapters), `SPRAY SYSTEM` (tanks, nozzles, pumps), `FRAME` (props, motors, arms), `CAMERAS & SENSORS` (gimbal accessories, lens covers, lidar mounts), `SAFETY & TRAINING` (PPE, hi-viz vests, [Part 137](../../../content/compliance-program.md) ground-crew kits), `PARTS` (replacement SKUs).
- **3-column responsive grid per category, no card chrome.** Within each category, products lay out in a 3-column grid (sometimes 1 or 2 cells for sparse categories). Each "card" is just a centered cutout photograph on black, with the product name in `~12–13px` uppercase white sans-serif beneath, and the price in `~13–14px` muted-gray (`$385`, `$150`, `$8,000–$8,000 +1` for color options) directly under the name. No bordering, no shadow, no hover chrome, no `Add to Cart` button on the grid — the entire cell is the click target routing to a dedicated PDP for the accessory. This restraint is the pattern's central move: the dark monochrome lets each product photograph carry its own weight without competing card chrome.
- **Cutout photography against the page background.** Every accessory is photographed as a clean cutout on `#000000` — the same backdrop as the page itself, so each product reads as a floating object rather than as an image-tile. Lighting is single-point top-down (or slightly off-axis) with subtle ambient fill; no environmental context, no model-in-use shots in the grid. The contained "OUT OF STOCK" and "AVAILABLE LATE 2024" overlays sit in the top-left of the product cutout, not on a separate badge ribbon — they're typography-only labels (`~10–11px` uppercase muted-gray inside a thin charcoal box) so they don't break the monochrome register.
- **Status overlays handle stock realities without breaking the register.** Two status states ride in the top-left of the cutout: `OUT OF STOCK` (for currently-sold-out SKUs) and `AVAILABLE LATE 2024` (for forthcoming SKUs). Both are tiny uppercase typography on a semi-transparent dark backdrop — no red urgency colors, no green availability colors. The visual register stays calm even for sold-out merchandise. For a dealer this matters because seasonal accessory inventory (heated battery wraps in winter, swarm props in spray season) creates predictable scarcity windows; the typography-only overlay handles that without turning the catalog into a sales-pressure page.
- **Mixed category granularity is OK.** Some categories carry 6+ items (`INTERIOR` has 9 in the live page); others carry 1–2 (`FLOOR MATS` has 2, `WHEELS AND TIRES` has 3). The pattern accommodates uneven counts without forcing balance — a sparse section reads as "this is the complete list" rather than as a placeholder. For a dealer with one airframe + one premium spray system, this means it's fine to ship a `CAMERAS & SENSORS` section with 2 items rather than padding to 6 with marketing-thin SKUs.
- **No section dividers, no footer until the end of the page.** Between categories there's just generous vertical whitespace (~80–120px) and the next uppercase header. The black monochrome bridges the whitespace visually; the customer experiences the page as one continuous scroll across all zones rather than as a series of independent product blocks. The only persistent chrome is the top nav strip.

## What NOT to take
- **Don't import this pattern for FIELD or PATRIOT dealers.** The pure-black monochrome is structurally incompatible with FIELD's warm-interior register (cream + amber + golden-hour photography) and PATRIOT's cream / heritage register (off-white + barn-red + deep-navy). FIELD/PATRIOT dealers needing an accessory catalog should adapt the [color-tile category row](./refero-dc38a5c1-color-tile-category-row.md) into a multi-row cream-base catalog with per-row category headers, or run the [editorial cream bands](./refero-ecfaaa4f-editorial-cream-bands.md) pattern with each band carrying one zone.
- **Don't substitute card chrome (borders, shadows, hover lift) for the dark monochrome.** Adding card chrome on top of the dark background turns the pattern into a generic dark e-commerce grid. The restraint — products floating on the page background with no enclosure — is the structural move. Card chrome would also force the cutout photography to fight a second visual layer, dimming the products.
- **Don't add per-cell "Add to Cart" buttons or quantity steppers.** The grid is browse-only; the cell links OUT to a PDP where add-to-cart, configuration (color, length, fit), and stepper UI live. Forcing cart UI onto the catalog cell breaks the "informational catalog" register and assumes the buyer doesn't need to configure (most accessories DO need configuration — battery count, hopper size, lens mount fit). This is the same structural decision as the [bundle / starter-kit catalog](./refero-27801b0e-bundle-kit-catalog.md).
- **Don't replace cutout photography with lifestyle / in-use shots.** Lifestyle photography inside the grid breaks the "every product as a clean object" register. The customer is here to identify the specific SKU that fits their airframe — lifestyle context belongs on the destination PDP, not in the catalog grid.
- **Don't introduce red urgency colors on the OUT OF STOCK badge.** Red breaks the calm monochrome register and conflicts with most dealer archetypes' accent colors. The typography-only treatment scales across FORGE (ember-orange equivalent would shout) / TACTICAL (steel-cyan would feel like an alert) / CLEAN (red would feel pharmaceutical) / TERRAIN (red would clash with terrain-clay). Stick with muted-gray typography.
- **Don't paginate the page or break categories across pages.** The catalog's affordance is "I can see every accessory the dealer offers for this product in one scroll." Pagination forces the customer back into navigation chrome; infinite-scroll-load adds latency. If the accessory count exceeds ~50 items, split the page by airframe (`R-32 Accessories` / `I-19 Accessories`) rather than by pagination.

## Adaptation notes (cross-archetype)
- **FORGE**: native fit. Pure-black page (`#000000`), white uppercase category headers in a forged-sans like Eurostile / Industry, ember-orange (`#FF6A1F`) for prices and the `Cybertruck`-equivalent nameplate, muted-gray for product names. Cutout photographs gain a faint ember-rim light on the bundles' metal edges — single accent moment. The `OUT OF STOCK` overlay uses muted-gray text on a charcoal box (no orange — keeps the urgency calm). Sub-nav strip uses ember-orange underline on the active tab only. Pairs with the [hardware-lineup dark hero](../../hero/research/refero-0945a36c-hardware-lineup-dark-hero.md) and the [emblem program hero](../../hero/research/refero-c1c2abcf-emblem-program-hero.md) for a full FORGE page rhythm. See [FORGE archetype](../../../archetypes/forge.md).
- **TACTICAL**: near-black page (`~#0E0E0E`), white uppercase category headers in a precision-sans (`JetBrains Mono`-style or Inter at semibold), steel-cyan (`#4FB2D9`) for prices and active sub-nav indicator. Cutout photographs gain a faint corner-bracket motif (`⌐` glyph) at the top-left of each cell — matches the archetype's tactical-bracket motif and serves as the stock-status anchor when needed. The `OUT OF STOCK` overlay becomes `OFFLINE` or `UNAVAILABLE` in steel-cyan typography. Sub-nav strip uses a 1px steel-cyan underline. Pairs with the [3d-object dark hero](../../hero/research/refero-ea86d249-3d-object-dark-hero.md) and the [interactive product-demo hero](../../hero/research/refero-d6176d7e-interactive-product-demo-hero.md). See [TACTICAL archetype](../../../archetypes/tactical.md).
- **TERRAIN**: deep terrain-green page (`~#0A1612`) instead of pure black — keeps the dark register but ties it to the archetype's earthen-night palette. White uppercase category headers, terrain-clay (`#C25A2A`) for prices and active sub-nav indicator. Cutout photographs gain a single faint topographic-contour line at low opacity behind the products in `FEATURED PRODUCTS` (only — not on every section, or it becomes wallpaper). The `OUT OF STOCK` overlay uses muted moss-green text. Sub-nav strip uses a terrain-clay underline. Pairs with the [topographic layered-bg hero](../../hero/research/refero-ae84b62a-topographic-layered-bg.md). See [TERRAIN archetype](../../../archetypes/terrain.md).
- **CLEAN**: this is the inversion case. CLEAN normally runs white-dominant, but for the OEM accessory catalog the dark register CAN work IF the dealer's brand has a corporate-dark presence (institutional buyers + clinical photography conventions). Run charcoal-near-black page (`~#161616`) rather than pure black, brand-navy (`#1F3F75`) for prices and active sub-nav, white uppercase category headers in Inter. Cutout photographs stay on `#161616` with no rim light. The `OUT OF STOCK` overlay uses muted-gray on charcoal. If the dealer's brand is genuinely white-dominant and a dark catalog page would feel jarring, switch to a pure-white version of this pattern: white page, charcoal category headers, brand-navy prices, soft-shadow cutouts on white. See [CLEAN archetype](../../../archetypes/clean.md).
- **FIELD / PATRIOT — incompatible.** See the "What NOT to take" section above for the alternative patterns these archetypes should reach for instead. Forcing the dark monochrome onto a FIELD or PATRIOT brand would conflict with the archetype's core register (warm-interior / cream-heritage respectively) and break the rest of the page rhythm.

## Source brief
[Tesla's `/category/vehicle-accessories` (Cybertruck)](https://shop.tesla.com/category/vehicle-accessories) — Tesla organizes 30+ Cybertruck accessories and replacement parts into 7 spatial / functional zone categories (`FEATURED PRODUCTS`, `FLOOR MATS`, `INTERIOR`, `EXTERIOR`, `VAULT`, `WHEELS AND TIRES`, `PARTS`) on a pure-black page with cutout product photography and no card chrome. Each category carries 1–9 items with mixed counts; `OUT OF STOCK` and `AVAILABLE LATE 2024` status overlays use typography-only treatments to avoid breaking the dark monochrome register. The structural move (spatial / functional zone categorization + cutout photography on page-background + no card chrome + typography-only status overlays) is reusable for any Terraplex dealer wanting to surface the complete first-party accessory inventory for [R-32](../../../products/r-32.md) or [I-19](../../../products/i-19.md) — `POWER`, `SPRAY SYSTEM`, `FRAME`, `CAMERAS & SENSORS`, `SAFETY & TRAINING`, `PARTS` zones map naturally to the airframe's functional areas, and the dark register fits dealers running the FORGE / TACTICAL / TERRAIN archetypes.
