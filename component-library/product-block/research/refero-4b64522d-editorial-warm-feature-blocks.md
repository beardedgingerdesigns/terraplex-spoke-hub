## Why this is library-worthy
A product-block cadence in the editorial / heritage register: asymmetric two-column blocks where the photograph dominates, interleaved with a mid-page material/attribute "swatch grid" of close-up photographs and closed by text-only operational rows. The whole page reads more like a magazine spread than a marketing surface — the right tone for [FIELD](../../../archetypes/field.md) and [PATRIOT](../../../archetypes/patriot.md) dealers whose customer trust comes from craft and transparency rather than sales velocity. Adds a fifth structurally-distinct product-block register to the library: editorial / heritage, complementing the existing four — [alternating-pastel-stripes](./refero-4bcd783f-alternating-pastel-stripes.md) (lifestyle / systemic-stripe rhythm), [dark-feature-bands](./refero-eb12db31-dark-feature-bands.md) (cinematic / dark-dominant), [spec-sheet](./refero-27715bc5-spec-sheet-product-block.md) (documentation / engineering-grade), and [head-to-head comparison](./refero-a1142f19-head-to-head-comparison.md) (decision / multi-model disambiguation). The editorial-register slot specifically does work the documentation register can't: a heritage-oriented spec page that needs to feel hand-crafted rather than CAD-precise.

## What to take
- Open with a single asymmetric two-column block: short heading + 2–3 lines of copy on the left ~40%, a single warm-lit photograph (wheat field, raw material close-up, equipment-in-context) on the right ~60%. No CTA in this block — the heading IS the thesis.
- Follow with an inverted block where the photograph dominates ~70% width on one side, with a small text caption (one bold word + one short paragraph) sitting in the remaining ~30% on the other side. The inversion (text-dominant → image-dominant) sets up a "zoom in" rhythm: thesis, then evidence.
- Insert a row of 3 small attribute cards directly below the image-dominant block: each card is a thin horizontal cell with a bold label ("Care for Fiber" / "Engineered for Durability") + one-line description. No icons, no chrome, no CTAs — they're informational dividers between large editorial blocks, not feature cards.
- Mid-page, drop a material/attribute swatch grid: 6–10 close-up photographs in a tight irregular grid (some 1×1, some 1×2 — gutter ~12–16px), each captioned with a small bold label below ("Recycled cotton" / "European linen" / "Composite frame" / "Carbon spar"). Captions are below the photo, never overlaid. The grid proves material/capability breadth visually — replaces a dense feature table with imagery.
- Trail with one full-bleed warm photograph + adjacent text block ("Suppliers" in the source; "Our process" or "Service standards" for a dealer) — single continuous paragraph, no bullets, no CTA.
- Close with a text-only operational rows section: ~6 rows, each a bold heading + 1-line description ("Aerate / Hanging clothes outside is more sustainable…"). No icons, no numbering, no accordions — just typography. Generous vertical rhythm (~24–32px between rows).
- Vertical pacing across the whole page is generous (~80–120px between blocks); section breaks are white space, not rules. No CTAs in the editorial sequence — the page reads as a publication, not a funnel. The first CTA shows up only at the page-level newsletter sign-up at the bottom.

## What NOT to take
- Don't import the literal fashion-textile photographs (wheat fields, fabric drapes, glass shards) — that's Zara Home's category. The transferable move is "warm-lit close-up imagery of the dealer's actual material vocabulary." For a Terraplex dealer this is composite drone frames, swappable batteries, spreader hoppers, GTEEX rotor assemblies, soil swatches, crop close-ups — photographed with the same cinematic restraint.
- Don't add CTAs inside the editorial blocks. The pattern's affordance is "this is not a sales pitch." Adding a "Buy Now" pill to any of the warm-photography blocks collapses the editorial register into a catalog. Sales lives elsewhere on the site (the [head-to-head comparison page](./refero-a1142f19-head-to-head-comparison.md) for multi-model decisions, the [spec-sheet page](./refero-27715bc5-spec-sheet-product-block.md) for single-product Buy CTAs, the contact section for inquiries).
- Don't make the swatch grid uniform. The irregular tile sizing IS the affordance — uniform swatches read as a product gallery, not a material vocabulary. The same affordance that makes the [photo-mosaic services pattern](../../services/research/refero-2fdf9ace-photo-mosaic-services.md) work for services applies here at the material/spec scale.
- Don't replace the close-up photographs with icons. The whole reason the swatch grid earns its space is that it shows the actual material; icon swatches collapse the credibility move into a generic capability grid.
- Don't crowd the attribute-card row with more than 3–4 cells. The cards are dividers, not a feature grid; 5+ cells turn them into the page's main pattern and compete with the editorial blocks.
- Don't pick this pattern when the dealer brief calls for hard specs (dimensions, payload weight, flight time). The editorial register is intentionally vibe-led and prose-led; for spec-heavy pages use the [spec-sheet pattern](./refero-27715bc5-spec-sheet-product-block.md), and for multi-model comparison use the [head-to-head pattern](./refero-a1142f19-head-to-head-comparison.md). This pattern is the right pick when the page's job is positioning ("what we believe", "how we build"), not specification.
- The warm-photography editorial register and predominantly cream/beige palette directly conflicts with FORGE's ember-on-charcoal dark-dominant rule and TACTICAL's monochrome-precision aesthetic. For dark archetypes use the [dark feature bands pattern](./refero-eb12db31-dark-feature-bands.md). For ABABAB systemic rhythm in any light archetype, use the [alternating-pastel-stripes pattern](./refero-4bcd783f-alternating-pastel-stripes.md).

## Adaptation notes (cross-archetype)
- FIELD: native fit. Cream / warm-off-white background (~#F5F1E8), warm-lit operator-and-equipment photographs (golden hour, weathered hands, fenceline shots, drone over crop rows). Attribute cards use amber bold labels on cream. Swatch grid is composed of close-up photographs of the dealer's actual stock — composite frames, batteries, spreader components, rotor assemblies. Operational rows use a hand-drawn tick or a simple en-dash separator. Pairs naturally with the [manifesto-creed hero](../../hero/research/refero-b86571f2-manifesto-creed-hero.md) for a full FIELD page rhythm. See [FIELD archetype](../../../archetypes/field.md).
- PATRIOT: cream background, deep-navy headings throughout. Photography is late-afternoon American-farmland light — equipment-in-operating-context, weathered hands, real farmer shots. Attribute-card labels in deep navy; swatch-grid captions stay charcoal so the navy doesn't fight the imagery. The single closing operational-rows block is the right place for one barn-red accent — use it on the row dividers OR on a single bold operational heading, not both. The full-bleed photograph mid-page is the moment for a real PATRIOT-coded image (American-flag-on-equipment shot only if it's documentary; otherwise straight farmland). Pairs with the [manifesto-creed hero](../../hero/research/refero-b86571f2-manifesto-creed-hero.md). See [PATRIOT archetype](../../../archetypes/patriot.md).
- CLEAN: white background (#FFFFFF), neutral grey body (~#666), single brand accent on the attribute-card labels and the closing newsletter CTA. Photography stays clean and product-forward — drone hardware on a pale studio backdrop, app-screenshot cropped to one device, satellite map. Swatch grid uses brand-tinted close-ups (composite weave, control-surface knurl, battery contact). Operational rows in brand-charcoal. See [CLEAN archetype](../../../archetypes/clean.md).
- TERRAIN: works as a sequence of "light bands" in the alternating light/dark rhythm — every editorial block is a light band; the swatch grid is the page's main "concentrated light" moment; the closing operational-rows section can be the transition into the next dark band. The swatch-grid gutter doubles as a topographic divider line at micro-scale. Pair with [dark feature bands](./refero-eb12db31-dark-feature-bands.md) for the alternating dark bands. See [TERRAIN archetype](../../../archetypes/terrain.md).
- FORGE / TACTICAL: skip — the warm-photography editorial register requires a light/cream-dominant palette by definition. See the [dark feature bands pattern](./refero-eb12db31-dark-feature-bands.md) for the dark-archetype product-block.
