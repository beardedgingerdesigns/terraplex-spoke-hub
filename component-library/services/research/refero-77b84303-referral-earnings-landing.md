## Why this is library-worthy
A referral / affiliate / ambassador program landing where the EARNINGS TABLE — concrete worked examples of what partners actually earn ("publishers with X clicks earn $Y") — is the credibility move that licenses the program. None of the existing 17 services notes cover the "refer customers, earn a cut" use case; the closest siblings ([specialist-marketplace](./refero-d30b8a54-specialist-marketplace.md), [numbered-process-shopper](./refero-d030e2b2-numbered-process-shopper.md), [protection-plan-explainer](./refero-a52e1484-protection-plan-explainer.md)) handle different services-page jobs. Right pick for a dealer-network ambassador program, a "Refer-a-Grower" credit, or an authorized operator-partner recruit page.

## What to take
- Hero with a single-question value-prop headline ("What is the [Program Name]?") + 1-line supporting subhead + one centered CTA. No imagery in the band — the page is information-led, not photography-led; reserve the visual weight for the earnings table further down.
- Three-column "what you get" feature row immediately below the hero: each column is an icon + 2–3-word headline + 1-line description. Total 3 cells; resist the urge to grow to 4–6. The three columns answer "why partner with us," "how does it work," "what do I earn" — the program's three credibility legs.
- Horizontal step-flow indicator on a saturated dark or brand-color band: 4–5 icons in a row with thin connecting lines, captions underneath, no numbers in the band itself. This is the "commission earning process" laid out as a one-glance diagram, distinct from the numbered-stepper later in the page.
- **Earnings table** as the page's anchor: 3–5 rows, alternating subtle row backgrounds, columns are roughly (Partner tier or volume, Activity proxy, Earnings). Real numbers, not "up to $X." This is the load-bearing credibility move — without concrete figures, the program reads as fluff.
- "Getting started" numbered onboarding stack at the bottom: 3–5 numbered circular badges in a vertical stack, each with a 1-line action ("Sign up", "Connect your account", "Create your first referral link"), plus one filled CTA. Different shape from the horizontal step-flow above — the horizontal flow explained "how it works," the vertical stack explains "what YOU do to start."
- One restrained brand-accent color used consistently: on the primary CTA, on the dark step-flow band background, on the earnings-table row tint, and on the numbered badges. No secondary accent on the page.

## What NOT to take
- Don't drop the earnings table for "up to X%" copy. The table IS the page's reason to exist. If real earnings can't be disclosed (NDA, MAP rules, regulatory ambiguity), this pattern isn't the right pick — use [specialist-marketplace](./refero-d30b8a54-specialist-marketplace.md) for a vetted-partner directory framing instead.
- Don't add testimonials or logo walls above the earnings table. The table speaks for itself; social proof before the figures reads as preemptive defense.
- Don't import multiple CTAs of equal weight across the page. There's exactly one primary action — sign up — and it appears in the hero, after the table, and at the bottom of the "Getting started" stack. Anything else is noise.
- Don't carry the literal travel-industry "publishers" terminology. For a Terraplex dealer, partners are "referring operators," "ambassador growers," "field-day hosts," or "co-op partners" — name the audience explicitly in the headline so it doesn't read as generic affiliate boilerplate.
- The currency/country footer dropdown is travel-industry-specific (regional rate cards). For a single-region dealer or a US-only program, drop it.
- Don't pair this pattern with a heavy hero photograph (the [manifesto-creed hero](../../hero/research/refero-b86571f2-manifesto-creed-hero.md) or [hands-on-craft hero](../../hero/research/refero-be842c1e-hands-on-craft-hero.md)). The earnings table is the rhetorical anchor; a competing emotional hero above it dilutes the "here's exactly what you'll make" register. Pair instead with a typographic / restraint-led hero like [type-only stats hero](../../hero/research/refero-14f39897-type-only-stats-hero.md) or [emblem program hero](../../hero/research/refero-c1c2abcf-emblem-program-hero.md).

## Adaptation notes (cross-archetype)
- FIELD: warm cream base, dark-leaf-green accent on the step-band and table-row tint. Replace the travel-brand sans-serif with the archetype's heritage serif for headings. Drop the saturated dark step-flow band; use a warm tan tonal-shift band instead so the page stays light-dominant. See [FIELD archetype](../../../archetypes/field.md).
- CLEAN: native fit. Lightly grey table rows, navy accent on CTA + numbered badges, neutral sans-serif throughout, generous whitespace between sections. See [CLEAN archetype](../../../archetypes/clean.md).
- TERRAIN: drop the page on the alternating cream/dark band cadence — the dark step-flow band IS the dark band; the earnings table sits on the cream band; the hero and onboarding stack are cream too. Add a thin topographic-line divider above each section. See [TERRAIN archetype](../../../archetypes/terrain.md).
- PATRIOT: heritage register works only if the program has a real "grower's roots / co-op heritage" narrative. Cream base, deep red accent ONLY on the CTA and the numbered badges; the step-band gets sage or navy, not red — red on the band would collide with the flag-stripe rhythm. See [PATRIOT archetype](../../../archetypes/patriot.md).
- FORGE: dark mode — charcoal background throughout, ember-orange accent on CTA + numbered badges. The earnings table sits on a slightly-lighter dark panel (`#1A1A1A`) with white-90 text and ember-tinted row dividers. Use this archetype when the program is positioned as "professional operator earning" rather than "neighbor referring neighbor." See [FORGE archetype](../../../archetypes/forge.md).
- TACTICAL: tighten the type — monospace for the earnings figures, condensed/stencil for the badges. Drop the dark step-flow band's saturation; use pure black with thin steel-cyan hairlines instead. The earnings table uses tabular-nums and right-aligned figures so dollar amounts line up vertically. See [TACTICAL archetype](../../../archetypes/tactical.md).
