## Why this is library-worthy
A dark-archetype services pattern that frames every capability as "what we do for [industry/audience X]" rather than as a generic feature list. Opens with a dark hero band carrying an industry-specific isometric illustration on the right + a small breadcrumb-style audience tag ("Hospitality" / "Custom applicators" / "Co-op operations") above the headline; below, the page is a series of dark bands — "Key uses for [product] in [industry]" 3-up cards, "The right [capability] for the job" with a lifestyle mockup, "Warm, personalized [outcome] for [industry]" alternating capability rows with small device mockups, and an enterprise-grade trust band closer. Closes the long-running services × FORGE / TACTICAL gap that prior passes (especially [pass 11](https://github.com/beardedgingerdesigns/terraplex-spoke-hub/commits/main)) couldn't fill — the trick is that the audience-anchored framing gives the page enough specificity to read as services, not as another generic dark SaaS landing.

## What to take
- Open with a contained dark hero band (~50–60vh, near-black ~#0F0F12). Top-left of the band carries a small breadcrumb-style audience tag ("Hospitality", or for a Terraplex dealer "Co-op operations" / "Custom applicators" / "Specialty crops") in muted gray (~#9E9E9E) — the audience anchor sets up the entire page. Below the tag, a 2–3 line headline naming the industry directly ("[Capability X] for the [industry] industry"), a 1–2 line subhead, and a single filled CTA pill ("Give it a try" / "Book a demo"). Right ~50% of the band holds an isometric or 3D illustration specific to the audience (warehouse boxes for logistics, guest-room geometry for hospitality, field-block grid for ag) — bright accent-color blocks against the dark backdrop. The illustration is the only saturated color in the band.
- "Key uses for [product] in [industry]" — 3-up dark cards directly below the hero. Each card has a thin red/orange vertical accent rule on the left edge, a bold lead label ("Automate booking" / "Fly missions" / "Log applications"), a 2-line paragraph, and a small device-mockup chip below — a phone screen, a chat bubble, a controller HUD. Cards live on the same dark backdrop with a thin 1px hairline border (~#2A2A2A); no card shadow, no inner color fill.
- Mid-page, "The right [capability] for the job" — left text column with a checklist-style sub-capability list (3–4 rows, each a small icon + bold label), right column a single colorful product mockup card (gradient border or colorful screenshot). Variety enters through ONE saturated mockup, not through the chrome.
- "Warm, personalized [outcome] for [industry]" — alternating capability rows. Each row is a 2-column band: text-left (~40%) with a small monochrome icon + bold heading + 2-line paragraph; right (~60%) a small device mockup framed as a chat or HUD callout. Rows stack vertically with ~80–120px rhythm; the alternating side-swap is optional (the source keeps the mockup right for all 3 rows, but a swap every-other works for longer lists).
- "Cutting-edge technology for [industry]'s future" — 4-up dark mini-card grid showing data-viz proof: each card is a small chart or numeric callout (uptime, language coverage, latency metric, recognition rate). Cards have the same hairline-border treatment as the Key Uses cards. This is the credibility-by-numbers band; no marketing prose.
- Closer band: dark globe / earth illustration on the left, 3-row capability stack on the right ("Privacy through flexible deployments / Top-notch security / Reliability at scale") each with a small badge row of compliance certs (SOC II / HIPAA / Part 137). The globe carries the "operates anywhere" claim visually so the prose can stay tight.
- Final CTA band: centered headline + subhead + paired filled-light + outline-light CTA pills.

## What NOT to take
- Don't import Cartesia's specific voice-AI lifestyle mockups (chat bubbles, waveform chips, language-flag table) — they're product-specific. The transferable move is "small device-mockup chip per capability," not the chat UI. For a Terraplex dealer this is a controller HUD, a mission-plan timeline, a spray-pattern overlay, a compliance-log row.
- Don't substitute photography for the isometric illustration in the hero. The pattern's affordance depends on a constructed graphical asset — illustration reads as "we built this for you, deliberately"; photography reads as "stock farm landscape." If the dealer can't commission an iso illustration, use a high-contrast geometric pattern or a single product render on a rim-lit dark sweep instead.
- Don't add this pattern when the dealer covers a single audience. The audience-anchored framing breaks down at one audience — the page becomes "what we do" again, which the [kicker-band services pattern](./refero-02ae136e-kicker-band-services.md) handles better. Use this pattern when the dealer surfaces 3+ distinct audience verticals (commercial farms / co-ops / custom applicators / specialty crops), one per page.
- Don't put more than one CTA in any individual capability row. The pattern's restraint depends on the dark cadence carrying the page; per-row CTAs fight the rhythm.
- Don't use this pattern for FIELD or PATRIOT — the dark-dominant backdrop conflicts with both archetypes' cream-default and warm-photographic rules. For warm-archetype services use the [photo-mosaic services](./refero-2fdf9ace-photo-mosaic-services.md) (irregular mosaic) or [kicker-band services](./refero-02ae136e-kicker-band-services.md) (alternating bands) patterns instead.
- Don't try to mix this with [three-tier inverted-middle](./refero-15fcbffc-three-tier-inverted-middle.md) on the same page. Tier cards introduce a pricing register; this pattern is positioning-led. Pick one.

## Adaptation notes (cross-archetype)
- FORGE: native fit. Push the iso illustration toward ember-warm gradient blocks (golden-hour cubes against #0E0E0E). Single ember-orange accent on the hero CTA pill, the Key Uses card-rule, and the 4-up data-viz card chart highlights — everywhere else stays monochrome white-on-charcoal. The compliance badge row at the closer becomes ember-stamped instead of muted gray. Pairs naturally with the [hardware-lineup dark hero](../../hero/research/refero-0945a36c-hardware-lineup-dark-hero.md) above and the [dark feature bands product-block](../../product-block/research/refero-eb12db31-dark-feature-bands.md) for adjacent product pages. See [FORGE archetype](../../../archetypes/forge.md).
- TACTICAL: native fit with tighter geometry. Iso illustration uses precision blocks at matched scale and a single steel-cyan gradient (no warmth). Card chrome gets corner brackets at the hairline border corners (consistent with the archetype's bracket motif). Headlines in precision-sans, monochrome white-on-near-black with a single steel-cyan accent on the active state and CTA. The 4-up data-viz card grid reads as a tracking display rather than a marketing band — small numeric cells on dark, no gradients. See [TACTICAL archetype](../../../archetypes/tactical.md). Pairs with the [topographic layered-bg hero](../../hero/research/refero-ae84b62a-topographic-layered-bg.md).
- TERRAIN: works as the page's "dark band" half of the alternating rhythm. After every 2 dark capability rows, drop a single cream-or-clay light insert with a topographic divider (a short editorial paragraph or stat callout); the dark cadence resumes. The iso illustration in the hero picks up the archetype's clay/sage accents. See [TERRAIN archetype](../../../archetypes/terrain.md).
- CLEAN: invert to a light variant — neutral light backgrounds (~#F8F8F6) replace dark, hairline borders stay dark gray (~#D4D4D4), brand-navy accent on the audience tag and CTA. The iso illustration becomes a pale-tinted geometric block composition. The pattern works in CLEAN because the structural cadence (audience-anchored hero → key-uses 3-up → capability rows with mockups → data-viz proof grid → trust closer) is archetype-neutral once the dark backdrop is removed. Pairs with the [centered-screenshot-trio hero](../../hero/research/refero-f619e700-centered-screenshot-trio.md). See [CLEAN archetype](../../../archetypes/clean.md).
- FIELD / PATRIOT: skip — the dark-dominant aesthetic and constructed-illustration register conflict with both archetypes' cream-default and warm-photographic rules. For warm-archetype services use the [photo-mosaic services](./refero-2fdf9ace-photo-mosaic-services.md) or [kicker-band services](./refero-02ae136e-kicker-band-services.md) patterns instead.

## Source brief
Cartesia's [/industries/hospitality](https://cartesia.ai/industries/hospitality) page — an industry-vertical landing for an AI voice-platform. The library-worthy move is the structural cadence (dark audience-anchored hero → "key uses in [industry]" 3-up cards → "right [capability] for the job" mockup band → alternating capability rows with device mockups → data-viz proof grid → trust closer), reusable for a Terraplex dealer's [Part 137 service](../../../content/compliance-program.md), [operator training](../../../content/service-patterns.md), or fleet-management page when the dealer's brief is "we do this for [audience]" rather than "here are our generic capabilities." Closes the long-deferred services × FORGE / TACTICAL gap from prior curator passes — most directly relevant for dealers in the [49-location Terraplex network](../../../research/manufacturers/terraplex.md) who serve distinct audience verticals (commercial farms vs co-ops vs custom applicators vs specialty crops) where each audience earns its own page.
