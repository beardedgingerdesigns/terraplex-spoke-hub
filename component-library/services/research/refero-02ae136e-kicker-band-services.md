## Why this is library-worthy
A services-page structure that frames each capability as its own category-band with a small uppercase kicker label → bold short heading → 2–3 line paragraph → side photo, alternating image-left / image-right between bands. Mid-page, a single dark capability sub-grid (3-column dense list) breaks the rhythm and shows breadth without fragmenting the main service narrative. End-cap is a real case study (one photo, one paragraph) — proof, not pitch. Closes the [services × patriot](../../../archetypes/patriot.md) 0-gap and adds a non-tier-card services pattern for every other archetype too.

## What to take
- Open the services page with a full-bleed hero photograph that has 3 small numeric stats overlaid in the lower third (e.g. "65+ Commercial Installations", "GWh Energy Deployed", "Industrial Sites in Operation"). Stats use a real-looking metric the dealer can defend; otherwise drop the overlay.
- Each service category gets its own band: a tiny all-caps kicker label (~12px, muted) on top, a bold sentence-case heading (~28–32px) below, a 2–3 line paragraph, and an outlined CTA pill ("Learn More", "Order Now"). The image lives in the other half of the band; alternate sides every 2 bands so the rhythm reads ABAB.
- Mid-page, insert a single dark capability sub-grid: a black or near-black full-bleed band with one product hero-shot on the left and a 3-column × 2-row capability list on the right. Each capability cell is a small bold label + one-line description (e.g. "Peak Shaving — Discharge during peak hours…"). This is where the 6–10 long-tail capabilities go, NOT in the main alternating bands.
- Trail with a single case-study band: photograph at full width, then below it a short paragraph or two-up of customer-name + one-line outcome. No logos, no quotes — just one customer, one photograph, one paragraph.
- Close with a long inquiry form (Name / Phone / Address / State / ZIP / Country / Role / "Tell us about your project") — the form length itself signals "real conversation, not a lead funnel."

## What NOT to take
- Don't import Tesla's literal industrial product photography (battery containers, server-room shots) — adapt the photographic concept ("equipment in operating context") to the dealer's domain (drone hardware on dock, operator at controller, aerial spray pattern).
- Don't repeat the dark capability sub-grid more than once on the page. Its job is to break the rhythm; using it twice just creates a second main pattern.
- Don't fabricate hero-overlay stats. If the dealer can't defend "65+ installations" with real numbers, drop the overlay entirely — the alternating bands carry the page without it.
- Don't import all-caps everywhere; the kicker label is the only all-caps element. Headings are sentence-case so the page reads conversational, not corporate.
- Don't add a CTA to every band. Reserve the pill CTA for the 1–2 bands that genuinely warrant a click; on the others, the kicker+heading+paragraph stands alone.

## Adaptation notes (cross-archetype)
- CLEAN: native fit — neutral light backgrounds, single brand accent on the kicker labels, clean equipment photos with soft drop shadow. Dark capability sub-grid stays dark — it's the deliberate inversion in the page rhythm.
- FIELD: warm-cream backgrounds, amber kickers, photography is warm-lit operator-in-field shots (golden-hour drone launch, hands on equipment). Replace the dark capability sub-grid with a deeper cream sub-band — FIELD's no-dark-dominant rule means the inversion goes light-to-deeper-cream rather than light-to-black. See [FIELD archetype](../../../archetypes/field.md).
- TERRAIN: this maps natively onto the alternating light/dark archetype rhythm — the alternating service bands ARE the light/dark bands, with a topographic divider rule between every pair. The dark capability sub-grid is the page's main "dark band" moment. See [TERRAIN archetype](../../../archetypes/terrain.md).
- FORGE: keep the dark capability sub-grid as the page's dominant treatment and lighten the alternating bands only slightly (charcoal, not full white). Ember-orange shows up only on the kicker labels and the hero-overlay stat numbers — everywhere else stays monochrome white-on-charcoal. Pairs naturally with the [dark feature bands product-block](../../product-block/research/refero-eb12db31-dark-feature-bands.md). See [FORGE archetype](../../../archetypes/forge.md).
- TACTICAL: tighten geometry — kicker labels in precision-sans tracking, sharper CTA rectangles (~6px radius), corner brackets on the capability sub-grid cells, monochrome black/white only with a single steel or cyan accent on the hero-overlay stats. Photos are studio-shot product renders, no environmental context. See [TACTICAL archetype](../../../archetypes/tactical.md).
- PATRIOT: cream backgrounds with deep-navy kickers and headings; one barn-red accent reserved for a single capability label inside the sub-grid (NOT for the hero stats — keep stats charcoal). Photography is golden-hour operator-and-equipment, weathered hands, American farmland. The dark capability sub-grid translates to a deep-navy band rather than pure black so it reads as flag-derived, not generic-dark. See [PATRIOT archetype](../../../archetypes/patriot.md).

The library-worthy move is the structural cadence (kicker-label band → alternating photo-text bands → single dark capability sub-grid → case-study photo → long inquiry form), reusable for a Terraplex dealer surfacing service capabilities (fleet management, [Part 137 compliance](../../../content/compliance-program.md), demo flights, operator training, [Part 107 prep](../../../content/compliance-program.md)) without dragging luxury-tech copy along for the ride.
