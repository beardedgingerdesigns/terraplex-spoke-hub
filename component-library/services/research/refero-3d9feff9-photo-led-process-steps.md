## Why this is library-worthy
A service-program walkthrough where every step in the process gets its own **photographic illustration** — a 6-step grid (3-up + 3-up rows) with each cell carrying a real photograph of that step happening, a numbered title, and a 1-line description. The photographs do the heavy lifting; the customer sees the process happening before reading any prose about it. Below the photo-grid, a "What qualifies" bullet list and a "What we do / don't accept" 2-column comparison band, then an FAQ accordion. Different from existing services patterns: the [kicker-band services](./refero-02ae136e-kicker-band-services.md) uses photos for category illustration (one photo per service); the [photo-mosaic services](./refero-2fdf9ace-photo-mosaic-services.md) uses irregular photo tiles for breadth (no process); the [maintenance-program-recommender](./refero-d928f889-maintenance-program-recommender.md) uses an interactive recommender after a flat tier comparison (no per-step photos). This pattern is the right pick when the dealer's service is **a multi-step transaction** (buyback / trade-in / repair / fleet check-in / Part 137 audit) and the customer's hesitation is "I don't know how this works." The photographs answer that hesitation directly.

## What to take
- Open with a tight 2-column hero (~30vh, contained): left ~50% has a bold sentence-case service-program name (~32–40px) — "Buyback & Resell service" → for a dealer "Trade-in your equipment" / "Fleet check-in program" / "Part 137 audit prep" — followed by 2–3 line paragraph ("Why let your much-loved IKEA products go to waste? Bring them back to us…"). Below the paragraph, 3 paired text-link CTAs ("How it works | Get an estimate | Eligible products | FAQ"). Right ~50% is one bold full-bleed photograph of a real customer or operator returning / inspecting / preparing the equipment — environmental, not studio.
- Promotional band below the hero (optional): a saturated-color full-bleed band (~12–15vh) with a single offer headline ("Get extra discount as an IKEA Family member") + small filled CTA pill in the inverted color. Use only if the dealer's program has a real members-only or seasonal-only tier; otherwise drop.
- "How it works" 6-step photo grid — this is the pattern's distinguishing move. Centered heading above the grid (~28–32px sentence-case). Below the heading, 6 cells in 2 rows of 3 (3 cells row 1, 3 cells row 2). Each cell is:
  1. A landscape-oriented photograph (~16:10 aspect, ~280–340px wide) of THAT specific step happening — a real person at the step. Photographs are warm-lit and environmental: someone calculating value at a kitchen table, someone receiving an estimate by email, someone bringing equipment back to a counter, someone inspecting/assessing the returned item, someone receiving a refund card, someone seeing the equipment in its new home. The photographs are the affordance — the customer sees what each step looks like.
  2. Below the photograph, a numbered bold sentence-case title ("1. Calculate your product's value" → for a dealer "1. Submit your equipment list").
  3. Below the title, a 2–3 line description in muted charcoal explaining what the customer does in that step ("Determine what you want to resell. Find out what products you can sell back to us on our list of eligible products. IKEA Family members earn 15% extra…").
  4. Below the description, an optional small filled CTA pill ("Get your Buyback & Resell estimate") — only on the cell where conversion happens (typically step 1 or step 6). Most cells have NO CTA; the photograph + title + description carries them.
- "Items that qualify" — short bullet list section directly below the 6-step grid. Heading ("Items that qualify should be:" → for a dealer "Equipment we accept must be:"), then 4–5 single-line bullets ("In good, reusable condition" / "Listed below as eligible" / "Complete and fully functional" / "Properly assembled when returned" / "Clean and unsmelled"). At the bottom, a single filled CTA pill ("Get your Buyback & Resell estimate"). The bullets are blunt — they set expectations.
- "What we buy / What we don't buy" — 2-column comparison band. Left column ~50% is "Second-hand products we buy" with a single landscape photograph + bullet list of categories ("All dressers, office furniture, small structures with shelves; display storage; sideboards; bookcases; …"). Right column ~50% is "Second-hand products we don't buy" with a single landscape photograph + bullet list of exclusions ("Non-IKEA products; component parts only; products that have been used outside of buyback; non-assemblable IKEA products; …"). The two-column comparison clarifies what the customer should and shouldn't expect to qualify. Plain-text bullets, no chrome.
- "Frequently asked questions" — single-column accordion below the comparison band. Each row is a question + caret toggle on the right; expanding shows a 2–3 line answer with optional inline link ("See full article →"). 6–10 questions covering the operational unknowns (where to drop off, payment timing, what happens to rejected items, refund mechanics, store-network coverage). The FAQ is the trust-trail — customers reading it have already decided they want to do this and are checking the operational details.
- Closing "Becoming a circular company" cross-sell band: a single 2-column block — left has a bold headline + 2-line paragraph + outline-pill CTA ("See how we're becoming a circular business" → for a dealer "See how we're certifying every operator we sell to"), right has one photograph reinforcing the closing message. Optional related-services list below ("Re-shop and reuse / Spare parts / Customer service" → for a dealer "Annual fleet check-in / Replacement parts / Compliance review").

## What NOT to take
- Don't use this pattern with fewer than 5 steps in the process. The 6-step photo-grid affordance depends on having enough steps that the grid reads as "comprehensive walkthrough" — with 3 steps the grid is too small, and the [Square Specialists 3-step "How it works" row](./refero-d30b8a54-specialist-marketplace.md) handles that case better. With 4 steps, drop one column and use 2×2 instead.
- Don't replace the photographs with icons. The whole reason this pattern earns its space is that each step shows a real person doing the step — icons collapse the section into a generic capability grid that any SaaS landing carries. The photographs must be defensible: real people in real contexts, even if the dealer has to hire a local photographer to capture each step at the dealer's actual facility.
- Don't put a CTA on every step cell. The pattern's restraint depends on most cells being informational; per-cell CTAs create a sales loop that fights the "let me see the whole process before I commit" register. ONE conversion CTA at the bottom of the bullet-list section is the right pattern.
- Don't import IKEA's literal "Buyback & Resell" service copy or the Family-member discount tier — that's brand-program-specific. The transferable move is the structural cadence (program-named hero → 6-step photo grid → qualifying-criteria bullets → 2-column accept/reject comparison → FAQ accordion → cross-sell band). For a Terraplex dealer this maps to a [Part 137 audit prep program](../../../content/compliance-program.md), an annual fleet check-in service, an equipment trade-in program, or an operator certification onboarding flow.
- Don't make the 2-column "What we accept / don't accept" comparison feel adversarial. The bullets are matter-of-fact, not accusatory — "Non-IKEA products" reads as "we can't help with this kind"; it would read wrong as "DON'T BRING US your competitor's stuff." For a dealer this means the exclusion list is clear and operationally honest, not gatekeeping.
- Don't combine this with the [maintenance-program-recommender](./refero-d928f889-maintenance-program-recommender.md) on the same page. Both surface a service-program walkthrough; the recommender pattern adds an interactive widget that recommends a tier; this pattern adds a 6-step photo-grid that walks through a single program. Pick one — recommender for "which tier" (decision routing); this for "how does it work" (process clarity).

## Adaptation notes (cross-archetype)
- FIELD: native fit. Cream background, warm-amber on the headlines, golden-hour photographs in every step cell — real operator at sunrise calculating, real farmer dropping off equipment at the dealer's counter, real technician inspecting at a workbench under task light. The "What we accept / don't" 2-column comparison uses warm-charcoal headings on cream. The FAQ accordion uses cream rows with hand-drawn line-art chevron toggles. See [FIELD archetype](../../../archetypes/field.md). Pairs with the [manifesto-creed hero](../../hero/research/refero-b86571f2-manifesto-creed-hero.md) for a heritage-led parent landing.
- CLEAN: native fit. White background, brand-navy headings, clean equipment-and-operator photographs in every step cell with soft drop shadows. The 2-column comparison uses neutral-gray dividers. FAQ accordion uses brand-navy chevron toggles. See [CLEAN archetype](../../../archetypes/clean.md). Pairs with the [centered-screenshot-trio hero](../../hero/research/refero-f619e700-centered-screenshot-trio.md).
- TERRAIN: the 6-step photo grid IS the page's main "light band" — the 2-column accept/reject comparison drops to a slightly tonal-shifted clay or sage band (signals the alternating rhythm). The promotional band at the top uses a saturated clay hue. Add a thin topographic-line motif at the bottom edge of the grid (where it transitions to the bullet-list section). See [TERRAIN archetype](../../../archetypes/terrain.md).
- PATRIOT: cream background, deep-navy headings, late-afternoon American-farmland photographs in every step cell — real farmers, real operators, real technicians in working contexts. The promotional band at the top uses deep-navy with cream-on-navy CTA pill (the only saturated chrome). The "What we accept" comparison uses deep-navy column headings with charcoal bullets; the bullets stay plain (no flag stripes between rows). FAQ accordion uses deep-navy chevron toggles; one out of every 3 question-rows carries a barn-red caret stroke (restrained flag-color-at-micro-scale). See [PATRIOT archetype](../../../archetypes/patriot.md). Pairs with the [manifesto-creed hero](../../hero/research/refero-b86571f2-manifesto-creed-hero.md).
- FORGE: invert to a dark variant — charcoal page background, white step titles, ember-orange on the numbered prefix ("1." in ember). Photographs are moody equipment-at-dusk shots — operators with rim-lit equipment, technicians under task lamps in dim workshops. The 2-column comparison uses near-black background with white text. FAQ accordion uses white chevron toggles on dark rows; ember-orange on the "expand" state. See [FORGE archetype](../../../archetypes/forge.md). Pairs with the [hardware-lineup dark hero](../../hero/research/refero-0945a36c-hardware-lineup-dark-hero.md).
- TACTICAL: dark variant with tighter geometry — sharper rectangle CTA pills (~6px corner radius), precision-sans for step titles in tracked all-caps. Photographs are studio-shot equipment in controlled lighting (rotor on a service bench under bright task light, controller on a dark workbench, batteries laid out for inspection). Monochrome white-on-near-black with a single steel-cyan accent on numbered prefixes and the FAQ chevrons. Add thin 1px corner brackets on the photograph corners (consistent with the archetype's bracket motif). See [TACTICAL archetype](../../../archetypes/tactical.md). Pairs with the [topographic layered-bg hero](../../hero/research/refero-ae84b62a-topographic-layered-bg.md).
