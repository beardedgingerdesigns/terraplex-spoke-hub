## Why this is library-worthy
A **culture-led careers / recruitment landing pattern** — sells "join us" through employer-brand cues (editorial declarative hero + named values + employee testimonials + open-roles carousel) rather than through a numbered process. None of the existing services patterns target the careers / employer-brand register: the closest sibling is [numbered-process-shopper](./refero-d030e2b2-numbered-process-shopper.md) which is enrollment-led (path-based — "here's how the program works"), whereas this one is culture-led (people-based — "here's who we are, here are open seats"). The two patterns answer different recruitment questions for a Terraplex dealer: process-shopper for Part 107 cohort signups and operator-certification enrollments, this one for **hiring Part 137 spray operators, fleet pilots, technicians, dispatchers, or trainers** where the prospect is a working professional, not a customer. The credibility move is editorial restraint: large-serif all-caps headline + ambient interior photograph + values paragraphs + named employee avatars + concrete open-role cards — no brand-color hero band, no overlay UI receipts, no numbered timeline.

## What to take
- **Editorial declarative hero band** — centered all-caps serif headline ~48–64px on white (no brand-color band), placed above a 2-column hero with a wide rectangular ambient interior photograph on the left (~55%) and right column (~45%) holding a 3–4 sentence mission paragraph + small bold subhead line ("Join the future of [X]") + single rounded-black pill CTA ("Explore Open Jobs"). The hero is *editorial*, not transactional — the brand isn't a saturated band, the brand is the typographic restraint and the photo's stillness. For dealers this transposes to "Join Terraplex spraying" / "Careers at [Dealer]" / "Build the next Part 137 fleet" with a real shot of operators at the dealer's hangar / field / shop.
- **Employee testimonial avatar carousel** — full-width section under the hero with a section heading ("IT TAKES ALL OF US"), 1–3 circular avatar cards visible at a time, each card has a 96–140px avatar + 2–3-line testimonial in serif/humanist sans + bold name + small role line ("Senior Multiplatform Editor"). Left/right arrows on the side, dot pagination centered underneath. The avatars are large and the copy is short — the carousel reads as faces-first, not quotes-first. Distinct from [case-study-narrative](./refero-1e6c57eb-case-study-narrative.md) (one customer, long-form) and from the multi-quote testimonial band inside [numbered-process-shopper](./refero-d030e2b2-numbered-process-shopper.md) (two cards on a dark band). The carousel format scales — a dealer with 6+ team members surfaces them all without crowding the page.
- **Named values column with paragraphs** — section heading ("POWERED BY PASSION AND TALENT") followed by a 2-column band: LEFT column holds 4–5 stacked named values, each value is a small blue/accent-color headline ("Lead / Act Fast / Take Ownership / Build the Future") + 2–4-line paragraph of what the value means in practice ("Speed matters. Have a bias for action..."); RIGHT column holds a single contextual photograph spanning the height of the value stack. The named values function as the dealer's culture in 5 nouns — they pre-qualify candidates by signaling work-style, not by listing perks. This is structurally distinct from the [dense use-case grid](./refero-6f85d84c-dense-use-case-grid.md) (icon + title + description in a 5×3 grid) — that's breadth-of-coverage; this is depth-of-culture.
- **Open-roles horizontal card carousel** — section heading ("INVENT THE FUTURE OF NEWS" → for a dealer "Open Positions" or "Where you fit"), then a horizontal scroller showing 3–4 visible rectangular cards at once, each card with a 1px gray border + role title (16–18px bold) + 1-line location/department metadata in gray ("DC-Washington-TWP Headquarters"). Carousel arrows at the right, dot pagination centered underneath, centered secondary "Browse All Roles" pill below. The cards are deliberately spare — no salary, no description, no apply button — because the click leads to a job-detail page where those live. The card is a tease, not the listing. Native fit when the dealer has 3+ concurrent open positions; for fewer roles, collapse to a static 1-row grid (no carousel).
- **Internship / training-program summary** — directly below the open-roles carousel, a 2-column text block (left: "Engineering & Business Operations Internships"; right: "Newsroom Internships"), each with a 2–3-line paragraph + inline link to past cohort years ("View Past Intern Classes (2023, 2024, 2025)") + small black "Learn More" pill. For dealers this is the Part 107 cohort or operator-trainee program preview — distinct from the [filtered course catalog](./refero-518b4dac-filtered-course-catalog.md) (post-purchase self-paced training) because this surfaces a single named cohort program with retroactive credibility (past classes), whereas the catalog surfaces many courses.
- **Optional report / transparency band** — centered paragraph + small black pill CTA ("Read The Report"). For Terraplex dealers the analog is the annual Part 137 compliance report, fleet utilization disclosure, or the dealer's safety / training program audit. Skip if the dealer doesn't produce one — the band is a credibility move only when the report is real.
- **Behind-the-scenes media band** — 2-column block: LEFT is an embedded video thumbnail with standard play button overlay (YouTube embed or self-hosted); RIGHT is a phone mockup or social-feed collage image. Below the video sits a horizontal row of 5–6 social icons (24–28px circles). The video is *day-in-the-life*, not promotional — operator at sunrise prepping a sprayer, ride-along footage, the dealer's mechanic talking through a repair. Pairs with the [video-poster statement hero](../../hero/research/refero-507672c5-video-poster-statement-hero.md) when the dealer wants the same ride-along on the homepage.
- **Locations block** — 2-column listing where LEFT is a stack of office/hangar addresses with bold sans-serif headings ("Virginia Drone & Production Plant / 7171 Wimsatt Road / Springfield, VA 22151") and RIGHT is a single contextual photograph of one of those locations. For multi-location dealers, the list is the directory; for single-location dealers, swap to a single-card "Visit us" block with the address + embedded mini-map (see [single-storefront store page](../../contact/research/refero-1835be20-single-storefront-store-page.md) for the structurally similar contact pattern).
- **Editorial restraint at the page level** — thin 1px gray dividers between every section (not full-bleed band changes), max content width ~1120–1280px, generous 56–120px vertical section spacing, serif for display headings + sans-serif for body. The page argues credibility through restraint — the recruit reading it should feel "this is a serious place," not "this is a marketing campaign." Saturation, full-bleed bands, and Instagram-saturated brand colors fight the pattern's whole credibility move.

## What NOT to take
- Don't import this pattern unless the dealer actually has open roles to fill, a real training-program cohort to recruit into, and 3+ real team members willing to be photographed and quoted. Without those three the page reads as recruitment-theater. For dealers without open roles use the [protection-plan explainer](./refero-a52e1484-protection-plan-explainer.md) (single-program landing) or skip a careers landing entirely until hiring is real.
- Don't conflate this with the [numbered-process-shopper](./refero-d030e2b2-numbered-process-shopper.md) enrollment pattern. That one sells a *path* — apply, get reviewed, start earning — to a customer-shaped recruit (operators paying to certify into a program). This one sells a *culture* — values, faces, open seats — to a worker-shaped recruit (employees joining the dealer). A dealer with a Part 107 cohort + open hiring will run BOTH patterns on separate URLs (`/training` for the cohort, `/careers` for the hiring), not one combined page.
- Don't replace the editorial typography with brand-saturation. The pattern's whole credibility move is "we let the typography and the photographs speak." A full-bleed deep-amber hero band with white display sans-serif headline turns the page into a campaign and signals "marketing-led" — the opposite of what the page is trying to argue. The brand color appears only as accent treatment on the values headlines and the CTA pill, not as a band.
- Don't make the open-roles cards busy. Each card carries title + 1 metadata line only. Adding salary, posted-date, or apply-now buttons crowds the carousel and breaks the rhythm; the carousel is a navigation entry-point, not a job-listings page.
- Don't surface fewer than 3 employee testimonials. Two testimonials reads as decorative; three+ reads as a real team. If the dealer has only 1–2 willing team members on the avatar carousel, drop the carousel entirely and replace it with a static 2-up named card. The carousel format implies "and many more behind these" — promising more than the dealer can show defeats the move.
- Don't autoplay the behind-the-scenes video. The video is opt-in (click-to-play), and the page reads as still-and-editorial *until* the user chooses to engage. Autoplay reverts to "marketing video" and breaks the editorial register; in FIELD, autoplay specifically violates the no-motion rule (see [FIELD archetype](../../../archetypes/field.md)).
- Don't put a "Quick Apply" form anywhere on the landing. The page's only forward action is to the job-detail page (via the carousel card or the "Explore Open Jobs" CTA). Adding a form on the landing collapses the "evaluate culture first, apply second" frame; the [form-with-topic-checkboxes](../../contact/research/refero-23938dfd-form-with-topic-checkboxes.md) is the right pattern when the dealer wants in-page form capture, but not here.

## Adaptation notes (cross-archetype)
- FIELD: native fit. Cream base (~#F4EFE7), warm-charcoal serif display headlines ("YOUR STORY AWAITS" → "GROW WITH US" / "JOIN THE FIELD" set in warm-charcoal Playfair or Caslon), warm-charcoal body. Values headlines in warm-amber accent. Hero photograph at golden hour — operator at the hangar door or in-field debrief. Employee testimonial avatars over the cream base with warm-charcoal serif quotes. CTA pill in warm-charcoal with cream text. Pairs with the [hands-on craft hero](../../hero/research/refero-be842c1e-hands-on-craft-hero.md) for a homepage that opens with one operator and the careers page that opens with the team. See [FIELD archetype](../../../archetypes/field.md).
- CLEAN: also a native fit. White base, deep-navy serif display headlines, brand-navy accent on values headlines + CTA pill. Hero photograph in clean equipment-and-operator register, even cool lighting. Avatar circles with a thin 2px navy border. Behind-the-scenes video preview with navy chrome. The pattern's editorial restraint maps directly onto CLEAN's calibrated-restraint axis. See [CLEAN archetype](../../../archetypes/clean.md).
- TERRAIN: keep the editorial restraint, add the archetype's clay/sage tonal-shift bands between sections (thin 1px clay dividers replace gray dividers). Display serif in warm-charcoal or deep-clay. Hero photograph at a mid-day terrain register — operator under wide sky. Values headlines in clay accent. Employee testimonial avatars over cream-or-sage tonal base. See [TERRAIN archetype](../../../archetypes/terrain.md).
- PATRIOT: cream base, deep-navy serif display headlines, single barn-red accent on values headlines + CTA pill. Hero photograph late-afternoon American-farmland with operator and equipment. Employee testimonial section heading reads warmer ("THE TEAM BEHIND THE WORK" or "MEET THE OPERATORS") — same structure, more sincere copy register. See [PATRIOT archetype](../../../archetypes/patriot.md).
- FORGE: invert to a dark variant. Near-black base (~#0E0E0E), white serif display headlines, ember-orange accent on values headlines + CTA pill. Hero photograph at dusk or with rim-lighting from shop work-lights. Employee testimonial avatars on dark base with white serif quotes and ember-rim avatar treatment. Behind-the-scenes video chrome in ember. Skip the editorial cream — the credibility move on dark is workshop-light + rim-lit faces. See [FORGE archetype](../../../archetypes/forge.md).
- TACTICAL: tighten geometry — sharper rectangular CTAs (~6px corner radius), precision-sans for body and display, monochrome white-on-near-black base with steel-cyan accent on values headlines + CTA pill. Add 1px corner-bracket details around the avatar carousel and the open-role cards. Open-roles cards get a small monospace role-code badge ("ENG-117 / OPS-204") for the precision register. See [TACTICAL archetype](../../../archetypes/tactical.md).

## Source brief
The Washington Post's [careers.washingtonpost.com](https://careers.washingtonpost.com/) employer-brand careers landing — an editorial recruitment page for an institutional newsroom. The library-worthy move is the cadence (editorial declarative hero with photo + body 2-column → employee testimonial avatar carousel → named values column + ambient photo → open-roles horizontal card carousel → internship/training program summary → optional transparency report band → behind-the-scenes media → locations block), reusable for a Terraplex dealer's `/careers` URL where the recruit is a working professional: Part 137 spray operators, fleet pilots, technicians, dispatchers, trainers, or apprentices in a multi-cohort training program. The pattern complements rather than replaces the [numbered-process-shopper enrollment landing](./refero-d030e2b2-numbered-process-shopper.md) — process-shopper recruits customers into a program (cohort signup); this pattern recruits employees into the dealer (job hires). A dealer running both has a `/training` URL (process-shopper) and a `/careers` URL (this pattern).
