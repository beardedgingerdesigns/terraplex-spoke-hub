## Why this is library-worthy
A services pattern where the page **routes the customer to third-party specialists** rather than to the dealer's own services. Hero with bold headline carrying ONE accent-color keyword (the routed-to thing — "developers", "specialists", "operators"), 2-up benefit photo cards, "How it works" 3-step numbered process row, dark testimonial band with one quote, "Solutions designed to help you grow" 4-icon row of capability categories, vetted-partner logo wall, closing "Ready to take your business to the next level?" two-column band with photo. The page IS a marketplace landing: the dealer gets credit for vetting and routing; the actual service is delivered by third parties. Different from existing services patterns: the [kicker-band services](./refero-02ae136e-kicker-band-services.md), [three-tier inverted-middle](./refero-15fcbffc-three-tier-inverted-middle.md), [photo-mosaic](./refero-2fdf9ace-photo-mosaic-services.md), [dark industry-vertical](./refero-74b10c78-dark-industry-vertical.md), [audience-tabbed](./refero-fef890ec-audience-tabbed-services.md), and [maintenance-program-recommender](./refero-d928f889-maintenance-program-recommender.md) all surface the dealer's OWN services. This pattern surfaces a network of vetted third parties — right pick when the dealer's actual offering is "we know who to call" rather than "we do it ourselves." Most directly relevant for the [49-location Terraplex network](../../../research/manufacturers/terraplex.md) at the parent-brand level (find-a-dealer surfacing per-region certified specialists), or for a single dealer surfacing certified operators / Part 137 applicators / approved repair partners in their territory.

## What to take
- Open with a 2-column hero (~50–60vh): left ~50% is heading + paragraph + paired CTAs, right ~50% is a single photograph of a specialist at work (operator on a laptop, technician at a workbench, applicator in a field). The hero is intentionally restrained — no overlay panel, no big CTA pill on the photograph.
  1. Heading (~40–48px bold sentence-case): "[Brand Name] Specialists / Build your business vision with expert [routed-to thing]." The routed-to noun ("developers" / "operators" / "applicators" / "Part 137 specialists") is in a single accent color — the only saturated word in the hero. The accent keyword IS the headline's affordance.
  2. 2-line paragraph below the heading: "Easily hire the right [partner type] to help with [scope]." The paragraph names the matching mechanism explicitly.
  3. Paired CTAs: filled brand-accent pill ("Find your specialist" → for a dealer "Find a certified operator" / "Find a Part 137 specialist") + small text-link CTA with play icon ("Watch the video" → for a dealer "Watch how matching works"). Two CTAs only.
- Below the hero, a dark "Solve [problem] fast" band (~30vh): full-bleed near-black background, centered headline, 2-up photo card row beneath. Each card is a clean square photo on dark with a bold sentence-case caption + 2-line description below the photo. The 2-up cards reframe the dealer's value as "specialists understand [thing 1]" and "specialists deliver [outcome 2]."
- "How it works" 3-step numbered row: 3 equal-width cells, each a small numbered circle (~32px, "1" / "2" / "3" in muted gray) + small bold sentence-case label ("Submit a project" / "Get matched" / "See impactful growth" → for a dealer "Tell us your operation" / "Get matched with a certified specialist" / "Schedule the work"). 1-line description below each label. Generous internal padding; no card chrome — the numbered cells are the affordance.
- Mid-page testimonial band: a single dark full-bleed band with one large left-aligned quote in white serif italic, attribution underneath in muted-gray small caps. One customer photograph anchors the right side of the band (square, ~280px). One testimonial only — the trust comes from a real recognizable name, not from quote volume.
- "Solutions designed to help you grow" — 4-icon capability row directly below the testimonial. Each cell is a small monochrome line-art icon (~40px) + bold sentence-case label ("Online store setup" / "Marketing and search" / "Branding and design" / "Custom development" → for a dealer "Mission planning support" / "Compliance audit prep" / "Operator certification" / "Custom integration"). Reads as the categories the marketplace covers — what kinds of specialists you can find.
- "Trusted and affordable agencies at your fingertips" — vetted-partner logo wall on a light-gray inset band. 2-row × 3-column grid of partner logos, monochrome, no chrome. Caption above ("We vet and certify each specialist based on experience and proven success."). The wall IS the credibility move — the dealer's certification stamp on every partner.
- Closing CTA band: "Ready to take your business to the next level?" 2-column — left has the heading + paragraph + filled CTA pill ("Submit a project"), right has a single photograph of two operators at a laptop in conversation. Echoes the hero photograph register; closes the page with the same matching-action affordance the hero opened with.

## What NOT to take
- Don't use this pattern if the dealer's actual service model is "we do everything in-house." The marketplace framing requires real third-party specialists the dealer can route to — and a real vetting / certification process. Without those, the page collapses into a generic services landing pretending to be a marketplace, which damages trust on both sides ("you're a marketplace? show me the specialists" / "you're a service provider? why are you outsourcing?"). For in-house dealers use the [kicker-band services pattern](./refero-02ae136e-kicker-band-services.md) or the [maintenance-program-recommender](./refero-d928f889-maintenance-program-recommender.md) instead.
- Don't import Square's literal blue accent color or the "Find your specialist" CTA copy — those are brand-specific. The transferable move is "single accent keyword in the headline + 3-step numbered how-it-works + vetted-partner logo wall." For a Terraplex dealer the accent keyword is "operator" / "applicator" / "Part 137 specialist" — the noun the customer is searching for, called out at the top of the page.
- Don't omit the testimonial band. The testimonial is what differentiates a marketplace landing from a directory listing — the testimonial proves the marketplace produced a real customer outcome. Without a real quote from a real specialist's actual customer, the marketplace reads as a list of vendors rather than a proven matching service.
- Don't import the literal "Watch the video" text-link CTA without a real video to play. The text-link is the secondary path; if the dealer doesn't have a real explainer video, drop the text-link entirely and use only the filled "Find a specialist" CTA.
- Don't surface more than ~12 vetted-partner logos in the wall. Too many logos collapse the curation register — the whole credibility move depends on the dealer having vetted a manageable number. If the dealer's actual marketplace has 50+ certified specialists, surface only the ~12 most-recognized; the matching engine handles routing the rest.
- Don't combine this with the [audience-tabbed services pattern](./refero-fef890ec-audience-tabbed-services.md) on the same page. Both occupy the "main page mechanism" slot — pick one. If the dealer wants both audience-anchoring AND specialist-routing, use the audience-tabbed services on a parent page that links to per-audience specialist-marketplace pages built with this pattern.

## Adaptation notes (cross-archetype)
- CLEAN: native fit. White background, brand-accent on the keyword in the headline (single brand-tinted color), brand-navy filled CTA pills. Photographs are clean equipment-and-operator shots with soft drop shadows. The dark testimonial band uses near-black with white text and a single brand-accent star or quotation-mark glyph. Vetted-partner logo wall is monochrome on a pale-gray inset. See [CLEAN archetype](../../../archetypes/clean.md). Pairs with the [centered-screenshot-trio hero](../../hero/research/refero-f619e700-centered-screenshot-trio.md).
- TERRAIN: the page reads as a long sustained "light band" with the testimonial dark-band and the logo-wall light-inset providing the alternating rhythm. Add a thin topographic-line motif at the bottom edge of the testimonial band (where it transitions back to light). The accent keyword in the headline is in clay or sage. Vetted-partner logos pick up a thin contour-line stroke at logo edges (where the partner's brand allows). See [TERRAIN archetype](../../../archetypes/terrain.md).
- FIELD: cream background, warm-amber on the accent keyword ("operators" / "applicators" / "specialists" in amber), warm-charcoal headline. Photographs are golden-hour operator-and-equipment shots, weathered hands, real-farmer portraits. The testimonial band uses warm-charcoal background (not pure black) with cream text and a single amber quotation-mark glyph. Vetted-partner logo wall uses warm-charcoal logos on a deeper-cream inset. The 4-icon capability row uses hand-drawn line-art icons in warm-amber stroke. See [FIELD archetype](../../../archetypes/field.md). Pairs with the [manifesto-creed hero](../../hero/research/refero-b86571f2-manifesto-creed-hero.md).
- PATRIOT: cream background, deep-navy on the accent keyword ("operators" / "Part 137 specialists" in navy), charcoal paragraph. Photographs are late-afternoon American-farmland operator portraits. The testimonial band uses deep-navy background with cream text and a single barn-red quotation-mark glyph (the only saturated moment in the band). Vetted-partner logo wall uses deep-navy logos on a cream inset. The closing CTA is a deep-navy pill with cream label. See [PATRIOT archetype](../../../archetypes/patriot.md). Pairs with the [manifesto-creed hero](../../hero/research/refero-b86571f2-manifesto-creed-hero.md).
- FORGE: invert to a dark-dominant variant — charcoal page background, white text, ember-orange on the accent keyword. The 2-up photo cards in the "Solve [problem] fast" band use cinematic moody photography with rim-lit equipment. The testimonial band uses near-black with white text and an ember-orange quotation glyph. Vetted-partner logo wall uses white logos on a near-black inset. CTA pills in ember-orange. The dark page register fits FORGE's aesthetic; the marketplace framing transfers regardless of palette. See [FORGE archetype](../../../archetypes/forge.md). Pairs with the [hardware-lineup dark hero](../../hero/research/refero-0945a36c-hardware-lineup-dark-hero.md).
- TACTICAL: dark-dominant variant with tighter geometry — sharper rectangle CTAs (~6px corner radius), precision-sans for the headline and the 4-icon capability labels in tracked all-caps. Monochrome white-on-near-black with a single steel-cyan accent on the keyword and CTAs. The 3-step "How it works" numbered cells use precise rectangular numbered chips (no circle chrome). The testimonial band quotation glyph is steel-cyan. Photographs are studio-shot equipment renders, no environmental context. See [TACTICAL archetype](../../../archetypes/tactical.md). Pairs with the [topographic layered-bg hero](../../hero/research/refero-ae84b62a-topographic-layered-bg.md).
