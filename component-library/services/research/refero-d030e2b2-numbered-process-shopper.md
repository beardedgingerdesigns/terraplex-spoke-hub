## Why this is library-worthy
A services pattern that previews the **customer's journey through the service** before pitching it — the first below-hero block is a 3-step horizontal numbered timeline (not capability cards, not tier cards, not audience tabs). The structural cadence is novel for the library: brand-colored hero with offer headline → 3-step horizontal numbered process flow with connecting line → ZIP-or-area localization band → 3-up "how it works" cards → 4-up benefit cards → multi-quote testimonial band on a saturated brand-color surface → FAQ accordion → closer signup band. None of the existing services patterns ([three-tier inverted middle](./refero-15fcbffc-three-tier-inverted-middle.md), [kicker-band](./refero-02ae136e-kicker-band-services.md), [photo-mosaic](./refero-2fdf9ace-photo-mosaic-services.md), [dark industry-vertical](./refero-74b10c78-dark-industry-vertical.md), [audience-tabbed](./refero-fef890ec-audience-tabbed-services.md), [maintenance-program-recommender](./refero-d928f889-maintenance-program-recommender.md)) leads with the process itself — those are catalog-led, audience-led, or tier-led. The pattern fits two adjacent dealer briefs: (a) **enrollment / become-a-X programs** where the conversion is a literal sign-up (Part 107 prep cohort, fleet-share program, operator certification ladder, dealer-affiliate program), and (b) **process-clarity services** where the customer's hesitation is "what does this even involve?" (Part 137 spray-day onboarding, fleet-evaluation booking, service-application flow). The page's whole credibility move is "we're showing you the path before we ask you to walk it" — which fits dealers whose service is unfamiliar or operationally opaque.

## What to take
- Open with a contained brand-color hero band (~50–60vh, NOT full-bleed black or white — the page's brand is the background). Left ~50% holds a 3-line bold sentence-case headline with sentence breaks ("Work your way. / Get paid. / Make someone's day." → for a dealer "Work your fields. / Earn certification. / Stay current."), a 1–2 line subhead, and a single filled CTA pill ("Sign up" — short, transactional, not "Learn more"). Right ~50% holds a single photograph of a real member in working context with 1–2 small overlay UI cards floating over the photograph showing earnings or compliance status ("$23.37 / 1 shop & deliver order", "$11.99 / Battery delivery") — the overlays are credibility-by-receipts.
- **Numbered process flow** — the pattern's distinguishing affordance. A horizontal row of 3 numbered steps directly below the hero, on a soft-tinted band (slightly darker than the hero base or a complementary tonal shift). Each step is a circle with a number (1 / 2 / 3) at the top connected by a thin horizontal line that runs across all three circles, and below each number a bold sentence-case label ("Create account / Set up account in app / Start earning money") + 2-line description. The line connecting the circles IS the affordance — it visually argues "these are sequential, not equal-weight options." A vertical accordion or bullet list collapses the same content into "stuff to do" rather than "a path to follow."
- **Step taxonomy** — name the three steps as entry / dealer-side / outcome, in that order. Step 1 is the customer's entry action ("Apply" / "Submit your fleet info" / "Request a quote"). Step 2 is the dealer-side action that earns the customer's trust ("We review and route" / "Sam Welton confirms compliance" / "We schedule your demo"). Step 3 is the customer's outcome ("Start delivering" / "Begin training" / "Take delivery"). Skipping the dealer-side middle step (collapsing into apply → outcome) hides the dealer's work and weakens the credibility move; the middle step is where the page argues "you are not on your own here."
- **ZIP/area localization band** — directly below the process flow. Heading on the left ("Find out where you can shop as an Instacart shopper in your area" → for a dealer "Find a Part 107 cohort starting near you"), small ZIP input + Show CTA, and a small "Have a question? View our FAQs" link. Right side holds a flat 3×3 or 4×3 monochrome logo wall of partner stores / counties served / certification-issuing bodies. The ZIP affordance reframes the program from abstract ("we offer this") to concrete ("here's where it runs near you, today"). For dealers covering only one service area (single-county, single-state, single-cohort program), drop the ZIP input entirely and replace the row with a flat heading + 4–6-up partner / certification logo wall — the search reads as decorative chrome when there is only one possible result. The ZIP earns its weight only when the dealer covers multiple counties / states OR participates in the broader [49-location Terraplex network](../../../research/manufacturers/terraplex.md).
- **3-up "how it works" cards** — immediately below the localization band, a clean light-band 3-up card row with single photograph + bold caption + 1-line description per card ("Shop only the orders you want / Go to the store to pick items for the order / Chat with customers for questions or changes"). Cards are equal-height with consistent photography register (in-context, not staged).
- **4-up benefit cards** — "Why X choose [Program]" — short bold heading, then 4 horizontal cards each with bold word ("Community / Rewards / Earnings / Support") + 2-line description + small attribution photo + name + city of a real participant. The attribution under each benefit card is the credibility — "Andrew S. / Syracuse NY" alongside a quote means the benefit is claimed by a person, not by the brand.
- **Multi-quote testimonial band** — full-bleed dark-saturated brand-color band ("Members make it happen") with 2 cream-on-saturated cards floating side-by-side. Each card is a longer pull quote (~3–5 lines) with photo + name + city. This is where the page's emotional weight lives — a "members making it happen" banner with two long heartfelt anonymized stories. The saturation contrast (cream cards on deep brand-color band) is the affordance — it lifts the band out of the page's monochrome rhythm at the moment maximum emotional credibility is needed.
- **FAQ accordion** — "Common questions about X with [Program]" — 6–8 collapsed rows on plain background, each a single sentence-case question + plus-icon. Default state: ALL collapsed except the highest-friction one (e.g. "How do I get paid?") which expands to show 2–3 short paragraphs of explanation including formatting quirks ("Full-service shopper / Rewards…"). Default-expanding the highest-friction question is the affordance — surfacing the answer the user is most likely about to look for.
- **Closer signup band** — full-bleed brand-color band again, mirrors the hero ("Become a shopper today / Sign up now and start shopping in as soon as 1 hour" + Sign up CTA + photograph of a member at the right). The page opens and closes with the same conversion offer at the same brand-color register; the body is the journey from "what / how / who / why" to "yes."
- **Vertical rhythm rule** — alternate light bands and deep brand-color bands at section scale, with three deep-band moments (hero / "how it works" or testimonial / closer) and never two deep bands adjacent. The rhythm cream → cream-tonal → deep → cream → deep → cream → deep is the page's compose; if every band is cream the process flow reads as a long FAQ, and if every band is deep the deep-band moments lose their conversion weight. The deep-band intervals are where the page argues, the cream-band intervals are where the page explains.

## What NOT to take
- Don't import this pattern unless the dealer's service has a real path the customer walks — either an enrollment journey (Part 107 prep cohort, fleet-share program, operator certification ladder) or an operationally unfamiliar service that needs demystifying up-front (Part 137 spray-day onboarding, fleet-evaluation booking, service-application flow). Without a path, the 3-step process flow becomes generic chrome. For catalog-shaped services where every offering is equally weighted use [kicker-band](./refero-02ae136e-kicker-band-services.md) (capability-led); for audience-shaped services where the differentiation is who-we-serve use [audience-tabbed](./refero-fef890ec-audience-tabbed-services.md) (audience-led).
- Don't make the numbered process flow more than 3–4 steps. The horizontal connecting line maxes out at 4 circles before the eye loses the path; at 5+ steps the pattern collapses into a vertical timeline and loses the at-a-glance "this is short and doable" affordance.
- Don't import the literal Instacart green palette (the source uses dark forest green for hero/closer + cream for body bands). The transferable move is "saturated brand-color base for hero/closer + tonal-shift band for body sections + saturated brand-color band for testimonials." For a Terraplex dealer the brand colors come from the archetype: deep navy/cream/barn-red for PATRIOT, warm clay/cream for FIELD, charcoal/ember for FORGE, etc.
- Don't fabricate the ZIP-localization affordance. The ZIP input MUST return real results — a real list of cohort locations, certified dealers, or service areas the user can navigate to. A ZIP field that returns "We'll be there soon" defeats the whole credibility move; the rest of the page assumes the ZIP just worked.
- Don't surface only one testimonial. The pattern's emotional credibility comes from MULTIPLE real stories on the dark band — at minimum 2, ideally 3, each with photo + name + city. A single testimonial collapses the band into decorative chrome and the page feels astroturfed. If the dealer doesn't have 2+ real participant testimonials, drop the testimonial band entirely; the FAQ + closer signup do the work.
- Don't put a CTA in every section. The pattern has TWO conversion moments: the hero CTA and the closer CTA. Sections in between are explanation, not conversion. Adding "Sign up" buttons in the process flow / benefits / testimonials fights the natural rhythm of "explain → convince → commit."
- Don't make the FAQ default-collapsed. One question (the highest-friction one — "how do I get paid" / "what does it cost" / "what are the requirements") MUST default-expand. The user's eye lands on it, sees an immediate answer, and trusts the rest are equally answered. Fully-collapsed FAQ is just navigation chrome.

## Adaptation notes (cross-archetype)
- FIELD: cream base (~#F4EFE7), warm-charcoal headlines, single deep-amber accent on the hero CTA pill and the numbered process-flow circles. Hero photograph is a real operator-and-equipment shot at golden hour with cream-bg overlay UI cards showing certification status or earnings. Multi-quote testimonial band uses warm-clay (~#3A2B1F) deep-tonal backdrop instead of saturated green; testimonial cards on cream with hand-drawn underlines on attribution. Logo wall uses warm-charcoal monochrome partner names. Pairs with the [manifesto-creed hero](../../hero/research/refero-b86571f2-manifesto-creed-hero.md) on a parent landing page. See [FIELD archetype](../../../archetypes/field.md).
- CLEAN: native fit with restraint. White or pale-gray base, brand-navy headlines, single brand-navy accent on hero CTA and process-flow numbered circles. Hero photograph in clean equipment-and-operator register; overlay UI cards with charcoal-on-white chrome. Multi-quote testimonial band uses deep-navy (~#1F2C44) backdrop with white cards. Logo wall in monochrome. Pairs with the [centered-screenshot-trio hero](../../hero/research/refero-f619e700-centered-screenshot-trio.md). See [CLEAN archetype](../../../archetypes/clean.md).
- TERRAIN: the alternating brand-color → tonal-shift → brand-color rhythm IS the archetype's light/dark band rhythm at section scale. Add a thin topographic divider above the numbered process flow and again above the FAQ accordion. Multi-quote band uses clay-or-sage saturated tone instead of generic dark. Process-flow circles pick up the archetype's clay accent. See [TERRAIN archetype](../../../archetypes/terrain.md).
- PATRIOT: cream base, deep-navy headlines, single barn-red accent on hero CTA and numbered process-flow circles. Multi-quote testimonial band uses deep-navy (~#1F2C44) backdrop with cream-on-navy testimonial cards — the flag colors live at the conversion + testimonial moments only. Hero photograph is late-afternoon American-farmland operator-with-equipment with cream-bg overlay UI cards. Logo wall in deep-navy monochrome. Pairs with the [manifesto-creed hero](../../hero/research/refero-b86571f2-manifesto-creed-hero.md). See [PATRIOT archetype](../../../archetypes/patriot.md).
- FORGE: invert to a dark variant. Charcoal base (~#1A1A1A), white headlines, single ember-orange accent on hero CTA and numbered process-flow circles (the connecting line picks up an ember rim). Hero photograph at dusk or with rim-lighting; overlay UI cards on near-black with ember-rim treatment. Multi-quote testimonial band uses near-black (~#0E0E0E) deep contrast with dark-card testimonial blocks. Logo wall in white-on-charcoal monochrome. See [FORGE archetype](../../../archetypes/forge.md). Pairs with the [hardware-lineup dark hero](../../hero/research/refero-0945a36c-hardware-lineup-dark-hero.md).
- TACTICAL: tighten geometry — sharper rectangle CTAs (~6px corner radius), precision-sans for headlines and body, monochrome white-on-near-black with a single steel-cyan accent on hero CTA and process-flow circles. Add 1px corner-bracket details to the overlay UI cards (consistent with the archetype's bracket motif). Multi-quote testimonial band on near-black with white precision-sans cards. Logo wall in tracked all-caps monochrome. See [TACTICAL archetype](../../../archetypes/tactical.md). Pairs with the [topographic layered-bg hero](../../hero/research/refero-ae84b62a-topographic-layered-bg.md).

## Source brief
Instacart Shoppers's [/](https://shoppers.instacart.com/) become-a-shopper recruitment landing — a B2C gig-marketplace recruitment page. The library-worthy move is the structural cadence (brand-color hero + 3-step horizontal numbered process flow with connecting line + ZIP localization band + 3-up "how it works" cards + 4-up benefit cards with attribution + multi-quote testimonial band on saturated brand color + FAQ with smart-default expansion + closer signup band), reusable for a Terraplex dealer's enrollment programs: a [Part 107 prep cohort signup page](../../../content/compliance-program.md), a fleet-share or fleet-leasing program enrollment, an operator certification ladder, or a dealer-affiliate program. The structural pattern travels because every component has an enrollment-page equivalent — the dealer just substitutes domain-appropriate copy and palette.
