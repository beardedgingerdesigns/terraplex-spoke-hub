## Why this is library-worthy
A service-program landing for ongoing maintenance — the same shape a Terraplex dealer would build for [Part 137 compliance](../../../content/compliance-program.md), fleet maintenance contracts, or annual service plans. Three structurally novel moves that together aren't covered by any existing services note: (1) a flat 3-tier card grid (Basic / Standard / Premium) with visible checkmark feature lists — same cards but no inverted-middle accent, so the comparison is the affordance, not the recommendation; (2) an **interactive recommender widget** that takes model + flight-time and renders a personalized recommendation paragraph below — a service-routing widget instead of static tier copy; (3) a 4-up photo proof grid (2×2) of operators-in-context, each with a paragraph reinforcing one trust dimension. Same product domain (drones) as Terraplex's [R-32](../../../products/r-32.md) and [I-19](../../../products/i-19.md) — most directly transferable services reference in the library. Different from the existing [three-tier inverted-middle](./refero-15fcbffc-three-tier-inverted-middle.md) (subscription tiers without recommender) and [kicker-band](./refero-02ae136e-kicker-band-services.md) (general capabilities) patterns.

## What to take
- Open with a contained hero (~50vh): full-bleed photograph of an operator-with-equipment in working context (high-vis vest, drone, sky), text overlay in the lower-left ~40% with a 2-line bold dark-charcoal title ("Maintenance Program / Enjoy Safe, Reliable Flight" → for a dealer "Compliance Program / Stay Part 137 Current"), 2–3 line paragraph, and 2 inline link CTAs (no pills) — "Check Maintenance Records" + "Maintenance Application." Hero is restrained; the credibility comes from the photograph + the program name, not from heavy chrome.
- "Keep [Equipment] at Top Performance" — 3-up icon row directly below the hero. Each cell is centered: a small monochrome line-art icon (~40px) + bold sentence-case label ("Raise Efficiency" / "Ensure Safety" / "Improve Recordkeeping") + 1-line description. No cards, no chrome — just the icon, label, and one line of copy. Generous white space between cells. This is the program's value-prop summary — kept tight so the cards below earn their weight.
- "[Program Name]" — 3-tier card grid (Basic / Standard / Premium for tiered service; or Annual / Quarterly / Monthly for cadence-based). Each card is the same width and chrome (~16px corner radius, white background, hairline border) with the same content order: tier name, 2-line tier descriptor, checkmark feature list (3–5 lines). **No inverted middle** — the three cards are visually identical; the page lets the reader compare. Below the grid, a small disclaimer line ("Note: dealer will adjust the above maintenance services accordingly in different areas") + a "Learn More" text link.
- **Interactive recommender** — this is the pattern's distinguishing move. A bordered light-gray panel containing 2 inputs ("Aircraft Model" dropdown / "Flight Time / Time Since Activation" dropdown) + a slider with ticks at 6 / 12 / 18 / 24 / 30 / 36 months. Below the inputs, a recommendation panel that updates: bold tier name + 1-paragraph rationale + a "Find a Dealer" link + a contact email. The recommender doesn't replace the static tier cards — it supplements them. The static cards do the comparison; the recommender does the routing.
- "Why should you choose [Program]?" — 4-up photo card grid (2×2). Each card is a single equipment-in-context photograph (drone close-up, technician inspecting parts, operator at controller, repair bench) with a bold caption + 2–3 line paragraph below the photo. No icons, no checkmarks — the photographs carry the proof. Cards are uniform width and equal photo aspect ratio.
- Closer band: a single related-service tile ("More [Brand] Services") on a light-gray background — a small product image on the left, bold service name + 1-line description + arrow link on the right. This is the cross-sell anchor; one tile only.

## What NOT to take
- Don't import DJI's literal "DJI Care Refresh" / "Maintenance Application" copy or blue brand accent — those are brand-specific. The transferable move is the structure (program-named hero → 3-up value-prop icons → flat 3-tier card comparison → interactive recommender → 4-up photo proof → cross-sell tile), not the chrome.
- Don't add the interactive recommender if the dealer's actual service tiers are static (one-size-fits-all). The recommender's affordance is "the right tier depends on YOUR situation" — without real branching logic (model × usage × age) it becomes performative chrome. For a dealer with one tier, drop the recommender entirely; the 4-up photo proof grid carries the page.
- Don't make the 3 tier cards visually different from each other. The flat-three (no inverted middle, no "POPULAR" pill, no scaling) is what differentiates this pattern from [three-tier inverted-middle services](./refero-15fcbffc-three-tier-inverted-middle.md) — flat-three is the right pick when the dealer wants the customer to genuinely compare without nudging; inverted-middle is the right pick when the dealer wants to recommend.
- Don't substitute icons for photographs in the 4-up proof grid. The photographs ARE the credibility move — icons collapse the section into a generic "why us" card row that any SaaS landing carries. Use real operator-in-context shots (technician inspecting a rotor, hands on a controller, drone on a service bench, parts laid out for an annual).
- Don't put a price on the interactive recommender output. The recommender returns a tier name + rationale + dealer-routing link — never a number. Prices live on the static tier cards above (or stay off the page entirely if the dealer's pricing is "request quote" only). Mixing pricing into the recommender output collapses the conversational register into a quote calculator.
- Don't add a CTA on every photo proof card. The pattern has ONE conversion path: read the tiers → use the recommender → contact the dealer. Per-card CTAs fight that single funnel.

## Adaptation notes (cross-archetype)
- FIELD: cream background, warm-charcoal hero overlay text, golden-hour operator-and-equipment hero photograph. Tier cards are warm-cream (~#F8F2E8) with hairline-amber borders; checkmark icons are hand-drawn amber ticks rather than sans-serif checks. Recommender panel uses a torn-cream-paper inset rather than a hard hairline. The 4-up photo proof grid is golden-hour shots (technician at sunrise, hands on a controller, weathered tools laid out). See [FIELD archetype](../../../archetypes/field.md). Pairs with the [manifesto-creed hero](../../hero/research/refero-b86571f2-manifesto-creed-hero.md) on a parent compliance page.
- CLEAN: native fit. White background, neutral grey hairlines, single brand-navy accent on the recommender output panel border and the "Learn More" text links. Tier cards in white with charcoal text. The 4-up proof grid uses clean equipment shots with soft drop shadows on flat backdrops. See [CLEAN archetype](../../../archetypes/clean.md). Pairs with the [centered-screenshot-trio hero](../../hero/research/refero-f619e700-centered-screenshot-trio.md).
- TERRAIN: the hero photograph reads as a landscape band; the 3-up icon row becomes the "dark band" inversion (drop the page to a clay or sage tonal moment for those 3 cells, then return to cream). Tier cards stay in light bands; the recommender panel is the page's main interactive moment with a thin topographic divider on the panel's bottom edge. The 4-up photo grid uses contour-line dividers between the 4 photographs. See [TERRAIN archetype](../../../archetypes/terrain.md).
- PATRIOT: cream background, deep-navy headings, late-afternoon American-farmland hero photograph (operator with equipment in a field). Tier cards are cream with deep-navy headings and a single barn-red accent on the bottom-edge of the middle card only — restraint move that respects PATRIOT's flag-color-at-micro-scale rule. Recommender panel border in deep navy; "Find a Dealer" link in barn-red as the single saturated CTA. The 4-up photo proof grid is operator-and-equipment shots in late-afternoon light — heritage register, no stock corporate shots. See [PATRIOT archetype](../../../archetypes/patriot.md). Pairs with the [manifesto-creed hero](../../hero/research/refero-b86571f2-manifesto-creed-hero.md).
- TACTICAL: tighten geometry — sharper rectangle tier cards (~6px corner radius), precision-sans labels, monochrome black/white only with a single steel-cyan accent on the recommender's output panel border and active-state checkmarks. Hero photograph is studio-shot equipment on seamless black, no environmental context. The 4-up proof grid uses cinematic studio shots of the equipment under test (rotor in motion, gimbal in calibration) with thin corner brackets at the photograph corners. See [TACTICAL archetype](../../../archetypes/tactical.md). Pairs with the [hardware-lineup dark hero](../../hero/research/refero-0945a36c-hardware-lineup-dark-hero.md).
- FORGE: invert to a dark variant — charcoal background (~#1A1A1A), white text, ember-orange accent on the recommender output panel and the middle tier card's edge. Hero photograph picks up rim-lighting at golden hour or dusk (forge-glow on the closest edge of the equipment). Tier cards in near-black (~#0E0E0E) with white text and ember-orange checkmarks. The 4-up photo proof grid uses moody equipment-at-dusk shots. See [FORGE archetype](../../../archetypes/forge.md). Pairs with the [dark feature bands product-block](../../product-block/research/refero-eb12db31-dark-feature-bands.md).
