## Why this is library-worthy
A focused **financing / payment-options pattern** — Cash / Finance pathway tabs, a structure-explainer (down-payment + term inputs, what each path means), and an "apply / talk to us about credit" CTA — that answers the "can I afford this?" question for high-ticket equipment. It is the third distinct member of the library's "money-tool" family and the only one about **purchase financing**: the [ROI calculator](./refero-29ece5fd-roi-calculator-projection.md) answers "will this pay for itself vs. my current method?" and the [natural-language quote calculator](./refero-28c45270-natural-language-calculator.md) answers "what does a job cost?" — neither answers "how do I pay for the machine over time?" For dealers selling $46,250–$72,000 GTEEX drones to farmers who routinely finance or lease equipment, a financing surface is a real conversion lever none of the existing 16 services patterns covers. **Critically, the source's specific UI is a MAP minefield for Terraplex dealers — the value of this note is as much in what to strip as what to keep** (see What NOT to take).

## What to take
- **Two pathway tabs at the top: `Finance` / `Cash`** (a dealer could add `Lease` as a third). Tabs swap the body between purchase structures rather than between products — the affordance is "here are the ways to acquire this," not "here are SKUs." This framing is the transferable core.
- **A small set of structure inputs**, grouped horizontally: a **Term selector** (dropdown — e.g. 36 / 48 / 60 / 72 months) and a **Down Payment** field. These let the buyer see how structure changes the commitment shape (longer term = lower per-period, more total) without the dealer having to quote a deal.
- **A single dominant figure** as the visual anchor of the panel, with a quiet supporting line beneath it. On a compliant Terraplex build this anchor is the **`Starting MSRP`** number (per [pricing-policy.md](../../../content/pricing-policy.md)), NOT a computed monthly payment — see What NOT to take.
- **A short disclaimer block** in muted small type at the bottom of the panel covering taxes, fees, and "estimate only / subject to credit approval." Terraplex's required `"Additional configurations available — call for details."` line lives here.
- **One primary CTA that funnels to a human/lender** — `Apply for Financing`, `Request Financing Options`, or `Talk to us about credit` — routing to the dealer's intake (pair with the [calendar-led rep card](../../contact/research/refero-701496e7-calendar-led-rep-card.md) or [quote-quiz modal](../../contact/research/refero-a0808dc4-quote-quiz-modal.md)). This satisfies pricing-policy rule 3: "upgrade paths go to the phone."
- **Compact, contained panel** (works as a modal off a PDP buy-button, OR as the centerpiece of a standalone `/financing` page). The contained two-column shape — product/context image on one side, the structure panel on the other — keeps it from reading as a full storefront.
- **Education over computation.** The strongest MAP-safe version leans into explaining *how financing through the dealer works* (which lenders, typical terms, what to bring, ag-lending programs like FSA / Farm Credit) — closer to a financing **explainer** than a live payment engine. This pairs naturally with the [numbered-process shopper](./refero-d030e2b2-numbered-process-shopper.md) "how it works" cadence and the [protection-plan explainer](./refero-a52e1484-protection-plan-explainer.md) register.

## What NOT to take
- **Do NOT show a computed monthly-payment headline** (the source's giant `$906/mo`). A monthly figure derived from a real configuration is effectively an actual transaction price, which violates [pricing-policy.md](../../../content/pricing-policy.md) rule 1 ("never publish an actual transaction price… on a dealer site"). The anchor number stays `Starting MSRP $X` with the standard disclaimer; financing math happens with the lender, off-site.
- **Do NOT replicate the "Est. APR 8.29%" line or any rate quote.** A published APR implies a specific deal and invites MAP scrutiny. Terms/rates are "subject to credit approval — contact us," never a number on the page.
- **Do NOT replicate the green "Include Est. Gas Savings −$108/mo" savings checkbox.** Subtracting a savings figure from a payment is exactly the discount/savings framing pricing-policy rules 1 and 5 prohibit. If the dealer wants to make an operating-cost-savings argument, that belongs in the separate [ROI calculator](./refero-29ece5fd-roi-calculator-projection.md) (framed against the customer's *current method*, with a defensible benchmark dataset), NOT folded into the purchase price.
- **Do NOT expose kit-tier naming** in the term/structure inputs ("Kit 1 financing," etc.) — pricing-policy rule 2. Configuration shoppers funnel to the `Request Pricing` / `Apply` CTA.
- **Do NOT let the calculator imply below-MAP pricing** ("finance and save," "our best rate beats MSRP"). The pattern surfaces *how to pay*, never *how to pay less*.
- Note there is **no archetype this structurally violates** — a financing surface is an archetype-neutral utility. The constraint here is regulatory (MAP), not aesthetic. The incompatibility to watch is with the hub's pricing policy, not with any of the six archetypes' anti-patterns.

## Adaptation notes (cross-archetype)
- **CLEAN / TACTICAL** (native fit): the calculator's structured-input register is most at home here. CLEAN — brand-navy anchor, generous whitespace, the panel reads like a corporate finance tool. TACTICAL — tracked-caps labels, 1px corner-bracket detailing on the input group, term selector as a precise stepped control. Pairs with the [spec-comparison grid](../../product-block/research/refero-713b66c0-spec-comparison-grid.md) on the buy flow.
- **FORGE / TERRAIN**: FORGE — dark contained panel (~#0E0E0E), single ember accent on the `Apply` CTA only; the MSRP anchor in white. TERRAIN — pair the financing panel with a cream "light band" explainer above it and a thin topographic divider; clay/sage accent on the active tab. See [FORGE](../../../archetypes/forge.md) / [TERRAIN](../../../archetypes/terrain.md).
- **FIELD / PATRIOT** (the sparsest archetypes — adapt, don't force a SaaS calculator): soften the utility register. Replace tabbed-input chrome with a **plain-language financing explainer** — warm-lit photograph of the equipment in-field, a short prose paragraph ("We work with Farm Credit and FSA-backed lenders to get you into an R-32 this season"), the `Starting MSRP` block, and a single `Talk financing with us` CTA. FIELD keeps the cream-default / warm-photographic register ([FIELD](../../../archetypes/field.md)); PATRIOT keeps cream-base with a single barn-red accent on the CTA and can lean on a "support for the operations that feed the country" framing ([PATRIOT](../../../archetypes/patriot.md)). For these archetypes the [natural-language calculator](./refero-28c45270-natural-language-calculator.md)'s sentence-shaped approach ("I want to finance an [I-19] over [60 months]") is a warmer alternative to the input-grid.

## Source brief
Tesla's vehicle-order **Financing Options** modal (`/ms/order/...` → Finance tab): Cash/Finance tabs, `$906/mo` headline, Down Payment + Term inputs, Est. APR, an includable gas-savings line, and a disclaimer block. The transferable move for a Terraplex dealer is the **structure of a financing surface** — pathway tabs + term/down-payment education + a fund-it CTA that routes to a lender/dealer — most directly useful as a `/financing` services page or a financing modal off the [PDP product-detail hero](../../hero/research/refero-d4c69c08-pdp-product-detail-hero.md). The source's live payment math is the part Terraplex must NOT copy: it collides head-on with MAP. The defensible adaptation is an MSRP-anchored, education-led financing explainer that funnels rate/payment questions to the dealer.
