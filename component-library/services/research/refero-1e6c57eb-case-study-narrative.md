## Why this is library-worthy
A long-form per-customer case-study layout: customer name + descriptor at the top → pull quote with oversized colored quotation mark + author headshot + role → a sticky-feeling **right-rail customer card** (logo, website, category) that anchors the page → a three-act narrative ("The challenge" → "The solution" → "The result") in plain editorial prose → prev/next case nav at the bottom → dark CTA closer with an adjacent customer-quote card. Distinct from every existing services note: [kicker-band](./refero-02ae136e-kicker-band-services.md) (capability-led overview), [three-tier inverted-middle](./refero-15fcbffc-three-tier-inverted-middle.md) (price-led tier comparison), [photo-mosaic](./refero-2fdf9ace-photo-mosaic-services.md) (breadth-led mosaic), [dark industry-vertical](./refero-74b10c78-dark-industry-vertical.md) (audience-anchored single page), [maintenance-program recommender](./refero-d928f889-maintenance-program-recommender.md) (program-led with widget), [audience-tabbed](./refero-fef890ec-audience-tabbed-services.md) (multi-audience tab strip) all surface services breadth or routing — none handle the "here's one customer's full story" register. This pattern fills that gap: the page is one customer's outcome, the proof is the depth of the story, the conversion happens through "if [customer] could do this, so can I."

## What to take
- Open with a tight 2-column header band: left ~65% has the customer name as a huge bold display headline (~48–64px black sans-serif on white) + a small sentence-case descriptor below ("Photo filter app" → for a dealer "Row-crop sprayer fleet, NW Iowa"). Right ~35% is the customer-card rail (described below) — pinned to the top of the page band, NOT in the page footer.
- The right-rail customer card is the page's visual anchor: an elevated white card (~16–24px corner radius, soft drop shadow) with the customer logo at top-center (~80–100px square in a tinted background tile), a thin rule below, then 2–3 attribute rows ("Website / [domain]", "App Category / Photo" → for a dealer "Operation / 12,000 acres", "Equipment / 2× R-32 + 1× I-19", "Services Used / Part 137 Compliance + Fleet Maintenance"). The card stays visible as the user reads the narrative — it's the "who is this" anchor for skimmers.
- Below the customer name + card row, an oversized pull quote: a large colored opening quotation mark (~64px in a saturated brand accent — red, navy, amber, ember, cyan per archetype) followed by 2–3 lines of the customer's own words (~18–22px italic-or-roman). Below the quote, a small attribution row: avatar (~40px circle) + name in bold + role-and-company in muted gray. The quote IS the lead — every word in the rest of the page exists to substantiate it.
- Three-act narrative structure with bold left-aligned section headings (no rules, no chrome, just the heading at ~24–28px sentence-case bold):
  1. **"The challenge"** — bold sub-heading naming the specific pain ("Unreliable data straining marketing performance and engineering capacity" → for a dealer "Three planting seasons short on Part 137 documentation"). Below the sub-heading, 2–4 paragraphs of plain editorial prose. No bullets, no callout boxes, no inline images. The prose IS the credibility — bullets compress the story into a feature list and break the documentary register.
  2. **"The solution"** — same heading + sub-heading + prose pattern. Names what the dealer / team did, in plain language, NOT in marketing copy. Multiple paragraphs are fine; the depth is the affordance.
  3. **"The result"** — same pattern. Numeric outcomes appear inline in prose ("a Membership churn reduction of almost 5%" → for a dealer "logged 1,800 acres of compliant Part 137 applications across the 2026 season"), never in pull-out stat callout cards. The numbers are claims the customer made; they're embedded in the customer's voice, not extracted as marketing chrome.
- After the three-act narrative, a horizontal **prev/next case nav** with two small tile cards — left tile is the previous case (small logo + customer name + arrow pointing left), right tile is the next case (mirrored). This is the only navigation chrome inside the case content; it signals "this is one of many" without breaking the per-customer focus.
- Dark CTA closer band at the bottom: full-bleed near-black (~#0E0E0E) band with a centered "Ready to get started?" headline + paired filled + outline CTAs on the left, AND on the right of the same band, a separate small dark-card customer testimonial (a different customer's quote + headshot + "READ CASE STUDY" link). The closer band's job is "if this customer story landed, here's another, and here's how to start" — two conversion paths at once, but visually subordinated to the main case study above.
- The page is otherwise extremely restrained: white background, charcoal text, no decorative chrome, generous vertical rhythm (~96–120px between major sections). The customer's voice is the visual treatment.

## What NOT to take
- Don't replace the editorial prose with bullet lists or feature-grid callouts. The pattern's whole credibility move is the depth of the narrative — bullets compress the story into a feature card, and the documentary register dies. If the dealer doesn't have ~2–4 paragraphs per act, the customer's story isn't ready for this pattern; use the [kicker-band services](./refero-02ae136e-kicker-band-services.md) (with a small "case study" band as one of its slots) or wait until the story is real.
- Don't import this pattern when the dealer doesn't have a real, named customer who consented to be quoted. The pull quote + customer card requires a real logo, a real person, a real outcome with numbers the customer is willing to stake their name on. Anonymized "Customer A reduced X by 30%" testimonials collapse the entire trust register — the credibility comes from "Shaheen Essabhoy, Business Intelligence at VSCO" being a real, verifiable person. For a Terraplex dealer this is a real farm name + a real principal + a real outcome they'll back in a phone call.
- Don't add stat callout cards above the three-act narrative ("5% churn reduction" / "$2M ARR added"). The numbers belong inside the customer's prose, in the customer's voice. Stat cards above the narrative collapse the page into a marketing infographic and break the "the customer is telling this story" affordance.
- Don't crowd the customer-card rail with marketing copy. The card is informational (logo + 2–4 attribute rows). Adding "Why they chose us" / "Their results" sections in the rail turns it into a second narrative competing with the main page — the rail's job is to anchor "who is this," not to summarize the case.
- Don't omit the prev/next case nav. The pattern depends on the customer being one of several — the nav signals "this is documentary, we have many of these." Without it, the case study reads as a single anomalous testimonial, and the marketing-page register creeps back in.
- Don't add a CTA inside the three-act narrative. The CTA lives ONCE in the dark closer band; in-narrative CTAs ("Schedule a demo" mid-paragraph) interrupt the customer's voice and turn the page into a sales loop. The customer's story IS the CTA — the closing "if this resonated, here's how to start" is the only place it surfaces.
- Don't import the literal RevenueCat blue-text accent or VSCO-specific copy. The transferable move is the structural cadence (customer-name header → quote with oversized colored mark → right-rail customer card → three-act prose narrative → prev/next nav → dark CTA closer), not the saturation. For a Terraplex dealer the accent shifts archetype-by-archetype; only the structure travels.

## Adaptation notes (cross-archetype)
- FIELD: native fit. Cream background (~#F4EFE7), warm-charcoal customer-name headline, a single warm-amber pull-quote opening mark. Customer-card rail uses a torn-cream-paper inset rather than a hard drop shadow. Photography (avatar headshots) is golden-hour real-farmer portraits. Three-act prose stays in warm-charcoal sans-serif. Prev/next case tiles use small farmstead photographs as the customer-thumbnail. Dark CTA closer becomes a deep-warm-charcoal (~#3A2B1F) band rather than pure black — keeps the warm palette intact at the closer. Pairs naturally with the [manifesto-creed hero](../../hero/research/refero-b86571f2-manifesto-creed-hero.md) on a parent /case-studies index page. See [FIELD archetype](../../../archetypes/field.md).
- CLEAN: native fit (most directly transferable). White background, charcoal text, single brand-navy or brand-color pull-quote opening mark. Customer-card rail in white with neutral-gray hairlines. Avatar photography is clean equipment-and-operator shots in daylight. Prev/next case tiles in white with thin hairline borders. Dark CTA closer in pure near-black. See [CLEAN archetype](../../../archetypes/clean.md). Pairs with the [centered-screenshot-trio hero](../../hero/research/refero-f619e700-centered-screenshot-trio.md) on a parent index.
- TERRAIN: works as a long sustained "light band" with the dark CTA closer providing the page's only band-rhythm break. Customer-card rail picks up clay or sage on the attribute-label color. Pull-quote opening mark in clay. Add a thin topographic-line motif between the three-act sub-headings (where a rule would normally go, but only at micro-scale, ~30% opacity) — signals the alternating rhythm without committing to it. See [TERRAIN archetype](../../../archetypes/terrain.md). Sibling: [topographic layered-bg hero](../../hero/research/refero-ae84b62a-topographic-layered-bg.md) on a parent /case-studies index.
- PATRIOT: cream background, deep-navy customer-name headline. Single barn-red pull-quote opening mark — the only saturated moment on the page (the flag colors stay at micro-scale per PATRIOT's chrome-restraint rule). Customer-card rail in cream with deep-navy attribute labels. Avatar photography is late-afternoon American-farmland portraits. Prev/next case tiles use cream backgrounds with deep-navy customer-name labels; the right-pointing arrow on the next-case tile is the second barn-red moment. Dark CTA closer band in deep navy (~#1F2C44) rather than pure black. Pairs with the [manifesto-creed hero](../../hero/research/refero-b86571f2-manifesto-creed-hero.md) on a parent index. See [PATRIOT archetype](../../../archetypes/patriot.md).
- FORGE: invert to a dark variant — near-black background (~#0E0E0E), white customer-name headline, ember-orange pull-quote opening mark. Customer-card rail in slightly-lighter dark (~#1A1A1A) with ember-orange attribute labels. Avatar photography is rim-lit at golden hour or dusk (forge-glow on the closest edge). Three-act prose in muted-cream on dark. Prev/next case tiles in near-black with thin charcoal hairline borders and ember chevron arrows. Dark CTA closer integrates seamlessly (no transition needed — the page is already dark). See [FORGE archetype](../../../archetypes/forge.md). Pairs with the [hardware-lineup dark hero](../../hero/research/refero-0945a36c-hardware-lineup-dark-hero.md) or [emblem-program hero](../../hero/research/refero-c1c2abcf-emblem-program-hero.md) on a parent /customers index.
- TACTICAL: tighten geometry — sharper rectangle customer-card and prev/next tiles (~6px corner radius instead of 16–24px), precision-sans for headings in tracked all-caps, monochrome white-on-near-black with a single steel-cyan accent on the pull-quote mark and active prev/next chevron. Add 1px corner-bracket details to the customer-card rail corners (consistent with the archetype's bracket motif). Avatar photography is studio-shot operator-with-equipment, no environmental context. Dark CTA closer uses precision-sans headings and a sharp-rectangle CTA pair. See [TACTICAL archetype](../../../archetypes/tactical.md). Pairs with the [emblem-program hero](../../hero/research/refero-c1c2abcf-emblem-program-hero.md) for a TACTICAL precision page rhythm.
