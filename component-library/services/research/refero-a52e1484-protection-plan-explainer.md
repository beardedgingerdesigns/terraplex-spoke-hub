## Why this is library-worthy
A services pattern for a **single named protection / extended-warranty / service-contract product** — the page sells one specific plan rather than tiers. Structure: SKU-style picker + price at the top (like a product), then a saturated brand-color full-bleed billboard with the plan name as a wordmark, then a stack of 5–7 named "promises" — each promise gets a real photograph + bold label + 2–3 line paragraph in a single column. No testimonials, no comparison tables, no upsell rail. Closes the gap between the [three-tier inverted-middle](./refero-15fcbffc-three-tier-inverted-middle.md) (multi-tier service picker) and the [maintenance-program recommender](./refero-d928f889-maintenance-program-recommender.md) (recommender widget) — this is the **single-product-plan-explainer** register.

## What to take
- Page opens like a product page, not a marketing page: small product preview (the equipment the plan covers) + plan name + price + SKU/option dropdown + Add-to-cart CTA. The plan is sold as a SKU, not a "request a quote" lead. ~25–30vh.
- Below the buy band, a fine-print disclaimer block (3–5 short bulleted conditions: "must be purchased within 30 days of equipment", "applies to original equipment only", "not transferable") in muted gray ~11–12px. The disclaimer is part of the page's credibility — by stating the boundaries upfront, the plan reads as legitimate insurance rather than vague reassurance.
- "Take a Closer Look" centered heading (~28–32px) anchors the body section.
- **Brand-billboard band**: a single full-bleed saturated brand-color rectangle (~30–40vh tall) with the plan name as an oversized wordmark in white (~96–120px display weight) + a 1-line value-prop subhead beneath the wordmark (~18–22px white). The billboard sets the plan's identity — it's the single piece of "marketing chrome" on the page.
- Below the billboard, a **stacked promise grid** — 5–7 single-column promise rows, each row laid out as: full-width photograph (~40vh, single landscape image) → bold sentence-case label below the photo (~22–28px) → 2–3 line paragraph (~14–16px). Source uses 6 promises: "Unlimited Number of Repairs", "Factory Maintenance", "Global Service and Support", "Service Extension Rewards", "Free Shipping", "Exclusive Service". Each promise is concrete (states a coverage limit, a service count, a benefit cap) — not aspirational adjectives.
- One promise in the stack uses a **brand-color illustration band** instead of a photograph (in the source: a stylized world map on the brand-blue surface for "Global Service and Support"). The map / diagram / illustration replaces a photograph for promises whose visual proof is geographic-coverage or process-flow rather than equipment-and-operator. Use sparingly — at most one illustration band per stack.
- Photographs are real operator / real-equipment / real-service-bench shots — NOT stock smiles. Source shows actual cinema rigs being repaired, actual technicians with tools, actual customer-service representatives at desks. The photographic register matches the plan's domain (equipment-service plan = equipment-service photographs).
- No closing CTA banner. The buy-band at the top IS the page's CTA; the stack below is pure explanation. The reader who scrolled the whole stack scrolls back to the top to buy, or doesn't.

## What NOT to take
- Don't use this pattern for multi-tier plans. The three-tier-inverted-middle pattern handles "good / better / best" tiering; this pattern is the **single-plan deep explainer**. Mixing the two collapses both — the tiered-comparison readers want a comparison, the single-plan readers want the promise depth.
- Don't put a contact form on this page. The page is transactional (SKU + price + cart) or it isn't this pattern. A contact-form variant of this page is structurally a different pattern (closer to [horizontal-row directory](../../contact/research/refero-c29ba321-horizontal-row-directory.md) or [calendar-led rep card](../../contact/research/refero-701496e7-calendar-led-rep-card.md)).
- Don't substitute icons for the promise photographs. The page's credibility depends on each promise being grounded in a real-operator / real-service-bench / real-coverage-map photograph. Icon-led variants exist (closer to [dense use-case grid](./refero-6f85d84c-dense-use-case-grid.md)) but they're a different pattern with a different register.
- Don't write aspirational promise labels ("Peace of Mind", "World-Class Service"). The pattern earns trust by stating concrete coverage details: "Unlimited Number of Repairs (limit $7,199 within 2 years)", "Two Factory Maintenance services per term". Aspirational adjectives kill the legitimacy.
- Don't add testimonials or 5-star review chrome. The page is the plan's spec sheet; testimonials live on a separate page. The disclaimer block + concrete coverage limits IS the credibility.
- Don't use this pattern for a free-with-purchase plan. The buy-band-at-top affordance assumes the plan is a paid SKU. For complimentary / bundled / included-with-purchase plans, the page rhythm collapses (no price, no cart) — use the [maintenance-program recommender](./refero-d928f889-maintenance-program-recommender.md) or a service-bullet block on the parent product page instead.

## Adaptation notes (cross-archetype)
- FIELD: warm-cream page background. Brand-billboard band uses warm-amber or sage at matched saturation (not blue). Photographs in promise stack are golden-hour operator-and-equipment shots — real applicators at real spray-day prep, real technicians at the dealer's bench. Plan name on billboard in cream / off-white. See [FIELD archetype](../../../archetypes/field.md). Most native fit when the plan is a [Sam Welton compliance / Part 137 maintenance offering](../../../content/compliance-program.md).
- CLEAN: white page background. Brand-billboard uses brand-navy or brand-teal at moderate saturation. Photographs are clean equipment-and-operator shots with soft drop-shadows. Disclaimer block in brand-charcoal ~11px. See [CLEAN archetype](../../../archetypes/clean.md).
- TERRAIN: cream-or-clay page. Brand-billboard uses topographic-charcoal or deep-clay at matched saturation. Photographs are landscape-anchored (equipment-on-field, drone-over-crops). Optional thin contour-line motif at the bottom edge of the billboard. See [TERRAIN archetype](../../../archetypes/terrain.md).
- PATRIOT: cream page. Brand-billboard uses deep-navy with the plan name in cream wordmark; barn-red is the SINGLE accent in the buy-band CTA only — not in the billboard (the billboard stays single-color-saturated). Photographs are late-afternoon American-farmland real-operator shots (real farmers, real dealers, real compliance technicians). See [PATRIOT archetype](../../../archetypes/patriot.md).
- FORGE: dark-dominant page (charcoal background). Brand-billboard uses ember-amber-to-warm-charcoal solid color (not gradient) at high saturation. Photographs are rim-lit equipment-at-dusk shots. Plan name on billboard in cream wordmark + ember-orange underline. See [FORGE archetype](../../../archetypes/forge.md). Pairs with [stats-led impact services](./refero-41c5ab43-stats-led-impact-services.md) for a dealer whose differentiation is operational scale + a paid protection plan on top.
- TACTICAL: dark page with tighter geometry. Brand-billboard uses near-black with a single steel-cyan accent line. Plan name in tracked all-caps precision-sans. Promise photographs are studio-shot equipment in seamless lighting. Add 1px corner-bracket motif to the billboard rectangle. See [TACTICAL archetype](../../../archetypes/tactical.md).

## Source brief
DJI's Care Pro page for the Ronin 4D-6K — DJI's paid extended-service program for their flagship cinema rig. The library-worthy move is the structural cadence (SKU-style buy-band + disclaimer → brand-billboard with plan wordmark → 6 stacked promise rows with concrete coverage details + real photographs → no closing CTA). Reusable for a Terraplex dealer offering an [extended R-32 / I-19 protection plan](../../../products/r-32.md), a [Part 137 compliance-maintenance subscription](../../../content/compliance-program.md), a fleet-protection program for multi-aircraft customers, or an enrollment-based [Part 107 prep-course package](../../../content/service-patterns.md) where the page wants to be transactional (SKU + buy) rather than lead-generation (form + follow-up).

Pick guide: protection-plan explainer when a dealer offers ONE specific paid plan with concrete coverage limits and the page needs to be transactional — buy now, not request a quote. Sibling services notes for adjacent decisions:
- [three-tier inverted-middle](./refero-15fcbffc-three-tier-inverted-middle.md) — when the plan offering is GOOD / BETTER / BEST tiering rather than a single SKU
- [maintenance-program recommender](./refero-d928f889-maintenance-program-recommender.md) — when the plan needs an interactive tier-recommender widget
- [stats-led impact services](./refero-41c5ab43-stats-led-impact-services.md) — when scale-of-operations is the differentiation
- [case-study narrative](./refero-1e6c57eb-case-study-narrative.md) — when a single named customer's outcome carries the credibility
- [numbered-process shopper](./refero-d030e2b2-numbered-process-shopper.md) — when the plan is enrollment-led (Part 107 cohort, fleet-share recruitment) rather than transactional buy-now
