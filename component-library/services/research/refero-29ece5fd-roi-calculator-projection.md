## Why this is library-worthy
A services-page calculator pattern that **projects dollar-savings and operational improvements** rather than routing the customer to a tier or generating a quote. Two-pane layout: left panel "Tell us where you are today" (input form: industry / acreage / current cost-per-acre / annual flight hours / labor count), right panel "This is what your operation could look like tomorrow" with 4–5 small projected-savings metrics in muted text and ONE oversized brand-color anchor metric (e.g. "Projected ROI: 1,149%") in a saturated tile. A footnote band beneath both panels discloses the benchmark methodology ("estimates based on real benchmarks from real Zendesk omnichannel customers"). Distinct from existing services calculators / recommenders: the [maintenance-program-recommender](./refero-d928f889-maintenance-program-recommender.md) takes model + flight-time and routes to a service tier; the [natural-language-calculator](./refero-28c45270-natural-language-calculator.md) is a sentence-shaped quote estimator ("[N] acres of [crop] in [zip]"); the [audience-tabbed services pattern](./refero-fef890ec-audience-tabbed-services.md) shows different value props per audience but doesn't quantify them. This pattern's job is **money — not routing, not quoting, just the dollar argument** for switching from ground rig / helicopter / manned-fixed-wing to a GTEEX drone-spray operation. Right pick when the dealer's sale collapses on price-objection ("I can't justify $50K for a drone") and the dealer needs a single calculator that turns the customer's existing operation cost into a comparable annual savings figure. Adjacent fit: [Part 107 prep cohort](../../../content/compliance-program.md) ROI for an applicator weighing certification cost vs. job-rate increase; [Sam Welton compliance program](../../../content/compliance-program.md) ROI for a custom applicator weighing audit-prep retainer vs. fine exposure.

## What to take
- Open with a contained light hero (~24–30vh) carrying ONE centered headline ("Check your ROI" → for a dealer "See your spray-operation savings" / "Project your drone-fleet ROI" / "Compare your current cost per acre"). No subhead, no CTAs. The page IS the CTA — every input the user types is conversion friction reduced.
- **Two-panel main block** — a single horizontal row that splits the page exactly in half on desktop, stacks on mobile. Left ~50% panel is white with a thin grey border (~1px), heading "Tell us where you are today." (~24–28px sentence-case bold), small instruction line beneath ("All fields required to get a projection."), then 4–6 input fields stacked vertically with sentence-case labels above each input:
  1. **Industry / Operation type** — small dropdown (Row crop / Specialty crop / Pasture / Orchard / Custom applicator service / Other). Sets the benchmark profile the projection pulls from.
  2. **Annual sprayed acreage** — single numeric input. The page's primary scale variable.
  3. **Current cost per acre** — single numeric input. The page's primary cost variable. Default placeholder shows the regional industry-benchmark figure as a hint.
  4. **Annual flight / spray hours** — single numeric input. Drives the labor-savings projection.
  5. **Number of operators** — single numeric input. Drives the headcount-savings projection.
  6. (Optional) **Current method** — small dropdown (Ground rig / Helicopter / Manned fixed-wing / No current spray service). Modifies the comparison's reference baseline.
- Right ~50% panel is white with the same thin grey border, heading "This is what your operation could look like tomorrow." (~24–28px sentence-case bold), small subhead ("Based on real benchmarks from [N]+ Terraplex dealers, this is what we project for your operation."). Below the subhead, **a 2×2 grid of small projected-improvement metrics** in bold dark navy (or archetype-charcoal), each with a sentence-case caption beneath:
  - "−$X / acre" — Cost-per-acre reduction
  - "−Y hours" — Annual labor-hours saved
  - "+Z%" — Acres-per-day throughput increase
  - "−W%" — Input-cost reduction (chemical / fuel)
- **Anchor metric tile** — a single saturated brand-color square (~140–200px square) in the lower-right corner of the right panel, white sans-serif "X%" or "$X,XXX" in heavy weight (~36–48px) with "Projected annual savings" or "Projected ROI" in smaller cream beneath. The tile is the page's emotional payload — every input the user types updates this number in real time. Without the anchor tile, the four small metrics read as a generic dashboard; with it, the page reads as a single argument.
- **Methodology footnote band** beneath the two-panel block (~10vh, full-width, muted-grey small-text). Three lines max:
  - "The above estimates are based on [data source: Terraplex dealer benchmarks / GTEEX OEM data / industry survey] for [scope: row-crop / specialty-crop / pasture] operations."
  - "Projected savings are calculations based on [comparison baseline: ground rig / helicopter / manned fixed-wing] reference benchmarks. Your operation's actual results may differ."
  - "*Please note, this tool does not include data from [excluded scenario: night-spray operations / orchard low-canopy / >5,000-acre operations]."
- **One closer CTA below the footnote** — single filled brand-color pill ("Talk to a [Dealer] specialist about these numbers" → routes to [calendar-led-rep-card contact](../../contact/research/refero-701496e7-calendar-led-rep-card.md) or [split-screen-trust-curtain contact](../../contact/research/refero-7c41e8a8-split-screen-trust-curtain.md)). The CTA earns its place because the calculator's job is to lower price-objection; the next step is a real conversation about whether the projection holds for the specific operation.
- **Vertical rhythm** — generous whitespace (~80–100px) above and below the two-panel block. The block is the page; nothing else competes with it. No testimonials band, no logo wall, no FAQ — those belong on adjacent service pages, not this one. The page is a single tool.

## What NOT to take
- Don't build a multi-step wizard. The pattern's affordance is the side-by-side input-and-projection — the user sees the projection update as they type, not after clicking "Next" three times. Wizards turn the calculator into a form; the form belongs on [form-with-topic-checkboxes contact](../../contact/research/refero-23938dfd-form-with-topic-checkboxes.md), not here.
- Don't skip the anchor tile. The 4 small metrics on their own read as generic — the saturated-color anchor tile is what makes the page argue. If the dealer has nothing to put in the anchor tile (no single headline savings figure), this pattern is wrong; use [stats-led-impact-services](./refero-41c5ab43-stats-led-impact-services.md) instead.
- Don't put fake numbers in the anchor tile. The methodology footnote is the credibility bind — it explicitly attributes the projection to a real benchmark source. If the dealer doesn't have a defensible benchmark dataset (their own dealer's customer outcomes, OEM-supplied benchmark from Revolution Drones / GTEEX, or an industry report from [Terraplex Ag](../../../research/manufacturers/terraplex.md)), the page will be challenged by any sophisticated buyer and the dealer's credibility collapses. Better to skip this pattern entirely than to ship a calculator with made-up math.
- Don't expose the projection formula. The page argues by showing the projection, not by exposing the model. Hover-tooltips or "How is this calculated?" expansions belong in the methodology footnote disclosure, not inline with the metrics. If the buyer needs the exact math, the dealer-rep call is the right channel.
- Don't add this pattern alongside the [maintenance-program-recommender](./refero-d928f889-maintenance-program-recommender.md) or [natural-language-calculator](./refero-28c45270-natural-language-calculator.md) on the same services-section index. Three calculators on adjacent pages dilute every one of them — pick the one that fits the dealer's primary objection (cost-objection → ROI calc; service-tier-objection → recommender; quote-objection → natural-language calc) and feature it; relegate the others to deeper pages.
- Don't import the literal Zendesk green / brand-color combination. The pattern is the two-pane structure + anchor-tile + methodology footnote — the saturated brand color in the anchor tile and the disclaimer-band styling are archetype concerns. Use the dealer's archetype palette throughout.
- Don't add a "Save this projection" or "Email me a report" capture without a privacy disclosure. The calculator runs client-side by design — the moment it captures inputs to a server, it becomes a lead-capture form with regulatory implications (acreage and current-cost figures are operationally sensitive). Either keep the calculator strictly client-side, or treat the email-capture step as a separate form with explicit consent language pulled from [content/content-exclusions.md](../../../content/content-exclusions.md).

## Source brief
Zendesk's CX Champion ROI calculator — a two-pane "tell us where you are today / this is tomorrow" projection tool that maps an enterprise's call/chat/email volume to projected handle-time and cost-per-contact reductions, anchored by a single oversized "Projected ROI" tile. Adapted here as a services pattern for Terraplex dealers selling against ground-rig / helicopter / manned-fixed-wing baselines, where the conversion-blocking objection is dollar-cost rather than service-tier or scope.
