## Why this is library-worthy
A services pattern organized around the customer's **journey through the service**, not around the dealer's capability menu. The page leads with a 3-step horizontal numbered timeline (numbered circular icons connected by a thin rule, each step captioned with title + helper copy) that previews the entire experience before the customer commits. The pattern fits two distinct dealer briefs that share the same structural cadence:

- **Service-application variant** (light contained hero) — for "show me how this works" pages: service application, [Part 137 onboarding](../../../content/compliance-program.md), fleet-evaluation booking, demo scheduling. Uses a contained light-hero with photo card + floating UI mockups.
- **Enrollment / become-a-X variant** (saturated brand-color hero) — for recruitment programs: [Part 107 prep cohort signup](../../../content/compliance-program.md), fleet-share enrollment, operator certification ladder, dealer-affiliate program. Uses a saturated brand-color hero band with offer-shaped headline and a multi-quote testimonial band on a saturated brand-color surface mid-page.

Different from existing services patterns: the [kicker-band](./refero-02ae136e-kicker-band-services.md) shows alternating capabilities; the [three-tier inverted-middle](./refero-15fcbffc-three-tier-inverted-middle.md) shows price tiers; the [photo-mosaic](./refero-2fdf9ace-photo-mosaic-services.md) shows breadth; the [dark industry-vertical](./refero-74b10c78-dark-industry-vertical.md) shows audience anchoring; the [audience-tabbed](./refero-fef890ec-audience-tabbed-services.md) swaps audiences; the [maintenance-program-recommender](./refero-d928f889-maintenance-program-recommender.md) shows tier comparison + interactive routing. None of those preview the **process itself** — this pattern fills that gap.

## What to take

### Hero (pick one variant)
- **Variant A — service-application (contained light hero):** ~50–60vh two-column layout. Left ~60% holds a large left-aligned multiline headline (~72–96px desktop, heavy-weight grotesque, line-height 0.95–1.05) + short paragraph + single rounded-pill primary CTA ("Apply now" / "Book a fleet evaluation" / "Get started"). Right ~40% holds a rounded-rectangle photo card showing the service in action (operator at controller, technician on a service bench, farmer reviewing a quote) with one or two small floating UI mockups (price tile, status chip) layered on top.
- **Variant B — enrollment / become-a-X (saturated brand-color hero):** ~50–60vh contained brand-color band — the page's brand IS the background. Left ~50%: 3-line bold sentence-case headline with deliberate sentence breaks ("Work your fields. / Earn certification. / Stay current.") + 1–2 line subhead + single filled CTA pill ("Sign up" — short, transactional, NOT "Learn more"). Right ~50%: photograph of a real participant in working context with 1–2 small overlay UI cards showing earnings or compliance status — the overlays are credibility-by-receipts.
- Both variants drop into the same body cadence below the hero.

### Numbered process flow (the pattern's core affordance — required)
- Full-width band directly under the hero. Three equal columns; each column holds a numbered circular icon (~48–64px diameter, 2–3px archetype-accent border, the digit centered inside in bold) at the top, a short bold step title below the circle (~16–18px, sentence-case), and 1–2 lines of helper copy underneath.
- The three circles are connected by a thin horizontal rule running through the centerpoint of each circle — the rule is the visual proof that the steps are sequential, not parallel. Without the connecting rule the affordance collapses into "stuff to do."
- Step labels follow an entry → dealer-side → outcome cadence:
  - Step 1 names the entry action ("Apply" / "Submit your fleet info" / "Request a quote" / "Create account")
  - Step 2 names the dealer-side action ("We review and route" / "Sam Welton confirms compliance" / "We schedule your demo")
  - Step 3 names the customer outcome ("Take delivery" / "Begin training" / "Start earning")

### ZIP-or-region localization band
- Directly below the process flow. Single-line input field (~240–300px wide, rounded, light-gray border) + small dark-filled solid CTA ("Find a dealer near you" / "Check service availability in your county" / "Find a Part 107 cohort starting near you").
- To the right of the search field, a horizontal logo grid (4–6 partner / dealer-network / certification-issuing-body logos in evenly-spaced white circles ~72–96px diameter with a subtle drop shadow). Or a flat 3×3 / 4×3 monochrome logo wall.
- The ZIP input MUST return real results (real list of cohort locations, certified dealers, service areas the user can navigate to). A ZIP field returning "We'll be there soon" defeats the credibility move.

### "How [the service] works" — 3-up cards on a tonal-shifted band
- Full-bleed dark band (deep brand-charcoal or archetype-aligned dark color) with a large white left-aligned heading and a 3-column equal-width feature card row beneath. Each card holds a rounded-corner photograph (~16px radius) showing one moment in the service experience — the apply screen, the dealer review, the delivered outcome — with optional small floating chat-bubble or price-tile mockups layered over the photo and a short caption underneath.
- Optional left/right carousel arrows positioned to the right of the section title for navigating to additional slides if the dealer has more than 3 service moments to show.

### 4-up benefit cards with attribution (enrollment variant only)
- "Why [audience] choose [Program]" — short bold heading, then 4 horizontal cards each with bold word ("Community / Rewards / Earnings / Support") + 2-line description + small attribution photo + name + city of a real participant. The attribution under each benefit card is the credibility — "Andrew S. / Syracuse NY" alongside a quote means the benefit is claimed by a person, not by the brand.

### Multi-quote testimonial band (enrollment variant only)
- Full-bleed dark-saturated brand-color band ("Members make it happen") with 2 cream-on-saturated cards floating side-by-side. Each card is a longer pull quote (~3–5 lines) with photo + name + city.
- The saturation contrast (cream cards on deep brand-color band) is the affordance — it lifts the band out of the page's monochrome rhythm at the moment maximum emotional credibility is needed.

### FAQ accordion with smart-default expansion
- Long vertical list on cream, each row a bold question with a chevron toggle, expanded answers in regular weight. ~6–10 questions, no tag-filter strip.
- Default state: ALL collapsed except the highest-friction one (e.g. "How do I get paid?" / "What does it cost?" / "What are the requirements?") which expands to show 2–3 short paragraphs of explanation. Default-expanding the highest-friction question is the affordance — surfacing the answer the user is most likely about to look for.

### Closing CTA band
- Full-bleed brand-color band again (mirrors the enrollment hero) OR deep tonal band (service-application variant). Headline + repeated hero CTA + parallel photo card on the right.
- The page opens and closes with the same conversion offer at the same brand-color register; the body is the journey from "what / how / who / why" to "yes."

### Vertical band rhythm
- The cadence is cream → cream → deep-band → cream → deep-band → cream → deep-band, with three deep-band moments, never two adjacent. The band rhythm carries the page. If the page is all cream, the timeline's framing role collapses and the page reads as a long FAQ.

## What NOT to take
- Don't skip the numbered timeline. The timeline IS the pattern — without it, the page collapses into a generic alternating-bands services page covered structurally by the [kicker-band pattern](./refero-02ae136e-kicker-band-services.md). The 3 numbered steps with the connecting rule are the affordance; everything else is supporting material.
- Don't expand the timeline beyond 3 steps. Four-step processes (apply → review → onboard → deploy) are tempting but lose the "this is fast / this is simple" register. If the dealer's actual process has 4+ stages, group them into 3 customer-perspective phases (e.g., "Tell us about your operation" / "We confirm fit" / "Start flying") rather than naming every internal handoff. The customer doesn't want the org chart.
- Don't import the literal Instacart cream + deep-green + brand-typography palette ("Instacart Sans / Instacart Contrast"). The transferable move is "alternating cream/dark sections + heavy grotesque headline + circular numbered steps" — for a Terraplex dealer the colors come from the archetype, not from this source.
- Don't add a CTA inside any of the timeline cells. The timeline is non-interactive — it's an explanation. A "Start step 1" pill inside the first circle collapses the timeline-as-preview into a wizard-as-form and breaks the cadence.
- Don't combine the timeline with the [maintenance-program-recommender](./refero-d928f889-maintenance-program-recommender.md) on the same page. The recommender's interactive widget and the timeline's non-interactive preview compete for the same conversion slot. Pick one — the timeline is for "show me how this works," the recommender is for "tell me which one I need."
- Don't import Instacart's specific recruitment copy ("flexible pay, autonomy"). The transferable move is process-clarity, not gig-work positioning. For a Terraplex dealer the parallel claims are "compliance handled / equipment ready / your operation grows."
- Don't add the ZIP localization band if the dealer has only one service area. With one location, the field reads as decorative chrome. The search earns its weight only when the dealer either covers multiple counties / states OR is part of the broader [49-location Terraplex network](../../../research/manufacturers/terraplex.md). For single-location dealers, replace the search row with a flat heading + 4-up partner / certification logo wall instead.
- Don't crowd the hero photo card with more than 2 floating UI mockups. The photo carries the warmth; mockups carry the proof. Three or more mockups turn the photo into a feature-grid backdrop.
- Don't use the enrollment variant if the dealer's services aren't actually enrollment-shaped. Without an enrollment journey (Part 107 prep cohort, fleet-share program, operator certification ladder, dealer-affiliate program), the saturated-brand-color hero + multi-quote testimonial band reads as borrowed B2C-marketplace chrome.
- Don't surface only one testimonial in the enrollment variant. The pattern's emotional credibility comes from MULTIPLE real stories on the dark band — at minimum 2, ideally 3, each with photo + name + city. A single testimonial collapses the band into decorative chrome and the page feels astroturfed. If the dealer doesn't have 2+ real participant testimonials, drop the testimonial band entirely; the FAQ + closer signup do the work.
- Don't put a CTA in every section. The pattern has TWO conversion moments: the hero CTA and the closer CTA. Sections in between are explanation, not conversion. Adding "Sign up" buttons in the process flow / benefits / testimonials fights the natural rhythm of "explain → convince → commit."
- Don't make the FAQ default-collapsed. One question (the highest-friction one — "how do I get paid" / "what does it cost" / "what are the requirements") MUST default-expand. The user's eye lands on it, sees an immediate answer, and trusts the rest are equally answered. Fully-collapsed FAQ is just navigation chrome.
- Don't substitute generic stock photos in the "How it works" 3-up — the photos must show real people doing each step in a recognizable environment, or the timeline feels like a placeholder.

## Adaptation notes (cross-archetype)
- FIELD: native fit. Cream page background (~#F4EFE7), warm-charcoal headlines, golden-hour photographic hero of a real operator-and-equipment shot. Numbered step circles use a 2px warm-amber border with charcoal numbers; connecting rule in muted-amber. Alternating bands cream → warm-amber-tonal → cream → dark-wood-photographic. The 3-up "How it works" cards on dark wood (~#3A2B1F) instead of pure black so the warmth carries through. For the enrollment variant: hero brand-color is deep-amber or terracotta with cream overlay UI cards; multi-quote testimonial band uses warm-clay (~#3A2B1F) deep-tonal backdrop with cream cards. Pairs naturally with the [manifesto-creed hero](../../hero/research/refero-b86571f2-manifesto-creed-hero.md) above and the [editorial cream-bands product-block](../../product-block/research/refero-ecfaaa4f-editorial-cream-bands.md) below for a complete FIELD page rhythm. See [FIELD archetype](../../../archetypes/field.md).
- CLEAN: white page background, brand-charcoal headlines, clean equipment-and-operator hero photograph with soft drop shadow. Numbered step circles use a 2px brand-navy border with charcoal numbers. Alternating bands white → pale gray → white → brand-navy-photographic. The 3-up "How it works" cards on brand-navy (~#1F2C44) with white text. For the enrollment variant: hero brand-color is brand-navy with white overlay UI cards; multi-quote testimonial band on deep-navy with white cards. Pairs with the [centered-screenshot-trio hero](../../hero/research/refero-f619e700-centered-screenshot-trio.md) above. See [CLEAN archetype](../../../archetypes/clean.md).
- TERRAIN: the alternating bands ARE the archetype's rhythm — light → dark → light → dark — and the numbered timeline becomes the page's anchoring band that visually carries the contour-line motif as the connecting rule between circles. Step circles use clay or sage borders. The "How it works" 3-up cards on a dark-clay band with topographic dividers between cards. Add a thin topographic divider above the numbered process flow and again above the FAQ accordion. Multi-quote band uses clay-or-sage saturated tone instead of generic dark. See [TERRAIN archetype](../../../archetypes/terrain.md). Pairs with the [topographic layered-bg hero](../../hero/research/refero-ae84b62a-topographic-layered-bg.md) for an opening dark moment that the cream timeline-band then alternates against.
- PATRIOT: cream page background (~#F4EFE7), deep-navy headlines, late-afternoon American-farmland hero photograph (real farmer or operator at golden hour). Numbered step circles use deep-navy borders with cream numbers; the connecting rule is the only place barn-red appears (a thin barn-red rule connecting all 3 circles — the literal flag colors at micro-scale, doing the rhetorical work of "this is the path through American-grown service"). Alternating bands cream → deep-navy-photographic → cream → barn-red-tonal-strip-once. The 3-up "How it works" cards on deep-navy with cream text. For the enrollment variant: hero brand-color is deep-navy with cream overlay UI cards (1 in navy, 2 in barn-red, 3 in navy — flag-derived alternation kept at the digit-chrome scale); multi-quote testimonial band on deep-navy with cream cards. Pairs with the [manifesto-creed hero](../../hero/research/refero-b86571f2-manifesto-creed-hero.md) above. See [PATRIOT archetype](../../../archetypes/patriot.md).
- FORGE: invert to dark — charcoal page background (~#1A1A1A), white headlines, ember-rim-lit operator hero photograph (dusk lighting, equipment edge catching warm light). Numbered step circles in near-black (~#0E0E0E) with white numbers and a 2px ember-orange border; connecting rule in ember-orange. Alternating bands stay dark throughout — charcoal → near-black-photographic → charcoal → near-black-photographic, with the "alternation" coming from the photographic intensity rather than from a light/dark swing. The timeline's demystification job lands harder on FORGE because the dark register usually feels gatekept; a 3-step plain-language stepper softens it. For the enrollment variant: hero brand-color is near-black with ember overlay UI cards; multi-quote testimonial band uses near-black (~#0E0E0E) deep contrast with dark-card testimonial blocks. Pairs with the [hardware-lineup dark hero](../../hero/research/refero-0945a36c-hardware-lineup-dark-hero.md) above and the [dark feature bands product-block](../../product-block/research/refero-eb12db31-dark-feature-bands.md) below. See [FORGE archetype](../../../archetypes/forge.md).
- TACTICAL: tighten geometry — sharper rectangle CTAs (~6px corner radius), precision-sans for headlines and step titles, monochrome white-on-near-black with a single steel-cyan accent on the step circle borders + connecting rule + ZIP search field focus state. Step circles become rounded squares (~8px corner radius) rather than circles, consistent with the archetype's bracket motif. Hero photograph is studio-shot equipment + operator with no environmental context. Add a thin 1px corner-bracket detail to the step number tiles and the 3-up "How it works" card corners. For the enrollment variant: hero brand-color is steel-cyan-on-near-black with white overlay UI cards. Multi-quote testimonial band on near-black with white precision-sans cards. Logo wall in tracked all-caps monochrome. See [TACTICAL archetype](../../../archetypes/tactical.md). Pairs with the [topographic layered-bg hero](../../hero/research/refero-ae84b62a-topographic-layered-bg.md) for a TACTICAL precision-led page.

## Source brief
Instacart's [/shoppers](https://shoppers.instacart.com/) recruitment landing for prospective Instacart shoppers — a B2C marketplace surfacing the path to becoming a delivery operator. The library-worthy move is the structural cadence (two-column hero with floating-mockup photo card OR saturated brand-color hero → 3-step horizontal numbered timeline with connecting rule → ZIP locator + partner logo wall → "How it works" 3-up cards on dark band → optional 4-up benefit cards + multi-quote testimonial band → FAQ with smart-default expansion → closer signup band), reusable for a Terraplex dealer's service-application flow, [Part 137 enrollment](../../../content/compliance-program.md), [Part 107 prep program](../../../content/compliance-program.md), or any service where the customer needs to see the entire path before committing.

The pattern's whole credibility move is "we're showing you the process, not just the price" — which fits dealers whose brief is "this is unfamiliar territory, walk me through it" rather than "I already know what I want."
