## Why this is library-worthy
A services pattern organized around the customer's **journey through the service**, not around the dealer's capability menu. The page leads with a 3-step horizontal timeline (numbered circular icons connected by a thin rule, each step captioned with title + helper copy) that previews the entire experience before the customer commits. The pattern serves two registers — both rooted in the same Instacart-Shoppers source — and the dealer picks one based on whether the page's job is *demystification* or *enrollment*:

- **Process-clarity register** — neutral two-column hero with a photo card, the stepper as page-anchor, ZIP locator beneath, dark-band 3-up "How it works" cards, alternating cream/dark testimonial-FAQ-CTA bands. The right pick when the customer's hesitation is "what does this even involve?" — service application, [Part 137 onboarding](../../../content/compliance-program.md), fleet-evaluation booking, demo-day intake.
- **Enrollment-led register** — saturated brand-color hero band carrying a 3-line offer headline + single transactional CTA ("Apply now" / "Sign up"), the stepper directly underneath, ZIP localization band, 3-up "how it works" cards, **4-up benefit cards with named-attendant attribution** (name + city under each benefit claim), a **multi-quote testimonial band on a saturated brand-color surface** (cream cards floating on deep brand color, 2–3 long quotes), FAQ with **smart-default expansion** of the highest-friction question, then a closer brand-color band that mirrors the hero. The right pick when the dealer is recruiting *people* into a *program* with a literal sign-up moment — [Part 107 prep cohort](../../../content/compliance-program.md), fleet-share / fleet-leasing program, operator certification ladder, dealer-affiliate or dealer-network recruiter pipeline.

Both registers are structurally distinct from the library's other services patterns: the [kicker-band](./refero-02ae136e-kicker-band-services.md) shows alternating capabilities; the [three-tier inverted-middle](./refero-15fcbffc-three-tier-inverted-middle.md) shows price tiers; the [photo-mosaic](./refero-2fdf9ace-photo-mosaic-services.md) shows breadth; the [dark industry-vertical](./refero-74b10c78-dark-industry-vertical.md) shows audience anchoring; the [audience-tabbed](./refero-fef890ec-audience-tabbed-services.md) swaps audiences; the [maintenance-program-recommender](./refero-d928f889-maintenance-program-recommender.md) shows tier comparison + interactive routing; the [photo-led process steps](./refero-3d9feff9-photo-led-process-steps.md) is a *6-step* photo-grid for transactional walkthroughs (drop-off / consignment), not a 3-step preview. None preview the **process itself** at the 3-step horizontal-timeline scale — this pattern fills both the demystification gap and the enrollment-recruitment gap.

## What to take
- Open with a contained hero (~50–60vh). Two register choices:
  - **Process-clarity register**: neutral page-base two-column layout. Left ~60% holds a large left-aligned multiline headline (~72–96px on desktop, heavy-weight grotesque, line-height 0.95–1.05) with a short paragraph beneath and a single rounded-pill primary CTA ("Get started" / for a dealer "Book a fleet evaluation" / "Schedule a demo"). Right ~40% holds a rounded-rectangle photo card showing the service in action — operator at controller, technician on a service bench, farmer reviewing a quote — with **one or two** small floating UI mockups (price tile, status chip) layered on top. No more than 2 mockups — three or more turn the photo into a feature-grid backdrop.
  - **Enrollment-led register**: saturated brand-color hero band (the page IS the brand register, NOT full-bleed black-or-white neutral). Left ~50% holds a 3-line bold sentence-case headline with deliberate sentence breaks ("Work your fields. / Earn certification. / Stay current."), 1–2 line subhead, and a **single short transactional filled CTA pill** ("Apply now" / "Sign up" — never "Learn more"). Right ~50% holds a single photograph of a real participant in working context with 1–2 small overlay UI cards floating over the photograph showing **earnings or compliance status** ("$23.37 / order", "Part 107: Active") — credibility-by-receipts.
- **The 3-step numbered timeline is the pattern's core affordance.** Full-width band directly under the hero. Three equal columns; each column holds a numbered circular icon (~48–64px diameter, 2–3px archetype-accent border, the number centered inside in bold) at the top, a short bold step title below the circle (~16–18px, sentence-case), and 1–2 lines of helper copy underneath. The three circles are connected by a thin horizontal rule that runs through the centerpoint of each circle — the rule is the visual proof that the steps are sequential, not parallel.
  - Step 1 names the entry action ("Apply" / "Submit your fleet info" / "Request a quote")
  - Step 2 names the dealer-side action ("We review and route" / "Sam Welton confirms compliance" / "We schedule your demo")
  - Step 3 names the customer outcome ("Start delivering" / "Begin training" / "Take delivery")
- Below the timeline, a **ZIP-or-region search anchor**: single-line input field (~240–300px wide, rounded, light-gray border) + a small dark-filled solid CTA ("Show stores" / for a dealer "Find a dealer near you" / "Check service availability in your county"). To the right of the search field, a horizontal logo grid (4–6 partner / dealer-network logos in evenly-spaced white circles with ~72–96px diameter and a subtle drop shadow). The search routes the customer who's not yet ready to apply but wants to confirm the service exists in their area; the logos provide credibility-by-affiliation.
- "How [the service] works" — full-bleed dark band (deep brand-charcoal or archetype-aligned dark color) with a large white left-aligned heading and a 3-column equal-width feature card row beneath. Each card holds a rounded-corner photograph (~16px radius) showing one moment in the service experience — the apply screen, the dealer review, the delivered outcome — with small floating chat-bubble or price-tile mockups layered over the photo and a short caption underneath. Optional left/right carousel arrows positioned to the right of the section title for navigating to additional slides if the dealer has more than 3 service moments to show.
- **Enrollment-register only**: a **4-up benefit card row** between the dark "How it works" band and the testimonial band. Short bold heading ("Why operators choose [Program]"), then 4 horizontal cards each with a single bold word ("Community / Earnings / Support / Rewards" → for a dealer "Compliance / Earnings / Network / Currency") + 2-line description + small attribution photo + name + city of a real participant. The attribution under each benefit card is the credibility move — the benefit is claimed by a person, not by the brand.
- **Enrollment-register testimonial band** — full-bleed **saturated brand-color** band ("Operators make it happen") with **2–3 cream-on-saturated cards floating side-by-side**. Each card is a longer pull quote (~3–5 lines) with photo + name + city. The saturation contrast (cream cards on deep brand-color band) is the affordance — it lifts the band out of the page's monochrome rhythm at the moment maximum emotional credibility is needed. **At least 2 testimonials** — a single testimonial collapses the band into decorative chrome and the page feels astroturfed; if the dealer doesn't have 2+ real participant testimonials, drop the testimonial band entirely. Process-clarity register uses the simpler cream-on-cream testimonial cadence covered in the alternating-bands rhythm below.
- **Alternating bands** carry the rest of the page: testimonial cards on cream backgrounds → next FAQ-band on dark → final "Become a [customer/operator]" CTA band on dark with the hero CTA repeated and a parallel photo card on the right. The alternation is at section scale, not within bands; each band stays uniform internally. **Enrollment register**: closer band is full-bleed brand-color again, mirroring the hero ("Become a [program-member] today / Apply now and start [outcome] in as soon as [timeframe]" + Apply CTA + photograph of a member at the right). The page opens and closes with the same conversion offer at the same brand-color register; the body is the journey from "what / how / who / why" to "yes."
- Closing FAQ accordion: long vertical list on cream, each row a bold question with a chevron toggle, expanded answers in regular weight. ~6–10 questions, no tag-filter strip (tags are over-engineered for the process-led register; if the dealer needs more than 10 FAQ items, [search-led help center](../../../contact/research/refero-00cda269-search-led-help-center.md) is the better pattern). **Smart-default expansion**: ALL rows collapsed *except* the highest-friction question ("How do I get paid?" / "What does Part 107 prep cost?" / "What are the requirements?"), which defaults open and pre-answers the question the user is most likely about to ask. Fully-collapsed FAQ is just navigation chrome.

## What NOT to take
- Don't skip the numbered timeline. The timeline IS the pattern — without it, the page collapses into a generic alternating-bands services page covered structurally by the [kicker-band pattern](./refero-02ae136e-kicker-band-services.md). The 3 numbered steps with the connecting rule are the affordance; everything else is supporting material.
- Don't expand the timeline beyond 3 steps. Four-step processes (apply → review → onboard → deploy) are tempting but lose the "this is fast / this is simple" register that drives the affordance. If the dealer's actual process has 4+ stages, group them into 3 customer-perspective phases (e.g., "Tell us about your operation" / "We confirm fit" / "Start flying") rather than naming every internal handoff. The customer doesn't want the org chart.
- Don't import Instacart's literal warm cream + deep green + brand-typography palette ("Instacart Sans / Instacart Contrast"). The transferable move is "alternating cream/dark sections + heavy grotesque headline + circular numbered steps" — for a Terraplex dealer the colors come from the archetype, not from this source.
- Don't add a CTA inside any of the timeline cells. The timeline is non-interactive — it's an explanation. A "Start step 1" pill inside the first circle collapses the timeline-as-preview into a wizard-as-form and breaks the cadence.
- Don't combine the timeline with the [maintenance-program-recommender](./refero-d928f889-maintenance-program-recommender.md) on the same page. The recommender's interactive widget and the timeline's non-interactive preview compete for the same conversion slot. Pick one — the timeline is for "show me how this works," the recommender is for "tell me which one I need."
- Don't import Instacart's specific recruitment copy ("flexible pay, autonomy"). The transferable move is process-clarity, not gig-work positioning. For a Terraplex dealer the parallel claims are "compliance handled / equipment ready / your operation grows."
- Don't add the ZIP-or-region search anchor if the dealer has only one service area. With one location, the field reads as decorative chrome. The search earns its weight only when the dealer either covers multiple counties / states OR is part of the broader [49-location Terraplex network](../../../research/manufacturers/terraplex.md). For single-location dealers, replace the search row with a flat heading + 4-up partner / certification logo wall instead.
- Don't fabricate the ZIP-localization affordance — the ZIP input MUST return real results (real list of cohort locations, certified dealers, or service areas the user can navigate to). A ZIP field that returns "We'll be there soon" defeats the whole credibility move; the rest of the page assumes the ZIP just worked.
- Don't crowd the hero photo card with more than 2 floating UI mockups. The photo carries the warmth; mockups carry the proof. Three or more mockups turn the photo into a feature-grid backdrop.
- **Enrollment-register only**: don't put a CTA in every section. The pattern has TWO conversion moments — the hero CTA and the closer CTA. Sections in between (process flow, benefits, testimonials, FAQ) are explanation, not conversion. Adding "Apply now" buttons in the process flow / benefits / testimonials fights the natural rhythm of "explain → convince → commit."
- **Enrollment-register only**: don't import this register if the dealer's services aren't actually enrollment-shaped. The pattern's affordance is "here are 3 steps to join → join now." Without an enrollment journey ([Part 107 prep cohort](../../../content/compliance-program.md), fleet-share program, operator certification ladder, dealer-affiliate program), the brand-color hero + 4-up benefit cards + multi-quote testimonial collapses into generic chrome. Drop back to the process-clarity register, or use [kicker-band](./refero-02ae136e-kicker-band-services.md) (capability-led) or [audience-tabbed](./refero-fef890ec-audience-tabbed-services.md) (audience-led) instead.

## Source brief
Instacart's [/shoppers](https://shoppers.instacart.com/) recruitment landing for prospective Instacart shoppers — a B2C marketplace surfacing the path to becoming a delivery operator. The library-worthy move is the structural cadence at two registers: **process-clarity** (neutral two-column hero with floating-mockup photo card → 3-step horizontal numbered timeline with connecting rule → ZIP locator + partner logo wall → "How it works" 3-up cards on dark band → alternating testimonial / FAQ / final CTA bands) and **enrollment-led** (saturated brand-color hero with offer headline → 3-step process flow → ZIP localization → 3-up "how it works" → 4-up benefit cards with named-attendant attribution → multi-quote testimonial band on saturated brand color → smart-default-expanded FAQ → closer signup band that mirrors the hero). Reusable for a Terraplex dealer's service-application flow, [Part 137 enrollment](../../../content/compliance-program.md), [Part 107 prep cohort recruitment](../../../content/compliance-program.md), fleet-share program signup, dealer-affiliate program, or any service where the customer needs to see the entire path before committing. The whole credibility move is "we're showing you the process, not just the price" (clarity register) or "here's the path to join us, signup" (enrollment register) — both fit dealers whose brief is "this is unfamiliar territory, walk me through it" rather than "I already know what I want."
