## Why this is library-worthy
A services pattern organized around the customer's **journey through the service**, not around the dealer's capability menu. The page leads with a 3-step horizontal timeline (numbered circular icons connected by a thin rule, each step captioned with title + helper copy) that previews the entire experience before the customer commits. The pattern is the right pick when the dealer's brief is "show me how this works" — service application, [Part 137 onboarding](../../../content/compliance-program.md), [Part 107 prep enrollment](../../../content/compliance-program.md), fleet-evaluation booking, or any service where the customer needs to see the path before they start. Different from existing services patterns: the [kicker-band](./refero-02ae136e-kicker-band-services.md) shows alternating capabilities; the [three-tier inverted-middle](./refero-15fcbffc-three-tier-inverted-middle.md) shows price tiers; the [photo-mosaic](./refero-2fdf9ace-photo-mosaic-services.md) shows breadth; the [dark industry-vertical](./refero-74b10c78-dark-industry-vertical.md) shows audience anchoring; the [audience-tabbed](./refero-fef890ec-audience-tabbed-services.md) swaps audiences; the [maintenance-program-recommender](./refero-d928f889-maintenance-program-recommender.md) shows tier comparison + interactive routing. None of those preview the **process itself** — this pattern fills that gap.

## What to take
- Open with a contained hero (~50–60vh): two-column layout. Left ~60% holds a large left-aligned multiline headline (~72–96px on desktop, heavy-weight grotesque, line-height 0.95–1.05) with a short paragraph beneath and a single rounded-pill primary CTA ("Get started" / for a dealer "Apply now" / "Book a fleet evaluation"). Right ~40% holds a rounded-rectangle photo card showing the service in action — operator at controller, technician on a service bench, farmer reviewing a quote — with one or two small floating UI mockups (price tile, status chip) layered on top.
- **The 3-step numbered timeline is the pattern's core affordance.** Full-width band directly under the hero. Three equal columns; each column holds a numbered circular icon (~48–64px diameter, 2–3px archetype-accent border, the number centered inside in bold) at the top, a short bold step title below the circle (~16–18px, sentence-case), and 1–2 lines of helper copy underneath. The three circles are connected by a thin horizontal rule that runs through the centerpoint of each circle — the rule is the visual proof that the steps are sequential, not parallel.
  - Step 1 names the entry action ("Apply" / "Submit your fleet info" / "Request a quote")
  - Step 2 names the dealer-side action ("We review and route" / "Sam Welton confirms compliance" / "We schedule your demo")
  - Step 3 names the customer outcome ("Start delivering" / "Begin training" / "Take delivery")
- Below the timeline, a **ZIP-or-region search anchor**: single-line input field (~240–300px wide, rounded, light-gray border) + a small dark-filled solid CTA ("Show stores" / for a dealer "Find a dealer near you" / "Check service availability in your county"). To the right of the search field, a horizontal logo grid (4–6 partner / dealer-network logos in evenly-spaced white circles with ~72–96px diameter and a subtle drop shadow). The search routes the customer who's not yet ready to apply but wants to confirm the service exists in their area; the logos provide credibility-by-affiliation.
- "How [the service] works" — full-bleed dark band (deep brand-charcoal or archetype-aligned dark color) with a large white left-aligned heading and a 3-column equal-width feature card row beneath. Each card holds a rounded-corner photograph (~16px radius) showing one moment in the service experience — the apply screen, the dealer review, the delivered outcome — with small floating chat-bubble or price-tile mockups layered over the photo and a short caption underneath. Optional left/right carousel arrows positioned to the right of the section title for navigating to additional slides if the dealer has more than 3 service moments to show.
- **Alternating bands** carry the rest of the page: testimonial cards on cream backgrounds → next FAQ-band on dark → final "Become a [customer/operator]" CTA band on dark with the hero CTA repeated and a parallel photo card on the right. The alternation is at section scale, not within bands; each band stays uniform internally.
- Closing FAQ accordion: long vertical list on cream, each row a bold question with a chevron toggle, expanded answers in regular weight. ~6–10 questions, no tag-filter strip (tags are over-engineered for the process-led register; if the dealer needs more than 10 FAQ items, [search-led help center](../../contact/research/refero-00cda269-search-led-help-center.md) is the better pattern).

## What NOT to take
- Don't skip the numbered timeline. The timeline IS the pattern — without it, the page collapses into a generic alternating-bands services page covered structurally by the [kicker-band pattern](./refero-02ae136e-kicker-band-services.md). The 3 numbered steps with the connecting rule are the affordance; everything else is supporting material.
- Don't expand the timeline beyond 3 steps. Four-step processes (apply → review → onboard → deploy) are tempting but lose the "this is fast / this is simple" register that drives the affordance. If the dealer's actual process has 4+ stages, group them into 3 customer-perspective phases (e.g., "Tell us about your operation" / "We confirm fit" / "Start flying") rather than naming every internal handoff. The customer doesn't want the org chart.
- Don't import Instacart's literal warm cream + deep green + brand-typography palette ("Instacart Sans / Instacart Contrast"). The transferable move is "alternating cream/dark sections + heavy grotesque headline + circular numbered steps" — for a Terraplex dealer the colors come from the archetype, not from this source.
- Don't add a CTA inside any of the timeline cells. The timeline is non-interactive — it's an explanation. A "Start step 1" pill inside the first circle collapses the timeline-as-preview into a wizard-as-form and breaks the cadence.
- Don't combine the timeline with the [maintenance-program-recommender](./refero-d928f889-maintenance-program-recommender.md) on the same page. The recommender's interactive widget and the timeline's non-interactive preview compete for the same conversion slot. Pick one — the timeline is for "show me how this works," the recommender is for "tell me which one I need."
- Don't import Instacart's specific recruitment copy ("flexible pay, autonomy"). The transferable move is process-clarity, not gig-work positioning. For a Terraplex dealer the parallel claims are "compliance handled / equipment ready / your operation grows."
- Don't add the ZIP-or-region search anchor if the dealer has only one service area. With one location, the field reads as decorative chrome. The search earns its weight only when the dealer either covers multiple counties / states OR is part of the broader [49-location Terraplex network](../../../research/manufacturers/terraplex.md). For single-location dealers, replace the search row with a flat heading + 4-up partner / certification logo wall instead.
- Don't crowd the hero photo card with more than 2 floating UI mockups. The photo carries the warmth; mockups carry the proof. Three or more mockups turn the photo into a feature-grid backdrop.

## Adaptation notes (cross-archetype)
- FIELD: native fit. Cream page background (~#F4EFE7), warm-charcoal headlines, golden-hour photographic hero of a real operator-and-equipment shot. Numbered step circles use a 2px warm-amber border with charcoal numbers; connecting rule in muted-amber. Alternating bands cream → warm-amber-tonal → cream → dark-wood-photographic. The 3-up "How it works" cards on dark wood (~#3A2B1F) instead of pure black so the warmth carries through. Pairs naturally with the [manifesto-creed hero](../../hero/research/refero-b86571f2-manifesto-creed-hero.md) above and the [editorial cream-bands product-block](../../product-block/research/refero-ecfaaa4f-editorial-cream-bands.md) below for a complete FIELD page rhythm. See [FIELD archetype](../../../archetypes/field.md).
- CLEAN: white page background, brand-charcoal headlines, clean equipment-and-operator hero photograph with soft drop shadow. Numbered step circles use a 2px brand-navy border with charcoal numbers. Alternating bands white → pale gray → white → brand-navy-photographic. The 3-up "How it works" cards on brand-navy (~#1F2C44) with white text. Pairs with the [centered-screenshot-trio hero](../../hero/research/refero-f619e700-centered-screenshot-trio.md) above. See [CLEAN archetype](../../../archetypes/clean.md).
- TERRAIN: the alternating bands ARE the archetype's rhythm — light → dark → light → dark — and the numbered timeline becomes the page's anchoring band that visually carries the contour-line motif as the connecting rule between circles. Step circles use clay or sage borders. The "How it works" 3-up cards on a dark-clay band with topographic dividers between cards. See [TERRAIN archetype](../../../archetypes/terrain.md). Pairs with the [topographic layered-bg hero](../../hero/research/refero-ae84b62a-topographic-layered-bg.md) for an opening dark moment that the cream timeline-band then alternates against.
- PATRIOT: cream page background (~#F4EFE7), deep-navy headlines, late-afternoon American-farmland hero photograph (real farmer or operator at golden hour). Numbered step circles use deep-navy borders with cream numbers; the connecting rule is the only place barn-red appears (a thin barn-red rule connecting all 3 circles — the literal flag colors at micro-scale, doing the rhetorical work of "this is the path through American-grown service"). Alternating bands cream → deep-navy-photographic → cream → barn-red-tonal-strip-once. The 3-up "How it works" cards on deep-navy with cream text. Pairs with the [manifesto-creed hero](../../hero/research/refero-b86571f2-manifesto-creed-hero.md) above. See [PATRIOT archetype](../../../archetypes/patriot.md).
- FORGE: invert to dark — charcoal page background (~#1A1A1A), white headlines, ember-rim-lit operator hero photograph (dusk lighting, equipment edge catching warm light). Numbered step circles in near-black (~#0E0E0E) with white numbers and a 2px ember-orange border; connecting rule in ember-orange. Alternating bands stay dark throughout — charcoal → near-black-photographic → charcoal → near-black-photographic, with the "alternation" coming from the photographic intensity rather than from a light/dark swing. Pairs with the [hardware-lineup dark hero](../../hero/research/refero-0945a36c-hardware-lineup-dark-hero.md) above and the [dark feature bands product-block](../../product-block/research/refero-eb12db31-dark-feature-bands.md) below. See [FORGE archetype](../../../archetypes/forge.md).
- TACTICAL: tighten geometry — sharper rectangle CTAs (~6px corner radius), precision-sans for headlines and step titles, monochrome white-on-near-black with a single steel-cyan accent on the step circle borders + connecting rule + ZIP search field focus state. Step circles become rounded squares (~8px corner radius) rather than circles, consistent with the archetype's bracket motif. Hero photograph is studio-shot equipment + operator with no environmental context. Add a thin 1px corner-bracket detail to the step number tiles and the 3-up "How it works" card corners. See [TACTICAL archetype](../../../archetypes/tactical.md). Pairs with the [topographic layered-bg hero](../../hero/research/refero-ae84b62a-topographic-layered-bg.md) for a TACTICAL precision-led page.

## Source brief
Instacart's [/shoppers](https://shoppers.instacart.com/) recruitment landing for prospective Instacart shoppers — a B2C marketplace surfacing the path to becoming a delivery operator. The library-worthy move is the structural cadence (two-column hero with floating-mockup photo card → 3-step horizontal numbered timeline with connecting rule → ZIP locator + partner logo wall → "How it works" 3-up cards on dark band → alternating testimonial / FAQ / final CTA bands), reusable for a Terraplex dealer's service-application flow, [Part 137 enrollment](../../../content/compliance-program.md), [Part 107 prep program](../../../content/compliance-program.md), or any service where the customer needs to see the entire path before committing. The pattern's whole credibility move is "we're showing you the process, not just the price" — which fits dealers whose brief is "this is unfamiliar territory, walk me through it" rather than "I already know what I want."
