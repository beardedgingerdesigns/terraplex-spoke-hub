## Why this is library-worthy
A services pattern where the page is organized by the customer's professional category — a horizontal tab strip ("Consulting Firms / Law Firms / Staffing Agencies / Accounting Firms" or for a dealer "Row crops / Specialty crops / Co-ops / Custom applicators / Research") swaps the value-prop bullets and supporting photo without leaving the page. The tab-strip framing reframes services from "here's what we do" to "here's what we do FOR YOU," which is the conversion move when a single dealer serves multiple distinct customer types whose service needs only partially overlap. Distinct from the existing [kicker-band](./refero-02ae136e-kicker-band-services.md) (capability-led), [three-tier inverted-middle](./refero-15fcbffc-three-tier-inverted-middle.md) (price-led), and [photo-mosaic](./refero-2fdf9ace-photo-mosaic-services.md) (breadth-led) patterns — this one is audience-led.

## What to take
- Open with a tight 2-column hero: left ~50% has a kicker label ("CALENDLY FOR PROFESSIONAL SERVICES" / "TERRAPLEX FOR ROW-CROP OPERATIONS"), a 2–3 line headline, a 1-line subhead, and paired filled + outline CTA pills. Right ~50% holds a photograph of an operator in working context with a small overlapping testimonial card (avatar + quote + outcome) anchored to the photo's lower-left. The overlay testimonial is the credibility move — proof shows up before the page even starts naming services.
- Below the hero, a flat 6-up logo wall — single row of customer or partner logos, monochrome, no decorative chrome.
- Customer-story highlight band: 2-column, left ~40% has a small "USER STORY" tag + 2-line headline + "Read customer story" link, right ~60% has a single landscape photograph (operator-in-context, no studio chrome). One story per page; the band is the high-trust anchor.
- **The audience tab strip** — this is the pattern's core affordance. A single horizontal row of 4–6 audience-type tabs ("Consulting Firms / Law Firms / Staffing Agencies / Accounting Firms" → for a dealer "Row crops / Specialty crops / Co-ops / Custom applicators / Research"). Active tab carries a thin underline (~2px) in a single brand accent; inactive tabs are muted text. Below the strip, the active tab's value-prop block: 3-row capability checklist with bold-label + 1-line description ("Enhance client experience — Ensure consultants are accessible to clients…") on the left ~50%, a single supporting photograph of that audience-type in their working context on the right ~50%. Switching tabs swaps both the checklist AND the photo without scrolling.
- Below the audience tabs, "A [solution] built for [audience]" — alternating image-text feature blocks in the same kicker → headline → 2-line paragraph → "Learn more" arrow-link cadence as the [kicker-band services pattern](./refero-02ae136e-kicker-band-services.md). Each feature block alternates side every other row.
- Mid-page, a single quote testimonial band: large left-aligned quote in italic, attribution underneath (avatar + name + role + company). One quote, no carousel — the trust comes from a single recognizable name, not from quote volume.
- Workflow-tab strip near the page bottom: a second tab row ("Workflows / Embeds / Extensions / Analytics / CRM Integrations / Customers / Meeting Polls" → for a dealer "Demos / Training / Compliance / Fleet Logging / Service Tickets") that swaps the example-card visual beneath. Same affordance as the audience tabs but applied to features, not customer types.
- 3-up testimonials grid + pricing-tier comparison (Teams / Enterprise) close the page. Both are flat, restrained, no animation.

## What NOT to take
- Don't import Calendly's specific consulting / legal / accounting tab labels — they're SaaS-vertical-specific. The transferable move is "audience-type tab strip that swaps both the value-prop checklist AND the supporting photograph." For a Terraplex dealer this is "Row crops / Specialty crops / Co-ops / Custom applicators / Research" — pick the 4–6 audiences the dealer actually serves, no more. Tabs beyond 6 wrap and lose scannability.
- Don't make the audience tabs a vertical accordion. The horizontal strip IS the affordance — it shows the audience breadth at a glance. An accordion hides the other audiences and turns the page into a single audience-type story.
- Don't put two tab strips side-by-side. The audience strip and the workflow strip should be far apart on the page (audience-tabs near top after the customer-story band; workflow-tabs near the bottom before testimonials). Stacking them collapses the page into a tab-soup.
- Don't omit the overlapping testimonial card on the hero photograph. The card is what differentiates this hero from a generic "person + text + CTA" pattern — it's a credibility anchor that pre-loads the page's trust before any service is named.
- Don't add a "RECOMMENDED" badge on any audience tab. Tabs are equal-weighted by definition; flagging one collapses the audience-served-equally affordance into a feature-grade hierarchy.
- Don't use this pattern when the dealer serves a single audience. With one tab, the strip becomes decorative chrome. Use the [kicker-band services pattern](./refero-02ae136e-kicker-band-services.md) (alternating capability bands) instead.

## Adaptation notes (cross-archetype)
- CLEAN: native fit. White background, brand-navy active-tab underline and accent on the "Learn more" arrow links. Hero photograph is a clean equipment-and-operator shot with a soft drop shadow on the overlay testimonial card. Audience-tab labels in brand-charcoal sentence-case. See [CLEAN archetype](../../../archetypes/clean.md). Pairs with the [centered-screenshot-trio hero](../../hero/research/refero-f619e700-centered-screenshot-trio.md).
- TERRAIN: the audience tab strip becomes the page's main "interactive band" moment. Add a thin topographic divider above the tab strip and again below the active-tab content panel. Tab labels and active-state accent in the archetype's clay or sage. Switching tabs feels like turning a contour-line page in a field guide. See [TERRAIN archetype](../../../archetypes/terrain.md).
- FIELD: cream background, warm-charcoal tab labels, single amber active-tab underline. Hero photograph is golden-hour operator-and-equipment with a torn-cream-paper testimonial card overlay (hand-feel, not corporate drop-shadow). The audience tabs become "Family farms / Co-ops / Custom applicators / Specialty growers" with audience-photographs in golden-hour light per tab. Pairs with the [manifesto-creed hero](../../hero/research/refero-b86571f2-manifesto-creed-hero.md). See [FIELD archetype](../../../archetypes/field.md).
- PATRIOT: cream background, deep-navy tab labels, barn-red active-tab underline (the only saturated moment in the band — restraint anchors PATRIOT's flag-color rule at micro-scale). Hero photograph is late-afternoon American-farmland operator-and-equipment, testimonial overlay card with deep-navy attribution text. Audience tabs frame the dealer's customer types in plain heritage language ("Multi-generation farms / Co-ops / Custom applicators / Specialty growers"). Pairs with the [manifesto-creed hero](../../hero/research/refero-b86571f2-manifesto-creed-hero.md). See [PATRIOT archetype](../../../archetypes/patriot.md).
- TACTICAL: tighten geometry — sharper rectangle CTA buttons (~6px radius), precision-sans for tab labels in tracked all-caps ("ROW CROPS / SPECIALTY / CO-OPS / CUSTOM"), monochrome black/white only with a single steel-cyan active-tab underline. Hero photograph is studio-shot equipment on seamless black with no environmental context — the absence IS the affordance. Audience-tab content panels get a thin 1px corner-bracket detail on the photograph corners (consistent with the archetype's bracket motif). See [TACTICAL archetype](../../../archetypes/tactical.md). Pairs with the [hardware-lineup dark hero](../../hero/research/refero-0945a36c-hardware-lineup-dark-hero.md).
- FORGE: invert to dark — charcoal background (~#1A1A1A), white tab labels, ember-orange active-tab underline. Hero photograph is rim-lit operator-and-equipment with golden / forge-glow lighting. Testimonial overlay card is near-black (~#0E0E0E) with white text and an ember accent on the role/title line. The audience tabs work as well in dark as in light — the structural cadence travels. See [FORGE archetype](../../../archetypes/forge.md). Pairs with the [dark feature bands product-block](../../product-block/research/refero-eb12db31-dark-feature-bands.md) on adjacent product pages.

## Source brief
Calendly's [/solutions/professional-services](https://calendly.com/solutions/professional-services) page — a B2B SaaS landing organized by professional vertical. The library-worthy move is the structural cadence (kicker-tagged hero with overlapping testimonial card → logo wall → customer-story highlight → **audience-type tab strip** swapping value-prop checklists and photographs → alternating feature bands → workflow-tab strip → testimonials → pricing close), reusable for a Terraplex dealer who serves multiple distinct customer types and wants each customer to find their own page-within-a-page without page navigation. Most directly relevant for dealers in the [49-location Terraplex network](../../../research/manufacturers/terraplex.md) who explicitly serve different customer types ([Part 137 commercial applicators](../../../content/compliance-program.md), private farms, co-ops) where one-size-fits-all messaging would lose two of the three audiences.
