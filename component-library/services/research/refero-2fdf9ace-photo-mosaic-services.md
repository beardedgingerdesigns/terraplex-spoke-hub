## Why this is library-worthy
A services overview where breadth is communicated by an irregular photo mosaic — 6–8 photos of different service categories arranged in a tight Pinterest-style grid (mixed aspect ratios, each photo small-captioned with the service name) — paired on the right with a 3-row capability list ("In-studio classes / Salon & spa appointments / On-demand workouts" type structure). The mosaic does the "we cover all of this" work visually; the right column does the structured-text work for scanners. Closes the [services × patriot](../../../archetypes/patriot.md) 0-gap from a different angle than the [kicker-band services pattern](./refero-02ae136e-kicker-band-services.md), and is the right pick when the dealer's services are best understood through imagery (operator training, field demos, equipment delivery) rather than tier comparison.

## What to take
- Build a 2-column band: left ~60% is the photo mosaic, right ~40% is a heading + 3–4 capability rows.
- The mosaic is an irregular grid: tiles vary in size (some 1×1, some 1×2, some 2×1) and the gutter is ~8–12px. Each tile shows a representative photograph for that service category, with a small bold caption overlaid in the bottom-left of the tile (e.g. "Boxing", "Spa", "Yoga" in the source; "Fleet demos", "Operator training", "Compliance review", "Field service" for a dealer).
- Right column: bold sentence-case heading anchoring the section ("What's available for employees with ClassPass?" → "What's covered in our service program?"). Below it, 3–4 capability rows, each a small bold label + one-line description ("In-studio classes — Take classes at the best studios & gyms around the world"). No icons, no checkmark bullets — just bold-label-then-description, separated by ~16px of vertical rhythm.
- Anchor the section above the mosaic with stats or a logo wall — the source uses a "Thousands of top-rated businesses" logo wall (12 brand logos in a 4×3 grid) above. For a dealer this becomes a stats band (50+ farms served, 12 counties, 4 years) or a brand-partner row.
- Close the page with a stats band on a darker neutral (charcoal in the source, but cream-on-deep-cream or navy-on-cream works for warmer archetypes) — 3 large percentages with one-line captions.

## What NOT to take
- The cheerful saturated photography (purple-lit gym shots, bright spa) is brand-specific to ClassPass. The transferable move is "irregular mosaic of category-labeled photographs," not the saturation. For a Terraplex dealer this is warm-lit drone-and-operator photographs — keep the irregular tile layout, swap the visual register.
- Don't make the mosaic photos uniformly sized. The irregular tile structure IS the affordance — uniform tiles read as a product gallery, not a services overview.
- Don't use small icons in place of photographs. Icons in this slot collapse the "we actually do all of this" credibility move into a generic capability grid.
- Don't pair the mosaic with a third column (e.g. CTA list). Two columns only — adding a third turns the mosaic into a side decoration.
- The dark-dominant requirement of FORGE/TACTICAL conflicts with this pattern's photographic-mosaic affordance: forcing the mosaic onto a black backdrop loses the brightness contrast that makes the irregular tiles legible. For dark archetypes use the [kicker-band services pattern](./refero-02ae136e-kicker-band-services.md) or the [three-tier inverted-middle services pattern](./refero-15fcbffc-three-tier-inverted-middle.md) instead.

## Adaptation notes (cross-archetype)
- FIELD: native fit. Mosaic of warm-lit operator/equipment photographs (golden hour, weathered hands, fenceline shots), cream background, amber-on-cream capability headings. The irregular tile gutter can be a torn-paper edge or a soft cream rule rather than a hard line. See [FIELD archetype](../../../archetypes/field.md).
- CLEAN: white background, neutral grey gutter, photography stays clean and product-forward (drone hardware, app-screenshot, satellite map). Capability headings in brand-charcoal, single brand accent on the heading underline only. See [CLEAN archetype](../../../archetypes/clean.md).
- TERRAIN: works as the "light band" in the alternating rhythm. The mosaic itself is light, capability list stays light; the next section drops to dark with a topographic divider. The mosaic gutter becomes the topographic line motif at micro-scale. See [TERRAIN archetype](../../../archetypes/terrain.md).
- PATRIOT: cream background, deep-navy capability headings, the irregular mosaic is shot in late-afternoon American-farmland light — equipment-and-operator photographs that read as heritage rather than stock corporate. The bottom stats band uses navy and barn-red on alternating percentages (e.g. "50+" navy, "12 counties" barn-red, "4 years" navy) — keeps the flag colors at micro-scale. Pairs with the [manifesto-creed hero](../../hero/research/refero-b86571f2-manifesto-creed-hero.md) for a full PATRIOT page rhythm. See [PATRIOT archetype](../../../archetypes/patriot.md).

ClassPass's [/corporate-wellness](https://classpass.com/corporate-wellness) B2B landing page.
