## Why this is library-worthy
A services pattern for a **dealer-program × parent-network co-branded landing** — the page is NOT the dealer's standalone service offering, and NOT the parent network's program either; it's the *integration* of the two, explained as a single benefits walkthrough. Page anchors on two logos (dealer wordmark at the top nav; parent-program wordmark embedded as the mid-page section anchor) and the body cadence is benefit-category rows mapping how dealer-side actions earn / redeem credit in the parent program. None of the existing services patterns covers cross-org program integration: the [protection-plan explainer](./refero-a52e1484-protection-plan-explainer.md), [three-tier inverted-middle](./refero-15fcbffc-three-tier-inverted-middle.md), and [maintenance-program recommender](./refero-d928f889-maintenance-program-recommender.md) all explain a dealer-internal program, not a partnership.

## What to take
- **Hero is full-bleed photograph + centered headline naming BOTH brands**: "[Parent program] — Benefits with [dealer brand]". The headline IS the page's thesis — neither brand is implicit. ~30–35vh, no CTA pill in the band. Photograph shows the lifestyle outcome (relaxed customers at the venue) — not the program logo or the equipment.
- **Eyebrow strip beneath the hero** with a pastel-tinted band (warm-pink in the source) carrying a single-paragraph plain-English explainer: "Members of [parent program] enjoy special benefits when [doing dealer thing]. Unlock [N] perks, including [one named example], with a valid [parent program] membership." This strip is the page's load-bearing copy — anyone who reads only it understands the deal.
- **Mid-page wordmark anchor**: a large display rendering of the PARENT program's logo (Accor ALL in the source), centered, surrounded by generous whitespace, ~25vh. This anchor is the visual signal that the page now switches register from "marketing intro" to "program details" — the parent program is the substantive subject, the dealer is the venue.
- **Asymmetric image-text rows** for each benefit category (Earn Reward points / Enjoy soft benefits / Status Guarantee / Redeem Reward points), alternating image-left/text-right and image-right/text-left. Each row carries ~3–5 lines of body plus optional bullet list with small diamond/dot bullets for benefit specifics. Photographs are real-venue real-customer shots, not stock smiles. ~25–30vh per row.
- **Tier-mapping row** as a horizontal card group (3 cards in the source: Gold / Platinum / Diamond) with each card showing the PARENT program's tier name + the spend threshold + a brief 2-line description of what triggers the tier. Critical move: the tiers are the PARENT program's, NOT the dealer's. The dealer's role is venue-of-qualifying-purchases, not tier-grantor.
- **Cross-link card block** at ~70% scroll: a centered card with the headline "Wish to join [parent program]?", a 2–3-line paragraph, and a single saturated CTA pointing OUT of the dealer's site INTO the parent program's enrollment page. The page explicitly hands off to the parent program — this is a feature, not a flaw. Dealers who try to capture enrollment themselves break the integration.
- **Reciprocal-discovery rows** at ~80% scroll: 2–3 image-text rows under "Explore our [Collection]" — each row points at a dealer-side category that earns parent-program credit. Photograph + 2-line label + small text CTA. Closes the loop: "now that you understand the integration, here are the dealer-side surfaces that benefit from it."
- **Partner logo wall in the footer** showing the broader parent-network siblings (other venues / dealers / brands under the same parent program). The wall gives the dealer's integration credibility-by-association — they're one of N legitimate venues, not an outlier.
- **No form anywhere on the page.** Enrollment is the parent program's responsibility; the dealer's page is purely explanatory. The single CTA points to the parent-program signup.

## What NOT to take
- Don't put a dealer-side enrollment form on this page. The integration only works if the dealer is the venue and the parent program is the rewards system; collecting parent-program signups on the dealer's domain creates a confusing dual-enrollment flow. Use a [single-purpose form](../../contact/research/refero-a40c8cca-catalog-request-form.md) on a separate page if dealer-side opt-in is needed alongside.
- Don't render the dealer's own program tiers in the tier-mapping row. The row's identity is "here are the PARENT's tiers and what the dealer-side spend earns" — substituting dealer-side "Bronze / Silver / Gold" service tiers collapses this back into the [three-tier inverted-middle](./refero-15fcbffc-three-tier-inverted-middle.md) pattern, which is a different page.
- Don't omit the parent-program wordmark anchor in the middle of the page. Without it, the page reads as a dealer-internal benefits sheet (closer to [protection-plan explainer](./refero-a52e1484-protection-plan-explainer.md)) and the partner-integration story is lost — the reader doesn't realize a separate parent program exists.
- Don't use this pattern if the parent program isn't a real third-party program the dealer can point to. Inventing a "parent network" for marketing purposes reads as borrowed legitimacy and fails the [content exclusions](../../../content/content-exclusions.md) rule against unverifiable claims. Either the parent program exists with a real signup URL or this isn't the right pattern.
- Don't use stock-business smiles in the asymmetric image-text rows. The pattern's credibility lives in the photographs being real venues / real customers / real benefit-in-use — the integration is concrete, not aspirational. Substituting generic stock collapses the page into a brochure.
- Don't add a closing CTA banner back to the dealer's main funnel ("Shop now" / "Request a demo"). The page's entire purpose is to route the reader into the parent-program enrollment; a dealer-funnel CTA at the bottom undercuts the parent-program handoff and creates a CTA collision.

## Adaptation notes (cross-archetype)
- FIELD: warm-cream page background. Hero photograph is golden-hour real-applicator-at-real-spray-day. Eyebrow strip in soft sage or warm-amber (not pink). Mid-page parent-program wordmark in cream-on-charcoal block or charcoal-on-cream. Photographs in body rows are real Terraplex operators using their R-32s at customer farms. See [FIELD archetype](../../../archetypes/field.md). Most native fit when the parent program is the [Sam Welton compliance program](../../../content/compliance-program.md) or the Terraplex Operator Network.
- CLEAN: white page background. Hero photograph is precision-equipment-in-use with soft shadow. Eyebrow strip in tinted brand-teal at ~15% saturation. Mid-page parent-program wordmark in brand-navy. Asymmetric rows use flat ~3–4 line copy with hairline-rule dividers. See [CLEAN archetype](../../../archetypes/clean.md).
- TERRAIN: cream-or-clay page. Hero photograph is landscape-anchored (drone over crops, equipment-on-field). Eyebrow strip in topographic-charcoal-on-cream. Optional contour-line motif on the eyebrow strip's lower edge. See [TERRAIN archetype](../../../archetypes/terrain.md).
- PATRIOT: cream page. Hero photograph is late-afternoon American-farmland with real customers. Eyebrow strip in deep-navy-on-cream with a single barn-red diamond bullet. Mid-page parent-program wordmark in deep-navy. The barn-red accent appears ONLY in the cross-link CTA — not in the body rows. See [PATRIOT archetype](../../../archetypes/patriot.md).
- FORGE: dark-dominant page (charcoal background). Hero photograph is rim-lit equipment-at-dusk. Eyebrow strip uses ember-amber-on-deep-charcoal. Mid-page parent-program wordmark in cream-on-charcoal. Asymmetric rows use cream copy on charcoal with photograph cells inset. See [FORGE archetype](../../../archetypes/forge.md).
- TACTICAL: dark page with tighter geometry. Hero photograph is studio-shot equipment in seamless lighting (no environmental context). Eyebrow strip in steel-cyan-on-near-black with tracked all-caps. Mid-page parent-program wordmark in tracked precision-sans + corner-bracket motif. Tier-mapping cards in monospace numbers. See [TACTICAL archetype](../../../archetypes/tactical.md).

## Source brief
Onefinestay's `/lp/all-accor-live-limitless-onefinestay-benefits` landing — the luxury-villa-rental brand (Onefinestay) explaining how guests earn / redeem credit in the parent Accor hospitality group's Accor Live Limitless (ALL) loyalty program. The library-worthy move is the structural cadence (dual-logo hero → tinted-strip plain-English explainer → mid-page parent-program wordmark anchor → asymmetric image-text benefit rows → parent-program tier-mapping cards → "wish to join [parent]?" cross-link → dealer-side reciprocal-discovery rows → partner logo wall footer) for explaining a dealer's role as a benefit-earning venue within a larger parent-network program.

Pick guide: partner-program-integration landing when a dealer ties into a real parent-network program (Terraplex Care, Terraplex Operator Network, Sam Welton compliance certification ladder, GTEEX parts/service network) and the page's job is to make the integration concrete rather than to sell a dealer-internal service. Sibling services notes for adjacent decisions:
- [protection-plan explainer](./refero-a52e1484-protection-plan-explainer.md) — when the dealer sells ONE specific paid plan rather than integrating with a parent program
- [three-tier inverted-middle](./refero-15fcbffc-three-tier-inverted-middle.md) — when the tiers are the dealer's own, not a parent program's
- [maintenance-program recommender](./refero-d928f889-maintenance-program-recommender.md) — when the dealer offers tiered service plans with an interactive picker
- [numbered-process shopper](./refero-d030e2b2-numbered-process-shopper.md) — when the page is enrollment-led for a dealer-side cohort (Part 107) rather than a parent-program handoff
- [specialist marketplace](./refero-d30b8a54-specialist-marketplace.md) — when the dealer is routing customers to vetted third-party specialists rather than into a parent loyalty program

Pairs naturally on the same dealer site with the [named-people credibility hero](../../hero/research/refero-00b3b85b-named-people-credibility-hero.md) (when the parent program is a people-anchored network like the Sam Welton compliance team) and the [trust-badge two-tile hero](../../hero/research/refero-a0978a05-trust-badge-two-tile-hero.md) (when the dealer's authorization-within-the-parent-network is the moat).
