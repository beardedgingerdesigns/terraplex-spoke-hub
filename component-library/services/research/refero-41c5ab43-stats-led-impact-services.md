## Why this is library-worthy
A services pattern where **multiple stat bands carry the credibility before any feature copy appears**. Hero with single bold headline + 1-line subhead + single CTA pill, then a dark 4-up stat band ("100% offset / Just ~1% of cost / VERRA certified / $0 commission"), then a light "Impact by numbers" 3-up large-stat band ("7,225,513 t / 45,568 t / 3,152 t"), THEN a "Your projects. Your impact." section with detailed photo + paragraph rows for each project, THEN the "How it works" 5-icon row, THEN logo wall + testimonials + CTA close. The opening is stats-stats-then-projects — numbers do the heavy lifting, prose comes after. Different from existing services patterns: the [kicker-band services](./refero-02ae136e-kicker-band-services.md) has ONE optional hero-overlay stats moment (3 stats); the [maintenance-program-recommender](./refero-d928f889-maintenance-program-recommender.md) has a 3-up icon row but no stats; the [photo-mosaic services](./refero-2fdf9ace-photo-mosaic-services.md) has a closing stats band only. This pattern stacks 7+ stat callouts above the program walkthrough — right pick when the dealer's actual differentiation is operational scale ("16,400 acres treated by our network this year / 49 dealer locations / 100% Part 137 certified / $0 setup fee") and the customer's hesitation is "do you actually do this at the scale you claim?"

## What to take
- Open with a tight contained hero (~30vh, dark or near-black backdrop): centered single-line bold headline ("Offset Your Business Travel Carbon Footprint" → for a dealer "Run Compliant Part 137 Operations" / "Certify Your Operator Bench" / "Maintain Fleet at Scale"), 1-line muted subhead below ("[Program] is the new way for sustainable business travel. Transparent. Affordable. Impactful."). Single small filled CTA pill in a single saturated archetype-aligned color ("Get [program]" → for a dealer "Enroll your fleet" / "Schedule certification audit"). The hero is restrained — it sets up the program name; the credibility comes immediately below.
- Hero illustration / artifact band (~30vh, full-bleed): a cinematic full-bleed dark photograph (close-up of leaves, cropfield, equipment surface) with a single floating UI card overlay anchored to the right ~30% of the band. The overlay card is a small program-summary widget — "Carbon footprint overview" with category icons and short labels (Hotels / Trains / Cars / Flights → for a dealer "Spray / Spread / Inspect / Log"). The overlay card is the page's first concrete artifact — proves the program has a real surface, not just a marketing promise.
- **First stats band** — 4-up dark callout band (~12–15vh, near-black background): 4 equal cells, each a large bold sentence-case STAT in white or near-white (~32–40px) + 2-line label below in muted gray. Stats: "100% / CO2 emissions offset on business travel" / "Just ~1% / of the total travel cost" / "VERRA / certifications that make an impact" / "$0 / Commission. Forever." → for a dealer "100% Part 137 / certified across our bench" / "16,400 / acres treated this year" / "49 / dealer locations in the network" / "$0 / setup fee. ever." Cells are flat (no card chrome) — the typography IS the affordance. The dark band reads as a credibility footer for the hero above.
- **Second stats band** — light "Impact by numbers" 3-up large-stat callout band (~18–22vh, cream or light-gray background): 3 equal cells, each a very large bold number (~56–64px, in deep brand-tinted color) + small label below in muted-charcoal. The numbers are larger than the dark band's stats — these are the operational scale numbers the dealer most wants in the customer's head ("7,225,513 t / Estimated annual reduction" / "45,568 t / Total tCO2e offset volume" / "3,152 t / Total tCO2e offset volume by [Program]" → for a dealer "16,400 / Acres treated in 2025" / "8,950 / Hours of Part 137 service" / "612 / Operators certified"). The light-band placement after the dark band sets up an alternating rhythm.
- "Your projects. Your impact." — detailed projects band. Centered heading + small caption above ("All projects are VERRA-certified and screened by the [Program] Sustainability Team" → for a dealer "All operators are Part 137-certified and audited by Sam Welton's compliance team"). Below the heading, 3 stacked horizontal project rows. Each row is a 2-column band: left ~30% is a single landscape photograph (~280px wide) of the project / operator / location; right ~70% has a bold sentence-case project name + small bold "Region: [name]" line + 2–4 line description + small "Carbon Offset Volume: X tCO2e" detail line + small "Read more →" text-link. Rows are flat — no card chrome, no shadow; the photograph + text composition IS the row.
- "How [Program] works" — 5-icon row directly below the projects. 3-up icon cells in row 1, 2-up cells centered in row 2. Each cell is a small monochrome line-art icon (~40px) + 1-line bold sentence-case label ("Travel for business with [Program]" / "Carbon footprint is calculated" / "Funds are collected in a pool" / "All funds invested in certified projects" / "Get 100% transparent reports"). Operational mechanic — what happens behind the scenes — kept tight so the projects above carry the emotional weight.
- "Join the hundreds of [customers] making an impact" logo wall — centered heading + 4-row × 6-column monochrome partner logo grid below. The wall is the third credibility dimension after the stats and the projects.
- Testimonial carousel band — single quote testimonial with avatar + name + role on a light-cream surface. One quote at a time, scroll-controlled. Quote is short (3–4 lines) and operationally specific ("Having the option to track the carbon footprint by travel method and automatically offset it across all operations and services is incredibly useful." → for a dealer-style quote: "Having Sam Welton's team audit our Part 137 records quarterly took the operational anxiety off our shoulders.").
- Closing CTA band: a saturated full-bleed band (often the brand's primary color — for the source it's lime green) with 2-column layout — left has bold heading + 1-line paragraph, right has a short email-input + "Request demo" pill. Single conversion moment, after the stats / projects / how-it-works / logos / testimonial all built the credibility.
- Optional bottom FAQ accordion — 8–10 questions covering operational details (cost, eligibility, opt-out, transparency, certification process). Trust-trail for customers ready to commit but checking the operational fine print.

## What NOT to take
- Don't use this pattern without real defensible stats. The pattern's whole credibility move depends on every number being independently verifiable — VERRA-certified offset volume is real third-party data; "16,400 acres treated" must be a number the dealer can defend with operational logs. Made-up or aspirational stats ("up to 100%", "potentially thousands of acres") collapse the trust register the entire pattern is built on. If the dealer's stats aren't real, use the [kicker-band services pattern](./refero-02ae136e-kicker-band-services.md) (which has the optional hero-overlay stats but doesn't depend on them) instead.
- Don't import TravelPerk's literal carbon-credit copy or VERRA branding — those are sustainability-vertical-specific. The transferable move is the structural cadence (hero → cinematic illustration with overlay artifact → 4-up dark stat band → 3-up light large-stat band → projects detail rows → 5-icon how-it-works → logo wall → testimonial → closing CTA). For a Terraplex dealer this maps to a [Part 137 compliance program landing](../../../content/compliance-program.md), an annual fleet-maintenance program, a certified-operator network landing, or an aerial-coverage program.
- Don't make the dark and light stat bands carry the same numbers. The dark band's job is "credibility constants" — certifications, percentages, dollar-thresholds, badge counts. The light band's job is "operational scale" — total acres, total hours, total operators, total projects. Mixing the two collapses the alternating-credibility-tier read.
- Don't put more than 4 cells in the dark stats band or more than 3 cells in the light stats band. The pattern's affordance depends on each cell being scannable — 5+ cells dilute the typographic weight and the credibility-by-number move dies. Pick the most defensible 4 + 3.
- Don't import the literal "Read more →" link without real per-project landing pages to route to. The text-link is the secondary depth path; if the dealer doesn't have real per-operator / per-region detail pages, drop the link and let the project rows stand on their own.
- Don't combine this with the [maintenance-program-recommender](./refero-d928f889-maintenance-program-recommender.md) on the same page. Both are service-program landings; this stats-led pattern is for the "we operate at scale, here's the proof" angle; the recommender pattern is for the "which tier should I pick" angle. Pick one — stats-led for credibility, recommender for routing.

## Adaptation notes (cross-archetype)
- FORGE: native fit. Push the hero photograph toward dusk / forge-glow lighting (rim-lit equipment edge). The 4-up dark stat band stays near-black with white numbers; the ember-orange accent appears on ONE stat per band (the most credibility-loaded one — typically a certification or a 100% claim). The 3-up light stat band uses cream background with charcoal numbers and a single ember-orange accent on one project's "Read more" link. Logo wall is white logos on dark inset. Closing CTA in ember-orange. See [FORGE archetype](../../../archetypes/forge.md). Pairs with the [hardware-lineup dark hero](../../hero/research/refero-0945a36c-hardware-lineup-dark-hero.md).
- TACTICAL: native fit with tighter geometry. Sharper rectangle CTA pills (~6px corner radius), precision-sans for stat numbers in tracked all-caps for labels. The 4-up dark stat band uses near-black with cyan accent on numbers; the 3-up light stat band uses pale-gray with charcoal numbers and a single steel-cyan accent on units. Add 1px corner brackets on the floating UI card overlay in the hero illustration band (consistent with the archetype's bracket motif). Photographs are studio-shot equipment with no environmental context. See [TACTICAL archetype](../../../archetypes/tactical.md). Pairs with the [topographic layered-bg hero](../../hero/research/refero-ae84b62a-topographic-layered-bg.md).
- TERRAIN: the alternation between the dark stats band and the light stats band IS the archetype's light/dark band rhythm at micro-scale. Add a thin topographic-line motif at the bottom edge of the dark stats band (the contour-divider that signals the rhythm). Stats accents in clay or sage. The projects detail rows alternate between cream-on-clay and clay-on-cream backgrounds. See [TERRAIN archetype](../../../archetypes/terrain.md).
- CLEAN: the dark stats band becomes brand-navy (~#1F2C44) instead of near-black; the light stats band stays cream / pale-gray. Stat numbers in brand-navy on the cream band, in cream on the navy band. Single brand-accent on logos and testimonial attribution. Photographs are clean equipment-and-operator shots with soft drop shadows. See [CLEAN archetype](../../../archetypes/clean.md). Pairs with the [centered-screenshot-trio hero](../../hero/research/refero-f619e700-centered-screenshot-trio.md).
- PATRIOT: the dark stats band becomes deep navy (~#1F2C44), the light band stays cream. Stat numbers in cream on navy and in deep-navy on cream. ONE stat per band carries a barn-red accent — typically the "100% certified" or "Built in the Americas" credibility claim (the only saturated chrome in the band; flag-color-at-micro-scale rule). Project rows are golden-hour American-farmland photographs with deep-navy headings. Logo wall in deep-navy logos on cream inset. Closing CTA is a deep-navy pill with cream label. Don't add stripe rules between bands — the alternating dark/light cadence IS the patriotic structural move. See [PATRIOT archetype](../../../archetypes/patriot.md). Pairs with the [manifesto-creed hero](../../hero/research/refero-b86571f2-manifesto-creed-hero.md).
- FIELD: the dark stats band becomes warm-charcoal (~#3A2B1F) instead of pure near-black, so the dark band reads as warm-dark rather than corporate-dark. Stat numbers in cream / warm-off-white on the warm-charcoal band, in deep-charcoal on the cream band. Single warm-amber accent on the most credibility-loaded stat. Project rows are golden-hour operator-and-equipment photographs with warm-charcoal headings. Logo wall in warm-charcoal on cream. Closing CTA is an amber pill. The stats band still does the credibility work; only the palette warms. See [FIELD archetype](../../../archetypes/field.md). Pairs with the [manifesto-creed hero](../../hero/research/refero-b86571f2-manifesto-creed-hero.md).
