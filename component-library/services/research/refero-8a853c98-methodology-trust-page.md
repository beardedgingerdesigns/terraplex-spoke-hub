## Why this is library-worthy

A **standards / methodology** services page is a distinct trust register from every existing services note. It doesn't sell a tier, walk a process, project an ROI, or show a case study — it discloses **the dealer's own internal rigor** in long-form prose, organized as named H2 sections that an external reader (regulator, large-account buyer, journalist, skeptical operator) can audit. Hers's `/editorial-standards` shows the canonical structure: narrow reading column, eight-to-ten named sections, hyperlinked references to internal policies, and a single CTA banner closer.

## What to take

- **Centered-title header band with the page name in display type** ("Editorial Standards" centered on a saturated band) — establishes the page as a formal standards disclosure rather than marketing copy.
- **Constrained reading column (~600–700px) on a wide page background.** This is a prose page, not a product page; readability outranks visual density.
- **Eight-to-ten named H2 sections in a fixed order** that tell the story end-to-end:
  1. *Our Objective* (what the page is for)
  2. *Process / Integrity* (who does the work, how)
  3. *Team / Roles* (which roles do what, with what credentials)
  4. *Expert Review / Fact-Check* (independent verification layer)
  5. *Standards & Compliance* (the explicit rules followed)
  6. *Sources, Citations, References* (provenance of claims)
  7. *Currency / Updates* (when content is reviewed; how stale claims get retired)
  8. *Corrections* (the explicit "we make mistakes — here's the recourse" clause)
  9. *Transparency / Conflict-of-Interest* (commercial relationships disclosed)
  10. *Contact Us* (the named address for standards questions, not general sales)
- **Hyperlinked internal references inline in prose**, not as a separate sidebar — links to the dealer's privacy policy, training program, manufacturer certifications, regulatory affiliations. Trust by interconnection.
- **A single CTA banner between the prose and footer**, full-bleed photograph + light text overlay + one rounded-pill button. Visitor either acted on the trust just built or didn't — no upsell stack between sections.
- **Tiny disclaimer block at the end of prose** before the photo banner: "This article is for informational purposes only and does not constitute medical/legal/financial advice." The standards page is the right place to state these once, prominently, so other pages stay clean.
- **Footer carries trust badges and certifications** rather than scattering them across product pages — the standards page becomes the page where regulators and partners go to verify.

## What NOT to take

- **Don't pad with stock photography.** This is the one services page where text density is the credibility move. The closing photo-banner is the only image. Inline section illustrations dilute the auditable-document feel.
- **Don't link to a "free trial" or "demo" CTA mid-page.** The single closing CTA is intentional — interrupting with conversion asks reads as marketing-disguised-as-trust.
- **Don't structure sections as accordion / collapsible.** Auditors need to read top-to-bottom; collapsing the prose suggests something to hide. Use full anchor links instead so deep-links to a specific standard still work.
- **Don't write evergreen-sounding copy.** Date-stamp the page ("Last reviewed: March 2026") near the title — staleness on a standards page is itself a credibility failure.

## Adaptation notes (cross-archetype)

- **FIELD / PATRIOT**: cream background, warm serif H1 ("Our Spraying Standards"), single closing photo of a real Part 137 operator in the field; prose reads as personal craft commitment rather than corporate policy.
- **CLEAN / TERRAIN**: the Hers cream-and-blue-band register lands natively. Light neutral background, clean sans-serif H2s, muted brand-accent header band.
- **FORGE / TACTICAL**: dark mode (charcoal page background, white prose), all-caps H2s, monochrome closing banner with a single tactical/aerial photo and a high-contrast CTA. Reads as an internal-document brief made public.
- **All archetypes**: the eight-to-ten section spine stays constant — only typography, color, and the closing-banner imagery shift.

For Terraplex dealers, the natural titles are: *Our Part 137 Standards*, *Our Sam Welton Compliance Program Standards*, *Our Training & Certification Standards*, *How We Recommend Drones* (purchase methodology).
