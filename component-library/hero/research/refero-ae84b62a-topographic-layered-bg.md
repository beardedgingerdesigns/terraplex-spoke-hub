## Why this is library-worthy
A topographic-contour-line hero backdrop built from stacked, slightly-offset organic shapes in near-monochrome dark tones, with a faint dotted-halftone texture on some layers. Reads as "land, mapped" without using a literal map or photograph — a strong fit for any archetype that wants gravitas plus an outdoor / terrain reference.

## What to take
- Build the backdrop from 4–6 large organic curve-bounded shapes, each slightly inset from the last, fills stepping from charcoal (#1A1F1F) to near-black (#0E1213) — the contrast between adjacent layers stays low (~5–8% lightness step) so the contours read as embossed rather than striped.
- Add a fine dotted-halftone texture (1–2px dots, ~12% opacity) to one or two layers only — not all of them. The unevenness is what sells the topographic / printed-map feel.
- Keep the shapes' edges curved-organic, not geometric. No straight lines, no sharp corners, no perfect circles.
- Layer the foreground content (headline, subhead, CTA) on a single flat region of the backdrop — pick the largest negative-space valley between contour layers as the text bed so contrast stays high.
- The backdrop is static; no parallax, no autoplay loop. Motion would override the calm-precision read.

## What NOT to take
- The source uses this as a downloadable video asset with a 7s loop — DO NOT import the loop. A static export of the topographic still is the library-worthy artifact; the autoplay would violate FIELD's no-motion rule and add weight without information.
- Don't borrow the bright-purple "Download free" button — that's the source page's CTA, not part of the backdrop pattern.
- Don't tile or repeat the contours — each shape should be unique. Tiling kills the hand-drawn map feel.

## Adaptation notes (cross-archetype)
- TERRAIN: native fit. Use the alternating-light/dark archetype palette by inverting the layers in the next section — dark topographic hero, then a cream-on-paper section, then dark again.
- FORGE: keep the dark layers, push the contour-edge contrast slightly higher, and use a hot accent (ember orange) ONLY for the headline keyword or the CTA — the topographic backdrop stays monochrome.
- TACTICAL: tighten the contour curves into more deliberate, almost-isobaric lines (less freehand, more chartlike) and pair with a precision sans-serif headline. The dotted-halftone reads as map-grade documentation.
- FIELD / CLEAN: skip — the dark-dominant treatment conflicts with both archetypes' light palettes. If a light archetype wants topographic referencing, see the alternating-pastel-stripes note in product-block instead.
