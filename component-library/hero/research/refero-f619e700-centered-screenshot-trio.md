## Why this is library-worthy
A clean, centered light-hero pattern that pairs a single bold headline with a horizontal trio of product screenshots and a four-up benefit row directly beneath — establishes product credibility immediately without a hero photograph or video.

## What to take
- Centered single-column hero stack: headline → short subhead → one rounded pill CTA, all on a near-white background with generous vertical padding (~120px top).
- Below the CTA, a single-row trio of three product/UI screenshots with soft drop-shadows and rounded corners (~12px), spaced edge-to-edge with a moderate gutter. Each screenshot shows a different surface (map, mobile, desktop) so the trio reads as "the product, end-to-end."
- Beneath the trio, a four-column row of small numeric-prefixed benefit labels (e.g. "01 Fast registration") in a single accent color — replaces a logo strip with proof of value.
- Typography: bold sans-serif headline, neutral grey body, accent-color benefit labels. No reliance on display serifs.
- Negative space is the visual treatment — no gradients, blobs, or motion are needed.

## What NOT to take
- The pastel feature panels that follow this hero (light blue / lavender / yellow / pale green) are part of the page's product-block pattern, not the hero — don't import them as hero treatment.
- The "screenshot trio" depends on having three distinct product surfaces to show; if the dealer only has one product photo, this pattern collapses into a generic image-and-text hero.
- The cheerful pastel color story would violate FORGE/TACTICAL's dark-dominant requirement — this is a light-hero pattern only.

## Adaptation notes (cross-archetype)
- FIELD: keep the structure as-is, swap the green accent for warm cream/amber, and use earth-tone-tinted screenshots (drone overhead photo, app mockup, satellite map) so the trio reads as agricultural product context.
- CLEAN: native fit — keep neutral grey + a single brand accent for the benefit labels.
- TERRAIN: works if the alternating light/dark rhythm starts in the section AFTER the hero; the hero itself should stay light to set the "light band" baseline.

## Source brief
OneSoil — a precision-agriculture SaaS landing page. The dealer brief was platform credibility for farmers and agronomists; the centered light hero plus screenshot trio handles "show me the product" without a stock farm photo.
