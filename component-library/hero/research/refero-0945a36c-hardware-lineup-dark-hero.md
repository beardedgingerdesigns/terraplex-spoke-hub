## Why this is library-worthy
A dark hero band where the headline + subhead sit lower-left and the right two-thirds is a single cinematic studio-sweep photograph laying out the entire product family at small scale — drones, controllers, gimbals, accessories — on a dark seamless backdrop. The lineup IS the credibility move: one image proves "this is a real range," so the rest of the page can drop into a flat product card grid without working hard. Different from the [topographic layered-bg](./refero-ae84b62a-topographic-layered-bg.md) pattern (which is an abstract dark backdrop), and different from the [manifesto-creed hero](./refero-b86571f2-manifesto-creed-hero.md) (which leads with ideology, not hardware).

## What to take
- The hero is a contained dark band (~50–60vh, not full-viewport), not a hero "scene." Background is a near-black-to-charcoal vertical gradient (~#0E0E0E top → ~#1F1F1F bottom) reading as a studio sweep / cyclorama.
- One large studio photograph occupies the right ~65% of the band: the product family (4–8 hardware items at varied scales — large item closest to camera, smaller accessories receding) arranged in a loose triangular composition with directional rim lighting from one side. Items cast soft contact shadows on the sweep; no environmental context, no sky, no field — just hardware on dark.
- Headline sits left-aligned in the lower-left ~35% of the band: short proper-noun category name (~48–56px white, heavy sans-serif, single word — "Professional", "Enterprise", "Agriculture"). Subhead is one line of muted gray (~#9E9E9E, 14–16px) directly below: "Drone Solutions for [audience]." No CTA in the band — the hero introduces, the cards below convert.
- Below the dark band, the page drops to a light gray surface (~#F4F4F4) with a left-rail vertical category nav (~25% width) + a right-side product-card area (~75%). Each card is white with one centered product render, name, 2-line capability descriptor, and a stacked "Buy Now" (filled black pill) + "Learn More" (text link with chevron) pair. Cards are uniform; the dark hero is the only chrome-heavy moment.
- The dark-hero / light-product-card contrast is intentional: the band carries weight; the cards carry detail. Don't blend the two surfaces with a gradient transition — the hard edge is the pattern.

## What NOT to take
- Don't import the literal cinema-rig / Inspire-3 hardware photograph — that's DJI's lineup. The transferable move is "all the things we sell, photographed together on a dark sweep." For a Terraplex dealer this is an [R-32](../../../products/r-32.md) + [I-19](../../../products/i-19.md) pair (or whichever models the dealer carries) plus accessories — controller, swappable batteries, spreader add-on — arranged on a dark studio backdrop with rim lighting.
- Don't add a CTA to the hero band. The lineup photograph + category headline does the introducing; the product cards below do the conversion. A CTA in the band competes with the lineup composition.
- Don't recreate this hero with a SINGLE product render centered. The pattern's affordance is "a range," not "a hero product." If the dealer carries one drone, this isn't the right pattern — see [centered-screenshot-trio](./refero-f619e700-centered-screenshot-trio.md) for a single-product-forward light hero, or pair this with the [dark feature bands product-block](../../product-block/research/refero-eb12db31-dark-feature-bands.md) for a single-product narrative.
- Don't tile or composite the lineup from individual cutouts — it must be one studio photograph with consistent lighting and shared shadows. Composited lineups read as catalog imagery, not a hero band.
- The dark sweep + hardware-only composition conflicts directly with FIELD's warm-photographic, no-dark-dominant rule and CLEAN's light/airy default. PATRIOT's cream-default and warm-American-farmland imagery would fight the seamless dark backdrop — for those archetypes use the [manifesto-creed hero](./refero-b86571f2-manifesto-creed-hero.md) (warm-lit human-in-action) or the [centered-screenshot-trio](./refero-f619e700-centered-screenshot-trio.md) (light hero with product trio).

## Adaptation notes (cross-archetype)
- FORGE: native fit. Push the rim lighting toward ember-warm (golden hour / forge glow on the closest hardware item only). Single ember-orange accent shows up only on the "Buy Now" pill below; everywhere else stays monochrome white-on-charcoal. Pairs naturally with the [dark feature bands product-block](../../product-block/research/refero-eb12db31-dark-feature-bands.md) for the body of the page. See [FORGE archetype](../../../archetypes/forge.md).
- TACTICAL: native fit with cooler lighting. Use neutral-white rim light (no warmth) and tighter geometry — sharper card corner radius (~6px instead of pill), monochrome black/white only with a single steel-cyan accent on the "Buy Now" button. Hardware photographed on seamless black studio paper, not a charcoal gradient — the absence of warmth IS the affordance. See [TACTICAL archetype](../../../archetypes/tactical.md).
- TERRAIN: works as the opening "dark band" of the alternating light/dark rhythm. Follow the dark hero with a cream-or-clay light band carrying a topographic divider, then return to dark for the product cards. The category nav rail can carry a thin topographic-line motif at micro-scale in the rail dividers. See [TERRAIN archetype](../../../archetypes/terrain.md).
- FIELD / CLEAN / PATRIOT: skip — the dark studio-sweep is dark-dominant by definition. See sibling hero notes for light-archetype options.
