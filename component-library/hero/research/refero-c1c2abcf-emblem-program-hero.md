## Why this is library-worthy
A hero pattern that introduces a dealer **program** (compliance, certification, service plan, dealer-network membership) instead of a product — dark band, big program-name wordmark on the left, and a single hex-shaped emblem/shield on the right with faint radial halo lines. Two ghost-style outline CTAs sit under the wordmark; no product photograph, no studio sweep, no hardware. The emblem IS the credibility move — the page is about the program's existence, not what's inside the box. Closes the structural gap that none of the existing dark-archetype hero notes fill: [hardware-lineup dark hero](./refero-0945a36c-hardware-lineup-dark-hero.md) shows a product family, [topographic layered-bg](./refero-ae84b62a-topographic-layered-bg.md) is an abstract dark backdrop, [form-in-hero overlay](./refero-7d15989d-form-in-hero-overlay.md) collapses lead capture into the hero — none of them work as a "we issue this thing as a credentialed program" hero.

## What to take
- Contained dark hero band (~50–55vh, NOT full viewport). Background is a single near-black surface (~#0E0E0E to #1C1C1C) — flat, no gradient, no texture.
- Left ~50% of the band: program wordmark in heavy uppercase sans-serif (~48–60px, white). The wordmark is the program name itself ("DJI CARE PRO" → for a dealer "TERRAPLEX COMPLIANCE PROGRAM" / "PART 137 SERVICE TIER" / "DEALER PROMISE"), not a marketing tagline. Directly below the wordmark, a single short subhead (~14–18px, muted gray ~#9E9E9E) explaining the affordance in 4–8 words ("Create with Greater Peace of Mind" → for a dealer "Stay current. Fly compliant. Spray confident.").
- Below the wordmark + subhead, a paired ghost-CTA row: two outline-only pill buttons (~36–40px tall, 1px white border, transparent fill, white label, ~24px corner radius). Common pairings: "Activate [Program]" + "Check Your [Program]" / "Enroll Now" + "View Program Terms" / "Join the Program" + "Request a Walk-through." Both buttons are equal weight; the ghost treatment signals "this is informational, not a marketplace."
- Right ~40% of the band: a single hex-shaped emblem/shield centered vertically. The shield carries the program's brand-mark or icon at center (a stylized drone silhouette, a checkmark + leaf for compliance, an interlocking-rings logotype for partnership). Faint radial halo lines (~1px, ~15% opacity white) emanate outward in 4–8 directions, suggesting the emblem is broadcasting / radiating — the only ornament in the band. The emblem is the page's only saturated visual moment; everything else is monochrome.
- No CTA inside the emblem. No price below the wordmark. No social-proof badges in the band. The hero's job is to NAME the program; everything substantive lives below.
- Below the hero band, the page typically opens with an "Exclusive Service for [audience]" centered headline + 3-up icon row (Free Repairs / Factory Maintenance / Service Extension Rewards → for a dealer Free Inspections / Annual Calibration / Priority Service Slots) — a tight value-prop summary that the hero's restraint earns.

## What NOT to take
- Don't import the literal DJI Care Pro hex emblem or "Activate Your DJI Care Pro" copy — those are brand-specific. The transferable move is "centered shield/emblem with faint radial halo, paired with program-named wordmark on dark." For a Terraplex dealer the emblem is the dealer's compliance crest, the [Sam Welton compliance program](../../../content/compliance-program.md) seal, or a dealer-network membership badge — designed once for the dealer's identity, not borrowed.
- Don't substitute a product render for the emblem. The pattern's affordance depends on the emblem's abstract / heraldic register — "this is the seal of a thing." A product render in the same slot collapses the hero into a generic single-product introduction (which the existing [hardware-lineup dark hero](./refero-0945a36c-hardware-lineup-dark-hero.md) already covers structurally).
- Don't add a third CTA. The two ghost CTAs are the pattern — one to enroll/activate, one to verify/check. A third ("Learn More") fragments the affordance into a marketing-link list, and the hero's restraint dies.
- Don't fill either CTA with a saturated archetype accent. Both must stay outline-ghost; the saturation moves to the body of the page. A filled "Buy Now" or "Enroll Now" pill in the band collapses the program-introduction register into a transactional product-launch.
- Don't add a star-rating, customer-count, or trust-badge row inside the band. Those belong in the body — the [split-screen trust curtain contact pattern](../../contact/research/refero-7c41e8a8-split-screen-trust-curtain.md) is where logo walls / ratings live, not in this hero. Mixing them collapses the program-emblem affordance into a generic dark SaaS landing.
- Don't import this pattern when the dealer is launching a single product (drone, accessory, software). The hero introduces a PROGRAM — something the dealer issues credentials for, not something they sell once. If the brief is "launch new SKU," use [hardware-lineup dark hero](./refero-0945a36c-hardware-lineup-dark-hero.md) (multi-product family) or [centered-screenshot-trio](./refero-f619e700-centered-screenshot-trio.md) (light single-product).
- The dark-dominant + heraldic-emblem register conflicts directly with [FIELD's](../../../archetypes/field.md) cream-default + warm-photographic rule and [PATRIOT's](../../../archetypes/patriot.md) cream-base + flag-as-restraint rule. For warm archetypes use the [manifesto-creed hero](./refero-b86571f2-manifesto-creed-hero.md) (declarative ideology + creed photograph) or [hands-on craft hero](./refero-be842c1e-hands-on-craft-hero.md) (warm operator-mid-use) instead — those carry credibility through human warmth rather than heraldic restraint.

## Adaptation notes (cross-archetype)
- FORGE: native fit. Near-black band (~#0E0E0E), white wordmark, muted-gray subhead. Emblem is rendered with a faint ember-orange rim along the inside of the hex border — the only warm color in the band. Radial halo lines pick up an ember tint at ~15% opacity. Ghost CTAs stay white-outline; if the dealer wants ember in the hero, swap the rim to ember-orange (no fill change). Pairs with the [dark feature bands product-block](../../product-block/research/refero-eb12db31-dark-feature-bands.md) below for the program's value-prop story. See [FORGE archetype](../../../archetypes/forge.md).
- TACTICAL: native fit with tighter geometry. Sharper hex edges (no soft chamfer), precision-sans wordmark in tracked tracking, 1px corner-bracket details on the emblem's hex corners (consistent with the archetype's bracket motif). Single steel-cyan accent on the emblem rim only; CTAs stay white-outline ghost. Halo lines become precision dashed-segments rather than soft fades — reads as engineering-grade documentation rather than ornament. Pairs with the [spec-comparison-grid product-block](../../product-block/research/refero-713b66c0-spec-comparison-grid.md) for a TACTICAL precision program landing. See [TACTICAL archetype](../../../archetypes/tactical.md).
- CLEAN: lighten the contrast — band becomes deep brand-navy (~#1F2C44) instead of near-black. Cream/off-white wordmark, muted-cream subhead. Emblem is a clean monochrome shield (no ember rim, no radial dashes) with a single brand-navy fill behind a white center icon. Ghost CTAs in cream-outline. The pattern works in CLEAN when the dealer wants to introduce a corporate program (compliance, fleet management) without the heraldic / forge-glow register. Pairs with the [centered-screenshot-trio hero](./refero-f619e700-centered-screenshot-trio.md) on adjacent product pages and the [audience-tabbed services pattern](../../services/research/refero-fef890ec-audience-tabbed-services.md) below. See [CLEAN archetype](../../../archetypes/clean.md).
- TERRAIN: the dark band reads as the page's opening "dark band" anchor. Add a thin topographic-line motif as the bottom edge of the hero (a single contour curve replacing the hard band/body break). Emblem's halo lines are recolored to clay or sage at ~15% opacity. Below the hero, drop to a cream "light band" with the program's value-prop summary. See [TERRAIN archetype](../../../archetypes/terrain.md). Sibling: [topographic layered-bg](./refero-ae84b62a-topographic-layered-bg.md) for an abstract topographic dark hero on adjacent pages where the emblem isn't relevant.
- FIELD / PATRIOT: skip. The heraldic-emblem-on-dark register is fundamentally incompatible with FIELD's warm-photographic / no-dark-dominant rule and PATRIOT's cream-base / flag-as-restraint rule. The closest warm-archetype substitute when the dealer wants to introduce a program is the [manifesto-creed hero](./refero-b86571f2-manifesto-creed-hero.md) (declarative all-caps headline + warm-lit creed photograph + real-person attribution) — it carries the same "we stand for something" register through human warmth rather than heraldic restraint.

## Source brief
DJI's [/service/djicare-pro](https://www.dji.com/service/djicare-pro) page — the consumer-tier protection / maintenance program landing for the same product domain (drones) Terraplex dealers operate in. Most directly transferable to dealer-issued program landings: a [Sam Welton compliance program](../../../content/compliance-program.md) page, an annual fleet-service tier landing, a [Part 107 prep course](../../../content/compliance-program.md) signup, or a "Dealer Promise" credential page. The library-worthy move is the structural cadence (dark contained band → program-name wordmark + subhead + ghost CTAs on the left → centered hex emblem with radial halo on the right → no product render, no form, no trust badges in the band), reusable for any [FORGE](../../../archetypes/forge.md) / [TACTICAL](../../../archetypes/tactical.md) / [CLEAN](../../../archetypes/clean.md) / [TERRAIN](../../../archetypes/terrain.md) dealer who needs to introduce a credentialed program rather than launch a product.
