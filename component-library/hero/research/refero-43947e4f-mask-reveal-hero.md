## Why this is library-worthy
A hero pattern where the right ~50% of the band is dominated by an oversized geometric symbol (here a stylized "X") cut OUT of a black surface, and the cutout reveals a multi-cell collage of campaign / lifestyle / product photographs *inside* the symbol's silhouette. The text + paired CTAs sit in the left ~50%; the mask-reveal carries the visual weight without showing the photographs as separate gallery items. The structural move is "use a meaningful shape as a window into multiple proof images," which lets a single hero band carry breadth (multiple photographs) without losing focal weight (one anchor symbol). Different from existing dark heroes: the [topographic layered-bg](./refero-ae84b62a-topographic-layered-bg.md) is an abstract backdrop; the [hardware-lineup dark hero](./refero-0945a36c-hardware-lineup-dark-hero.md) shows real product hardware on a sweep; the [3D-object dark hero](./refero-ea86d249-3d-object-dark-hero.md) shows a single constructed render. None of them carries the "multi-image proof through a single symbol" affordance.

## What to take
- Two-column hero band (~60–70vh, contained — does not fill viewport). Left ~50% holds heading + subhead + paired CTAs on a flat dark backdrop. Right ~50% holds the mask-reveal: a black surface with a single oversized geometric cutout (~45vh tall, fills most of the right column's vertical space) revealing a multi-cell photo collage *inside* the cutout shape.
- The cutout shape itself is meaningful — for the source it's the company's letterform "X," for a Terraplex dealer it could be the dealer's territory state outline (e.g., a stylized Iowa silhouette), a stylized drone silhouette, a wheat/corn stalk graphic, or a contour-circle (TERRAIN). The shape's edges are sharp and geometric — the affordance dies if the cutout edges are organic, blurred, or feathered.
- Inside the cutout, the photographs tile in irregular cells — 3–5 photographs visible at once, sized to fit different parts of the cutout silhouette (the "X"'s arms hold tall portraits; the cutout's tighter spaces hold smaller landscape crops). Photographs are real and unrelated to each other — different operators, different fields, different equipment moments — so the collage reads as breadth, not as a single sequenced sequence.
- Left column content stack (top to bottom, ~64px internal padding):
  1. Heading (~36–48px bold, all-caps, white sans-serif, 2–3 lines max). Short and declarative ("GROW YOUR BUSINESS ON X" → for a dealer "OPERATE AT THE SCALE OF YOUR LAND" / "[DEALER NAME] SERVES [REGION]").
  2. Subhead (~16–18px, lighter weight, single line, muted gray ~#9E9E9E). One sentence, plain copy ("Drive sales & ROI" → for a dealer "Compliance, fleet management, and field service in one place").
  3. Paired CTAs stacked vertically (not side-by-side): primary filled white-on-dark pill (~140px wide) + secondary outlined pill below it (same width, transparent fill, 1.5px white border). Stacking the CTAs (rather than placing side-by-side) keeps the column visually anchored to the left of the cutout.
- The hard vertical edge between the dark text column and the dark cutout column reads as a single continuous black surface — there's no gradient, no rule, no neutral spacer. The cutout is visible only because the photographs *inside* it are bright; the surrounding black flows uninterrupted.
- Mobile collapse: text column stacks above the cutout (cutout becomes full-width, ~40vh tall, with the same multi-cell photo collage inside). Heading drops to ~28–32px. Paired CTAs become full-width pills.

## What NOT to take
- Don't import the literal "X" letterform — it's brand-symbol dependent. The transferable move is "oversized geometric cutout shape revealing multi-cell photo collage," not the specific shape. For a Terraplex dealer pick a shape that's defensible: state outline (territorial credibility), stylized drone silhouette (product credibility), contour-line circle (TERRAIN heritage), star + corner-bracket composition (PATRIOT, restrained).
- Don't make the cutout shape too organic. Soft edges and rounded organic outlines collapse the affordance into a generic "shaped image" — the pattern depends on the shape being recognizably geometric, with hard sharp edges, so the eye reads it as a deliberate cutout rather than a decorative crop.
- Don't import the source's specific photo subjects (people working, sports, performers). The transferable move is "real proof images filling the cutout" — for a Terraplex dealer this is operators in field, drones in flight, controller close-ups, equipment at golden hour. Pick 3–5 photos that show breadth (different settings, different operators, different equipment moments) — uniform photo subjects (e.g., 5 drone-in-sky shots) collapse the breadth affordance.
- Don't use this pattern when the dealer doesn't have 3+ defensible photographs of their own operations. The cutout depends on real images filling it; with 1 generic photograph, the cutout collapses into a "shaped image hero" with no breadth. For single-image dark heroes use [3D-object dark hero](./refero-ea86d249-3d-object-dark-hero.md) or [hardware-lineup dark hero](./refero-0945a36c-hardware-lineup-dark-hero.md).
- Don't add color saturation to the cutout shape itself (e.g., a colored border around the X, an accent fill on the cutout edge). The affordance is "a window cut out of black" — adding color to the shape collapses the cutout into a logo treatment with photos behind it, which is a different (and weaker) pattern.
- Don't combine this pattern with the [hardware-lineup dark hero](./refero-0945a36c-hardware-lineup-dark-hero.md) on the same page. Both occupy the dark hero slot and both carry photographs — stacking them turns the page into a "darkness with lots of photos" register that loses focal weight.
- The dark-dominant + monochrome-with-multi-cell-photos register conflicts with [FIELD's](../../../archetypes/field.md) warm-photographic, no-dark-dominant rule. PATRIOT can use the pattern with adaptations (deep-navy substrate instead of pure black; cutout shape leans state-outline rather than abstract symbol — see Adaptation notes).

## Adaptation notes (cross-archetype)
- TACTICAL: native fit. Cutout shape is a tactical-coded symbol (corner-bracket frame, stylized rotor cross-section, mission-grid square). Photographs inside the cutout are studio-shot equipment in controlled lighting (no environmental context). Heading in tracked all-caps precision-sans. Paired CTAs use sharper rectangle corners (~6px corner radius). Add a 1px corner-bracket detail to the photographs' visible corners inside the cutout — consistent with the archetype's bracket motif. See [TACTICAL archetype](../../../archetypes/tactical.md). Pairs with the [spec-comparison-grid product-block](../../product-block/research/refero-713b66c0-spec-comparison-grid.md) below the hero.
- FORGE: cutout shape is a stylized rotor disc, a forge-glow ring, or an abstract operator silhouette (helmet + controller). Photographs inside the cutout are dusk / golden-hour / forge-glow shots — drones at sunrise, equipment lit from one side, operators in late-afternoon shadow. Single ember-orange accent on the primary CTA pill (the only saturated moment on the page). The cutout's interior edge can carry a faint ember rim-glow (~12% saturation) that sells the "forged" register. See [FORGE archetype](../../../archetypes/forge.md). Pairs with the [dark feature bands product-block](../../product-block/research/refero-eb12db31-dark-feature-bands.md).
- TERRAIN: cutout shape is a stylized contour-line circle, a topographic basin silhouette, or a state outline. Photographs inside lean field-and-land — aerial farmland, hillside cuts, equipment crossing terrain. The substrate is near-black (~#0E0E0E) but the cutout's interior gets a subtle topographic motif at micro-scale on the cutout's inner edge (a 1px contour-line trace). After the hero band, the page drops to a cream "light band" with a topographic divider. See [TERRAIN archetype](../../../archetypes/terrain.md). Sibling: [topographic layered-bg](./refero-ae84b62a-topographic-layered-bg.md) when no defensible cutout shape exists.
- CLEAN: invert to a light variant — cutout is on a near-white substrate (~#F8F8F6) revealing color photographs inside. The cutout shape stays geometric and brand-aligned (corporate symbol or product silhouette). Heading in brand-charcoal sentence-case. Paired CTAs in brand-navy (filled) + outline-charcoal (secondary). The pattern works in CLEAN because the structural cadence (cutout + multi-cell photos + paired CTAs) is archetype-neutral once the dark backdrop is replaced. See [CLEAN archetype](../../../archetypes/clean.md).
- PATRIOT: cutout shape is the dealer's state outline or a stylized "USA" silhouette — a literal patriotic shape that earns its weight by being functional (territorial proof) rather than decorative. Substrate is deep navy (~#1F2C44), not pure black. Photographs inside are late-afternoon American-farmland shots — operators, equipment, fields in golden-hour light. Heading in cream all-caps. Paired CTAs: primary cream-on-navy pill, secondary cream-outline pill. Single barn-red accent on a small "Now serving [region]" eyebrow above the heading (~10px tracked all-caps). Do NOT add stripe rules or star motifs around the cutout — the state-outline shape IS the patriotic register. See [PATRIOT archetype](../../../archetypes/patriot.md). Pairs with the [manifesto-creed hero](./refero-b86571f2-manifesto-creed-hero.md) on a sibling page.
- FIELD: skip — the dark-dominant register and monochrome-multi-cell-photo affordance conflict with FIELD's warm-photographic, cream-default rule. Forcing the cutout onto a cream substrate with warm-toned photos collapses the contrast that makes the pattern legible. Use the [hands-on craft hero](./refero-be842c1e-hands-on-craft-hero.md) (single adult mid-use, warm-interior) for FIELD instead.

## Source brief
X Business's [/business](https://business.x.com/) (formerly Twitter Business) homepage — the advertising / business-services landing for X. The library-worthy move is the structural cadence (two-column dark hero → left ~50% dense text column with heading + subhead + stacked paired CTAs → right ~50% oversized geometric cutout revealing multi-cell photo collage), reusable for a Terraplex dealer who has 3+ defensible photographs of their own operations and wants the hero to carry breadth (multi-region, multi-operator, multi-equipment) without losing focal anchor. The pattern's brand-symbol origin is incidental — the transferable move is "use a meaningful geometric shape as a window into multiple proof images." For a dealer this is a state outline, a stylized drone silhouette, or a contour-circle.
