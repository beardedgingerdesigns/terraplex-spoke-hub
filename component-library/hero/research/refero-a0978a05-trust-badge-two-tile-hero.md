## Why this is library-worthy
A two-tile hero where the headline anchor is a *certification badge pill* — not the product itself. The hero leads with "we are the trusted/authorized one" before it shows what's for sale, which is the right frame for a dealer whose primary sell is "Terraplex authorized distributor / Part 137 certified" rather than the spec sheet.

## What to take
- Two side-by-side full-height tiles, ~45/55 split: photo tile left (worker mid-task, hands in the work, real environment), saturated brand-color tile right (no photo, no gradient — flat brand color holds the headline).
- Inside the saturated tile: a pill-shaped badge with a small icon + brand name ("Yelp Guaranteed" → "Terraplex Authorized" / "Part 137 Certified") sits ABOVE the headline and acts as the visual anchor — the headline is secondary to the badge.
- Headline below the badge is a confidence statement ("Hire pros with confidence" / "Spray with confidence"), short, 2-line max, white-on-saturated.
- One pill CTA below the headline, white-fill on saturated tile (color-inversion of the typical brand-fill CTA so it pops against the saturated backdrop).
- No hero subhead body copy — the pill + headline + CTA is the entire right-tile content. The photo tile carries the contextual proof; the saturated tile carries the claim.
- Below the hero, a "We've got you covered" support-language band with 3 icon-prefixed coverage tiles (what the certification means in concrete terms) — the badge gets unpacked, not left as a vague claim.

## What NOT to take
- Don't substitute a generic stock photo for the worker tile — the proof-of-context is the whole job of that tile. If the photo isn't a real adult mid-task with hands engaged, the badge reads as marketing varnish.
- Don't add a body subhead inside the saturated tile — the right tile's restraint (badge + headline + one CTA) is what makes the badge feel central rather than ornamental.
- Don't use this if the dealer doesn't have a real, defensible certification or authorization to anchor — no "Quality Guaranteed!" placeholder badges. The pattern collapses into kitsch.
- The pure-saturated-color right tile would feel jarring on FIELD without an amber/cream tone-shift (see adaptation notes).

## Adaptation notes (cross-archetype)
- FORGE: right tile is near-black with an ember accent rule under the badge; pill is white-fill with ember icon, headline is white. CTA is ember-fill white-text.
- TACTICAL: right tile is pure black with a single hairline corner-bracket (per [TACTICAL archetype](../../../archetypes/tactical.md)); pill is white-fill black-text with the certification icon, headline white, CTA white-fill black-text.
- CLEAN: right tile is the dealer's chosen accent color (e.g. brand blue), pill is white-fill brand-text, headline white, CTA white-fill brand-text. Straightforward [CLEAN archetype](../../../archetypes/clean.md) fit.
- FIELD: right tile is warm cream or terracotta (not red — saturated red would clash with the warm palette per [FIELD archetype](../../../archetypes/field.md)); pill is cream-fill with terracotta icon and label, headline charcoal, CTA charcoal-fill cream-text. The cream-on-cream low-contrast register is the FIELD-specific tonal move.
- TERRAIN: pair this hero with a light-band section directly below — the saturated right tile acts as the "dark band" in the alternating-band rhythm; the next section (services or product) starts on cream/white to keep the rhythm legible. See [TERRAIN archetype](../../../archetypes/terrain.md).
- PATRIOT: right tile is deep navy (not barn-red — barn-red as a full saturated tile reads as kitsch); pill is cream-fill navy-text with a small star or shield-mark icon, headline cream, CTA barn-red-fill cream-text. The flag-derived accents stay at the pill + CTA scale, not the tile scale. See [PATRIOT archetype](../../../archetypes/patriot.md).
- For dealers whose lead is more "we'll capture your interest with a form" rather than "we lead with credibility," pair instead with the [form-in-hero-overlay](./refero-7d15989d-form-in-hero-overlay.md) pattern. For dealers leading with a long-form mission/creed, the [manifesto-creed-hero](./refero-b86571f2-manifesto-creed-hero.md) is the stronger pre-frame.

## Source brief
Yelp's `/yelp-guaranteed` consumer-trust landing — Yelp leads with the certification pill rather than the searchable directory because the page is selling the *guarantee program*, not Yelp itself. Same shape works for a Terraplex dealer whose competitive moat is the [authorized distributor + in-house Part 137 compliance program](../../../content/compliance-program.md) — the badge is the lead, the product comes second.
