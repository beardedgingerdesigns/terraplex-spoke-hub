## Why this is library-worthy
A third structural variant of "video in the hero" that's distinct from the two already in the library: where [video-poster-statement hero](./refero-507672c5-video-poster-statement-hero.md) uses the video frame as the full-bleed BACKGROUND (centered statement floats over it) and [fullbleed-video-display hero](./refero-f4b01774-fullbleed-video-display-hero.md) autoplays the video AS the background, this pattern treats the video as a discrete contained rounded card below a left-aligned display-serif headline, all on a SOLID brand-color band (not a photo). The structural move — "brand-color band + locale-scoped serif H1 + contained video card + secondary H2/paragraph still on the same band" — is the editorial / restrained way to lead with video. It's the right pick when the page is a scoped landing (regional impact, state operations, county program, multi-locale dealer's "[Dealer] in [State]" page, or a "[Dealer]'s Compliance Program" page) rather than a vision-thesis or a product showcase, and the dealer wants the video to read as a documentary chapter rather than a poster or a backdrop loop.

## What to take
- Solid full-bleed brand-color band (~75–90vh) as the hero surface. The band is one saturated, dark, or deeply tonal brand color edge-to-edge — Instacart's forest-green (#0B4A3A) is one example; the band reads as the dealer's primary brand-color territory, not as a photograph or a video. No gradient overlay, no texture, no parallax.
- Centered max-width content column (~1100–1200px) inside the band. The hero is single-column on desktop and stacks the same way on mobile.
- Left-aligned display-serif H1 at the top of the column: large (~72–96px desktop), weight 700–900, line-height 0.95–1.05, high-contrast cream/off-white on the dark band. The headline names a SCOPE: "[Dealer] in [State]" / "[Dealer] in [County]" / "[Dealer]'s Compliance Program" / "[Dealer]'s Spring Program". The serif (Playfair / Noe / editorial-display register) is load-bearing — it signals "this page is a chapter, not a pitch."
- Contained video card below the H1, full-column-width (~1100px), border-radius 12–16px, no surrounding chrome other than the rounded corners. The poster frame is photographic and human-centered (close-up of the product / hands / scene the video documents). A centered circular play affordance (~64–84px circle, semi-opaque white) sits in the middle of the card with a white triangular play glyph. Clicking opens the video inline or in a lightbox.
- Below the video card and STILL on the same brand-color band: a smaller secondary headline (~32–40px, same serif, slightly less weight) + a single 2–4-sentence lead paragraph (sans-serif, off-white, ~18–22px, ~720px max-width). The H2 names the thesis ("Expanding access and delivering opportunity to New Yorkers" → for a dealer "Bringing precision aerial application to Buffalo County" / "Helping family farms get Part 137-ready"). The paragraph is plain prose — no bullets, no CTA pills inside the band.
- The hero band ENDS at the bottom of that paragraph. The page transitions to a cream/warm-tonal band immediately below for the next section (carousel, partner logos, stats, testimonials) — the alternation is the rhythm. The hero is the only deep brand-color band of the page until the resources accordion / footer.
- Optional underline/eyebrow: a small wordmark or logo pill in the top-left of the dark band can sit above the H1 (e.g., a "[Dealer logo] in [State]" pairing) as a quiet locale anchor, but this is OPTIONAL and the H1 alone carries the move.

## What NOT to take
- Don't replace the solid brand-color band with a photograph or video backdrop. The pattern's restraint depends on the brand color being the SURFACE and the video being a discrete card on it — collapsing the band into a photographic backdrop turns this into [video-poster-statement hero](./refero-507672c5-video-poster-statement-hero.md), which is a DIFFERENT pattern with a different affordance (centered manifesto statement, not left-aligned locale-scoped serif).
- Don't autoplay the video. The play affordance is part of the trust register — the viewer chooses to start the chapter. An autoplaying loop turns the contained card into a "fullbleed-video-display" variant with worse framing; if you want autoplay, use [fullbleed-video-display hero](./refero-f4b01774-fullbleed-video-display-hero.md) instead.
- Don't put a CTA pill inside the hero band. The pattern's whole logic is "scope → video → paragraph → trust by reading on." A "Get started" pill in the band breaks the chapter framing and competes with the play button. Conversion CTAs come later in the page (partner logos, "Talk to us" section, footer band).
- Don't import a sans-serif "tech homepage" headline in place of the display serif. The editorial serif is what separates this pattern from FORGE/TACTICAL hero patterns — swap it for font-black sans and the page reads as a product launch, which kills the chapter / documentary register the locale-scoping needs.
- Don't make the H1 generic ("Welcome to [Dealer]"). The locale-scoping IS the move. If the page isn't about a specific scope (region, state, program, season), use a different hero — [manifesto-creed](./refero-b86571f2-manifesto-creed-hero.md) for ideology, [hands-on craft](./refero-be842c1e-hands-on-craft-hero.md) for operator-in-context, [video-poster-statement](./refero-507672c5-video-poster-statement-hero.md) for vision.
- Don't widen the H1 past 2 lines or stack more than 3 small elements (eyebrow + H1 + video card + H2 + paragraph). Five things on one band is the cap; six starts to feel like a brochure cover.
- The dark-dominant FORGE/TACTICAL register conflicts on the typography axis: both archetypes' font-black sans-serif + accent-gradient headline rule collapses the editorial-serif chapter register, and the solid brand-color band (without ember glow / mission-grid texture) reads as neither dark archetype's surface. For dark-archetype video heroes use [hardware-lineup dark hero](./refero-0945a36c-hardware-lineup-dark-hero.md) (cinematic studio sweep) or [interactive product-demo hero](./refero-d6176d7e-interactive-product-demo-hero.md) (live UI panel) — both dark-native, neither editorial.

## Adaptation notes (cross-archetype)
- FIELD: native fit. The brand-color band is deep forest-green or warm forest-charcoal (#2E3A2D-ish range — not pure black). Display serif in cream (#F1EADB) or warm off-white. Video card poster is golden-hour photographic — operator at the controls, hands on the R-32, harvest field at scale. Below-hero cream band picks up the warm-amber palette family from [editorial cream-bands product-block](../../product-block/research/refero-ecfaaa4f-editorial-cream-bands.md) for a coherent FIELD page rhythm. Pairs naturally with [stats-led impact-services](../../services/research/refero-41c5ab43-stats-led-impact-services.md) further down the page for a "[Dealer] in [State]" full-page composition. See [FIELD archetype](../../../archetypes/field.md).
- PATRIOT: brand-color band in deep navy or barn-red-shaded charcoal. Display serif in cream, slightly higher weight (900). Video card poster is real-people-in-place — operators on a flatbed, an in-store handshake, a county fairground booth. Eyebrow wordmark uses a small flag/seal glyph (one only, not chrome). Locale-scoped H1 ("[Dealer] in [County]" / "[Dealer]'s Veterans-Operator Program") carries the territorial framing the archetype rewards. Pairs with [manifesto-creed hero](./refero-b86571f2-manifesto-creed-hero.md) on a sibling page (this for chapter-led; manifesto-creed for ideology-led). See [PATRIOT archetype](../../../archetypes/patriot.md).
- CLEAN: brand-color band in deep brand-navy or near-black brand-charcoal. Display serif swapped for a high-contrast modern-serif (Recoleta / GT Sectra / Editorial New) to keep the chapter register without heritage tilt. Video card poster shows the actual product UI or facility shot in even daylight. The transition to the cream band uses CLEAN's brand-accent rim on the divider. Pairs with the [stats-led services](../../services/research/refero-41c5ab43-stats-led-impact-services.md) further down for a coherent CLEAN program-landing rhythm. See [CLEAN archetype](../../../archetypes/clean.md).
- TERRAIN: the dark brand-color hero band IS the page's opening "dark band" — the immediate cream band below is the first alternation in the archetype's signature light/dark rhythm. The display serif works well; add a faint topographic contour line at low opacity inside the brand-color band (concentric-ellipse SVG behind the headline, ~6% opacity, accent-tinted) as the archetype's texture signature. Pairs with the [topographic layered-bg hero](./refero-ae84b62a-topographic-layered-bg.md) on a sibling page (this for chapter/scoped-locale openings; topographic for abstract-territorial openings). See [TERRAIN archetype](../../../archetypes/terrain.md).
- FORGE / TACTICAL: skip — the editorial display-serif and brand-color-not-glow band conflict with both archetypes' typographic and surface rules. For dark-archetype video-led heroes use [interactive product-demo hero](./refero-d6176d7e-interactive-product-demo-hero.md) (live UI inside the band) or [hardware-lineup dark hero](./refero-0945a36c-hardware-lineup-dark-hero.md) (cinematic studio sweep) instead.

## Source brief
Instacart's [/company/impact/ny](https://www.instacart.com/company/impact/ny) — the New-York-state-scoped impact landing page on the company / impact namespace. The hero band declares the scope with a left-aligned display-serif "Instacart in New York", places a contained video poster card with a centered play glyph below the headline (forest-green band, peppers-in-close-up poster frame), and follows with a secondary "Expanding access and delivering opportunity to New Yorkers" H2 + lead paragraph still on the same dark band before transitioning to alternating cream bands (news strip, partner family photo, partners logo row, stats + dark-band, shopper photo, testimonials, resources accordion, footer). The library-worthy move is the HERO band alone — the alternating cream/dark bands below are already covered by [editorial cream-bands product-block](../../product-block/research/refero-ecfaaa4f-editorial-cream-bands.md) and [stats-led impact-services](../../services/research/refero-41c5ab43-stats-led-impact-services.md). For a Terraplex dealer, the natural use cases are: a "[Dealer] in [State]" or "[Dealer] in [County]" regional-impact page (multi-state operations, county-specific programs); a "[Dealer]'s Compliance Program" page that pairs with [content/compliance-program.md](../../../content/compliance-program.md); a season-scoped program landing ("[Dealer]'s 2026 Spring Cohort"); or an investor / community-impact page where the chapter framing is more appropriate than a product pitch.

Sibling hero notes that share the "video in hero" axis:
- [video-poster-statement hero](./refero-507672c5-video-poster-statement-hero.md) — video AS background, centered statement, single play button (vision-led).
- [fullbleed-video-display hero](./refero-f4b01774-fullbleed-video-display-hero.md) — autoplay video AS background, oversized display headline, scroll-arrow CTA (motion-led).

Pick guide: this hero for state-scoped / region-scoped / program-scoped landings where the video is a chapter inside a brand-color band. Video-poster-statement for "we believe X" vision pages. Fullbleed-video-display for hardware-launch motion-led openings.
