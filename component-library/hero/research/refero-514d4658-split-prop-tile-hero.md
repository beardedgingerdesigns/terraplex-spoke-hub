## Why this is library-worthy
A split hero where the LEFT ~50% is a tight typographic content stack (display-serif headline + 3-line paragraph + 2 paired CTAs) on a cream / warm-off-white surface, and the RIGHT ~50% is a single saturated-color tile (~50% page width, full hero height) holding ONE iconic vertical-specific prop photograph — a clapperboard for filmmakers, a microphone for podcasters, a controller for operators, a rotor for technicians. The split between the two halves is a hard vertical edge, no transition. The prop carries the audience-recognition weight; the saturated color block carries the visual energy; the typography carries the message. Right pick for "for-[vertical]" landing pages where the dealer wants every audience-vertical to have its own page with an instantly recognizable opening signal. Different from existing hero patterns: the [centered-screenshot-trio](./refero-f619e700-centered-screenshot-trio.md) is product-UI-led; the [manifesto-creed hero](./refero-b86571f2-manifesto-creed-hero.md) is photo+quote ideology-led; the [form-in-hero](./refero-7d15989d-form-in-hero-overlay.md) collapses lead capture; the [hands-on craft hero](./refero-be842c1e-hands-on-craft-hero.md) shows a person mid-use. This pattern uses a single iconic OBJECT — a tool, a prop, a piece of equipment cut out and dropped onto a saturated archetype-aligned tile — as the audience signal.

## What to take
- Two-column hero band (~50–60vh, contained — does not fill viewport, glimpse of section below shows). Hard vertical edge between the two halves; no soft transition, no overlap, no neutral spacer.
- LEFT ~50% (cream / warm-off-white surface, ~#F4EFE7):
  1. Display-serif (or heritage-tinted) bold headline at the top, ~48–64px, 2–3 lines: "Epidemic Sound for Filmmakers" → for a dealer "Terraplex for [Specialty Crops]" / "Terraplex for [Multi-Generation Farms]" / "Terraplex for [Custom Applicators]". Headline names the audience explicitly.
  2. 2–4 line paragraph in muted charcoal (~16–18px) directly below the headline — explains the program in one breath ("You're a filmmaker. You need professional, original music for filmmakers to captivate your audience…" → for a dealer "You're a [vertical]. You need [equipment + service] sized for [their actual operation type].").
  3. Paired CTAs at the bottom of the left column: a filled black pill ("Start 30-day free trial" → for a dealer "Schedule field demo" / "Get a tailored quote") and an outline / text-link CTA ("Pricing" → for a dealer "Compare programs" / "View specs"). Two CTAs only — primary action + secondary information path. Both ~120–160px wide, anchored bottom-left.
- RIGHT ~50% (saturated single-color tile — pink, mustard, sage, navy, barn-red, deep amber depending on the audience-vertical):
  1. The whole right half is ONE flat color block — no gradient, no texture, no chrome. Color choice is the audience signal: warm pink for filmmakers, sage for sustainability, mustard for hospitality, deep amber for crop-row operators, navy for compliance, barn-red for heritage-leaning farms. ONE color per page; multiple "for-[vertical]" pages on the same dealer site rotate through the dealer's chosen audience palette so readers learn "this color = this vertical."
  2. Centered on the color tile: ONE iconic prop photograph, cut out (transparent background), large enough that it dominates the tile (~70% of tile height). The prop is the most-recognizable single object for that vertical: a clapperboard for filmmakers, a microphone for podcasters, a controller for operators, a rotor for technicians, a spreader hopper for application crews, a clipboard for compliance audits.
  3. The prop is photographed flat — no environmental context, no operator hand, no studio sweep. Just the object on transparent background, dropped onto the color tile. The flatness IS the affordance: the tile + prop together signal "we built this page specifically for you."
- Below the hero band, the page proceeds with normal alternating content sections — each section can be either a 2-column image-text band ("Soundtrack your world" + cinematic photograph of the actual product / equipment in use) or a centered photograph + caption block.

## What NOT to take
- Don't use this pattern without a real iconic prop for the audience-vertical. A generic stock photo (a generic farm, a generic city skyline, a generic equipment shot) collapses the affordance — the whole pattern depends on the prop being **the** object that audience uses every day. For Terraplex dealers that means one prop per vertical: a real GTEEX [R-32](../../../products/r-32.md) controller for operators, a real spreader hopper for application crews, a real Part 137 logbook for compliance audits, a real rotor assembly for technicians. The prop must be the dealer's actual stock, photographed cleanly.
- Don't make the right-side color tile a gradient. The pattern's affordance depends on the flat saturated color block — gradients dilute the "tile" register into a generic background and the prop loses its anchor. One color, no gradient, no texture.
- Don't put any typography inside the right-side color tile. The tile holds the prop only; the headline + paragraph + CTAs live in the left column. Putting copy on the tile collapses the split and makes the tile look like a decorative half rather than a discrete content panel.
- Don't import Epidemic Sound's literal pink color or the clapperboard prop. Pink reads as fashion / consumer-brand for a Terraplex dealer; the clapperboard is film-vertical-specific. The transferable move is "saturated archetype-aligned color tile + audience-iconic prop" — not the specific pink-and-clapperboard.
- Don't use this pattern for a generic dealer landing page. The pattern's whole job is "for-[vertical]" landing pages — pages built specifically for one audience type. On a generic homepage the audience-anchor logic dies because there's no single audience to anchor to. For generic landings use the [centered-screenshot-trio hero](./refero-f619e700-centered-screenshot-trio.md), the [manifesto-creed hero](./refero-b86571f2-manifesto-creed-hero.md), or the [hands-on craft hero](./refero-be842c1e-hands-on-craft-hero.md).
- Don't pair this with the [audience-tabbed services pattern](../../services/research/refero-fef890ec-audience-tabbed-services.md) on the same page. Both surface "we serve different audience types"; the audience-tabbed services pattern is the single-page version (one page, multiple audiences via tab swap), this hero is the multi-page version (one page per audience). Pick one model — single-page-tabbed for low-overhead, multi-page-with-this-hero for SEO-and-paid-ad-targetable per-vertical pages.
- The dark-dominant register of FORGE / TACTICAL conflicts with this pattern's cream-base + saturated-tile affordance. Forcing the left half onto a dark backdrop collapses the cream / saturated-color contrast that makes the prop tile pop. For dark archetypes that need audience targeting, use the [dark industry-vertical services pattern](../../services/research/refero-74b10c78-dark-industry-vertical.md) (which is the dark-archetype audience-anchored pattern, applied at services scale).

## Adaptation notes (cross-archetype)
- FIELD: native fit. Cream left column, deep-amber or sage saturated tile on the right with the prop cutout (real R-32 controller, real spreader hopper, real GTEEX rotor — whichever fits the audience-vertical). Display-serif heritage headline ("Terraplex for [Multi-Generation Farms]"), warm-charcoal paragraph, amber filled CTA + amber-outline secondary CTA. The tile color rotates per vertical: amber for row-crop, sage for specialty crops, deep clay for hill-country, warm cream for orchard. See [FIELD archetype](../../../archetypes/field.md). Pairs with the [aerial-photo-spine product-block](../../product-block/research/refero-4c2ed506-aerial-photo-spine.md) below for sustained aerial-farmland register.
- CLEAN: white left column with neutral-charcoal headline, brand-navy saturated tile on the right with the prop cutout. Sentence-case headline (less heritage than FIELD), brand-navy filled CTA + brand-navy-outline secondary CTA. The tile color rotates per vertical in matched-saturation brand-tinted neutrals (navy / slate / teal / charcoal). See [CLEAN archetype](../../../archetypes/clean.md). Pairs with the [centered-screenshot-trio hero](./refero-f619e700-centered-screenshot-trio.md) on the parent home page (this for the for-vertical drill-downs; trio for the home).
- PATRIOT: cream left column, deep-navy saturated tile on the right with the prop cutout (a real Part 107 prep manual for compliance, a real R-32 controller for operators, a real spreader hopper for applicators). Display-serif heritage headline in deep navy, charcoal paragraph, deep-navy filled CTA + barn-red text-link secondary CTA — the single barn-red moment on the page anchors the flag-color-at-micro-scale rule. The tile color rotates per vertical: deep navy for compliance / institutional, barn-red for heritage / multi-generation, deep amber for crop-row / commercial. Three colors max across the dealer's audience-vertical pages — fewer than that reads as monotone, more reads as confused. See [PATRIOT archetype](../../../archetypes/patriot.md). Pairs with the [manifesto-creed hero](./refero-b86571f2-manifesto-creed-hero.md) on the parent home page (manifesto for the ideology pre-frame; this for the for-vertical drill-downs).
- TERRAIN: cream left column, clay or sage saturated tile on the right (TERRAIN's organic palette). Sentence-case headline in deep clay, charcoal paragraph, clay-filled CTA. The tile color rotates per vertical through contour-derived hues (cream / clay / sage / charcoal) — the rotation IS the alternating-band rhythm at hero scale. Add a thin topographic line motif at the bottom edge of the saturated tile (the page's first contour-divider, signaling the alternating rhythm to come). See [TERRAIN archetype](../../../archetypes/terrain.md). Pairs with the [topographic layered-bg hero](./refero-ae84b62a-topographic-layered-bg.md) on the parent dark-band moment elsewhere in the dealer site.
- FORGE / TACTICAL: skip — the cream + saturated-tile contrast is light-dominant by definition. For dark-archetype audience-vertical pages use the [dark industry-vertical services pattern](../../services/research/refero-74b10c78-dark-industry-vertical.md) which handles audience targeting at services scale on a dark backdrop.

## Source brief
Epidemic Sound's [/filmmaking](https://www.epidemicsound.com/filmmaking/) — the "for filmmakers" vertical landing for the music-licensing platform. The library-worthy move is the structural cadence (split-hero with cream-on-left + saturated-color-tile on the right + single iconic prop cutout on the tile + display-serif audience-named headline + paired CTAs), reusable for a Terraplex dealer building per-vertical landing pages where each page is built for one audience type ([Part 137 commercial applicators](../../../content/compliance-program.md), multi-generation row-crop farms, custom applicators, specialty growers, co-op operations, research operations) and needs an instantly recognizable opening signal that telegraphs "this page is for YOU." The prop tile's flat saturated color carries the visual identity; the prop itself carries the audience-recognition; the typography carries the message.
