## Why this is library-worthy
A hero pattern where the hero band IS the lead-capture form — full-bleed photograph (real-person, in-context) on the right ~60%, dark or accent-color overlay panel on the left ~40% containing all-caps headline + a callout pill ("0% commissions for up to 30 days" → for a dealer "Free fleet evaluation" / "Same-day Part 137 quote") + an embedded short signup form (3–4 fields + submit) + small disclaimer line. Conversion happens IN the hero — the rest of the page is supporting material, not a separate funnel. Different from existing hero patterns: the [centered-screenshot-trio](./refero-f619e700-centered-screenshot-trio.md), [manifesto-creed](./refero-b86571f2-manifesto-creed-hero.md), [hardware-lineup dark](./refero-0945a36c-hardware-lineup-dark-hero.md), [topographic layered-bg](./refero-ae84b62a-topographic-layered-bg.md), and [hands-on craft hero](./refero-be842c1e-hands-on-craft-hero.md) all defer the form to a separate contact page or a footer band. This pattern collapses the funnel into a single hero band — the right pick when the dealer's brief is "lead velocity, not story-telling."

## What to take
- Two-column hero band (~60–70vh, contained — does NOT fill viewport so a glimpse of the section below shows). Right ~60% is a full-bleed photograph of a real person in working context (operator with equipment, farmer with the product, dealer principal at a workbench). Left ~40% is an overlay panel that floats over the photograph's left edge with a soft drop-shadow — panel background is a saturated archetype-anchored color (deep red for the source; for a dealer this becomes archetype-aligned).
- Overlay panel content stack (top to bottom, ~40–48px internal padding):
  1. All-caps bold headline (~36–48px) in white or near-white, 2–3 lines max ("GROW SALES, NOT JUST ORDERS ON [PLATFORM]" → for a dealer "BUILT IN THE AMERICAS, SERVICED IN YOUR COUNTY").
  2. A small callout pill anchored under the headline: rounded pill with a contrasting background (cream / pale-yellow) and bold sentence-case label ("0% commissions for up to 30 days" → for a dealer "Free fleet evaluation through harvest" or "Same-day Part 137 service quote").
  3. Embedded signup form: 3–5 short fields stacked vertically (Phone, Email, Store/Farm Name, Business Type dropdown), each ~40–44px tall with light backgrounds and no visible labels (placeholder text only, e.g. "Phone number", "Email address"). Form is intentionally short — under 6 lines.
  4. Single filled CTA pill ("Start Free Trial" → for a dealer "Get Free Quote" / "Schedule Free Demo") in a contrasting bright color (yellow / amber on red, navy on cream, ember on charcoal). The CTA is ~44px tall and full-width inside the panel.
  5. Below the CTA, one line of small disclaimer text in muted contrasting color ("By clicking 'Start Free Trial', I agree to receive electronic communications from [dealer]") — sets the legal expectation without breaking the conversion register.
- Below the hero band, the page proceeds with normal product / about / testimonial sections — but the conversion has already happened in the hero, so the rest of the page is for users who scrolled past without filling the form.
- Mobile collapse: photograph stacks above the overlay panel (panel becomes full-width), form fields remain stacked. The hero remains the page's first conversion moment regardless of viewport.

## What NOT to take
- Don't import this pattern if the dealer's offer doesn't justify a "fill out a form right now" register. The pattern reads as transactional — "give us your details for an immediate benefit." Without a real benefit (free quote, free fleet evaluation, free demo flight, money-off promotion), the form-in-hero collapses into a generic email-capture and the customer perceives it as a lead-capture trap rather than a conversion offer.
- Don't make the form longer than ~5 fields. The pattern's conversion affordance depends on the form being too short to refuse — at 6+ fields, the user defers, the rest of the page becomes the conversion path, and the in-hero form is just decoration.
- Don't import the literal DoorDash dark-red overlay color or yellow callout-pill / Submit pill. The overlay panel color must be archetype-aligned: deep red works for high-energy commerce brands (DoorDash, fast-food, gig-work platforms) but reads as kitsch for a dealer site. Use deep navy (PATRIOT/CLEAN), warm clay (FIELD/TERRAIN), near-black with ember (FORGE), or near-black with cyan (TACTICAL).
- Don't add a second CTA below the form ("Or learn more →"). The pattern has ONE conversion path: the form. A second CTA dilutes the affordance and signals to the user that the form is optional. If the user wants to learn more, they scroll past the hero.
- Don't import the literal full-bleed photograph of a single grocery-bagger — that's the source's specific lifestyle. The transferable move is "real-person-in-working-context, mid-action," not stock model. For a Terraplex dealer this is a real operator on a controller mid-mission, a real farmer at a kitchen table reviewing a quote, or a real Sam-Welton-style compliance technician inspecting a rotor — never a stock smile-at-camera shot.
- Don't combine this with the [hands-on craft hero](./refero-be842c1e-hands-on-craft-hero.md) on the same page. Both occupy the "hero photograph + headline" slot; this one collapses the conversion into the hero, the other defers it. Pick one — if the dealer's brief is "lead velocity," use this one; if it's "show what's possible, then have them call," use the craft hero.
- Don't put a price inside the callout pill. The pill carries an offer ("Free X for Y"), not a SKU price. A literal price in the pill ("R-32 from $X") collapses the conversion register into a product-shop card (covered by the [color-tile category row](../../product-block/research/refero-dc38a5c1-color-tile-category-row.md) at product-block scale — different pattern, different page job).

## Adaptation notes (cross-archetype)
- CLEAN: native fit. Overlay panel in brand-navy (~#1F2C44) with cream callout pill and brand-navy CTA pill. Hero photograph is a real operator with equipment in a clean, well-lit context (hangar, prep room, daylight in a field). Headline in cream sentence-case (slightly less aggressive than all-caps for CLEAN's restrained register). Form fields with light-cream input chrome on the navy panel. See [CLEAN archetype](../../../archetypes/clean.md). Pairs with the [audience-tabbed services pattern](../../services/research/refero-fef890ec-audience-tabbed-services.md) below.
- PATRIOT: native fit. Overlay panel in deep navy (~#1F2C44), cream all-caps headline, single barn-red callout pill with cream label, cream-on-barn-red CTA pill — the flag colors all show up at this conversion moment, but at restrained micro-scale (the pill backgrounds, not as stripes or stars). Hero photograph is a real farmer or operator in late-afternoon American-farmland light. Disclaimer text in muted-cream below the CTA. See [PATRIOT archetype](../../../archetypes/patriot.md). Pairs with the [editorial cream-bands product-block](../../product-block/research/refero-ecfaaa4f-editorial-cream-bands.md) below the hero.
- FIELD: overlay panel in warm clay (~#C4876C) with cream callout pill and amber CTA pill. Hero photograph is a real farmer or operator at golden hour with hands on equipment. All-caps headline reads as a printed-poster moment in cream on clay. Form fields with cream backgrounds, hand-drawn focus ring rather than corporate. See [FIELD archetype](../../../archetypes/field.md). Pairs with the [aerial-photo-spine product-block](../../product-block/research/refero-4c2ed506-aerial-photo-spine.md) below.
- TERRAIN: the overlay panel reads as the page's first "dark band" anchor; the hero photograph is the "light band." Add a thin topographic divider on the panel's bottom edge that traces back into the photograph. Panel color is a deep clay or charcoal-sage; callout pill in warm cream; CTA pill in clay. The page's alternating rhythm starts with this contrast. See [TERRAIN archetype](../../../archetypes/terrain.md).
- FORGE: native fit. Overlay panel in near-black (~#0E0E0E) with cream callout pill and ember-orange CTA pill. Hero photograph is shot at dusk or with rim-lighting (forge-glow on the equipment edge, operator face partially in shadow). All-caps headline in white. Optional ember-warm rim along the panel's right edge (where it meets the photograph). See [FORGE archetype](../../../archetypes/forge.md). Pairs with the [dark feature bands product-block](../../product-block/research/refero-eb12db31-dark-feature-bands.md) below the hero.
- TACTICAL: tighten geometry — sharper rectangle overlay panel (~6px corner radius, no soft drop-shadow), precision-sans all-caps headline in tracked tracking, monochrome white-on-near-black panel with a single steel-cyan callout pill and cyan CTA rectangle. Hero photograph is studio-shot equipment in a controlled environment with a real operator (controller close-up, monochrome environment). Add a thin 1px corner-bracket detail to the panel corners (consistent with the archetype's bracket motif). See [TACTICAL archetype](../../../archetypes/tactical.md). Pairs with the [hardware-lineup dark hero pattern below... wait this is hero] — pair with the [spec-comparison-grid product-block](../../product-block/research/refero-713b66c0-spec-comparison-grid.md) below the hero for a TACTICAL precision-led page.

## Source brief
DoorDash Merchants's [/products/marketplace](https://merchants.doordash.com/en-us/products/marketplace) page — a B2B merchant-acquisition landing where the hero band is structured as a lead-capture form rather than a brand statement. The library-worthy move is the structural cadence (full-bleed photographic right column + saturated overlay panel left column carrying all-caps headline + offer callout pill + 3–5-field signup form + filled CTA + disclaimer line), reusable for a Terraplex dealer whose brief is "leads on this page right now" — typically a promotion landing, a free-evaluation page, a [Part 107 prep](../../../content/compliance-program.md) signup, or a seasonal offer. The pattern collapses the conversion funnel into the hero so the dealer doesn't lose the visitor to a separate contact page or a deferred call-back.
