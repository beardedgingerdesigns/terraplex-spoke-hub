## Why this is library-worthy

A hero pattern where the dealer is framed as a **publisher**, not a product seller: the hero is a centered serif publication wordmark (`"Magazine"`, `"Field Report"`, `"Compliance Briefing"`) immediately followed by a split-cell lead-article tile (large left photograph + right text column with category pill + serif headline + date + 2-line teaser + `"Read more →"`). The whole rest of the page is then the publication's content rail (more articles, city/insider guides, carousels), so the hero IS the cover of a real issue rather than a brand-introduction band. Different from every existing hero pattern: [video-poster statement hero](./refero-507672c5-video-poster-statement-hero.md) opens with a vision statement, [hands-on craft hero](./refero-be842c1e-hands-on-craft-hero.md) opens with a single operator-mid-use photograph, [type-only stats hero](./refero-14f39897-type-only-stats-hero.md) opens with restraint-as-affordance, [manifesto-creed hero](./refero-b86571f2-manifesto-creed-hero.md) opens with a declarative creed photo + one named quote. None of them treat the hero as "this is the cover of our latest issue." Right pick when a dealer's actual differentiation is a **regular content/editorial cadence** — e.g. [Sam Welton's compliance program](../../../content/compliance-program.md) publishing quarterly Part 137 / Part 107 briefings, a [field-archetype](../../../archetypes/field.md) dealer publishing season-by-season yield outlooks or grower stories, or a [terrain-archetype](../../../archetypes/terrain.md) dealer running a recurring "Field Report" research drop.

## What to take

- **Top band: bare publication wordmark.** Single full-width band (cream / off-white / per-archetype warm-light surface; on dark archetypes, a single saturated stripe). Centered. Two stacked lines: small dealer / program name (`"Expedia"` → for a dealer `"Sam Welton"` / `"Prairie Aerial"` / `"New Heights Ag"`); below it, the publication name in a very large display serif (`"Magazine"` → `"Field Report"` / `"Compliance Briefing"` / `"Yield Outlook"` / `"Operator Notes"`). No CTA pill in this band. No nav inside the band. Generous vertical breathing room top + bottom.
- **Lead-article tile sits directly under the wordmark, contained (not full-bleed).** Two equal cells. Left cell ~50% width: a single tall photograph (portrait-orientation crop preferred — operator, field, equipment in use, weather). The photograph is the single largest visual on the page. No text overlay. Right cell ~50% width: a vertical text column with consistent left-aligned padding.
- **Right cell content stack (top → bottom):** small category pill (saturated brand-color badge with white uppercase label, e.g. `"COMPLIANCE"` / `"FIELD REPORT"` / `"PROFILE"`) → 2–4 line serif headline (display weight, same family as the publication wordmark above) → date line in small subdued caps (`"OCTOBER 2026"`) → 2–3 line paragraph teaser → text-link CTA (`"Read more →"` — no filled pill). 
- **No second hero feature.** Below the lead-article tile, the page continues with the publication's content rails (3-up article grids, secondary feature blocks, named-rail carousels — that's the rest of the page, not part of the hero). The hero is the wordmark band + lead-article tile, full stop.
- **Typography hierarchy is serif-led.** Publication wordmark and lead headline share one display serif family. Body / teaser / date / category-pill use a clean sans. This is the inverse of every other hero in the library, which is sans-led; the serif primacy IS the publication signal.
- **Color rhythm:** one warm-neutral band (wordmark) → one neutral surface (lead-article cell) → photographs carry the only saturation. Category pill is the lone saturated UI element.
- **Implicit cadence anchor:** the date line under the headline (`"OCTOBER 2026"`) is non-negotiable. Without a visible date, the pattern collapses into a generic split-photo hero and the "you publish on a real cadence" signal evaporates.

## What NOT to take

- **Do not use without a real, recurring content program.** This pattern presupposes the dealer actually publishes new editions on a known cadence. A dealer with one evergreen article reading "Read more →" will look like dead infrastructure. If there isn't at least quarterly cadence and 4+ back-issues to fill the rest of the page, use [hands-on craft hero](./refero-be842c1e-hands-on-craft-hero.md) or [type-only stats hero](./refero-14f39897-type-only-stats-hero.md) instead.
- **Do not put product, pricing, badges, or CTAs in the wordmark band.** The wordmark is sacred to the publication identity. Adding `"Authorized Terraplex Dealer"` / `"Get a quote"` / R-32 product render breaks the publication frame and the pattern reads as confused brand-marketing.
- **Do not add a second feature article in the hero.** The Expedia source places multiple articles below the lead; only the wordmark + the one lead-article tile are the hero. Two equal feature tiles in the hero would dilute the "this is the current cover" signal.
- **Do not autoplay video in the lead photograph cell.** The pattern's restraint is the credibility move. A motion-on element would violate the [FIELD archetype](../../../archetypes/field.md) no-motion preference and undermine the serif-editorial register for CLEAN/PATRIOT.
- **Do not use this on a [FORGE-archetype](../../../archetypes/forge.md) dealer site.** FORGE's design vocabulary is `"animated glow blobs, gradient overlay tinted with accent color, ember accent lines"` — radial drama and saturation everywhere. The publication-hero pattern requires editorial restraint and serif primacy; the two cannot coexist without diluting either FORGE's capability-drama identity or the publication signal. FORGE dealers wanting a content-led hero should use the [interactive product-demo hero](./refero-d6176d7e-interactive-product-demo-hero.md) instead (which carries FORGE's drama via the working product surface).
- **Do not omit the date line.** Without a visible date under the lead headline, the pattern degrades to a split-photo hero and the "we publish on a real cadence" anchor is gone — at that point use [split-prop tile hero](./refero-514d4658-split-prop-tile-hero.md), which is the proper split-hero pattern without the publication framing.

## Adaptation notes (cross-archetype)

- **FIELD:** native fit. Cream wordmark band, warm-amber category pill, display serif (`Lora` / `Source Serif` / `Cormorant`) for `"Field Report"` + headline, agricultural photograph in the left cell (operator + crop + golden-hour light). Date in warm charcoal small caps. This is the most natural application — the pattern was practically designed for a FIELD dealer publishing season-by-season grower-story or yield-outlook editions.
- **CLEAN:** white wordmark band, navy/forest category pill, restrained transitional serif (`PT Serif` / `Source Serif`) for the wordmark, sans body. Photograph treatment: clean architectural / equipment-detail shots, not warm-golden. This reads as a B2B research / industry-briefing publication — fits [Great River](../../../research/manufacturers/terraplex.md) and similar B2B-integrator dealers publishing quarterly fleet-share or compliance briefings.
- **TERRAIN:** alternating-rhythm logic adapts naturally — the wordmark band can be the "light" tonality and the lead-article cell carries the first dark-band moment (or vice versa). Topographic contour SVG at very low opacity in the wordmark band only. Issue cadence pairs well with TERRAIN's `"the rhythm IS the identity"` design rule.
- **PATRIOT:** push the publication name harder: use `font-black` uppercase wide-tracking display sans for the publication wordmark (NOT serif) — `"FIELD BULLETIN"` / `"DISPATCH"` / `"OPERATOR REPORT"`. The lead headline can stay serif for editorial weight contrast. Category pill in navy or red. Stripe / star motif as the wordmark band background. The pattern's restraint still applies — bulletin-style commanding, not editorial-soft.
- **TACTICAL:** the most adventurous adaptation. Pure-black wordmark band with corner-bracket framing on the wordmark; the publication name in display-weight uppercase sans (TACTICAL is anti-serif). Lead photograph cell with corner brackets at the four corners; category pill as a saturated accent-color rectangle with a thin geometric prefix (`— BRIEFING`). Treat the publication as a `"FIELD BRIEF #014 / OCT 2026"`-style numbered bulletin rather than a magazine. Date line carries the issue number.

## Source brief

Expedia Magazine landing (`expedia.com/magazine`) — a major travel brand framing its blog as a periodical with a centered serif `"Magazine"` wordmark and a lead-article tile (Gladiator-Europe feature) directly underneath, then the rest of the page as numbered article rails (`"Unpack '25"`, `"Insider guides"`, `"City guides"`, `"Explore more"`). The pattern's transferable move is the wordmark-as-cover framing; the rest of the page rhythm is incidental.
