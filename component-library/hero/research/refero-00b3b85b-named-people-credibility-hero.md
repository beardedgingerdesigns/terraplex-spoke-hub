## Why this is library-worthy
A hero pattern where the page's central credibility move is the **named people behind the brand** — left ~50% is a tight headline + paragraph + single CTA introducing the program; right ~50% is a stacked rail of overlapping avatar circles (3–5 named contributors) on a soft brand-glow rounded backdrop. Below the hero, a "Meet our [people]" grid of named cards (avatar + name + role + 1-paragraph bio + social links) makes the people themselves the page content. Different from existing hero patterns: the [manifesto-creed hero](./refero-b86571f2-manifesto-creed-hero.md) leads with one anonymous creed photo + one named pull quote (one person carries the credibility); the [hands-on craft hero](./refero-be842c1e-hands-on-craft-hero.md) shows one operator mid-use; the [hardware-lineup dark hero](./refero-0945a36c-hardware-lineup-dark-hero.md) shows hardware. This pattern shows the **whole network** of named humans — credibility through breadth-of-people rather than breadth-of-product. Right pick when the dealer's differentiation is "we know everyone in this region" or when the [49-location Terraplex dealer network](../../../research/manufacturers/terraplex.md) wants to surface the named principals of every dealer, or when [Sam Welton's compliance team](../../../content/compliance-program.md) is the page's actual product.

## What to take
- Two-column hero band (~50–60vh, contained — does not fill viewport). Left ~50% is a vertical content stack: small dealer logotype top-left → 2–3 line bold headline ("Appwrite Heroes" → for a dealer "Our Operators" / "The Compliance Network" / "Meet Your Dealer Bench"); 2–3 line muted paragraph below the headline; single filled CTA pill ("Become a Hero" → for a dealer "Apply to join the bench" / "Become a certified operator" / "Get in touch").
- Right ~50% is a stacked **avatar rail**: 3–5 circular portrait photographs (~80–120px each, real headshots — not stock, not silhouettes), arranged in a vertical or diagonal cascade, each avatar slightly overlapping the rounded rail behind it. The rail itself is a soft archetype-glow shape (ember in FORGE, navy curtain in CLEAN, contour-clay in TERRAIN, navy-with-barn-red rim in PATRIOT) — the rail's job is to lift the avatars off the dark backdrop.
- Hero backdrop is dark (~#0E0E0E or near-black). Subtle gradient or soft-glow accents in the upper-right behind the avatar rail. No video, no scroll-triggered animation.
- Below the hero, a "Meet our [people]" centered headline (~32–40px) followed by a 3-column grid of named cards. Each card is white (or light-cream depending on archetype) on a contrasting page surface, with: small circular avatar (~40px) in the upper-left → name in bold sentence-case (~14px) → role/title in muted gray (~12px) below the name → 3–5 lines of biographical paragraph → 2 small monochrome social-link icons (LinkedIn, GitHub, X, Instagram — whichever the dealer's people actually use). Cards are equal-width, equal-height, generous internal padding (~24px).
- Below the named-card grid, an optional 4-up "How [people] benefit" row: small monochrome icon + bold label + 1-line description per cell (e.g. "Channel access", "In-person gatherings", "Priority support", "Travel paid for"). Operational benefits — what joining the network gets you.
- Page closes with a dark "Become a [Hero/Operator/Member]" band echoing the opening hero treatment — same backdrop, same avatar rail (smaller), centered headline + CTA pill below.

## What NOT to take
- Don't use this pattern without real named people. The pattern's whole credibility move depends on every avatar being a real headshot of a real named person with a real bio. Generic "meet the team" stock photos collapse the affordance into a brochure-style about-us block. For a Terraplex dealer this means real photographs of the dealer principal, named operators, named compliance technicians — never stock or silhouettes.
- Don't compress more than ~5 avatars into the hero rail. 6+ avatars start to look like a logo-wall of faces and lose the personal-credibility move; the named-card grid below is where the full network appears at scale.
- Don't import Appwrite's pink-glow rail color literally — that's brand chrome. The rail color must be archetype-aligned (see Adaptation notes below). Pink glow on a Terraplex dealer page would read as fashion / consumer-brand, not equipment-and-service.
- Don't add a CTA inside the named-card grid (e.g. "Hire [Name] →"). The cards are credibility surfaces, not lead-gen tiles. A CTA per card collapses the network-as-credibility move into a marketplace listing — for which the [Square Specialists / specialist-marketplace pattern](../../services/research/refero-d30b8a54-specialist-marketplace.md) is the right pick instead.
- Don't pair this with the [hardware-lineup dark hero](./refero-0945a36c-hardware-lineup-dark-hero.md) on the same page. Both occupy the "dark hero with right-anchored composition" slot — pick one. If the dealer's brief is "the people are the differentiator," use this; if it's "the equipment range is the differentiator," use the hardware-lineup.
- Don't add a video autoplay loop on the avatar rail. Subtle CSS hover-zoom on individual avatars is fine; rotating-carousel of avatars or autoplay video is not — both shift the register from "documented network" to "promotional reel."

## Adaptation notes (cross-archetype)
- FORGE: native fit. Near-black backdrop, ember-glow rail behind the avatar stack (soft warm gradient ~#3A1A0F → #5A2510 fading to transparent). Avatars in monochrome black-and-white with a thin ember rim-light. CTA pill is ember-orange. The "Meet our operators" grid below has dark cards (~#1A1A1A) with white text. See [FORGE archetype](../../../archetypes/forge.md). Pairs with the [dark feature bands product-block](../../product-block/research/refero-eb12db31-dark-feature-bands.md) below.
- TACTICAL: tighten geometry — avatars are square or hexagon-cropped (~6px corner radius) instead of circular; rail behind them is a precise rectangle with thin 1px corner brackets. Monochrome white-on-near-black with a single steel-cyan accent on the CTA pill and the social-link icons. Headline in tracked all-caps precision-sans. The named-card grid uses the same bracket motif on each card's corners. See [TACTICAL archetype](../../../archetypes/tactical.md). Pairs with the [topographic layered-bg hero](./refero-ae84b62a-topographic-layered-bg.md) on adjacent pages.
- TERRAIN: replace the dark backdrop with a deep contour band — the avatar rail becomes a stacked-cards moment in the page's "dark band" register, with topographic line motifs traced behind the avatars. Rail color in clay or sage. Below the dark hero, the "Meet our operators" grid lifts back to a cream / light band. See [TERRAIN archetype](../../../archetypes/terrain.md).
- CLEAN: lighten — backdrop becomes brand-navy (~#1F2C44) instead of near-black, avatars are circular with thin white rim, rail behind them in cream or pale-navy gradient. CTA pill is brand-cream-on-navy. The "Meet our team" grid below is on white with neutral-gray hairline borders. See [CLEAN archetype](../../../archetypes/clean.md). Pairs with the [centered-screenshot-trio hero](./refero-f619e700-centered-screenshot-trio.md) on adjacent pages.
- PATRIOT: replace the dark backdrop with deep navy (~#1F2C44), avatars in circular cream-rimmed frames, rail behind them as a soft barn-red glow gradient on the right edge — flag colors at restrained micro-scale. CTA pill in cream-on-navy. Below the hero, the "Meet our farmers" or "Meet our operators" grid uses cream cards with deep-navy headings; the named-card photographs are golden-hour American-farmland portraits (real farmers, real operators, real compliance leads). The social-link icons in the cards stay charcoal — restraint anchors the flag-colors-at-micro-scale rule. Don't add stripe rules between cards or stars overlaid on avatars; the named real-person register IS the patriotic move. See [PATRIOT archetype](../../../archetypes/patriot.md). Pairs with the [manifesto-creed hero](./refero-b86571f2-manifesto-creed-hero.md) on a sibling page where ideology leads (this hero leads with people).
- FIELD: warm-charcoal backdrop (~#3A2B1F instead of pure black) so the dark hero reads as warm-dark rather than corporate-dark. Avatars are golden-hour-lit portraits (real farmers, real operators in the field — weathered hands, work shirts, ball caps). Rail behind them is a soft amber glow. CTA pill in amber with cream label. The "Meet our operators" grid below is on warm-cream with deeper-cream cards; named-card photographs match the warm-photographic register. See [FIELD archetype](../../../archetypes/field.md). Pairs with the [editorial cream-bands product-block](../../product-block/research/refero-ecfaaa4f-editorial-cream-bands.md) below.

## Source brief
Appwrite's [/heroes](https://appwrite.io/heroes) page — the developer-ambassador / community-hero program landing for the Appwrite open-source platform. The library-worthy move is the structural cadence (dark hero with named-avatar rail on a brand-glow backdrop → "Meet our heroes" 3-up named-card grid → 4-up benefits row → closing dark CTA band echoing the opening hero), reusable for a Terraplex dealer whose differentiation is "named humans" — the [49-location dealer network](../../../research/manufacturers/terraplex.md) at the parent-brand level, [Sam Welton's compliance team](../../../content/compliance-program.md) at the service-vertical level, or a dealer's named principals + technicians at the dealer level. The pattern's affordance is "credibility through breadth-of-people" — the more named real humans on the page, the stronger the trust signal.

Pick guide: this hero is for "the network is the product" pages — dealer recruitment, compliance-team profiles, operator-network pages, ambassador / certified-partner pages. For product-led pages use hardware-lineup, screenshot-trio, or hands-on craft instead.
