## Why this is library-worthy
A dark-mode hardware product page that establishes "premium equipment" through studio-lit photography rim-lit against pure-black surfaces and a long-scroll cadence of `Pro-Grade [Feature]: [Subhead]` callouts in thin sans, each paired with one isolated product shot. Carries gravitas without flame, glow blobs, or animated chrome — the photography itself is the texture. A cleaner alternative when an archetype wants dark-dominant heat without the literal ember/flare motif.

## What to take
- Hero band: full-width product still on a near-black ground (#0A0B0D), dramatic rim/edge light from one side only (single-source, never two), product silhouette doing 60–70% of the visual work. Headline as overlay caption rather than dominant text.
- Repeating section unit below the hero: small all-caps eyebrow ("Pro-Grade Stabilization") in --text-muted → larger headline in display sans → 1 short paragraph → one isolated product shot lit identically to the hero. Same unit repeats 6–8 times down the page; rhythm comes from photographic consistency, not from layout variation.
- Studio lighting rule: every product photo on the page uses the same key-light angle and the same off-black backdrop. Lock this once, apply to all hardware shots — drift breaks the catalog feel.
- Feature-checklist tables (e.g. "in-the-box" comparison) sit on a slightly raised surface (#15–18 grey) with thin 1px hairline rules in --ink-foundry. Checkmark glyph is monochrome white, never the brand accent.
- Typography stays restrained: one display weight for headlines, one neutral sans for body, no decorative serifs. Section captions are short — three to seven words.

## What NOT to take
- The colored studio gels (purple, cyan) on the hardware photos are camera-product-specific brand voice. For a Terraplex dealer, light the drone in warm tungsten or cool moonlight, not stage gels — keeps the read photographic, not theatrical.
- Don't import the literal "Pro-Grade" copy formula — it's brand-specific. The transferable move is the eyebrow → headline → caption micro-pattern, not the wording.
- This pattern requires real product photography. If the dealer only has stock drone renders, the long-scroll photo cadence collapses — the eye sees the rendered seams immediately. Use this only when there's a shoot day in the budget.
- The pure-black ground (#000) reads harsh on large monitors. For FORGE, lift to stone-950 / #0B0B0C; pure black is fine on hardware product sites where the whole page is a portfolio, less so on a B2B landing where users dwell.

## Adaptation notes (cross-archetype)
- FORGE: native fit. Use the dealer's accent ONLY on a primary CTA fill and one bg-clip-text headline keyword per section — the photography carries the heat; the accent doesn't need to.
- TACTICAL: tighten the eyebrow caption into stencil/condensed sans, replace the soft rim light with hard top-down key light (longer shadows, more contrast), add corner-bracket framing on each product shot.
- TERRAIN: works only as the "dark band" half of an alternating cadence — pair every two photo sections with a cream-band intermission so the page doesn't read as a single dark scroll.
- FIELD / CLEAN / PATRIOT: skip — the dark-dominant photography rhythm conflicts with all three archetypes' light-or-cream backgrounds.
