## Why this is library-worthy
A canonical e-commerce product-detail-page (PDP) hero — large left-side product image with a thumbnail row beneath, right-side stack of product title + price + quantity stepper + filled CTA + all-caps DESCRIPTION + PRODUCT FEATURES bullet list + italic regulatory/disclaimer text. Closes a hero gap none of the existing six hero patterns covers: every existing hero is a *landing-page* hero (manifesto, hardware-lineup, hands-on craft, form-in-hero, screenshot-trio, topographic). None handles the dealer's actual single-product *buy* page — the page where a customer who already knows they want the [R-32](../../../products/r-32.md) or [I-19](../../../products/i-19.md) reads specs and clicks ADD TO CART (or REQUEST QUOTE under MAP rules). The all-caps body + strict label/value rhythm is what makes it a TACTICAL-precision-native pattern; the same skeleton lifts cleanly into all six archetypes via per-archetype palette and casing.

## What to take
- Two-column band with no hero photograph behind — pure white (or archetype base) page background, both columns sit on the page.
- Left ~50% column: one large square white tile (~600×600px) holding a single cutout product photograph centered, with consistent contact shadow only — no environmental context, no studio sweep, no rim-lighting drama. Below the tile, a horizontal thumbnail strip of 3–5 alternate product views (close-ups, in-context, accessory variants), each ~120×120px, white tile, gentle hover.
- Right ~50% column content stack (top to bottom):
  1. **Product name** — bold sans-serif, ~32–40px, all-caps for TACTICAL register or sentence-case for warmer archetypes ("CYBERQUAD FOR KIDS" → "R-32").
  2. **Price** — bold ~16–18px, single line, no sale-strike-through unless there's a real promotion. Under MAP/MSRP rules ([content/pricing-policy.md](../../../content/pricing-policy.md)), this becomes "MSRP $X" or "Request Quote" — the price slot is non-negotiable structurally; the value is what changes per dealer.
  3. **Quantity stepper** — small "QUANTITY" label in tracked all-caps muted gray (~12px), then a `– 1 +` stepper (~36–40px wide).
  4. **Single filled CTA** — full-width within the column (~44px tall), gray-on-gray "ADD TO CART" or per-archetype-accent "REQUEST QUOTE" / "SCHEDULE DEMO." One button only — the column has ONE conversion path.
  5. **DESCRIPTION** — small all-caps tracked label, then 4–6 lines of body copy in **all-caps** sentence-rhythm (this is the precision register that makes the pattern travel to TACTICAL natively). Body copy stays factual ("BE READY FOR ANY ADVENTURE…") with no marketing adjectives.
  6. **Inline cross-sell sentence** — one short line linking to a related product or accessory ("STORE WITH THE [R-32 COVER](#) WHILE NOT IN USE.") with the link as the only saturated element in the column.
  7. **PRODUCT FEATURES** — small all-caps tracked label, then a tight bullet list (5–8 short specs: voltage, motor, max speed, range, range, headlight). One unit per line. No icons.
  8. **Disclaimer paragraph** — italic, slightly smaller, muted gray. Carries regulatory or safety qualifiers ("THE NEW MODEL 915 CYBERQUAD IS A CERTIFIED ELECTRIC RIDE-ON TOY UNDER ASTM F963…" → for a Terraplex dealer "THE R-32 IS APPROVED FOR PART 137 AGRICULTURAL OPERATIONS UNDER FAA WAIVER…"). The italic block is the credibility move at this scale — every Terraplex dealer page has this kind of disclaimer; here it gets a deliberate slot.
  9. **CPSC/regulatory modifications block** — italic, small bullet list of compliance notes ("CPSC MODIFICATIONS TO MODEL 915 FROM MODEL 914: AGE RANGE: 9–12 YEARS / TIRE PRESSURE WARNING LABEL ADDED…"). Optional — drop when the product has no relevant modifications, but the slot is reserved.
- Below the hero band, a "RECOMMENDED PRODUCTS" 3-up tile row of related accessories or alternate models — same chrome as the thumbnail strip but at full card size (image + caption + price). This is where cross-sell to the [I-19](../../../products/i-19.md) lives if the page is the [R-32](../../../products/r-32.md) PDP.

## What NOT to take
- Don't put a hero photograph behind the band. The pattern's affordance depends on the product photo being a single white-tile cutout, not a lifestyle scene. A photographic hero behind this layout collapses it into a generic landing-page hero and the precision register dies. For lifestyle-led product heroes use the [hands-on craft hero](./refero-be842c1e-hands-on-craft-hero.md) or the [hardware-lineup dark hero](./refero-0945a36c-hardware-lineup-dark-hero.md) instead.
- Don't import the literal Tesla Cyberquad imagery — it's a kids' ride-on. The transferable move is "single-cutout product photo on white tile + thumbnail strip beneath." For a Terraplex dealer this is the [R-32](../../../products/r-32.md) drone on white with thumbnails showing top-down, front-quarter, controller close-up, and an in-flight reference shot.
- Don't soften the all-caps DESCRIPTION body to sentence-case if the dealer is on TACTICAL or CLEAN. The all-caps body IS the precision-register affordance — it sets the page's tone differently from any sentence-case PDP. For warm archetypes (FIELD, PATRIOT) sentence-case is appropriate; see Adaptation notes.
- Don't crowd the right column with multiple CTAs. ONE filled button per column. A "Buy Now / Or Learn More" pair dilutes the conversion path and turns the PDP into a marketing landing. If the dealer needs a "Learn More" affordance, put it as a text link inside the disclaimer paragraph, not as a second button.
- Don't drop the italic disclaimer paragraph. Every Terraplex dealer site needs a regulatory note (Part 137, Part 107, FAA waiver scope, kit-tier exposure rules — see [content/pricing-policy.md](../../../content/pricing-policy.md)); the italic slot is where it lives. A PDP without disclaimers reads as consumer-grade and undermines the [Terraplex compliance positioning](../../../content/compliance-program.md).
- Don't enlarge the thumbnail strip or replace it with a carousel arrow. The static row of 3–5 thumbnails is the affordance; auto-rotating carousels lose track-of-state and the customer can't return to a specific view. Static, click-to-swap-main-image, full stop.
- Don't import the price + quantity-stepper + ADD TO CART trio if the dealer's checkout is "request quote / schedule demo" only. The structural slot remains; the LABELS change. "ADD TO CART" → "REQUEST QUOTE"; the quantity stepper drops; the price stays as MSRP. Don't fake an e-commerce checkout the dealer doesn't actually run.

## Adaptation notes (cross-archetype)
- TACTICAL: native fit. All-caps everywhere (product name, DESCRIPTION body, PRODUCT FEATURES label, disclaimer caps). Precision-sans for the body; tracked tracking on labels (~120 letterspacing). Sharper rectangle ADD TO CART (~6px corner radius), monochrome white-on-near-black or near-black-on-white only with a single steel-cyan accent on the inline cross-sell link. Add a thin 1px corner-bracket detail to the white product-tile corners (consistent with the archetype's bracket motif). Thumbnail strip cells stay sharp rectangles, not pills. See [TACTICAL archetype](../../../archetypes/tactical.md). Pairs with the [spec-comparison-grid product-block](../../product-block/research/refero-713b66c0-spec-comparison-grid.md) below the hero for a TACTICAL precision-led product page.
- CLEAN: native fit with sentence-case body (CLEAN's restrained register reads slightly less aggressive than full all-caps). White background, neutral-gray tile borders, single brand-navy accent on the ADD TO CART pill and the inline cross-sell link. Disclaimer paragraph in muted-gray italic. See [CLEAN archetype](../../../archetypes/clean.md). Pairs with the [head-to-head comparison product-block](../../product-block/research/refero-a1142f19-head-to-head-comparison.md) below for dealers who carry both R-32 and I-19.
- FORGE: invert to a dark variant — near-black page background (~#0E0E0E), white text, ember-orange ADD TO CART pill. Product-tile background stays light cream (~#F5F1E8) so the cutout product reads with contrast — the dark page is the chrome, not the photo backdrop. All-caps body works in FORGE as a precision-with-warmth register. Disclaimer in muted-cream italic. Pairs with the [dark feature bands product-block](../../product-block/research/refero-eb12db31-dark-feature-bands.md) below the hero. See [FORGE archetype](../../../archetypes/forge.md).
- TERRAIN: the white product-tile is the page's "light band" anchor; the right column with the all-caps DESCRIPTION reads as the alternating-rhythm partner. Add a thin topographic divider on the bottom edge of the product-tile and again above the RECOMMENDED PRODUCTS row. Single clay or sage accent on the ADD TO CART pill and the inline cross-sell link. See [TERRAIN archetype](../../../archetypes/terrain.md). Pairs with the [aerial-photo-spine product-block](../../product-block/research/refero-4c2ed506-aerial-photo-spine.md) below.
- PATRIOT: cream page background (~#F4EFE7), warm white product-tile, deep-navy product name + sentence-case body (drop all-caps for the body — PATRIOT's heritage register reads better in sentence-case). Single barn-red accent on the ADD TO CART pill (cream label on barn-red). Disclaimer paragraph in muted-charcoal italic. Thumbnail strip cells get a thin navy hairline border. See [PATRIOT archetype](../../../archetypes/patriot.md). Pairs with the [editorial cream-bands product-block](../../product-block/research/refero-ecfaaa4f-editorial-cream-bands.md) below.
- FIELD: cream page background, warm-white product-tile (~#F8F2E8) with a torn-cream-paper border instead of a hard rule, sentence-case body (drop all-caps — FIELD's hand-feel register doesn't carry the precision affordance). Warm-charcoal product name, deep-amber ADD TO CART pill. Thumbnail strip cells in matched warm cream. Disclaimer paragraph in muted-warm italic with hand-drawn underlines on link words rather than corporate inline links. See [FIELD archetype](../../../archetypes/field.md). Pairs with the [editorial warm feature blocks product-block](../../product-block/research/refero-4b64522d-editorial-warm-feature-blocks.md) below.

## Source brief
Tesla's [/product/cyberquad-for-kids](https://shop.tesla.com/product/cyberquad-for-kids) page — the consumer-shop PDP for a small electric ride-on toy. Most directly transferable hero pattern in the library because it's the canonical *single-product buy page* shape: large cutout image left, label-driven spec column right, single ADD TO CART, italic regulatory block. Reusable for a Terraplex dealer's actual [R-32](../../../products/r-32.md) or [I-19](../../../products/i-19.md) product pages — the page shape doesn't need re-invention; it just needs archetype-aligned palette and label substitution. The library's existing six hero patterns all serve marketing landings; this one serves the buy page itself.
