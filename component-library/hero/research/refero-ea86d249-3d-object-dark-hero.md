## Why this is library-worthy
A dark-hero pattern that puts a single 3D-rendered symbolic object (here a glowing crypto sphere wrapped in an isometric line-graph mesh) dead-center as the hero's only image, with a centered short headline + 1-line subhead + small filled CTA pill stacked over it, then drops directly into a 2-row monochrome logo wall. The pattern's affordance is "we built this on purpose, look at it" — the constructed render reads as engineering and intent in a way photography doesn't. Different from existing dark heroes in the library: the [topographic layered-bg](./refero-ae84b62a-topographic-layered-bg.md) is an abstract backdrop, not a focal object; the [hardware-lineup dark hero](./refero-0945a36c-hardware-lineup-dark-hero.md) shows real product hardware on a sweep, not a constructed metaphor; the [form-in-hero overlay](./refero-7d15989d-form-in-hero-overlay.md) collapses lead capture into the hero band itself. This pattern is the right pick when the dealer wants the hero to argue a *capability metaphor* (precision, reach, integration) without showing literal hardware.

## What to take
- Contained dark hero band (~50–60vh, near-black ~#0E0E0E with a faint vignette darker at the corners). The band does not fill viewport — a strip of the next section (logo wall) shows below the fold.
- One 3D-rendered focal object centered horizontally, vertically anchored at ~40% from top. Object is symbolic, not literal: a sphere wrapped in line graphs, a wireframe globe, an isometric platform diagram, a stylized control-surface rendering. Object emits soft accent-colored rim glow (~12–18% saturation) so it reads as "lit from within." No environmental context, no shadow on a floor, no surrounding chrome.
- Headline floats centered over the object's upper third. Two-line break, ~48–60px heavy sans-serif, white. Short copy (5–7 words total) — declarative thesis, not a question. Below the headline, one line of muted-gray subhead (~16px, ~#9E9E9E) explaining the thesis in one sentence.
- Single small filled CTA pill (~36–40px tall, ~140px wide) directly below the subhead — the only saturated color in the band. Pill carries a single accent (cobalt for the source; ember/cyan/clay/navy per archetype). Single CTA only — no secondary outline pill, no "Learn more" link.
- Below the hero band, drop straight into a 2-row monochrome logo wall (12–16 logos total, white logos at ~40% opacity on the same dark backdrop, no card chrome, no rule between hero and wall). The logo wall IS the page's first credibility moment; it earns its place by being immediately under the symbolic render.
- After the logo wall, the page resumes on a normal light surface for the body — the dark hero + dark logo wall is a contained moment, not a page-wide treatment.

## What NOT to take
- Don't import the literal Ripple-blue glow color. The transferable move is "single accent rim-light on a constructed render," not the specific blue. Per-archetype the rim is ember (FORGE), cyan (TACTICAL), clay (TERRAIN), brand-navy (CLEAN).
- Don't substitute a real product photograph for the rendered object. The pattern's affordance depends on a *constructed* asset — a render reads as engineering and intent; a photograph reads as catalog. If the dealer wants a real product hero on dark, use the [hardware-lineup dark hero](./refero-0945a36c-hardware-lineup-dark-hero.md) instead.
- Don't add multiple objects, decorative orbs, or a parallax field of background symbols. The pattern's restraint is the affordance — one object, centered, lit. Adding decorative chrome turns the hero into a generic "tech/futuristic" landing.
- Don't add a secondary CTA, a play-video chip, or a scroll-arrow indicator below the hero. The single accent-pill is the only interaction; everything else is read.
- Don't extend the dark band beyond the logo wall. The contained 60vh + 12-logo strip is the moment; the rest of the page should drop to a light surface so the dark moment retains its weight. A page-wide dark treatment collapses the hero's gravity.
- The constructed-render + dark-dominant register conflicts with [FIELD's](../../../archetypes/field.md) warm-photographic, no-dark-dominant rule and [PATRIOT's](../../../archetypes/patriot.md) cream-default + heritage-photography requirement. For warm archetypes use the [hands-on craft hero](./refero-be842c1e-hands-on-craft-hero.md) (single adult mid-use, warm-interior) or the [manifesto-creed hero](./refero-b86571f2-manifesto-creed-hero.md) (declarative + creed photo).

## Adaptation notes (cross-archetype)
- FORGE: native fit. 3D render gets ember-warm rim lighting (golden glow on the leading edge, deeper shadow on the trailing edge). Symbolic object can be a stylized rotor assembly, a glowing battery cell, a wireframe drone silhouette. Single ember-orange accent on the CTA pill. Logo wall stays muted-white. See [FORGE archetype](../../../archetypes/forge.md). Pairs with the [dark feature bands product-block](../../product-block/research/refero-eb12db31-dark-feature-bands.md) on the body of the page.
- TACTICAL: native fit with tighter geometry. 3D render uses precision-grade modelling (visible polygon edges, no smoothing) with a single steel-cyan rim light. Headline in tracked all-caps precision-sans. Sharper rectangle CTA (~6px corner radius, not pill). Add a 1px corner-bracket detail to the CTA corners — consistent with the archetype's bracket motif. Logo wall stays monochrome white at ~30% opacity. See [TACTICAL archetype](../../../archetypes/tactical.md). Pairs with the [spec-comparison-grid product-block](../../product-block/research/refero-713b66c0-spec-comparison-grid.md) below.
- TERRAIN: the 3D object reads as a "constructed topographic artifact" — render carries a contour-line wrap or a stylized landscape mesh. Rim light in clay or sage. The dark hero + logo wall sequence is the page's opening "dark band"; below the wall, the next section drops to a cream "light band" with a topographic divider. See [TERRAIN archetype](../../../archetypes/terrain.md). Sibling: [topographic layered-bg hero](./refero-ae84b62a-topographic-layered-bg.md) for the abstract-backdrop variant when no defensible 3D asset exists.
- CLEAN: invert to a light variant — light off-white background (~#F8F8F6) with the 3D render in muted brand-navy mesh tones, no rim-light glow (CLEAN's restraint is "no atmospheric chrome"). Headline in brand-charcoal sentence-case. Single brand-navy CTA pill. Logo wall in monochrome charcoal at ~40% opacity. The pattern works in CLEAN because the structural cadence (single render → centered headline → CTA → logo wall) is archetype-neutral once the dark backdrop is removed. See [CLEAN archetype](../../../archetypes/clean.md). Pairs with the [centered-screenshot-trio hero](./refero-f619e700-centered-screenshot-trio.md) on a sibling page (this hero is the constructed-metaphor opener; the screenshot-trio is the literal-product-screenshot opener).
- FIELD / PATRIOT: skip — the constructed-render + dark-dominant register conflicts with both archetypes' warm-photographic rules. For warm archetypes use the [hands-on craft hero](./refero-be842c1e-hands-on-craft-hero.md) or the [manifesto-creed hero](./refero-b86571f2-manifesto-creed-hero.md).

Ripple's [/](https://ripple.com/) homepage — the enterprise crypto-payments landing for the company building cross-border settlement infrastructure. The library-worthy move is the structural cadence (contained dark hero band → single 3D-rendered symbolic object centered → centered headline + subhead + single accent CTA pill → directly into a monochrome logo wall on the same dark backdrop), reusable for a Terraplex dealer who wants the hero to argue a *capability metaphor* — precision, integration with a fleet network, multi-state coverage — without showing literal hardware. Especially relevant for dealers carrying [R-32](../../../products/r-32.md) and [I-19](../../../products/i-19.md) when the hero is supposed to introduce the dealer's *operational platform* (compliance + service + training as one offer) rather than a single product.
