## Why this is library-worthy
A magazine / editorial homepage hero where the page is articles, not products: centered serif wordmark ("Magazine" / "Field Notes" / "Journal") at the very top of the band, ONE oversized lead-article split-row below (full-height vertical photo left + category-badge + headline + 1–2-line summary + date right), then carousel / grid rows of supporting articles beneath. The hero IS the magazine's masthead + the lead story. None of the existing 17 hero patterns covers the content-hub register — the closest, [manifesto-creed hero](./refero-b86571f2-manifesto-creed-hero.md), is a single creed; [centered-screenshot-trio](./refero-f619e700-centered-screenshot-trio.md) is product-UI-led; [hands-on craft hero](./refero-be842c1e-hands-on-craft-hero.md) shows a single operator; [type-only stats hero](./refero-14f39897-type-only-stats-hero.md) is a typographic statement. This pattern is the right pick when the dealer publishes real ongoing editorial content (seasonal field reports, customer-operation stories, regulatory-update explainers, Part 107 cohort dispatches) and needs the landing page to read as "we cover this beat" rather than "we sell this thing."

## What to take
- Top of the band: a single centered serif WORDMARK in the publication name ("Magazine" / "Field Notes" / "The Journal" / "[Dealer Name] Field Reports") at ~64–80px, deep brand-charcoal or deep-navy, on a light warm-neutral background (beige / cream / off-white). One word or a 2–3-word title; no tagline, no subhead beneath. The wordmark is the masthead — generous whitespace above and below (~80–120px). Optional small italic-serif tagline directly under in ~16px muted ("Stories from the field." / "What we're seeing this season.") — single line, centered, no period if it's a fragment.
- Directly below the masthead: ONE lead-article horizontal split row (~60–70vh, contained), no card chrome, no rounded corners on the photo, no shadow. Left column ~45–50% of the band width: a single tall-portrait photograph that runs nearly full band-height (cinematic crop, one human or one piece of equipment as subject — NOT a stock illustration, NOT a product render). Right column ~45–50%: vertically centered content block — a small all-caps category badge in a dark-fill pill (~12px, ~6px horizontal padding, "FIELD REPORT" / "REGULATORY" / "OPERATOR PROFILE") + headline in serif at ~36–48px in 2–3 lines + 2–3 line dek/summary in smaller serif at ~16–18px in muted charcoal + small date line ("May 20, 2026") in ~13px muted gray. No CTA button in the lead article — the whole article surface IS the affordance; clicking anywhere on the row navigates.
- Below the lead row, the page proceeds with named-section carousel / grid rows, each with a small serif section heading flush-left ("This Season" / "Insider Guides" / "From the Cohort") + a horizontally arranged 3–4 card row beneath (each card: 16:9 or 3:4 photograph on top + category badge + 2-line title + date — no body summary, no CTA). Sections are separated by generous whitespace (~80px) rather than rules.
- The navigation bar above the hero is intentionally restrained — small text links (Magazine sections / Account / Search) without a primary CTA pill. Letting the wordmark carry the page's identity means the nav stays out of its way.
- Typographic system: serif for masthead, lead headline, section headings, and card titles; smaller serif or modest sans-serif for body + dek + date stamps. ONE accent color, used only in category badges (dark-navy badge or deep-brand-charcoal pill — never the body text). The accent must NEVER spread into body type, dividers, or section backgrounds — its containment is what makes the masthead read as editorial rather than promotional.
- The whole hero is light-dominant — soft warm beige (~#FAF3E8) header section, white content rows below. No saturated brand color anywhere on the page surface; the brand identity lives in the masthead serif and the category badges only.

## What NOT to take
- Don't put a CTA button in the lead-article row. The pattern's affordance depends on the article BEING the CTA — adding a "Read more" button collapses the editorial register into a marketing landing and the article stops looking like a real article. If the dealer needs a primary action on the page, it goes in the nav bar (a small newsletter signup link) or beneath the article carousels (a "Subscribe to Field Notes" band) — not in the hero.
- Don't substitute illustrations or product renders for the lead-article photograph. The whole editorial credibility depends on the lead image being a REAL photograph of a real subject (a farmer mid-conversation, equipment in field use, a regulatory office, a Part 137 cohort moment). Stock illustrations, 3D renders, or generic product photography collapse the affordance — the page reads as marketing-pretending-to-be-magazine. If the dealer doesn't have real editorial photography, skip this pattern entirely and use the [manifesto-creed hero](./refero-b86571f2-manifesto-creed-hero.md) (which needs only one photograph + one quote) or the [hands-on craft hero](./refero-be842c1e-hands-on-craft-hero.md) instead.
- Don't substitute "fake articles" — the pattern presumes the dealer publishes real editorial content with real dates, real bylines, real ongoing cadence. A pattern showing 3 magazine cards that are actually product feature pages dressed as articles is dishonest; the customer notices instantly. Pre-flight: the dealer must have ≥6 real articles already published, with a stated cadence (e.g. "biweekly during spray season") and at least one named author or contributor.
- Don't import a saturated yellow navigation bar (the Expedia source has a bright yellow #FFD500 nav). The source's saturated nav is brand-specific to Expedia and pulls weight away from the masthead. For a Terraplex dealer the nav stays neutral (transparent or off-white background, charcoal text links, no fills).
- Don't add a hero search bar or input field inside the band. A search affordance turns the magazine landing into a knowledge-base / help-center page — different pattern (see [search-led help center](../../contact/research/refero-00cda269-search-led-help-center.md) for that register). The magazine landing is editorial browsing, not retrieval.
- Don't pair this pattern with the [form-in-hero overlay](./refero-7d15989d-form-in-hero-overlay.md) or the [interactive product-demo hero](./refero-d6176d7e-interactive-product-demo-hero.md) on the same page. The editorial register depends on the hero being a reading invitation; combining it with a lead-capture form or a live UI panel collapses both registers. These patterns are for different page types — pick one model per landing.
- The cream + warm-beige + light-editorial register directly conflicts with FORGE's dark-dominant ember-on-charcoal aesthetic and TACTICAL's monochrome-precision-no-warmth register. For a dark-archetype content surface, use the [topographic layered-bg hero](./refero-ae84b62a-topographic-layered-bg.md) (abstract dark) or the [hardware-lineup dark hero](./refero-0945a36c-hardware-lineup-dark-hero.md) (cinematic studio-sweep) as the landing surface — both dark heroes can lead into article rows beneath, but the warm-editorial register stays off the page.

## Adaptation notes (cross-archetype)
- FIELD: native fit. Soft warm beige hero (~#FAF3E8) + display-serif masthead in warm-charcoal ("Field Notes" / "The Growing Season"). Lead-article photograph is a real farmer or operator at golden-hour (the same photographic register as the [hands-on craft hero](./refero-be842c1e-hands-on-craft-hero.md), but framed as an article subject not a product user). Category badges in deep amber or warm-clay. Article rows below carry the same warm-photographic register. Pairs cleanly with the [editorial cream-bands product-block](../../product-block/research/refero-ecfaaa4f-editorial-cream-bands.md) BELOW the magazine landing — the cream-band rhythm in the product block extends the editorial cadence into the product surface on adjacent pages. See [FIELD archetype](../../../archetypes/field.md).
- PATRIOT: native fit. Cream header (~#FAF3E8) + deep-navy serif masthead ("The Field Journal" / "American Farm Notes"). Lead photograph is a real farmer or piece of heritage equipment in late-afternoon light (documentary, not staged — same register as [manifesto-creed hero](./refero-b86571f2-manifesto-creed-hero.md)). Category badges alternate deep-navy and barn-red across the carousel — 1 in 4 badges carries red (the PATRIOT flag-color-at-micro-scale rule). Article rows below stay heritage-editorial; no saturated background tiles, no flag motifs anywhere. Pairs naturally with the [manifesto-creed hero](./refero-b86571f2-manifesto-creed-hero.md) on the parent home page (manifesto for the ideology pre-frame; this for the editorial archive). See [PATRIOT archetype](../../../archetypes/patriot.md).
- CLEAN: works with a slight modernization. Replace the warm beige with a cool neutral (off-white ~#F9F9F9 or pale brand-tinted gray) and the deep-navy serif with a brand-charcoal modern-serif or geometric-sans. The editorial register is preserved but the heritage warmth lifts — the page reads as "knowledge hub" / "research notes" rather than "heritage magazine." Lead photograph is a clean operating environment (hangar, compliance office, flight-prep room) or a charted-data interior; category badges in brand-navy. Article rows below stay in the same neutral palette. Pairs with the [centered-screenshot-trio hero](./refero-f619e700-centered-screenshot-trio.md) on the parent product home (trio for the product surface; this for the knowledge / research surface). See [CLEAN archetype](../../../archetypes/clean.md).
- TERRAIN: works. The magazine landing is the page's opening "light band" of TERRAIN's alternating light/dark rhythm — the masthead + lead-article row sit on cream / warm-stone, and the section below this landing drops to a dark band with a topographic backdrop. Section headings ("This Season" / "From the Watershed") carry a thin contour-line motif beneath them (TERRAIN's signature divider). Category badges in clay or sage. Article rows stay light; the rhythm-shift happens at section scale, not within the magazine surface. Pairs cleanly with the [topographic layered-bg hero](./refero-ae84b62a-topographic-layered-bg.md) on adjacent pages where TERRAIN's dark-band moment leads. See [TERRAIN archetype](../../../archetypes/terrain.md).
- FORGE / TACTICAL: skip — the warm + light editorial register conflicts with both archetypes' dark-dominant + precision rules. A "Field Reports" content arm CAN exist for FORGE / TACTICAL dealers, but it uses a different surface: the [topographic layered-bg](./refero-ae84b62a-topographic-layered-bg.md) or [hardware-lineup dark hero](./refero-0945a36c-hardware-lineup-dark-hero.md) opens the landing on a dark band, and the article rows beneath sit on a dark-card grid (similar in shape to the [compatibility-ecosystem grid](../../product-block/research/refero-70415597-compatibility-ecosystem-grid.md) — same dark-grid cadence, swap product photographs for article thumbnails). The editorial register can live in either light-archetype or dark-archetype form; this specific note is the light-archetype form.

## Source brief
Expedia's [/magazine](https://www.expedia.com/magazine/) — the brand's editorial content hub home (article-led, no booking flow on the page). The library-worthy move is the structural cadence (centered serif masthead → one oversized lead-article photo+text split → named carousel rows of supporting articles → no CTAs anywhere in the hero band), reusable for a Terraplex dealer that publishes real ongoing editorial content (seasonal field reports, customer-operation case stories, regulatory updates from [content/compliance-program.md](../../../content/compliance-program.md), Part 107 cohort dispatches, drone-industry watch). The pattern presumes a real editorial cadence — pre-flight the dealer must have ≥6 published articles with named contributors and a stated update rhythm; otherwise skip this pattern and use a single-shot manifesto or operator-photograph hero instead.
