## Why this is library-worthy
A "manifesto hero" sequence: one centered all-caps declarative headline → full-bleed warm-lit photograph that visually proves the claim → a large pull-quote with a real portrait+attribution underneath. The whole top of the page reads as a creed before any product or pricing shows up — a fit for archetypes that need ideological gravitas without resorting to flag motifs or stock patriotism. PATRIOT especially benefits because the structure carries the weight, so the chrome can stay restrained.

## What to take
- Open the page with a centered, all-caps, two-line declarative headline that ends in a period (not a question, not a CTA). The headline is the thesis, not a hook. Use a heavy sans-serif at ~52–64px, charcoal on cream / black on white.
- Directly below the headline, one full-bleed photograph (no padding rails) — warm-lit, human-in-action, taken at golden-hour or with naturally warm tones. Silhouettes work; faces close-up also work. The image must visually argue for the headline's claim, not decorate it.
- Below the photo, a large left-aligned pull quote (~24–28px italic-or-roman) introduced by an oversized opening quotation mark, paired with a small attribution block on the right: portrait headshot (~120px circle or square), name in bold caps, role/title in smaller all-caps muted text. The quote is the credibility move — a real person, a real claim.
- After the manifesto sequence, transition into category cards: 2–3 photo-on-top cards in a single row, each with a small category-tag chip in the top-left ("PEOPLE", "PLANET" in the source; "FARMERS", "LAND" for a Terraplex dealer), a bold caption at the bottom-left, and a "+" plus-icon affordance bottom-right. Cards are equal-height with consistent corner radius (~8px). The category-tag chip is the only place a single accent color shows up.
- Vertical rhythm is generous (~80–120px between bands) and section breaks come from white space, not rules. No gradients, no glow, no animation.

## What NOT to take
- Don't use the manifesto pattern if the dealer doesn't have a real quote from a real person. A fabricated or stock quote collapses the whole structure — the credibility comes from "Bjorn Gulden, CEO" being identifiable. For a Terraplex dealer this means a real farmer testimonial or owner quote, with a real headshot.
- Don't add a CTA button inside the manifesto band. The first CTA appears AFTER the category cards. The opening sequence is meant to be read, not clicked.
- Don't import the literal sport / soccer photography — it's adidas-specific. The transferable move is "warm-lit human-in-action photograph that argues for the headline." For a Terraplex dealer that's farmers walking a field at sunset, an operator launching a drone over corn rows, or a hands-on-equipment shot with golden light.
- The full-bleed photo + heavy headline + dark video would conflict with FORGE/TACTICAL's dark-dominant aesthetic — a manifesto-style headline on a black hero exists, but it's a different pattern (see the [topographic layered-bg note](./refero-ae84b62a-topographic-layered-bg.md) for the dark-archetype hero option).
- Don't crowd the category-tag chip with multiple accent colors. One accent only — the whole point is the manifesto isn't competing with chrome.

## Adaptation notes (cross-archetype)
- PATRIOT: native fit. Cream or warm-off-white background, deep navy headline, full-bleed photograph in warm late-afternoon light (American farmland, harvest equipment, weathered hands). Quote attribution block uses a single navy or barn-red accent on the role line. Category-tag chips alternate navy and red across the row (e.g. "FARMERS" navy, "STEWARDSHIP" red, "HERITAGE" navy) — that's the PATRIOT restraint move: the flag colors live in micro-chrome, not banners.
- FIELD: same structure, swap navy for warm amber/clay accents. The headline can be a farm-poetic sentence rather than a declarative thesis. Photo stays warm-lit.
- TERRAIN: works as the opening "light band" of the alternating light/dark rhythm — the manifesto sequence is the light band, then the next section drops to a dark band with a topographic backdrop. Single accent matches the archetype's clay/sage choice.
- CLEAN: works but loses some of the heritage feel. Use a brand-tinted neutral (gray-50 or pale brand color) instead of cream, and keep typography modern-sans rather than serif-leaning. The pull-quote becomes more boardroom than barn.
