## Why this is library-worthy
A full-bleed autoplay-video hero where the affordance is the **motion itself** — the headline + small eyebrow tag + circular scroll-down arrow sit centered over a slowly-panning aerial or environmental video loop, and there is no other CTA in the band. The video carries the "we operate at this scale, in this place" claim that a still photograph can only suggest. Different from existing hero patterns: the [hands-on craft hero](./refero-be842c1e-hands-on-craft-hero.md), [manifesto-creed hero](./refero-b86571f2-manifesto-creed-hero.md), and [hardware-lineup dark hero](./refero-0945a36c-hardware-lineup-dark-hero.md) all use still photography by design; the [topographic layered-bg](./refero-ae84b62a-topographic-layered-bg.md) explicitly rejects autoplay; the [form-in-hero overlay](./refero-7d15989d-form-in-hero-overlay.md) and [centered-screenshot-trio](./refero-f619e700-centered-screenshot-trio.md) are still-image-led. None of the existing patterns cover a video-led hero, and for a Terraplex dealer the motion register is the right pick when the dealer wants to show **the act of using the equipment** (drone in flight, spray pattern over crop rows, operator commanding from the field) rather than the equipment as object.

## What to take
- One full-bleed video occupies 60–75vh of the hero band — autoplay, muted, looping, no controls visible. Video footage is environmental and slow: aerial drone over a field, water surface from above, golden-hour landscape pan, equipment in slow motion. Camera movement is gentle (parallax-on-scroll at ~10% translateY across the band, never more) — the slowness is what keeps the loop watchable rather than busy.
- Subtle dark overlay (~rgba(0,0,0,0.25)) on the entire video so the centered text remains legible across all video frames. Without the overlay, the white headline strobes against bright frames and disappears against dark frames.
- Centered text stack on the video, vertical position ~50%:
  1. Tiny all-caps eyebrow (~10–12px, ~3px letter-spacing, weight 600–700) — single short label naming the page or campaign ("ALL JOBS" → for a dealer "ABOUT US" / "OUR TERRITORY" / "WHAT WE DO").
  2. Below the eyebrow (~16px gap), one or two lines of oversized display headline. ~72–120px on desktop, weight 800–900, tight tracking (~ -1px to -2px), line-height 0.9–1.0. Centered. Heavy condensed display family (Cooper Black / Druk / similar) — the headline has to be heavy enough to hold its own against the moving video.
  3. ~32px below the headline, a circular scroll-down arrow indicator (~48–56px diameter, white circle, thin border, animated pulse loop scale 1.0 → 1.08 → 1.0 over ~1.6s ease-in-out). The arrow IS the CTA — clicking it scrolls to the next section.
- No filled CTA pill in the band. No secondary outline pill. No "Get started" / "Learn more" buttons. The hero's job is to be watched, not clicked. The first conversion CTA appears in the section below.
- Top navigation stays minimal and overlaid on the video (transparent background, white text, single hover-state). Logo at top-left, 4–5 nav links at top-center, single utility link top-right (no buttons, no badges).
- Mobile collapse: video continues to autoplay at lower bitrate (~480p, ~24fps) with the same overlay. Headline drops to ~48–64px to remain readable on small viewports. Scroll arrow stays centered.

## What NOT to take
- Don't add a CTA button to the hero band. The whole pattern depends on the video being uninterrupted by chrome — adding a "Watch reel" or "Get started" pill collapses the affordance into a generic SaaS hero with a video background. The scroll arrow is the only interactive affordance.
- Don't use stock footage. The pattern's credibility is "this is OUR territory, OUR equipment, OUR operators" — stock alpine drone footage or generic stock farm-aerial shots collapse the pattern into a "looks like a drone company" template. For a Terraplex dealer the video must be defensible — the dealer's own field, the dealer's own R-32 in flight over the dealer's own region.
- Don't make the video busy. The pattern relies on slow, contemplative motion — fast-cut highlight reels, jump-cuts between multiple scenes, or motion-graphics overlays turn the hero into a promotional reel and the headline becomes unreadable. Single sustained shot, ~7–15 seconds before loop, slow camera move.
- Don't import the literal Manychat headline weight (Cooper Black-style condensed display) without considering if it suits the archetype. The display weight is the affordance for FORGE / TACTICAL / TERRAIN; FIELD and CLEAN want more restrained display sans (see Adaptation notes for per-archetype typography).
- Don't add audio. The video must be muted by default — autoplay with sound is blocked on most browsers, and even when allowed, an unsolicited audio loop is the fastest way to lose a visitor. If the dealer wants sound, place a separate "watch with sound" video card below the hero (see [hardware-lineup dark hero](./refero-0945a36c-hardware-lineup-dark-hero.md) body partners).
- Don't autoplay video with motion-sickness triggers (rapid camera spins, dolly-in/dolly-out, drone descents). Slow horizontal pan or static shot only. Respect [`prefers-reduced-motion`](https://developer.mozilla.org/en-US/docs/Web/CSS/@media/prefers-reduced-motion) — fall back to a still-frame poster image when set.
- Don't combine this with the [form-in-hero overlay](./refero-7d15989d-form-in-hero-overlay.md) on the same page. Both occupy the hero slot; this one defers conversion, the form-in-hero collapses it. Pick by brief: "watch us, then scroll" vs "fill the form right now."

## Adaptation notes (cross-archetype)
- FORGE: native fit. Video is golden-hour or dusk drone footage over a field — rim-light on the horizon, ember tones in the sky. Headline in heavy condensed display (Druk / Cooper Black-derivative) at ~96–120px, white. Eyebrow in muted ember (~#FF8533). Scroll arrow in white. The dark overlay leans warm (~rgba(20,8,0,0.30)) so the video reads ember-lit even in mid-afternoon footage. See [FORGE archetype](../../../archetypes/forge.md). Pairs with the [hardware-lineup dark hero](./refero-0945a36c-hardware-lineup-dark-hero.md) on a sibling page (this hero introduces the dealer's operations register; the hardware-lineup introduces the product family).
- TACTICAL: native fit with cooler footage. Video is studio-shot equipment in motion (controller close-up with hands, rotor spinning in slow-mo, mission-plan UI scrolling) — not environmental. Daylight-temperature white balance, no warmth. Headline in tracked all-caps precision-sans (~72–96px) — heavier display weight conflicts with TACTICAL's restraint. Eyebrow in tracked steel-cyan. Scroll arrow as a sharp-cornered square (~6px corner radius), not a circle. See [TACTICAL archetype](../../../archetypes/tactical.md).
- TERRAIN: video is aerial-over-farmland with visible field boundaries (TERRAIN's signature land-mapped vocabulary at full motion). Slow oblique pan. Overlay lifts to ~rgba(40,30,20,0.25) so the dark cast warms toward clay. Headline in display sans (~72–96px) with tighter tracking. Eyebrow in clay or sage accent. Scroll arrow circle has a thin contour-line motif as its border instead of a solid stroke. The hero band serves as the page's first "dark band"; the next section drops to a cream "light band" with a topographic divider above the heading. See [TERRAIN archetype](../../../archetypes/terrain.md).
- CLEAN: lighten the overlay to ~rgba(0,0,0,0.15) so the video reads clearer. Footage is clean equipment-in-context (drone in clear blue sky, hangar interior with daylight, prep room with monitors). Headline in modern display sans (~64–80px) — keep the display heavy but trim the tracking. Eyebrow in brand-navy. Scroll arrow in white with a thin brand-navy ring (single accent moment). See [CLEAN archetype](../../../archetypes/clean.md). Pairs with the [centered-screenshot-trio](./refero-f619e700-centered-screenshot-trio.md) on a sibling page where the brief is product-led rather than operations-led.
- FIELD: warm-photographic register applies to video too — golden-hour aerial-over-cornfield, slow horizontal pan, real farmer or operator visible at small scale somewhere in the frame. Overlay warms to ~rgba(60,40,20,0.25). Headline in display sans (~64–80px) with looser tracking — heritage register, not modern. Eyebrow in muted amber. Scroll arrow circle has a torn-cream-paper border edge instead of a hard stroke. See [FIELD archetype](../../../archetypes/field.md). Pairs with the [hands-on craft hero](./refero-be842c1e-hands-on-craft-hero.md) on a sibling page where the brief is human-anchored craft rather than aerial-scale operations.
- PATRIOT: video is late-afternoon American-farmland aerial — golden hour light, real farmland with crop boundaries visible, no stock alpine landscapes. Overlay leans warm (~rgba(40,20,15,0.25)). Headline in display sans at ~64–80px in cream over the warm overlay. Eyebrow in single deep-navy or barn-red (1-in-3 pages alternates the eyebrow accent across the dealer site for restraint). Scroll arrow in cream. Do NOT add motion-graphics flag overlays, stripe transitions, or star particles — the aerial-American-farmland imagery IS the patriotic register; chrome flags would compete. See [PATRIOT archetype](../../../archetypes/patriot.md). Pairs with the [manifesto-creed hero](./refero-b86571f2-manifesto-creed-hero.md) on a sibling page.

## Source brief
ManyChat's [/culture](https://careers.manychat.com/culture) careers culture page — a careers/employer-brand landing whose hero pattern is reusable far beyond careers. The library-worthy move is the structural cadence (full-bleed autoplay video → centered eyebrow tag → oversized display headline → circular animated scroll-down arrow → no CTA pill in the band), reusable for a Terraplex dealer when the brief is "show what we do at scale" rather than "give us your contact info." The pattern's restraint (single video, no chrome, no buttons) is what makes it transferable across all 6 archetypes via per-archetype palette and footage choices — the structural cadence stays identical; only the video subject and typographic register adapt.
