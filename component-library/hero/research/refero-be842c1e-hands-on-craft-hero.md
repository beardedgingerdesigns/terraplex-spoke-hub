## Why this is library-worthy
A hero pattern that opens with one full-bleed warm-interior-light photograph of an adult **mid-use** with the product — hands engaged, body angled toward the equipment, focused expression — rather than the product staged as a hero render or in mid-flight as a beauty shot. The "craft-in-progress" register reframes the product from "the thing you'll buy" to "the thing you'll do," which is the conversion move when the customer hesitation is "can I actually use this myself?" rather than "is this the right spec?" Distinct from existing hero patterns: the [centered-screenshot-trio](./refero-f619e700-centered-screenshot-trio.md) shows product UI surfaces; the [manifesto-creed hero](./refero-b86571f2-manifesto-creed-hero.md) leads with ideology; the [hardware-lineup dark hero](./refero-0945a36c-hardware-lineup-dark-hero.md) shows a family of products on a dark sweep; the [topographic layered-bg](./refero-ae84b62a-topographic-layered-bg.md) is an abstract dark backdrop. None show an operator mid-use. This pattern fills that gap.

## What to take
- One full-bleed photograph (~50–60vh, edge-to-edge, no padding rails) of a single adult engaged with the product. The product is in their hands or on the workbench in front of them, not hovering / flying / styled. Lighting is warm-interior (afternoon-light-through-window, workbench-task-lamp, golden-hour-on-porch) — natural, not studio. Composition is environmental: visible workbench surface, hand tools, a pen/notebook, a mug — the working context shows up around the product.
- The adult faces the product, NOT the camera. Eye contact with camera collapses the affordance into a stock-photo-of-an-operator pose; eye contact with the equipment reads as in-progress concentration. The model's age and body language matter: a 30–60-year-old with focused posture and hands engaged, not a 20-something model in a clean apron.
- Above the photograph (or floating in the upper third of it on a small white pill chip): a small breadcrumb-style category tag ("Architecture" / "[product family]") + 2 small filled CTAs ("Products / Discover") — the navigation chips are tiny relative to the photograph, never competing with it.
- Below the hero photograph, a single full-width sentence-case headline (~40–48px) + 2–3 line paragraph in muted charcoal — the prose is the explanation; the photograph already carried the affordance.
- Below the headline, a small monochrome tag-chip row showing product family or sub-categories (single line, centered, ~14px, ~6 chips max). The chips are navigation aids, not feature claims.
- After the hero band, the page drops into a series of 3-up product / category cards — each card is its own small hero photograph (real product, similar warm-interior light register), title overlay, and play-button if the card carries video. The hero's craft-in-progress register propagates through the card photography.

## What NOT to take
- Don't substitute a beauty shot of the product alone. The pattern's whole affordance depends on a human IN the frame, hands engaged. A drone-on-a-pedestal photograph collapses into a generic single-product hero (covered structurally by the [hardware-lineup dark hero](./refero-0945a36c-hardware-lineup-dark-hero.md) on dark or by a centered-product-render hero on light — neither carries this pattern's "you can do this too" register).
- Don't import the literal LEGO desk-build photograph — that's product-specific to small-scale hobby modeling. The transferable move is "real adult, hands engaged, warm-interior light, product mid-use." For a Terraplex dealer this is an operator on a controller in the field at golden hour, hands inspecting a rotor on a workbench under a task lamp, or a farmer at a kitchen table reviewing a mission-plan printout — NOT the drone in mid-flight, NOT the operator posed for the camera, NOT a stock-photo handshake.
- Don't use a young model with a styled aesthetic (clean apron, perfect manicure, fashion lighting). The pattern reads as "regular person doing regular work" — the fashion register collapses the credibility.
- Don't add eye-contact-with-camera. The model's gaze MUST be on the equipment, not on the viewer. Eye contact is the difference between "I'm working" and "I'm a stock photo."
- Don't add a video autoplay loop on the hero photograph. The photograph's stillness is the affordance — a looping video shifts the register from "captured moment" to "promotional reel." If the dealer needs video, place it in the 3-up cards beneath the hero (each card with a play button) — not in the hero itself.
- The warm-interior-light, real-person-mid-use register conflicts directly with FORGE's dark-dominant ember-on-charcoal aesthetic and TACTICAL's monochrome-precision-no-environmental-context register. For dark archetypes use the [hardware-lineup dark hero](./refero-0945a36c-hardware-lineup-dark-hero.md) (cinematic studio-sweep) or [topographic layered-bg](./refero-ae84b62a-topographic-layered-bg.md) (abstract dark) instead.

## Adaptation notes (cross-archetype)
- FIELD: native fit. Photograph is a real farmer or operator at golden hour, hands on a controller or inspecting a rotor on a workbench, warm task-lamp light from one side. Cream / warm-off-white background below the photograph; warm-charcoal headline. Tag chips in muted-amber. The 3-up cards below carry the same warm-photographic register (operator-and-equipment shots, no studio chrome). Pairs naturally with the [editorial cream-bands product-block](../../product-block/research/refero-ecfaaa4f-editorial-cream-bands.md) below for a coherent FIELD page rhythm. See [FIELD archetype](../../../archetypes/field.md).
- PATRIOT: native fit. Photograph is a real farmer in late-afternoon American-farmland light — hands on a piece of equipment, focused on the work, wearing a workshirt and a ball cap. The image must be documentary, not staged — a candid late-afternoon shot reads as heritage; a styled "farmer with flag in background" shot reads as kitsch. Cream background below the photograph; deep-navy headline. Tag chips alternate deep-navy and barn-red across the row (1 in 3 chips carries red). Pairs with the [manifesto-creed hero](./refero-b86571f2-manifesto-creed-hero.md) on a sibling page or with the [editorial cream-bands product-block](../../product-block/research/refero-ecfaaa4f-editorial-cream-bands.md) below. See [PATRIOT archetype](../../../archetypes/patriot.md).
- TERRAIN: the hero photograph is the page's opening "light band." Below the photograph, the 3-up cards carry a small contour-line motif at the bottom of each card as a topographic divider. The next section below the cards drops to the page's first dark band with a topographic backdrop. The hero photograph itself stays warm-interior-light — TERRAIN's alternation is at section scale, not within the hero. See [TERRAIN archetype](../../../archetypes/terrain.md). Sibling: [topographic layered-bg](./refero-ae84b62a-topographic-layered-bg.md) for the matching dark band on adjacent pages.
- CLEAN: works but loses some of the "craft" warmth. Use a neutral-light background below the hero photograph (off-white or pale gray) instead of warm cream, and modernize the photographic register slightly — the operator can be in a cleaner environment (a hangar, a compliance office, a flight-prep room) with daylight rather than task-lamp warmth. Headline in brand-charcoal modern-sans. Tag chips in brand-navy. The pattern works in CLEAN when the dealer wants to surface "human credibility" without committing to the heritage / hand-feel register that FIELD and PATRIOT use this pattern for. See [CLEAN archetype](../../../archetypes/clean.md). Pairs with the [centered-screenshot-trio hero](./refero-f619e700-centered-screenshot-trio.md) on a sibling page (this pattern is the human-anchored opener; the centered-screenshot-trio is the product-anchored opener).
- FORGE / TACTICAL: skip — the warm-interior, real-person-mid-use register conflicts with both archetypes' dark-dominant + studio-precision rules. For dark archetypes use the [hardware-lineup dark hero](./refero-0945a36c-hardware-lineup-dark-hero.md) (cinematic studio-sweep with hardware lineup) or [topographic layered-bg](./refero-ae84b62a-topographic-layered-bg.md) (abstract dark backdrop) instead.

## Source brief
LEGO's [/themes/architecture/about](https://www.lego.com/en-us/themes/architecture/about) page — the LEGO Architecture brand-and-collections landing. The library-worthy move is the opening hero photograph (adult mid-build at a desk, warm-interior light, hands engaged, focused on the model — not on the camera) followed by 3-up category cards in the same warm-photographic register. Reusable for a Terraplex dealer when the customer's hesitation is "can I actually fly / maintain this myself?" — the photograph addresses that hesitation by showing a regular adult mid-use rather than a styled operator beauty shot. Pairs cleanly with the [manifesto-creed hero](./refero-b86571f2-manifesto-creed-hero.md) on a sibling page where ideology leads (this hero leads with hands-on credibility); both work as openings on different page types within the same dealer site.
