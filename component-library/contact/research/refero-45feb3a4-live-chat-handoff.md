## Why this is library-worthy
A **live-chat-handoff** contact pattern: a single page whose entire purpose is to launch the user into a real-human chat session, no form, no fields. Centered hero asks "Looking to connect with our [team]?", body paragraph explicitly states business hours ("Monday to Friday, 9 a.m. to 5 p.m. EST"), single brand-color CTA reads "Launch chat" — and below the fold a 2-column reassurance band addresses the two failure modes ("Can't chat right now? No worries!" + "Outside our working hours?"), each column with email-fallback explanation. A floating chat-bubble launcher in the lower-right corner is the persistent fallback. Distinct from the existing [AI assistant chat](./refero-de02c274-ai-assistant-help.md), which is an automated bot with example-question quick-pick chips — this pattern is **human agents during stated hours**, where the credibility move is the explicit hours-of-availability claim and the "what if we're not available right now" reassurance. Distinct from the [calendar-led rep card](./refero-701496e7-calendar-led-rep-card.md), which schedules a future appointment with a named rep — this pattern is "talk to someone NOW (or know exactly when you can)." Closes a real gap: the existing 12 contact patterns route by intent (decision-pivot, directory, tile-grid, typographic), capture by form (form-with-topic-checkboxes, catalog-request, split-screen-trust-curtain), or self-serve (search-led help center, AI assistant). None of them say "talk to a human, right now, here's when we're available."

## What to take
- **Single-purpose page**: the entire above-the-fold composition is one centered question + one paragraph + one CTA. No nav-rail, no sub-options, no form. The page has exactly one job and the layout proves it.
- **Centered question headline** (~48–56px display weight): phrased as a question, not a statement ("Looking to connect with our Sales team?" / "Want to talk to one of our agronomists?" / "Need to reach a Part 137 specialist?"). Question-form headlines pre-frame the CTA — the user reads the headline as "yes, I am" and clicks.
- **Body paragraph with explicit hours**: 2–3 lines of plain language explaining what the CTA does and **when the team is available**. Hours-of-availability is non-optional — "they're on standby Monday to Friday, 9 a.m. to 5 p.m. EST" makes the page truthful and sets the expectation. Without explicit hours the pattern degrades into a generic chat widget.
- **Single brand-color CTA**: filled pill button, brand color, sized large enough to be the only affordance (~140–180px wide). Label is action-verbal ("Launch chat" / "Start chat" / "Talk now") — not "Submit" or "Continue" or any form-language. One CTA. No secondary "Or fill out a form" affordance above the fold; that demotes the chat handoff.
- **Two-column reassurance band** below the fold: equal-width 2-column grid addressing the two failure modes. Left column: "Can't chat right now? No worries!" → 2–3-line explanation of email-reminder fallback ("we'll drop you an email reminder; just let your live chat agent know"). Right column: "Outside of our working hours?" → 2–3-line explanation of asynchronous catch-up ("leave us a message through chat above and we'll catch up with you when we're back"). Both columns lead with the user's worry, then resolve it.
- **Floating chat-bubble launcher** persists in the lower-right corner: same brand color as the hero CTA, ~48px circular, single chat-glyph icon. The launcher is the redundant affordance — if the user scrolls past the hero or lands on a sub-page, the bubble is always there. The hero CTA and the bubble launcher trigger the same chat session.
- **Generous whitespace**: the hero band reserves ~50% of the viewport for headline + paragraph + CTA, with ~120–160px of vertical breathing room above and below. Restraint is the credibility move — a busy page reads as "we're trying to deflect you to a form"; an empty page with one button reads as "we're confident enough to put a human on the other end."
- **Footer band** (optional): below the reassurance row, a thin row of secondary nav (Pricing / Help / Press / Jobs) keeps the page from feeling like an orphaned dead-end. Style as low-emphasis text-only links — they're not affordances, they're orientation.

## What NOT to take
- **Don't replace the live-chat handoff with an AI bot.** The pattern's credibility depends on "you'll get a human." If the dealer doesn't have human agents during stated hours, route to the [AI assistant chat](./refero-de02c274-ai-assistant-help.md) pattern instead — those are distinct moves, not interchangeable. Mislabeling a bot as a human agent breaks trust faster than honesty.
- **Don't omit the explicit hours-of-availability.** Without "Monday-Friday, 9-5 EST," the CTA reads as a generic always-on chat widget that may or may not be staffed. Explicit hours make the claim falsifiable, which is what makes it credible.
- **Don't add a form above the fold.** Even a small "leave us a message" form demotes the chat handoff — the user reads "they're going to make me fill out a form anyway." If a form fallback is needed, it lives inside the chat session itself ("Outside hours? Type your message here") or on a sibling [form-with-topic-checkboxes page](./refero-23938dfd-form-with-topic-checkboxes.md).
- **Don't use a sales-language CTA**. "Schedule a demo" or "Talk to sales" both pre-frame the conversation as a sales pitch. "Launch chat" / "Start chat" / "Talk now" keep the CTA neutral so non-sales inquiries (support, parts, compliance) read as welcome too. If the dealer specifically wants sales pre-qualification, use the [calendar-led rep card](./refero-701496e7-calendar-led-rep-card.md) instead.
- **Don't pair with a sticky "or call us at +1-800-XXX-XXXX" hotline number** above the fold. Two synchronous-contact options halve the conversion to either; pick chat OR phone for the page. Phone-as-fallback belongs in the reassurance band, not the hero.
- **Don't import Zapier's specific brand-orange or "9 a.m. to 5 p.m. EST" copy.** The brand color travels via per-archetype palette; the specific hours are dealer-set. If the dealer's hours are unusual ("Tue-Sat, 6 a.m. to 2 p.m. PT during spray season"), keep the explicit-hours rule and just say so honestly.

## Adaptation notes (cross-archetype)
- **CLEAN**: native fit. White background, charcoal text, single brand-blue or brand-green CTA. The reassurance band columns use a thin gray hairline to separate without adding chrome. Pairs naturally with the [search-led help center](./refero-00cda269-search-led-help-center.md) on a sibling tab — self-serve first, live chat second. See [CLEAN archetype](../../../archetypes/clean.md).
- **TERRAIN**: cream-beige background, deep terrain-green CTA, charcoal text. The reassurance band's column-separator picks up a subtle topographic-line micro-rule. Works as a calm light moment in TERRAIN's alternating rhythm — the page is intentionally quiet, which fits TERRAIN's measured register. See [TERRAIN archetype](../../../archetypes/terrain.md).
- **FIELD**: cream background (#F4F1EC), deep-charcoal text, single warm-amber CTA. The hero question headline picks up a serif display weight (e.g. cookbook-heritage feel) instead of a bold sans, which reads as "real conversation, not enterprise sales handoff." Pairs with FIELD's photographic register elsewhere; this contact page stays type-only as a deliberate restraint moment. See [FIELD archetype](../../../archetypes/field.md).
- **PATRIOT**: cream off-white background, deep-navy text, single barn-red CTA. The hero headline can pick up a small American-flag glyph (~16px) inline before the question if the dealer's PATRIOT register leans on flag motifs elsewhere; otherwise stay type-only. Reassurance band columns use deep-navy hairline rules. See [PATRIOT archetype](../../../archetypes/patriot.md).
- **FORGE**: invert to a dark background (#1A1A1A or similar). Off-white text, ember-orange CTA. The floating chat-bubble launcher picks up a faint glow-blob behind it (FORGE's signature ember radiance) at low saturation. The reassurance band columns use a faint warm-gray hairline rule. The hero question headline is in display-weight off-white; body paragraph in muted warm-gray. Pairs with the [emergency-triage contact page](./refero-56a7035b-emergency-triage-contact.md) for the two FORGE-native contact registers — emergency for spray-day blockers, live-chat-handoff for normal-hours sales/support. See [FORGE archetype](../../../archetypes/forge.md).
- **TACTICAL**: pure black background, off-white text, single steel or cyan CTA. Tighter geometry — CTA is sharper rectangle (~6px corner radius, not pill). Hero headline in precision-sans heavy weight. The reassurance band columns separate via a 1px crisp white hairline. The floating launcher is sharper-edged (square-with-rounded-corners, ~8px radius) rather than fully circular. See [TACTICAL archetype](../../../archetypes/tactical.md).

## Source brief
[Zapier's contact-sales confirmation](https://zapier.com/l/contact-sales-confirmation-chat) page — the post-form-submit "now talk to a human" handoff page, repurposed here as a standalone live-chat contact pattern. The structure (single question + paragraph with hours + one CTA + reassurance band addressing both failure modes + floating launcher fallback) translates directly to a Terraplex dealer contact page where a real sales rep or [Sam Welton-style compliance specialist](../../../content/compliance-program.md) is available during posted hours. The pattern is the right pick when the dealer's differentiation is "you'll get a real human, not a form ticket" — common for [Part 137 compliance prep](../../../research/manufacturers/terraplex.md) and high-stakes operational support that benefits from synchronous conversation.
