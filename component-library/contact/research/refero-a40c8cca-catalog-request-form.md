## Why this is library-worthy
A single-purpose contact pattern: not "contact us about anything" but "request the [thing]" — here a printed catalog mailer. The page collapses to a centered serif title + 1-paragraph instructional text + minimal form (address-collection only, no message field, no purpose dropdown) + a single primary CTA. The structural restraint is the affordance: the form's only job is to collect a shipping address so the dealer can mail something physical. Different from the existing 6 contact patterns: [two-card decision pivot](./refero-376ca585-two-card-decision-pivot.md) routes between sales and support; [form-with-topic-checkboxes](./refero-23938dfd-form-with-topic-checkboxes.md) frames the form as conversation-prep; [dealer-locator map](./refero-71707203-dealer-locator-map.md) routes by location; [calendar-led rep card](./refero-701496e7-calendar-led-rep-card.md) routes by time; [search-led help center](./refero-00cda269-search-led-help-center.md) defers contact to a fallback; [split-screen trust curtain](./refero-7c41e8a8-split-screen-trust-curtain.md) pre-frames credibility before the form. None of those handle "give me your address so we can mail you a thing" — this pattern is the right pick when the dealer offers physical brochures, printed sell-sheets, R-32 / I-19 spec PDFs as mailed printouts, operator-training catalogs, or compliance-program packets that ship by mail.

## What to take
- Centered single-column page with generous top margin (~120px from the site nav). No hero band — just the form floating on the page background.
- Centered display title at the top: ~36–48px serif (or display sans per archetype), all-caps, wide letter-spacing (~3–4px), medium weight ("REQUEST A CATALOG" → for a dealer "REQUEST OUR PRODUCT BROCHURE" / "REQUEST OUR R-32 SPEC PACK" / "REQUEST OUR PART 137 PROGRAM PACKET"). The all-caps + wide-tracking signals "official document," which sets up the printed-material register.
- Below the title, a short instructional paragraph (~14–15px serif, regular weight, line-height ~1.5, max ~480px width centered, justified or center-aligned). Two specific moves:
  1. **Thank-you opening** — "Thank you for your interest in our catalog!" — frames the user as already-engaged, not a stranger. Sets the conversational tone.
  2. **Explicit scope-limit disclaimer** — "Please include your first and last name along with your full address. We can only mail catalogs within the US. This does not include US territories or APO/FPO/DPO." The disclaimer earns the dealer trust by being specific about what they can't do, instead of letting the user fill out the form and find out later. For a Terraplex dealer this becomes "We can only mail printed packets within [state] and adjacent counties" or "Brochures ship by USPS Media Mail; allow 7–10 business days." The honesty is the conversion move.
- Form layout is a strict single column at ~480px width centered, with paired fields side-by-side where natural:
  1. First Name * + Last Name * (paired, ~50/50)
  2. Email Address (single, full width)
  3. Address 1 * (single, full width)
  4. Address 2 (single, full width, optional)
  5. City * + State/Province * dropdown (paired, ~50/50)
  6. Zip/Postal Code * + Country dropdown (paired, ~50/50)
- Field labels in tracked all-caps muted gray (~12px, ~1.5px letter-spacing) sitting *above* the input. Required asterisks in a single muted color (not red — keeps the documentation register). Inputs have light hairline borders (~1px ~#D4D4D4) with ~12px internal padding, ~40–44px tall.
- Below the address fields, a single small checkbox: "Sign Up For Our Newsletter" — un-checked by default. Tiny (~14px label), no marketing copy around it. The checkbox is the secondary conversion; the primary conversion is the catalog request.
- Single full-width primary CTA at the bottom: solid black (or per-archetype) pill, ~44px tall, centered uppercase label "SUBMIT" (or for a dealer "SEND ME THE BROCHURE"). One CTA only — no secondary "cancel" or "back."
- Footer / supporting nav lives below the form with normal site footer treatment — the form page doesn't carry hero photography, sidebar chrome, or related-content cards. The form IS the page.

## What NOT to take
- Don't add a message field, a "tell us about your project" textarea, or a "purpose" dropdown. The pattern's affordance is *single-purpose conversion* — adding free-text inputs collapses the page into a generic contact form (covered structurally by [form-with-topic-checkboxes](./refero-23938dfd-form-with-topic-checkboxes.md) and [calendar-led rep card](./refero-701496e7-calendar-led-rep-card.md) for richer-conversation use cases). If the dealer needs a real conversation, link to one of those patterns *after* the catalog ships.
- Don't soften the scope-limit disclaimer. The disclaimer's whole job is honesty — replacing "We can only mail catalogs within the US" with "Currently available in select regions" collapses the trust move. The user respects specific friction more than vague availability.
- Don't pre-check the newsletter opt-in checkbox. Pre-checked opt-ins violate most data-protection regulations (GDPR, CCPA) and erode trust. Default to un-checked; let the user opt in deliberately.
- Don't import the literal serif "REQUEST A CATALOG" display weight into FORGE / TACTICAL — the all-caps wide-tracking serif reads heritage / lifestyle. Per-archetype the typography adapts (see Adaptation notes), but the structural cadence (centered title + 1-paragraph honest instruction + minimal address form + single primary CTA + un-checked newsletter opt-in) stays identical.
- Don't add a "Thank you" success state that appears in-place after submit. The user expects a confirmation page (or a small success modal with the name of the catalog being shipped + estimated delivery date + "Subscribe to email updates" cross-sell). In-place success states leave the form looking suspicious — "did it submit?"
- Don't use this pattern when the dealer doesn't actually have something physical to mail. The page's whole credibility move is "we will mail you a printed thing." A dealer who doesn't ship anything physical and uses this pattern as a euphemism for "PDF download" collapses the affordance — the user opens their mail expecting a printed packet and finds an email instead. If the dealer offers PDF downloads, use the [split-screen trust curtain](./refero-7c41e8a8-split-screen-trust-curtain.md) with download CTAs instead.
- Don't add a hero photograph above the title. The form's restraint is the affordance — adding a hero photo of a printed catalog mockup turns the page into a marketing-led opt-in, which collapses the documentation-request register.

## Adaptation notes (cross-archetype)
- FIELD: cream background (~#F4EFE7), warm-charcoal serif title in ~36–44px wide-tracked all-caps. Instructional paragraph in warm-charcoal serif. Form input borders in warm-cream hairline (~#D4C8B8). Submit pill in deep amber with cream label. Newsletter checkbox uses a warm-amber filled-square check style. Pairs naturally with the dealer's [printed R-32 sell-sheet](../../../products/r-32.md) and [printed I-19 sell-sheet](../../../products/i-19.md) as the catalog being mailed. See [FIELD archetype](../../../archetypes/field.md). Pair with [manifesto-creed hero](../../hero/research/refero-b86571f2-manifesto-creed-hero.md) on the parent landing page that links here.
- CLEAN: native fit. White background (~#FFFFFF), brand-charcoal serif or modern-sans title (~36–44px wide-tracked all-caps). Instructional paragraph in brand-charcoal sans. Form input borders in neutral gray (~#E5E5E5). Submit pill in brand-navy. Newsletter checkbox uses brand-navy filled-square check. The pattern works in CLEAN as a corporate "request our brochure" page that complements the [centered-screenshot-trio hero](../../hero/research/refero-f619e700-centered-screenshot-trio.md) above. See [CLEAN archetype](../../../archetypes/clean.md).
- TERRAIN: cream background with a thin topographic-line motif at micro-scale below the title (a subtle contour-curve as a visual divider between the title and the instructional paragraph). Form input borders carry the same contour-line treatment instead of straight hairlines. Submit pill in clay or sage. The page is a contained "light band" moment between two darker sections elsewhere on the dealer site. See [TERRAIN archetype](../../../archetypes/terrain.md).
- FORGE: invert to a dark variant — near-black background (~#0E0E0E), white title in heavy display sans (~36–44px wide-tracked all-caps; the FORGE register prefers display sans over serif here). Instructional paragraph in muted-light gray (~#BDBDBD). Form fields with charcoal backgrounds (~#1A1A1A) and white text. Submit pill in ember-orange with charcoal label. Newsletter checkbox uses ember filled-square. The dark page substrate sustains FORGE's dark-dominant rule even on a documentation-request page. See [FORGE archetype](../../../archetypes/forge.md). Pairs with the [hardware-lineup dark hero](../../hero/research/refero-0945a36c-hardware-lineup-dark-hero.md) on the parent product page.
- TACTICAL: tighten geometry — title in tracked all-caps precision-sans (~36–44px), monochrome black on white. Form inputs use sharper corner radius (~4px) and a thin 1px steel-cyan focus ring. Submit pill becomes a sharp rectangle (~6px corner radius) with steel-cyan accent on the active state. Add a 1px corner-bracket detail to the form container's outer corners — consistent with the archetype's bracket motif. See [TACTICAL archetype](../../../archetypes/tactical.md).
- PATRIOT: cream background, deep-navy serif title (~36–44px wide-tracked all-caps). Instructional paragraph in deep-navy serif. Form input borders in muted cream hairline. Submit pill in deep navy with cream label. The single barn-red moment is the small required-asterisk color (a tiny barn-red star next to required field labels — restraint, single-cell-saturation). The newsletter checkbox check fill is deep navy. Don't add stripe rules around the form or stars near the title — the documentation register is the patriotic register at this scale. See [PATRIOT archetype](../../../archetypes/patriot.md). Pairs with the [manifesto-creed hero](../../hero/research/refero-b86571f2-manifesto-creed-hero.md) above.
