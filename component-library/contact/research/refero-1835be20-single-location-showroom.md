## Why this is library-worthy
A **single-location showroom-as-venue** contact landing, distinct from every existing contact pattern. The [dealer-locator-map](./refero-71707203-dealer-locator-map.md) handles multi-location dealer networks with filter chips and a "find nearest store" affordance; the [two-card decision-pivot](./refero-376ca585-two-card-decision-pivot.md), [horizontal-row directory](./refero-c29ba321-horizontal-row-directory.md), [typographic directory](./refero-4a5e550a-typographic-directory.md), and [tile-grid inquiry routing](./refero-c891061f-tile-grid-inquiry-routing.md) all assume the customer's question is "who do I talk to" rather than "where do I go." This pattern fills the **single-location dealer** gap where the dealer's physical place IS the credibility move and the page's job is "come visit our shop, here's what's happening, here's how to book a session with us." The pattern composes a store-hours block + embedded map + "what's happening in store" 3-up event cards + a flat list of bookable services & appointments — all framed around a single named address rather than a search-the-network experience.

## What to take
- **Page-title row anchored to the store name and city.** Top of page reads as a single phrase ("WEST ELM STORE, BROADWAY PALACE" / for a dealer "TERRAPLEX DEALER, ASHLAND OH" or "PRAIRIE AERIAL, GUTHRIE COUNTY IA"). The location is the page's identity, not a tab inside a network listing. A "Return to Store Results" link sits above the title for dealers who *are* part of the [49-location Terraplex network](../../../research/manufacturers/terraplex.md); single-location dealers omit it.
- **Two-column header block: store details left, embedded map right.** Left column is a stacked card with sections: address (street + suite + city + ZIP + phone), "Find Other Nearby Stores" link (network-only — single-location dealers omit), Regular Store Hours (Mon-Sat / Sun rows in a small flat grid), an italic single-line callout ("Offering In-Store Pickup" / for a dealer "Offering In-Store Demos" or "Repair Drop-Off Welcome"), and a "Get Directions" accordion that expands to show driving directions input. Right column is a wide embedded map (~600×400px) with the store pinned and surrounding road network visible — Bing Maps, Google Maps, or Mapbox depending on dealer infra. The map is information, not navigation — clicks open the customer's preferred maps app rather than navigating in-page.
- **"What's Happening In Store" 3-up event-card row.** Below the header block, a section heading ("What's Happening In Store") followed by a 3-up grid of upcoming-event cards. Each card holds a real photograph (~16:9 ratio) of the event, a bold title ("How to Decorate Your Tree & Mantel" / for a dealer "Spring Spray Calibration Workshop" or "Part 107 Prep Session" or "R-32 Demo Day"), a date+time line ("Thursday, November 16, 2023 / 6:00 PM"), a 2–3-line description, and a single filled brand-color "RSVP" CTA. The cards turn the showroom into a venue — the dealer's physical space hosts events, and the contact page's job is to surface them.
- **"Services & Appointments" flat list.** A single-column stack of named services, each row a bold service title + 1–3-line description + filled "Book Now" CTA. West Elm uses Free In-Home Design Appointment / Free In-Store Design Appointment / Free Virtual Design Appointment / Trade, Contract & Business Services / Registry Help. For a dealer the parallel list is: Free In-Field Demo (operator visits the customer's farm) / Free In-Shop Walkthrough (customer visits the dealer's facility) / Free Virtual Walkthrough (Zoom-led product demo for remote customers) / Fleet Account Setup (multi-drone customers) / [Part 137 Application Help](../../../content/compliance-program.md). The list is intentionally flat — no tabs, no expand-collapse, no card chrome — because each row is a *bookable affordance* and the customer scans for the right one.
- **Optional below-fold: recommendation rows from the e-commerce side.** West Elm appends "People who browsed items like you also browsed" and "Recently viewed" carousels at the bottom — useful for retail but typically not relevant for a Terraplex dealer's contact page. Drop these unless the dealer runs an active e-commerce catalog.
- **Footer matches the global site footer**, not a contact-specific footer. The page is part of the dealer's main site, not a standalone landing.

## What NOT to take
- **Don't use this pattern for multi-location dealers without a per-store landing.** This is the per-store landing — the [dealer-locator-map](./refero-71707203-dealer-locator-map.md) is the network-wide entry. A multi-location dealer wires the locator's pin clicks to per-store pages built from this template; a single-location dealer skips the locator entirely.
- **Don't ship the "What's Happening In Store" section with placeholder events.** A 3-up of "Coming Soon" cards collapses the showroom-as-venue affordance into chrome. If the dealer doesn't host real recurring events, drop the section entirely; the page still works as hours + map + services. The dealer can re-add the section once they have 2+ confirmed upcoming events.
- **Don't ship the Services & Appointments list with no working booking flow.** Each "Book Now" CTA must route to a real scheduling surface (calendar embed, intake form, or a phone-handoff page). A "Book Now" that opens a contact form with no scheduling logic feels broken on a page that promises bookable services. If the dealer's only booking infra is a phone call, label the row "Call to Book" + show the phone number directly rather than mimicking a calendar affordance.
- **Don't import retail-specific service language ("Registry Help", "Trade Services").** The transferable move is "named bookable affordances tied to the physical store," not the specific retail vocabulary. For a dealer the named services come from [content/service-patterns.md](../../../content/service-patterns.md) — Sam Welton handoff, Part 137 application help, R-32 calibration, etc.
- **Don't replace the embedded map with a static address image.** The map is the credibility move ("we're a real place at a real address with a real road network around us"). A pasted screenshot or static address card defeats the point — customers can't pan/zoom to see how the location relates to their farm.
- **Don't put a generic contact form on this page.** The page's job is "come visit us" — a generic form competes with the bookable-services list and the events RSVPs. If the dealer needs a generic-inquiry path, route to a separate `/contact` page using the [form-with-topic-checkboxes pattern](./refero-23938dfd-form-with-topic-checkboxes.md), [split-screen trust curtain](./refero-7c41e8a8-split-screen-trust-curtain.md), or [decision-pivot](./refero-376ca585-two-card-decision-pivot.md), and link to it from the dealer's main nav rather than from this showroom page.
- **Don't hide the store hours behind an accordion.** Hours are the second-most-scanned element on the page after the address. They sit visible in the left column always; only the directions input collapses behind an accordion.
- **Don't import West Elm's recommendation carousels** unless the dealer has an active product-detail-page / cart system. For a hardware-only dealer those rows look like dead chrome.

## Adaptation notes (cross-archetype)
- **FIELD**: cream page background (~#F4EFE7), warm-charcoal text, golden-hour photographs in the events 3-up (e.g. "Spring Spray Calibration Workshop" with a photo of an operator and equipment in late-afternoon light). Map keeps default colors but with a thin warm-amber border. Book Now / RSVP CTAs in deep amber. Services & Appointments list uses warm-charcoal hairline rules between rows. Pairs naturally with the [hands-on craft hero](../../hero/research/refero-be842c1e-hands-on-craft-hero.md) on the dealer's home page and the [editorial cream-bands product-block](../../product-block/research/refero-ecfaaa4f-editorial-cream-bands.md) elsewhere on the site. See [FIELD archetype](../../../archetypes/field.md).
- **CLEAN**: white page background, brand-charcoal text, clean equipment-and-operator photography in the events 3-up. Map in default colors with subtle drop shadow on the map container. Book Now / RSVP CTAs in brand-navy. Native fit — the pattern's restraint matches the archetype's. Pairs with the [centered-screenshot-trio hero](../../hero/research/refero-f619e700-centered-screenshot-trio.md) on the home page. See [CLEAN archetype](../../../archetypes/clean.md).
- **TERRAIN**: cream page background with a thin topographic-line divider above the events section and again above the services list. Map gets a slightly dimmer styling to let the topographic-line dividers carry the page's earth-line motif. Book Now / RSVP CTAs in clay or sage. Events 3-up photographs feature visible terrain context (contour lines, elevation in the background). Pairs with the [topographic layered-bg hero](../../hero/research/refero-ae84b62a-topographic-layered-bg.md) on the home page. See [TERRAIN archetype](../../../archetypes/terrain.md).
- **PATRIOT**: cream page background, deep-navy text, late-afternoon American-farmland photographs in the events 3-up. Map gets a thin deep-navy border. Book Now / RSVP CTAs in barn-red — the only place the flag colors saturate, kept at conversion-affordance scale. Services & Appointments list uses deep-navy hairline rules. Pairs with the [manifesto-creed hero](../../hero/research/refero-b86571f2-manifesto-creed-hero.md) on the home page. See [PATRIOT archetype](../../../archetypes/patriot.md).
- **FORGE**: invert to dark — charcoal page background (~#1A1A1A), white text, ember-rim-lit photographs in the events 3-up (dusk-lit dealership-floor or shop-bench shots). Map in a dark-themed map style (Mapbox dark-v11, Google Maps "dark" theme). Book Now / RSVP CTAs in ember-orange. Services & Appointments list uses ember-orange hairline rules between rows. The showroom-as-venue framing softens the dark register, which usually feels gatekept; an "events tonight" surface invites people in. Pairs with the [hardware-lineup dark hero](../../hero/research/refero-0945a36c-hardware-lineup-dark-hero.md) on the home page. See [FORGE archetype](../../../archetypes/forge.md).
- **TACTICAL**: tighten geometry — pure black page background, white precision-sans text, studio-lit photographs in the events 3-up (controlled lighting, no environmental context). Map in dark-monochrome style with steel-cyan road overlays. Book Now / RSVP CTAs in steel-cyan with sharper corner radius (~6px). Services & Appointments list uses 1px corner-bracket details on each row consistent with the archetype's bracket motif. The events 3-up cards each get a 1px corner-bracket detail. Pairs with the [3D object dark hero](../../hero/research/refero-ea86d249-3d-object-dark-hero.md) on the home page. See [TACTICAL archetype](../../../archetypes/tactical.md).

## Source brief
West Elm's [/stores/us-ca-los-angeles-broadway-palace/](https://www.westelm.com/stores/us-ca-los-angeles-broadway-palace/) — a single-store landing for a furniture retailer's Los Angeles location. The library-worthy move is the **store-name-as-page-title + two-column hours/map header + events 3-up + bookable-services flat list** structural cadence. Reusable for a Terraplex dealer's primary contact page when the dealer is a single-location shop whose physical place IS the credibility move (typical for the [49-location Terraplex network](../../../research/manufacturers/terraplex.md) member dealers). Different from the [dealer-locator-map](./refero-71707203-dealer-locator-map.md) (multi-location, network-wide search) and from generic [form-led contact](./refero-23938dfd-form-with-topic-checkboxes.md) (which assumes the customer's question is "what should I tell you" rather than "when can I come visit"). Pairs naturally with [content/service-patterns.md](../../../content/service-patterns.md) for the bookable-services list copy and with [content/compliance-program.md](../../../content/compliance-program.md) for the Sam-Welton-led service rows. For multi-location dealers, this is the per-store template that the [dealer-locator-map](./refero-71707203-dealer-locator-map.md) routes pin-clicks to.
