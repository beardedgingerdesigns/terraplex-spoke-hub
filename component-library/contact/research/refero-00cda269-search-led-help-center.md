## Why this is library-worthy
A "self-serve first, contact last" support pattern: full-bleed photographic hero with an embedded search field as the primary affordance → 4-card category-routing grid where each card holds a vertical link list of articles ("Purchasing / Products / Ownership / Company") → tag-filtered FAQ accordion → 3-column phone/email/chat band at the page bottom. Contact channels are the **fallback**, not the destination — the search and FAQ do most of the conversion-deflection work, then the channels band catches users who need a human. Right pick when the dealer accumulates documentation, FAQs, training material, and product knowledge — the support page becomes a knowledge surface rather than a contact form. Different from the existing 3 contact patterns: [two-card decision pivot](./refero-376ca585-two-card-decision-pivot.md), [form-with-topic-checkboxes](./refero-23938dfd-form-with-topic-checkboxes.md), and the [dealer-locator map](./refero-71707203-dealer-locator-map.md) all surface contact channels first; this one surfaces them last.

## What to take
- Open with a full-bleed landscape hero photograph (~50vh) — wide outdoor scene with depth (mountain pass with the product visible at small scale, field with equipment in the distance, sky with weather). Overlay a 2-line bold white headline ("Welcome to Support Center" → for a dealer "Find an answer fast") in the upper-left ~40% of the band, and directly below it, a single rounded search input pinned to the same column (~360px wide, white background, magnifying-glass icon left, placeholder "How can we help?"). The search is the primary affordance — the rest of the page is a fallback for searches that don't return a clear answer.
- "What kind of help do you need?" — 4-card category-routing grid below the hero. Each card is white with a 1px hairline border, ~16px corner radius, ~32px internal padding, ~280–360px wide. Top of the card holds a small monochrome category icon (~32px, line-art); below the icon, a bold sentence-case category name ("Purchasing / Products / Ownership / Company" → for a dealer "Buying / Products & Specs / Service & Repair / Compliance & Training"); below the name, a vertical link list of 3–6 article titles with each link in muted-charcoal ("Reserving and configuring", "Purchasing process", "Delivery"). Cards are equal-height; the link list is the card's content.
- "Frequently Asked Questions" — accordion below the cards. Above the accordion, a horizontal tag-filter strip ("Trending / Ownership / Products / Purchasing" — pill chips, active chip filled black with white label). The accordion shows ~6 questions per filter, each as a row with the question on the left and a + / – toggle on the right. Expanded answers show 2–3 lines of content with an inline "See full article" link to the deeper documentation. Two-column layout on desktop (3 expanded questions per side) so the user sees more answers at once.
- "Contact Us" footer band — light-cream or pale-warm full-bleed band with a single small heading + a thin rule beneath, then a 3-column flat-typography list: "Chat / Start a chat" + "Phone / (888) DEALER1" + "Email / customerservice@dealer.com." No icons, no cards, no chrome. The flat typography signals "these are the escape hatches; use them only if the search and FAQ didn't help."
- Below the channels band, a closing email-subscription strip: "Subscribe to learn about our latest news, updates and adventures" + email input + arrow submit. Optional — drop if the dealer doesn't have a real newsletter cadence.

## What NOT to take
- Don't import this pattern if the dealer doesn't have ~20+ real support articles to populate the category cards. With 3 articles per category, the link lists collapse into placeholder chrome and the affordance dies — the page looks like it's pretending to have a knowledge base. For dealers with thin documentation, use the [two-card decision pivot](./refero-376ca585-two-card-decision-pivot.md) (sales vs support intent routing) instead — it doesn't require a knowledge base.
- Don't make the hero search field cosmetic. The search MUST work — typing a query returns ranked results from the dealer's actual support corpus. A search field that does nothing is worse than no search field at all because it teaches the user the page is fake.
- Don't put the contact channels (phone / email / chat) above the FAQ. The pattern's whole logic is "search and FAQ first, contact last." Reversing the order collapses the page into a generic contact-with-FAQ-decoration layout. If the dealer's brief is "make contact obvious," use [form-with-topic-checkboxes](./refero-23938dfd-form-with-topic-checkboxes.md) or [two-card decision pivot](./refero-376ca585-two-card-decision-pivot.md) instead.
- Don't use a chat channel that doesn't actually have a human on the other end during business hours. "Start a chat" that opens an empty queue or a delayed-email-disguised-as-chat collapses the entire trust register the rest of the page builds.
- Don't replace the photographic hero with a flat color band. The hero photograph carries the brand register without copy — a flat color band makes the search field feel detached. The photograph should reference the dealer's actual context (their region, their crops, their equipment in operation) — not stock landscape.
- Don't add a CTA to the category cards. The card IS the CTA — clicking the card title or any link inside it is the action. A separate "Browse all articles" pill at the card's bottom collapses the link-list affordance into a generic feature card.
- Don't crowd the FAQ tag-filter with more than ~5 tags. Tags are a navigation aid, not a tag taxonomy — 6+ tags wrap and become a second category nav competing with the cards above.

## Adaptation notes (cross-archetype)
- FIELD: warm-cream background, golden-hour landscape hero photograph (the dealer's actual region in late afternoon — combine in operation, drone over corn, fenceline shot), warm-charcoal text. Category cards in cream with hairline-amber borders; card icons hand-drawn line-art (looser stroke than CAD-precise). FAQ tag chips in warm-amber active fill. Channels band uses cream-on-darker-cream tonal shift instead of a hard color band. See [FIELD archetype](../../../archetypes/field.md). Pairs with the [manifesto-creed hero](../../hero/research/refero-b86571f2-manifesto-creed-hero.md) on a parent compliance / about page.
- CLEAN: native fit. White background, neutral landscape hero photograph (a clean equipment-in-context shot), brand-navy active-tab fill. Category cards in white with neutral-gray hairlines. Channels band in pale gray. See [CLEAN archetype](../../../archetypes/clean.md).
- TERRAIN: the hero photograph reads as the page's opening "light band" with a topographic overlay at micro-scale on the search field's bottom border (a thin contour-line motif). Category cards alternate cream and clay backgrounds (2 cream + 2 clay) so the 4-up grid carries the alternating rhythm. FAQ accordion has topographic dividers between expanded answers. See [TERRAIN archetype](../../../archetypes/terrain.md).
- FORGE: invert to a dark variant — landscape hero photograph at dusk or under storm-cloud lighting, charcoal-with-rim-light register. Search field is white-on-dark with a thin ember-orange focus ring. Category cards near-black (~#0E0E0E) with white text and ember-orange icon strokes. FAQ tag chips ember-filled when active. Channels band on a slightly-lighter dark (~#1A1A1A) with ember accents on the channel labels. See [FORGE archetype](../../../archetypes/forge.md). Pairs with the [hardware-lineup dark hero](../../hero/research/refero-0945a36c-hardware-lineup-dark-hero.md).
- TACTICAL: tighten geometry — sharper rectangle category cards (~6px corner radius), precision-sans labels in tracked all-caps for category names, monochrome white-on-near-black with a single steel-cyan accent on search focus + active-tab fill. Hero photograph is studio-shot equipment in a controlled lighting environment, no environmental landscape. Add 1px corner-bracket details to the search field corners and category card corners (consistent with the archetype's bracket motif). See [TACTICAL archetype](../../../archetypes/tactical.md).
- PATRIOT: cream background, late-afternoon American-farmland hero photograph (dealer's actual region in golden-hour light). Search field white with a subtle deep-navy focus ring. Category cards cream with deep-navy headings; card icons in deep-navy line-art. FAQ tag chips deep-navy active fill; the single barn-red moment is the active "Trending" chip when the user lands (defaults to it). Channels band on warm-cream tonal shift; channel labels in deep-navy. See [PATRIOT archetype](../../../archetypes/patriot.md). Pairs with the [manifesto-creed hero](../../hero/research/refero-b86571f2-manifesto-creed-hero.md).

## Source brief
Rivian's [/support](https://rivian.com/support) page — a help-center / support-center landing built around self-serve search and FAQ, with contact channels relegated to a footer band. The library-worthy move is the structural cadence (full-bleed landscape hero with embedded search → 4-card category-routing grid with vertical link lists → tag-filtered FAQ accordion → 3-column flat-typography channels band → optional newsletter close), reusable for a Terraplex dealer who has built up real documentation around [R-32](../../../products/r-32.md) and [I-19](../../../products/i-19.md) operations, [Part 137 compliance](../../../content/compliance-program.md), [Part 107 prep](../../../content/compliance-program.md), or [Sam Welton's training program](../../../content/compliance-program.md). The pattern's whole credibility move is "we expect you to find the answer yourself" — which only works when the dealer's knowledge base is real.
