## Why this is library-worthy
A contact-page pattern where the page is **a grid of saturated-color inquiry-type tiles**, each tile a self-contained mini-routing card carrying its own audience-named heading, 1-paragraph context, photograph (or photo-overlap), and CTA / link. Right pick when the dealer has 6+ structurally distinct inquiry types (compliance audits, dealer recruitment, equipment trade-in, training enrollment, GDPR / privacy, key-note speaker requests, licensing / wholesale, parts requests, donation / charity, education partnerships) and forcing them all through a single contact form would damage the routing quality. Different from existing contact patterns: the [two-card decision pivot](./refero-376ca585-two-card-decision-pivot.md) handles 2 routing paths (sales / support); the [form-with-topic-checkboxes](./refero-23938dfd-form-with-topic-checkboxes.md) is one form with topic preview; the [dealer-locator map](./refero-71707203-dealer-locator-map.md) routes by location; the [calendar-led rep card](./refero-701496e7-calendar-led-rep-card.md) routes by time; the [search-led help center](./refero-00cda269-search-led-help-center.md) routes by topic-search; the [split-screen trust-curtain](./refero-7c41e8a8-split-screen-trust-curtain.md) is one form with credibility curtain; the [AI assistant chat](./refero-de02c274-ai-assistant-help.md) routes through conversational interaction. This pattern routes through **explicit inquiry-type selection** — every audience-type gets its own visually distinct tile, and the customer self-selects into the right routing path before ever seeing a form.

## What to take
- Contact page opens with a tight nav row (~6vh): centered "About Us" or "Contact" sub-nav with 4–5 small text-link tabs (in the source: "The LEGO Group / Discover / Newsroom / Podcast"). Optional — drop if the dealer's contact page is a single-purpose page rather than a section.
- Centered "Contact" heading (~32–40px sentence-case) below the sub-nav, anchoring the page.
- **First tile band** (~30vh): a single full-width 2-column tile with a saturated-color left half (~50% width) and a single landscape photograph right half (~50% width). The left half holds the most important contact information (in the source: "Corporate Headquarters" with address, phone, on a saturated yellow tile; for a dealer this becomes "Dealership Headquarters" with the dealer's primary address + phone + hours on a saturated archetype-aligned tile). The right photograph is a single bold image of the building, the operation, the team — the actual physical location.
- **Routing tile grid 1** (~40–60vh): 2-column row of 2 large square photo-and-text tiles (~50% page width each, ~40vh tall). Each tile is split horizontally — top half is a single photograph of that audience-type in context, bottom half is a saturated-or-cream surface with a bold sentence-case heading (~24–28px), a 1-line subhead, and a small text-link CTA ("Locations / Customer Service" → for a dealer "Find a dealer location / Customer service team"). The two tiles together set up the second-tier routing categories.
- **Routing tile grid 2** (~50–80vh): 2-column or 3-column row of 2–3 more photo-and-text tiles (one per audience-type — in the source: "Students" + "GDPR"; for a dealer: "Wholesale / Distribution" + "Privacy / GDPR" + "Press / Media"). Same structural pattern as grid 1 — photograph above + heading + paragraph + text-link CTA below.
- **"Other Enquiries" routing tile grid** — the pattern's distinguishing move. Centered heading ("Other Enquiries" → for a dealer "Other Enquiries" or "Specialty Inquiries"), then a multi-row 2-column grid (4–8 cells) where every cell is a fully-saturated single-color tile (~50% page width × ~40vh tall) with:
  1. A bold sentence-case heading at the top of the tile (~28–32px) in white or near-white ("Ideas" / "Charity and Donations" / "Licensing" / "How to become a LEGO retailer/distributor" / "Looking for a key note speaker?" / "LEGO Education" → for a dealer "Ideas / Charity / Wholesale / Become a Dealer / Training Partnerships / Speaker Requests").
  2. Below the heading, a 3–6 line paragraph of context explaining what kind of inquiry the tile routes ("We are nothing better than seeing our fans get creative…" / "We are honored and flattered by the requests we receive…").
  3. A small text-link or outline-pill CTA at the bottom of the tile ("Curious? Go to LEGO ideas" / "Apply for locations worldwide").
  4. A LARGE photograph anchored to the OPPOSITE side of the tile from the heading — half the cells have heading-left + photo-right; the other half have heading-right + photo-left, alternating per row to create rhythm. Photographs are real (real children playing, real adults discussing, real equipment in operation) — environmental, not stock.
  5. Tile colors rotate across the grid: a warm cream tile, a saturated teal-green tile, a muted purple tile, a saturated mustard tile, a cobalt-blue tile, a cream-on-cream tile (intentional mid-rhythm break), a muted pink tile. Colors are ARCHETYPE-aligned per dealer (see Adaptation notes); the rotation IS the visual identity of the page.
- Closing band: a single small 2-up tile row with "Newsroom" and "Locations" tiles (cream surface, small icon + label + text-link). The newsroom-locations close is the page's "if none of the above tiles fit, here's the fallback" affordance.

## What NOT to take
- Don't use this pattern with fewer than 6 distinct inquiry-types. The tile-grid affordance depends on having enough tiles to read as "we serve many audiences specifically" — with 3 tiles the grid collapses into a generic 3-card layout (covered better by the [two-card decision pivot](./refero-376ca585-two-card-decision-pivot.md) or [search-led help center's 4-card category routing](./refero-00cda269-search-led-help-center.md)). The pattern earns its space at 6+ tiles.
- Don't use uniform-colored tiles. The pattern's affordance depends on each tile carrying a distinct saturated color — the rotation is what signals "every audience-type has its own dedicated routing path." Uniform tiles collapse the visual breadth into a card grid.
- Don't import LEGO's literal cheerful saturated palette (mustard / pink / teal / purple). Those work for a children's-toy brand; for a Terraplex dealer the palette must be archetype-aligned (warm-amber + cream + sage for FIELD; navy + barn-red + cream for PATRIOT; charcoal + ember + cream-inset for FORGE) at MATCHED saturation across the rotation. The rotation is coordinated, not random.
- Don't substitute icons for photographs in the tiles. The photographs ARE the page's credibility move — each tile shows a real person / real operation / real equipment in the audience-type's context. Icons collapse the section into a generic capability grid that any SaaS landing carries.
- Don't put a contact form ON any tile. The tile is the routing surface; the form lives on the routed-to landing page (one form per audience-type, sized to that audience). Putting forms inside tiles collapses the routing affordance into a single contact-form-with-tabs layout, which is structurally a different pattern.
- Don't pair this with the [two-card decision pivot](./refero-376ca585-two-card-decision-pivot.md) on the same page — both occupy the "above the form" slot. Pick one based on tile count: 2 paths = pivot; 6+ paths = this tile-grid; 3–5 paths = use the [search-led help center's 4-card grid](./refero-00cda269-search-led-help-center.md) as the middle-ground compromise.
- Don't make the closing "Newsroom / Locations" 2-up tile bigger than the inquiry-type tiles above. The visual hierarchy is the affordance — inquiry-type tiles are large (the customer's primary path); the closing tiles are small (fallback navigation).

## Adaptation notes (cross-archetype)
- FIELD: cream page background, tile rotation through warm-amber + sage + clay + warm-cream + dusty-terracotta + pale-cream — matched ~60–70% saturation across the rotation. Photographs in every tile are golden-hour operator-and-equipment shots (real farmers, real applicators, real technicians at the dealer's facility). Tile headings in cream / off-white on the saturated tiles, in deep-charcoal on the cream tiles. Text-link CTAs in deeper amber. The opening "Dealership Headquarters" tile uses a warm-amber surface with a real photograph of the dealer's actual building or front gate. See [FIELD archetype](../../../archetypes/field.md). Pairs with the [hands-on craft hero](../../hero/research/refero-be842c1e-hands-on-craft-hero.md).
- CLEAN: white page background, tile rotation through brand-tinted neutrals — brand-navy, slate, teal, charcoal, cream-inset, pale-gray — at LOWER saturation than FIELD's so the page reads corporate-precise rather than playful. Photographs are clean equipment-and-operator shots with soft drop shadows. Tile headings in white on the navy / slate tiles, in brand-charcoal on the cream / pale-gray tiles. Text-link CTAs in brand-accent. See [CLEAN archetype](../../../archetypes/clean.md). Pairs with the [centered-screenshot-trio hero](../../hero/research/refero-f619e700-centered-screenshot-trio.md).
- TERRAIN: tile rotation through cream + clay + sage + dusty-amber + topographic-charcoal + cream — matched saturation. Add a thin topographic-line motif at the bottom edge of every tile (a contour-curve trace at 1px and ~30% opacity that traces the tile's color). Photographs in every tile are landscape-anchored — equipment in a field, drone over crops, technician at a workbench by a window. The alternating tile color rotation IS the page's light/dark band rhythm at tile scale. See [TERRAIN archetype](../../../archetypes/terrain.md).
- PATRIOT: cream page background, tile rotation through deep-navy + barn-red + cream + deeper-cream + warm-charcoal + cream — only 3 saturated colors (navy + red + 1 warm hue) at MATCHED saturation; the cream tiles act as visual bookends and breath-points. Photographs in every tile are late-afternoon American-farmland real-operator shots (real farmers, real compliance technicians, real American dealers — golden-hour, weathered-hands register). Tile headings in cream on the navy / red / warm-charcoal tiles, in deep-navy on the cream tiles. Text-link CTAs in deep-navy on cream tiles, in cream on navy tiles. The barn-red tile is the SINGLE flag-color-saturated tile in the rotation — restraint is the pattern; flag-color-everywhere kills the pattern. See [PATRIOT archetype](../../../archetypes/patriot.md). Pairs with the [manifesto-creed hero](../../hero/research/refero-b86571f2-manifesto-creed-hero.md).
- FORGE: invert to a dark-dominant variant — charcoal page background, tile rotation through near-black + ember-amber + warm-charcoal + ember-edge + near-black + charcoal-on-charcoal at matched saturation. Each tile has a soft ember rim along one edge (rotates per tile to create rhythm). Photographs are moody equipment-at-dusk / forge-glow rim-lit shots. Tile headings in white. Text-link CTAs in ember-orange. The pattern still earns its space because the routing affordance (multiple distinct audience-types) translates regardless of palette. See [FORGE archetype](../../../archetypes/forge.md). Pairs with the [hardware-lineup dark hero](../../hero/research/refero-0945a36c-hardware-lineup-dark-hero.md).
- TACTICAL: dark variant with tighter geometry — sharper rectangle tile corners (~6px corner radius), precision-sans for tile headings in tracked all-caps. Tile rotation through near-black + steel-cyan-edge + dark-charcoal + cyan-on-near-black + monochrome-charcoal — restricted palette, monochrome white-on-near-black with a single steel-cyan accent per tile (on a stripe at the bottom edge or on the text-link CTA). Photographs are studio-shot equipment in controlled lighting. Add 1px corner brackets to each tile's corners (consistent with the archetype's bracket motif). See [TACTICAL archetype](../../../archetypes/tactical.md). Pairs with the [topographic layered-bg hero](../../hero/research/refero-ae84b62a-topographic-layered-bg.md).

## Source brief
LEGO's [/aboutus/contact](https://www.lego.com/en-us/aboutus/lego-group/contact) page — the corporate contact page for the LEGO Group, organized as a grid of saturated-color inquiry-type tiles spanning consumer queries, GDPR / privacy requests, charity requests, retailer / distributor applications, key-note speaker requests, and education partnerships. The library-worthy move is the structural cadence (centered Contact heading → headquarters address tile (saturated color + photograph) → 2 large 2-up routing tiles for primary audience types → 2-up routing tiles for secondary audience types → "Other Enquiries" multi-row tile grid with rotating saturated colors and alternating heading-photo placement → closing small "Newsroom / Locations" 2-up tile), reusable for a Terraplex dealer with 6+ distinct inquiry types — most directly relevant for the [Terraplex parent brand at the hub level](../../../research/manufacturers/terraplex.md) (consumer queries / dealer recruitment / wholesale / press / education / training-partnerships / privacy / charity) and for individual dealers operating at the scale where multiple audience-types each warrant their own dedicated routing path.
