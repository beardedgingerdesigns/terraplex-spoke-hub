## Why this is library-worthy
A contact page that's pure typography — no form, no map, no booking widget, no chat. The page is a structured directory of phone numbers and emails segmented by **intent** ("Reservations", "Are you a guest?", "Become a homeowner", "Press", "Jobs", "Travel agents", "Become a [partner]") AND by **region** (United Kingdom, France, United States, Asia-Pacific). Each section is a heading + a two-column grid of region-name → phone/email rows. The whole page is hierarchy through whitespace and serif headings; restraint is the affordance. Different from the existing 6 contact patterns: the [two-card decision pivot](./refero-376ca585-two-card-decision-pivot.md) routes by 2 intents only; the [form-with-topic-checkboxes](./refero-23938dfd-form-with-topic-checkboxes.md) is form-led; the [dealer-locator map](./refero-71707203-dealer-locator-map.md) routes by location; the [calendar-led rep card](./refero-701496e7-calendar-led-rep-card.md) routes by time; the [search-led help center](./refero-00cda269-search-led-help-center.md) routes by self-serve search; the [split-screen trust curtain](./refero-7c41e8a8-split-screen-trust-curtain.md) is form + credibility curtain. None of those handle the "many distinct customer types AND many regions, each with its own contact channel" use case. This pattern fills that gap — right pick when the dealer or [Terraplex network](../../../research/manufacturers/terraplex.md) operates across multiple states / regions AND serves multiple distinct customer-purpose categories (operators, applicants, press, partners, careers, compliance).

## What to take
- Whole-page restraint: white background, serif headings (Tiempos Text or similar), sans-serif body (Akkurat Std or similar), single warm-accent color for clickable links and email addresses (~#E65C49 in the source; archetype-aligned for a dealer). No cards, no chrome, no icons in the directory body — just typography, hierarchy, and whitespace.
- Top of page: small horizontal navigation bar with brand logo center-aligned, primary nav items left-aligned, and a single utility "Inquire Now" CTA right-aligned. The nav stays minimal — the directory below carries the page.
- Page-level "Contact us" heading is large and serif (~48–56px), left-aligned, on its own line at the top of the directory body. Below it, NO subhead — the section headings below explain themselves.
- **Section pattern repeats vertically**, separated by horizontal rules. Each section is:
  1. **Section heading** in serif (~28–32px, sentence-case): names an intent ("Reservations" / "Are you a guest? Speak to the local team" / "Become a homeowner" / "Press" / "Jobs" / "Travel agents" / "Become a [partner]"). For a Terraplex dealer this becomes "Buying / Service / [Part 107 prep](../../../content/compliance-program.md) / [Part 137 ops](../../../content/compliance-program.md) / Press / Careers / Partner with us / Owner inquiries" — pick the 5–8 distinct intents the dealer actually handles.
  2. **Optional small sub-heading or one-line copy** beneath the section heading explaining what this contact path is for (e.g., "For booking inquiries and existing reservation changes" / for a dealer "For new-equipment quotes and demo scheduling").
  3. **Two-column flat-text grid** of region/category → contact-info rows. Left column carries the small label ("PHONE NUMBER" tracked all-caps muted gray, OR a region name like "United Kingdom" / for a dealer "North Dakota / Minnesota / Iowa"); right column carries the actual number or email. Numbers are flat text (no icons, no chip backgrounds); some are underlined to indicate clickable `tel:` links. Email addresses are styled in the warm-accent color and underlined, signaling the primary clickable affordance.
- **Region segmentation is OPTIONAL per section** — some sections (Reservations) carry a 4-region grid; others (Press) carry just a single email. The pattern's restraint is "use as much region structure as the section actually needs" — don't force every section into a 4-region grid.
- Section breaks: thin horizontal rules (~1px in muted-cream or muted-gray) span the content width. The rules are the only chrome; otherwise pure typography.
- Bottom of page: dark-gray footer (~#333333) with multi-column link grid by category (Home Collection / Villa Collection / etc. for the source — for a dealer "Equipment / Service / Compliance / Training / Resources / Company"), small white text, partner logos in a row at the very bottom, and a copyright notice. Footer is the only place the page abandons the white-and-typography register.

## What NOT to take
- Don't import this pattern if the dealer has only 1–2 distinct contact intents. With one phone number for everything, the page reads as an unstyled contact page. Use the [two-card decision pivot](./refero-376ca585-two-card-decision-pivot.md) if intent-routing matters but only between sales and support, or [form-with-topic-checkboxes](./refero-23938dfd-form-with-topic-checkboxes.md) if the dealer wants to capture data rather than expose channels.
- Don't import OneFineStay's literal warm-orange link color (#E65C49) or specific Tiempos serif. The transferable move is "serif headings + flat-text directory + single warm-accent on links" — the colors and fonts come from the archetype.
- Don't add a contact form anywhere on the page. The pattern's whole credibility move is "we expose every channel, you pick the right one." A form mid-page or at the bottom collapses the directory-as-affordance into "directory as decoration around the real form" and signals the dealer would prefer the form.
- Don't add a map embed alongside the directory. The pattern's restraint is the affordance — adding a map turns the page into a hybrid directory+locator that does neither well. If the dealer needs a map, use the [dealer-locator map](./refero-71707203-dealer-locator-map.md) on a dedicated /find-us page; this pattern's job is intent-and-region routing for customers who already know they need to make a call.
- Don't import every contact channel the dealer technically has. Phone + email is the right density; adding chat, SMS, fax, mail address, and physical address per section turns each block into a 6-row stack and the page loses scannability. If the dealer needs richer per-location info, use the [dealer-locator map](./refero-71707203-dealer-locator-map.md) (popovers carry hours, address, services).
- Don't make the section headings interactive (collapsible accordions). The pattern depends on EVERY section being visible at once — the customer scans the whole directory to find their intent. An accordion hides 6 of 7 sections at any given moment, defeating the affordance.
- Don't underline every body cell. Underlines are reserved for clickable contact info (`tel:` numbers, `mailto:` emails) — using them anywhere else (e.g. for region names) collapses the affordance signal that "underlined = click this."
- Don't use the pattern when the dealer's preference is "we want every customer to fill the form" (lead capture). The directory exposes channels; lead capture funnels them. If the dealer's brief is conversion velocity rather than customer-empowerment, use [form-with-topic-checkboxes](./refero-23938dfd-form-with-topic-checkboxes.md) or [split-screen trust curtain](./refero-7c41e8a8-split-screen-trust-curtain.md) instead.

## Adaptation notes (cross-archetype)
- CLEAN: native fit. White background, brand-charcoal serif headings (or modernize to a brand-sans semibold for stricter CLEAN restraint), single brand-navy accent on clickable links and email addresses. Section rules in light-gray (~#E5E5E5). Footer in pale gray (~#F8F8F8) instead of dark-gray. The pattern reads as corporate-formal in CLEAN — the right register for dealers serving institutional buyers ([Part 137 commercial applicators](../../../content/compliance-program.md), large co-ops). See [CLEAN archetype](../../../archetypes/clean.md).
- FIELD: cream background (~#F4EFE7), warm-charcoal serif headings (a slightly looser serif than corporate — Tiempos Text or similar), single warm-amber accent on links and emails. Section rules in muted-cream (~#E5DFD2). Footer on warm-dark-cream (~#3A2B1F) with cream text. The directory pattern's restraint pairs well with FIELD's no-motion rule and editorial register. Pairs with the [hands-on craft hero](../../hero/research/refero-be842c1e-hands-on-craft-hero.md) on a parent landing page. See [FIELD archetype](../../../archetypes/field.md).
- TERRAIN: the directory IS the page's main "light band" anchor — typography-only, no chrome. Add a thin contour-line motif on each section's horizontal rule (replaces the flat 1px line with a subtle 1px topographic curve at ~30% opacity). Section headings in clay or sage; link accent in archetype's primary clay. Footer drops to a dark band with topographic dividers. See [TERRAIN archetype](../../../archetypes/terrain.md).
- PATRIOT: cream background (~#F4EFE7), deep-navy serif headings, charcoal body text, single barn-red accent on links and emails. Section rules in muted-cream. The page's flag-color discipline is at micro-scale only — the section heading colors, the link accents, the rules — never as banner stripes or stars. The page reads as heritage-formal, the right register for [farmer-owned positioning](../../../content/positioning.md) where the dealer is signaling "we're listed and reachable, not hiding behind a contact form." Footer on deep-navy with cream text and a single muted-barn-red rule above the copyright notice. See [PATRIOT archetype](../../../archetypes/patriot.md). Pairs with the [manifesto-creed hero](../../hero/research/refero-b86571f2-manifesto-creed-hero.md) on a parent /about page.
- FORGE: invert to dark — charcoal background (~#1A1A1A), white serif headings, muted-gray body text, ember-orange accent on links and emails. Section rules in near-black (~#2A2A2A). Footer drops to near-black (~#0E0E0E). The pattern's restraint translates to FORGE because typography-only IS restrained — adding glow blobs or ember-rim chrome would betray the directory's affordance. The single ember accent on links carries the archetype's identity at micro-scale. See [FORGE archetype](../../../archetypes/forge.md). Pairs with the [hardware-lineup dark hero](../../hero/research/refero-0945a36c-hardware-lineup-dark-hero.md) on a parent product page.
- TACTICAL: tighten geometry — replace the source's serif headings with a precision-sans (Inter / IBM Plex Sans / similar) at semibold weight, tracked tracking on section labels, monochrome white-on-near-black with a single steel-cyan accent on links and emails. Section rules become 1px hairline with a small corner-bracket detail at each rule's left and right ends (consistent with the archetype's bracket motif). Footer on a slightly-lighter dark (~#1A1A1A) with corner-bracket details on the column-group dividers. The pattern reads as a tracking display rather than a contact directory — the absence of warmth IS the affordance. See [TACTICAL archetype](../../../archetypes/tactical.md). Pairs with the [topographic layered-bg hero](../../hero/research/refero-ae84b62a-topographic-layered-bg.md).

## Source brief
OneFineStay's [/contact-us](https://www.onefinestay.com/contact-us) page — a luxury vacation rental service surfacing contact channels for many distinct customer types (guests, prospective hosts, press, job-seekers, travel agents, partners) across many geographies (UK, France, US, Asia-Pacific, Italy, Australia). The library-worthy move is the structural cadence (page-level "Contact us" heading → repeated intent-named sections, each a heading + optional sub-line + 2-column flat-text grid of region/label → contact-info rows separated by horizontal rules → multi-column footer), reusable for a Terraplex dealer or [Terraplex hub](../../../research/manufacturers/terraplex.md) surfacing contact channels for many customer types ([Part 107 prep](../../../content/compliance-program.md) inquiries, [Part 137 ops](../../../content/compliance-program.md), equipment buyers, press, careers, owner partnerships, regional service requests) without forcing all of them through a single contact form. The pattern's whole credibility move is "everything is reachable" — which fits dealers whose brief is openness rather than lead-capture velocity.
