## Why this is library-worthy
A contact page where the contact channels are the smallest part — the page's main affordance is a stack of **downloadable brand assets** (logos, product imagery, lifestyle photography, video). The contacts row is segmented by audience (Media relations / Investor relations / Other) with a different email per audience, sitting inside a single section directly under the hero. Everything below is an asset-download surface laid out as full-width labelled rows. Distinct from the existing 12 contact patterns: [two-card decision pivot](./refero-376ca585-two-card-decision-pivot.md), [horizontal-row directory](./refero-c29ba321-horizontal-row-directory.md), [typographic intent + region directory](./refero-4a5e550a-typographic-directory.md), and [tile-grid inquiry routing](./refero-c891061f-tile-grid-inquiry-routing.md) all route by intent but none surface downloadable assets; [search-led help center](./refero-00cda269-search-led-help-center.md) self-serves answers but not files. This pattern fits a dealer when (a) regional ag-press / Part 137 trade pubs / local news / podcasts request brand assets, (b) the dealer wants a single canonical surface for its logo + product photography + sales-rep headshots, and (c) press inquiry is a real but low-volume channel that should not crowd the main contact page. It also doubles as the page a marketing partner or trade-show organizer lands on when they need the dealer's logo on short notice without emailing for it.

## What to take
- **Photographic hero band** (~30vh, contained): full-bleed lifestyle photograph on the right ~70% (real customers / operators / event attendees, environmental not staged) with a left-aligned inverted "Media assets" headline (~32–40px sentence-case) overlaid on the photograph's left edge. No CTA pill in the hero — the page IS the CTA.
- **Brand-use disclaimer band** directly below the hero (~6–8vh): full-bleed muted brand-color band with a single 2–3 line legal sentence ("Use of [Dealer]'s Brand Assets is governed by the [Dealer] Brand Use Guidelines. By downloading and using [Dealer] Brand Assets, you are agreeing to comply with these Guidelines in every instance of use."). The disclaimer is the page's only chrome — it pre-frames the asset downloads as licensed-not-public.
- **Contacts section** — first content section. Two-column block: left ~30% holds a sentence-case "Contacts" heading + small filled "Help Center" pill button as a fallback; right ~70% holds 3 audience-segmented contact callouts stacked vertically:
  1. **Media relations** — H3 sentence-case heading + "Email us at press@[dealer].com" + small parenthetical access rule ("(Only members of the media will receive a response.)"). Sets expectations: this is a press-only inbox.
  2. **Investor relations** (or **Partnership inquiries** for a dealer that doesn't have investors) — H3 + "Email ir@[dealer].com or visit investors.[dealer].com." Routes a structurally distinct audience to a structurally distinct destination.
  3. **Other** — H3 + "Contact us at [dealer].com/support." Catches everyone else and bounces them to the dealer's actual support page rather than answering them here.
- **Asset-download rows** — stack 4–6 full-width sections below the contacts block, each section a left-rail label + right-rail asset preview. Recommended sections in order:
  1. **Logos** — small left-rail "Logos" label, large centered logo preview on the right with a small carousel-arrow control (left/right) for variants (mark / wordmark / inverted / monochrome). Below the carousel, a "Download" link or filled pill that downloads a zip of all logo variants in PNG + SVG.
  2. **Product Imagery** — left-rail "Product Imagery" label, right side carries 1–3 stacked or carouseled product hero shots (R-32, I-19, controller, spreader hopper) on the dealer's standard light backdrop. Single Download link below the carousel for the full set.
  3. **Lifestyle Imagery** — left-rail "Lifestyle Imagery" label, right side carries 1–3 environmental in-context photographs (operators in field, training cohort, dealer-storefront with truck). Single Download link.
  4. **Featured Videos** — left-rail "Featured Videos" label, right side carries an embedded YouTube/Vimeo player + a small "View on YouTube" pill below for direct access to the dealer's video channel.
  5. (Optional, for dealers with media history) **Press coverage thumbnails** carousel — small horizontal strip of 4–6 article-thumbnail tiles (publication name + headline + small image), each linking to the source article. The thumbnail strip is a credibility band — "people have written about us, here's the trail."
- **Vertical rhythm** — generous whitespace between asset rows (~80–120px). Each row reads as a discrete deliverable; no horizontal rules between them. The label-on-left, asset-on-right grid is the page's repeating beat — once the visitor learns it on the Logos section, every subsequent section reads at a glance.
- **Footer is the standard site footer** (no special press-only navigation). The page is a destination, not an alternate site shell.

## What NOT to take
- Don't put a contact form on this page. The whole point is asset self-serve + audience-segmented emails. Adding a form collapses the page into a generic contact page and dilutes the press-inbox segmentation. If a press contact wants to pitch a story, they email; the form belongs on [form-with-topic-checkboxes](./refero-23938dfd-form-with-topic-checkboxes.md).
- Don't list every email a dealer has on this page. The audience segmentation is the pattern's affordance — three or four audience callouts max (press / partnerships / other). If the dealer needs to expose more contact intents, that's the [horizontal-row directory](./refero-c29ba321-horizontal-row-directory.md) or [typographic directory](./refero-4a5e550a-typographic-directory.md) pattern, not this one.
- Don't gate the asset downloads behind a form ("Enter your email to download the logo"). Press uses logos at deadline; gating breaks the page's whole "self-serve at speed" affordance. The brand-use disclaimer at the top + a download click is the gate.
- Don't make the asset rows into a tile grid. The full-width label-left + asset-right rhythm is what makes the page scannable — a tile grid forces the visitor to hunt for what each asset IS. Stack the rows.
- Don't add this page to the dealer's primary navigation. It's a destination linked from press releases, trade-show outreach emails, or the footer's "Press / Media" link — not a primary nav item. Press-resource pages in primary nav read as self-important for a regional dealer.
- Don't import a dealer-specific asset that isn't licensed for redistribution. If the R-32 hero photograph is a Revolution Drones / GTEEX OEM-supplied image, the dealer's media kit should serve only assets the dealer has rights to distribute (their own product photography, their own logo, their own lifestyle imagery). Cross-reference [content/content-exclusions.md](../../../content/content-exclusions.md) for the rules around OEM brand assets.
- Don't use this pattern for dealers who have no real media assets to share. A page that exists but has only a logo and an email row reads as press-theater — it suggests the dealer is performing media-readiness without actually being media-active. Skip the page entirely; the [horizontal-row directory](./refero-c29ba321-horizontal-row-directory.md) can carry a single "Press: press@[dealer].com" row instead.

## Adaptation notes (cross-archetype)
- FIELD: native fit. Cream base with warm-amber brand-use disclaimer band, warm-charcoal section labels, environmental lifestyle imagery (real operators in real fields). Display-serif "Media assets" headline. The label-left, asset-right rhythm reads naturally on the cream base. See [FIELD archetype](../../../archetypes/field.md). Pairs with the [hands-on-craft hero](../../hero/research/refero-be842c1e-hands-on-craft-hero.md) and [aerial-photo-spine product-block](../../product-block/research/refero-4c2ed506-aerial-photo-spine.md) for sustained warm-photographic register.
- CLEAN: native fit. White base with brand-navy disclaimer band, charcoal labels, brand-tinted-neutral logo carousel backgrounds. Sentence-case sans-serif "Media assets" headline. Brand-navy "Download" pills. See [CLEAN archetype](../../../archetypes/clean.md).
- TERRAIN: cream base with clay or sage disclaimer band, optional thin contour-line dividers between asset rows (the page's only decorative chrome — earns its weight by mirroring the archetype's contour motif at section-break scale). Asset-row rhythm carries the alternating tonal-shift register without needing literal alternating bands. See [TERRAIN archetype](../../../archetypes/terrain.md).
- PATRIOT: cream base with deep-navy disclaimer band, display-serif "Media assets" headline in deep navy, barn-red "Download" links (the single barn-red moment per asset row anchors the flag-color-at-micro-scale rule). The audience-segmented emails work especially well for PATRIOT dealers who have historical / heritage / institutional press relationships (regional ag pubs, county fair coverage, church-newsletter mentions). See [PATRIOT archetype](../../../archetypes/patriot.md). Pairs with the [manifesto-creed hero](../../hero/research/refero-b86571f2-manifesto-creed-hero.md) for a press kit that backs the homepage's heritage register.
- FORGE: dark charcoal base (#1A1A1A), inverted hero with white "Media assets" headline + ember-orange brand-use disclaimer band, near-white asset section labels, ember-orange Download pills. Logo carousel on a slightly-lighter dark panel (~#222) so the brand mark reads cleanly. The page's editorial restraint suits FORGE's "no decorative chrome" rule — every asset row earns its place. See [FORGE archetype](../../../archetypes/forge.md).
- TACTICAL: pure black base (#000), monochrome Download pills with a thin steel-cyan accent on a single hairline rule under each section heading (the bracket-motif equivalent at section-break scale). Tighter geometry — sharper rectangle Download buttons (~6px corner radius). Press-segmentation language stays clipped: "Media inquiries / Partnership inquiries / All other inquiries" rather than longer sentences. See [TACTICAL archetype](../../../archetypes/tactical.md).
