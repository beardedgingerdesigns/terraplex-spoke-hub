## Why this is library-worthy

A "store-as-page" pattern: a single brick-and-mortar location given the full landing-page treatment, with the store's specific address + hours + map at top, a "what's happening in store" local-events row in the middle, and a vertical stack of services each with its own Book Now CTA at the bottom. Different from every existing contact pattern in the library:

- The [dealer-locator-map](./refero-71707203-dealer-locator-map.md) handles **many** locations across a region — this handles **one** location with depth.
- The [two-card decision pivot](./refero-376ca585-two-card-decision-pivot.md), [tile-grid inquiry routing](./refero-c891061f-tile-grid-inquiry-routing.md), [horizontal-row directory](./refero-c29ba321-horizontal-row-directory.md), and [typographic directory](./refero-4a5e550a-typographic-directory.md) all route by **intent** — this anchors by **place**.
- The [form-with-topic-checkboxes](./refero-23938dfd-form-with-topic-checkboxes.md), [split-screen trust curtain](./refero-7c41e8a8-split-screen-trust-curtain.md), and [catalog request form](./refero-a40c8cca-catalog-request-form.md) are conversion forms — this is a destination page that *is* the dealer's storefront online.

Right pick when the dealer has a real physical showroom — demo bay, training classroom, service intake bench, parts counter — and the credibility move is "we're a real place you can drive to and walk into." Especially relevant for the [49-location Terraplex dealer network](../../../research/manufacturers/terraplex.md) where each dealer is a physical anchor, and for dealers running [Part 107 prep cohorts](../../../content/compliance-program.md) or [Part 137 service intake](../../../content/service-patterns.md) out of an actual building.

## What to take

- **Page-top store header**: store-name eyebrow ("WEST ELM STORE, BROADWAY PALACE" → for a dealer "TERRAPLEX DEALER, [TOWN] [STATE]") in tracked uppercase, small "Print" + "Visited this store lately?" review-prompt link top-right. Single line, no decoration — the store name carries the page.
- **Two-column store-details band** directly under the header (left ~40%, right ~60%):
  - Left card titled "Store Details": street address (2 lines including suite), phone number, "Find Other Nearby Stores" hairline link beneath, then bold "Regular Store Hours:" with a flat 2-row Mon–Sat / Sun table, then a barn-red kicker "Offering In-Store Pickup" (or for a dealer: "Authorized Service Center" / "Sam Welton on-site Tuesdays" / "Demo bay open by appointment") that names a single store-specific affordance. Below, a collapsed "Get Directions" accordion row.
  - Right canvas: embedded interactive map with the store pin centered, road-style toggle in the corner, zoom controls bottom-right. The map is the store's, not a regional locator — a single pin, not a cluster.
- **"What's Happening In Store" 3-up event row**: full-width section heading on the left, then 3 equal-width event cards. Each card holds a square photograph (in-store reality, not stock), a 2-line bold event title, a 2-line date + time (`Thursday, November 16, 2023 / 6:00 PM`), 3–4 lines of event description in regular weight, and a single filled rectangle "RSVP" CTA at the bottom (full-width within the card). For a Terraplex dealer this becomes "Part 107 prep cohort kickoff", "R-32 demo day", "Service-tier customer appreciation breakfast" — store-specific events the customer can show up to in person.
- **"Services & Appointments" vertical stack**: section heading, then a series of horizontally-paired rows each holding a bold service-name heading + 1–2 sentence description in muted body weight + a separate filled rectangle "Book Now" CTA on its own line directly below. Each row is its own bookable service (Free In-Home Design Appointment, Free In-Store Design Appointment, Free Virtual Design Appointment, Trade Contract & Business Services, Registry Help). Service-name + description + CTA all left-aligned; service rows separated by ~32px vertical whitespace, no card chrome, no dividers — the layout breathes via spacing alone. For a Terraplex dealer the parallel rows are "Schedule a demo flight" / "Book a service intake" / "Compliance consultation with Sam Welton" / "Fleet evaluation visit" / "Part 107 prep enrollment."
- **Below-the-page commerce strip** (optional, dealer-specific): West Elm appends "People who browsed items like you also browsed" + "People who browsed items like you bought" + "Recently viewed" — 4-up product-card carousels with arrow nav. For a Terraplex dealer this slot becomes "What dealers in your county also stock" or "Recent arrivals at this store" or "Demo units available this week" — only useful if the dealer has live inventory data; otherwise drop the strip entirely rather than fake it.
- **Layout discipline**: the page is a top-to-bottom narrative arc — location (where you'd drive) → events (when you'd come) → services (what you'd book) → inventory (what you'd see). The arc is the reading rhythm; reordering breaks it.

## What NOT to take

- **Don't use this pattern for multi-location dealer networks.** If the dealer has 3+ locations, [dealer-locator-map](./refero-71707203-dealer-locator-map.md) is the right pattern — this one's whole credibility move is "this single physical place is real, here's its map and hours and events." A multi-location dealer can give EACH location its own instance of this page (one /stores/[town] route per location), but the network's contact landing should still be the locator-map.
- **Don't fake the "What's Happening In Store" events.** If the dealer doesn't host in-person events at this location (no Part 107 cohort, no demo days, no service-tier breakfasts), drop the events row entirely. A row of stock-photo "events" with vague descriptions ("Stop by for our holiday cheer!") collapses the page's whole place-is-real affordance. The events row earns its weight only when the dealer can name 2–3 real upcoming events with real dates and real photographs.
- **Don't put a generic "Contact Us" form in the Services & Appointments stack.** Each row in the stack is a *bookable service* — the CTA literally schedules something on the dealer's calendar. A "Send a message" row in this position dilutes the "this is a place you can transact with right now" register. The general contact form belongs on a separate page (linked from the header nav), not inside the appointments stack.
- **Don't pre-fill the Services & Appointments stack with services the dealer doesn't actually offer at this location.** If only the [Part 107 prep program](../../../content/compliance-program.md) is run from this store and not the [Part 137 service intake](../../../content/service-patterns.md), only Part 107 belongs in the stack. Cross-listed services that route the customer to a different location should live as a "Services not at this location? See [other-location]" hairline link below the stack, not as inline rows.
- **Don't import West Elm's product-recommendation carousels** unless the dealer has real per-store inventory data. The carousels at the bottom assume an e-commerce backend with browsing-history data; without that, they read as ghost chrome.
- **Don't expand the "Regular Store Hours" table beyond Mon–Sat / Sun rows.** Two rows is the affordance; per-day rows make the table feel bureaucratic and weaken the "we keep simple hours, just come by" register. If the dealer's hours genuinely vary day-to-day (weekday split, seasonal closure), use a single line of small text below the table ("Holiday hours may vary — call (xxx) xxx-xxxx to confirm") rather than expanding the table.
- **Don't add an embedded form to the page.** This is a destination page, not a conversion page. The page's affordance is "here's where we are, come visit / call / book a service" — embedding a contact form fights that affordance and turns the page into a half-locator, half-form. If the dealer needs both, use the [split-screen trust curtain](./refero-7c41e8a8-split-screen-trust-curtain.md) on a different page for form-led conversion and keep this page place-anchored.

## Adaptation notes (cross-archetype)

- **FIELD**: native fit. Cream page background (~#F4EFE7), warm-charcoal headlines, single deep-amber accent on the "Book Now" rectangles and the "Offering In-Store Pickup" kicker. Map tile uses a warm-cream basemap with charcoal road lines. Event-row photographs are golden-hour real-store photographs (the demo bay at sunset, a customer-appreciation breakfast in the showroom). See [FIELD archetype](../../../archetypes/field.md). Pairs with the [hands-on-craft hero](../../hero/research/refero-be842c1e-hands-on-craft-hero.md) above for a place-anchored FIELD page.
- **CLEAN**: white page background, brand-charcoal headlines, single brand-navy accent on Book Now rectangles. Map tile uses default Mapbox/Google standard styling. Event-row photographs are clean equipment-and-people shots, no environmental clutter. See [CLEAN archetype](../../../archetypes/clean.md). Pairs with the [centered-screenshot-trio hero](../../hero/research/refero-f619e700-centered-screenshot-trio.md).
- **TERRAIN**: the alternating-band rhythm carries over — store-details band on cream → "What's Happening" 3-up on a tonal-shift sage band → "Services & Appointments" stack on cream again → optional inventory strip on a clay-tone band. Map tile picks up the archetype's contour-line motif via a custom Mapbox style if budget allows. See [TERRAIN archetype](../../../archetypes/terrain.md).
- **PATRIOT**: cream page background, deep-navy headlines, single barn-red accent on Book Now rectangles and on the "Authorized Service Center" kicker only. Event-row photographs are American-farmland and small-town storefront shots (the dealer's actual building from the road, the parts counter with a real human). Map tile uses cream basemap with deep-navy road lines. The flag colors live at micro-chrome (CTA fills, kicker accent), not at band scale. See [PATRIOT archetype](../../../archetypes/patriot.md).
- **FORGE**: invert to dark — charcoal page background (~#1A1A1A), white headlines, ember-orange accent on Book Now rectangles. Map tile uses a dark Mapbox basemap with ember-orange road highlights. Event-row photographs are dusk-lit / rim-lit interior store shots (the demo bay at night, the service bench under task lighting). The "Offering In-Store Pickup" kicker becomes "Equipment-on-hand" or "Service bench staffed." See [FORGE archetype](../../../archetypes/forge.md). Pairs with the [hardware-lineup dark hero](../../hero/research/refero-0945a36c-hardware-lineup-dark-hero.md).
- **TACTICAL**: tighten geometry — sharper rectangle CTAs (~6px corner radius), precision-sans for headlines and body, monochrome white-on-near-black with a single steel-cyan accent on Book Now rectangles + the kicker. Map tile uses a dark mono Mapbox style with steel-cyan road lines. Event cards use 1px steel-cyan corner-bracket detailing. See [TACTICAL archetype](../../../archetypes/tactical.md). Pairs with the [emblem-program hero](../../hero/research/refero-c1c2abcf-emblem-program-hero.md) for a credentialed-program TACTICAL page.

## Source brief

West Elm's `/stores/us-ca-los-angeles-broadway-palace/` single-store landing — a furniture retailer giving each brick-and-mortar storefront its own dedicated landing page that doubles as the location's contact + about-us + appointments-booking page. The library-worthy move is the structural narrative arc (store-header → store-details + map → "what's happening" event row → "services & appointments" stack → optional inventory strip), reusable for any Terraplex dealer with a real physical showroom that wants to anchor the dealer's "we're a place you can visit" credibility while also routing showroom-floor traffic into specific bookable services. Pairs naturally with [content/service-patterns.md](../../../content/service-patterns.md) for the dealer's authoritative service-tier copy and [content/compliance-program.md](../../../content/compliance-program.md) for Sam Welton-led service rows in the appointments stack. Composes with the [calendar-led rep card](./refero-701496e7-calendar-led-rep-card.md) at the next step — once the customer clicks Book Now on a service row, the calendar-led rep card is the right destination for the actual scheduling step.
