## Why this is library-worthy
A contact pattern for dealers with a real physical place and several in-person services beyond just sales. The page opens with a **secondary-nav strip** routing across the dealer's full menu of in-person services ("Book appointment / Pick up in-store / Find a store / Personalised items / Product care / Gift wrapping / Gift cards") then drops into a **2-column split**: appointment-request form left + ambient-store photograph right. The form is intentionally simplified — country / store / name / email / phone / optional date+time — no live-calendar grid, no rep avatar, no timeslot picker. The pattern's signal is "we have a real place, you can come visit, here's how" — without forcing the visitor through the calendar-grid integration that the [calendar-led rep card](./refero-701496e7-calendar-led-rep-card.md) requires. Distinct from existing contact patterns: the [calendar-led rep card](./refero-701496e7-calendar-led-rep-card.md) routes through a live calendar widget; the [tile-grid inquiry routing](./refero-c891061f-tile-grid-inquiry-routing.md) routes through audience-type tiles; the [horizontal-row directory](./refero-c29ba321-horizontal-row-directory.md) routes through phone/email rows; this pattern routes through **physical-place affordance** + simplified appointment form for a single visit purpose.

## What to take
- Top of the page: a **client-services nav strip** as a single horizontal row (~6vh tall) listing 6–8 in-person service categories ("Book appointment / Pick up in-store / Find a store / Personalised items / Product care / Gift wrapping / Gift cards" — for a Terraplex dealer this becomes "Book a visit / Demo flight / Drop off for service / Find a dealer / Training enrollment / Parts pickup / Trade-in appraisal"). The active category is underlined or carries a small accent dot; inactive categories are muted text-link. The strip is sentence-case 14–15px sans-serif, generously spaced (~32–40px between items), centered horizontally beneath the main nav.
- Below the strip, a left-aligned page heading (~28–32px sentence-case) — "Book a private appointment" / "Schedule a demo flight" / "Schedule a service drop-off" — followed by a 1–2 sentence intro paragraph explaining what the appointment is for and what the customer should expect.
- **2-column split below the heading** (~50/50 page width):
  - **Left column** is the form: stacked vertical fields with cream/off-white field surfaces, no field borders (just background-tint differentiation), labels in small all-caps tracked-tracking gray text inside the field at top-left ("COUNTRY/REGION/LOCATION", "STORE", "NAME", "EMAIL", "PHONE NUMBER OPTIONAL", "REQUESTED DATE & TIME OPTIONAL"). Country and store are dropdowns; the rest are text inputs. The "REQUESTED DATE & TIME" field is a free-form text or simple datetime input — NOT a live calendar widget. Single full-width primary CTA ("REQUEST APPOINTMENT") at the bottom in solid black with cream label, ~52px tall.
  - **Right column** is a single ambient photograph of the actual physical space (showroom interior, hangar, training classroom, service-bench area). The photograph is bleed-aligned to the right edge and ~50–60vh tall. NO product photography, NO operator-with-equipment shots — JUST the empty or near-empty place. The pattern's affordance is "this is a real place"; the emptiness is the affordance.
- Form submission is async (no page reload, just a confirmation overlay or inline thanks message) — but the dealer-side workflow is asynchronous human follow-up by phone or email, not an instant-confirmed booking. State this in the form intro paragraph: "We'll reach out within one business day to confirm your appointment time."
- Optional below-fold: a small 2–3 line address + phone + hours block (no map embed — the "Find a store" item in the nav strip handles maps for multi-location dealers). For a single-location dealer, the address block becomes the page's secondary content.

## What NOT to take
- Don't add a live calendar grid to the form. The pattern's distinctive move is the ABSENCE of calendar-grid chrome — the simplified form signals "real human will follow up" rather than "machine-confirmed booking". For machine-confirmed bookings use the [calendar-led rep card](./refero-701496e7-calendar-led-rep-card.md) instead.
- Don't put a rep-avatar pre-frame on the form. This pattern routes by service-type, not by named-rep — the avatar pre-frame signals "you'll meet THIS person", but here the customer is booking a place / a service category, not a rep. For rep-led bookings use [calendar-led rep card](./refero-701496e7-calendar-led-rep-card.md).
- Don't substitute equipment photography for the ambient-place photograph. The pattern's credibility is "you can come HERE" — equipment photographs collapse the affordance into a generic product page header. Even if the photograph is of empty wall + concrete floor + warehouse light, the empty-place register is the move. (For active-equipment-led contact, see the [emergency-triage contact](./refero-56a7035b-emergency-triage-contact.md) which uses operations photography intentionally.)
- Don't expand the client-services nav strip beyond ~8 items. With 9+ in-person services the strip overflows; switch to the [tile-grid inquiry routing](./refero-c891061f-tile-grid-inquiry-routing.md) pattern instead.
- Don't put a contact form on every category in the nav strip. Most categories route to dedicated pages (find-a-store goes to a map; pick-up-in-store goes to an order lookup; product-care goes to a service-tier overview). Only ONE category — the appointment-request — uses this form pattern. The other categories use whatever pattern fits THEIR purpose.
- Don't make the simplified form mandatory on every field. The "REQUESTED DATE & TIME" and "PHONE NUMBER" fields are explicitly optional — the optionality reinforces the asynchronous-human-follow-up affordance. Required fields are limited to country/region + name + email.

## Adaptation notes (cross-archetype)
- FIELD: warm-cream page background, ambient photograph is a golden-hour interior shot of the dealer's hangar / barn / service shop — natural light through doorway, weathered concrete floor, wood-beam rafters. Form field surfaces in pale-amber-tinted cream. CTA in deep-amber. Client-services strip text-link in warm-charcoal. See [FIELD archetype](../../../archetypes/field.md). Pairs with [hands-on craft hero](../../hero/research/refero-be842c1e-hands-on-craft-hero.md) on the home page.
- CLEAN: white page background, ambient photograph is a softly-lit empty showroom or training classroom (neutral concrete, plant in corner, ambient daylight). Form field surfaces in pale brand-tint. CTA in brand-accent. See [CLEAN archetype](../../../archetypes/clean.md).
- TERRAIN: cream page, ambient photograph is a near-empty workbench area with a window framing distant farmland — landscape-anchored even in interior shots. Add a thin contour-line motif at the bottom edge of the photograph. Form CTA in topographic-charcoal. See [TERRAIN archetype](../../../archetypes/terrain.md).
- PATRIOT: cream page, ambient photograph is a real American-dealership interior shot — flag visible on the wall but not centered; weathered-wood counter; training-classroom chairs in the background. Form field surfaces in pale-cream. CTA in deep-navy with cream label; barn-red is reserved for hover-state only. See [PATRIOT archetype](../../../archetypes/patriot.md).
- FORGE: dark-dominant page (charcoal background). Ambient photograph is the dealer's hangar at dusk with rim-lit silhouettes of equipment in the background. Form field surfaces in near-black with cream labels. CTA in ember-amber with cream label. Client-services strip in cream / muted-cream. See [FORGE archetype](../../../archetypes/forge.md). Pairs with [emblem-program hero](../../hero/research/refero-c1c2abcf-emblem-program-hero.md).
- TACTICAL: dark page with tighter geometry. Ambient photograph is a studio-lit empty service-bench (controlled lighting, seamless background, single-source key-light). Form fields in steel-charcoal with cream labels in tracked all-caps. CTA in steel-cyan with near-black label. Add 1px corner brackets to the photograph and the form. Client-services strip in tracked all-caps precision-sans. See [TACTICAL archetype](../../../archetypes/tactical.md).

## Source brief
Acne Studios' [Book a private appointment](https://www.acnestudios.com/us/en/client-services/store-appointment.html) page — the fashion retailer's in-store appointment-request page within their broader "Client Services" nav. The library-worthy move is the structural cadence (client-services secondary nav strip across multiple in-person service categories → page heading + intro paragraph → 2-column form-left / ambient-place-right split → simplified form with optional date/time, asynchronous human follow-up). Most directly transferable for a Terraplex dealer with a real physical location whose contact-page job is to route customers into one of several in-person service categories (visits, demo flights, service drop-off, training enrollment) without forcing each through the heavy [calendar-led rep card](./refero-701496e7-calendar-led-rep-card.md) integration.

Sibling contact notes for adjacent decisions:
- [calendar-led rep card](./refero-701496e7-calendar-led-rep-card.md) — when the dealer needs machine-confirmed time-slot booking with a named rep
- [tile-grid inquiry routing](./refero-c891061f-tile-grid-inquiry-routing.md) — when there are 6+ structurally distinct inquiry types (vs ~7 in-person service categories that this pattern handles via a strip)
- [horizontal-row directory](./refero-c29ba321-horizontal-row-directory.md) — when the routing is purely text/phone/email (no in-person component)
- [emergency-triage contact](./refero-56a7035b-emergency-triage-contact.md) — when the page is purpose-built for high-stakes operational support, NOT routine appointments

Pick guide: this pattern when (a) the dealer has a real physical place worth showing, (b) there are 5–8 in-person service categories worth listing as a strip, AND (c) appointment booking is asynchronous-human-follow-up rather than machine-confirmed.
