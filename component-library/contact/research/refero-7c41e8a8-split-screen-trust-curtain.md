## Why this is library-worthy
A 50/50 split-screen contact pattern where the LEFT half is a dark "credibility curtain" (capability bullets stacked under a heading + a logo wall + a review-stars-and-rating row), and the RIGHT half is the form on a white surface. The dark left half does ALL the conversion work; the form just collects the data. Different from existing contact patterns: [form-with-topic-checkboxes](./refero-23938dfd-form-with-topic-checkboxes.md) previews conversation topics (left), this previews credibility (left); [two-card decision pivot](./refero-376ca585-two-card-decision-pivot.md) routes intent before the form, this skips routing and stacks trust signals beside the form. Right pick when the dealer is high-stakes and the contact form needs to overcome buyer hesitation — the page assumes the customer arrived skeptical and uses the dark left half to address that head-on before the form is filled.

## What to take
- Single-band 2-column layout, no scroll. Left ~50% is a near-black or dark-charcoal surface (~#0E0E0E) running edge-to-edge top to bottom; right ~50% is white surface running edge-to-edge top to bottom. The hard vertical edge between the two halves is the affordance — no gradient transition, no neutral spacer.
- Left (dark) column content stack (top to bottom, ~64px internal padding):
  1. Small dealer logotype in white at the top-left (~24px tall, monochrome).
  2. Heading in white (~36–48px bold sans-serif): "Contact our sales team" (or for a dealer "Contact our [service-domain] team").
  3. 3-row capability bullet list directly below the heading (~16–18px), each row a small monochrome line-art icon + 1-line capability claim ("Unlimited characters, voices and concurrency" → for a dealer "[Part 137]-certified application support" / "Same-day fleet diagnostics" / "On-site demo flights"). Bullets are the "what you get" pre-frame.
  4. Vertical breathing space (~64px), then a small muted-gray "Trusted by 10,000+ leading businesses" caption.
  5. Below the caption, a flat 5-up monochrome logo wall (white logos on dark, no chrome) — recognizable customer or partner names.
  6. Below the logo wall, a single star-rating row: 5 white stars + "4.6 stars and 580+ reviews" in white. The stars + count is the third trust dimension after capability and logos.
  7. At the bottom of the dark column, an optional decorative element: a thin white wave or torn-edge motif that lifts the column off the page bottom. Optional — drop if the archetype doesn't want decorative chrome.
- Right (light) column content stack:
  1. Heading at the top (~32–40px bold): "Contact Sales Form" (or for a dealer "Request a Quote").
  2. Form fields in a 2-column grid (First Name / Last Name on row 1, Phone / Website on row 2, Company Size dropdown / Country dropdown / Industry dropdown stacked, then a textarea "Please tell us about your use case", then "How did you hear about us?" dropdown). Field labels in tracked all-caps muted gray (~12px), field input borders in light gray (~#E5E5E5), focus state in a single accent color.
  3. Paired CTAs at the bottom-right: a "Back" outline pill + a filled "Submit" black pill (or per-archetype accent), both ~120px wide.
  4. Small reCAPTCHA badge anchored bottom-right of the white column (out of the way; required for spam control).
- Mobile collapse: dark left column stacks ABOVE the form (capability + logos + reviews), then the form below. The credibility curtain still leads — the form still follows.

## What NOT to take
- Don't soften the hard edge between the two halves. The vertical contrast IS the pattern's affordance — a gradient or neutral spacer collapses the credibility-curtain register into a generic dark-themed contact page. The dark column's job is to feel like a separate panel from the form.
- Don't use this pattern if the dealer doesn't have real customer logos or real reviews to populate the trust stack. Both stand alone (capability bullets + reviews works without logos; capability bullets + logos works without reviews) but the page needs at least 2 of the 3 (capability / logos / reviews) populated. With only the capability bullets, the dark column reads as decorative chrome rather than credibility curtain.
- Don't make the form longer than ~8 fields. The whole reason the credibility curtain earns its weight is that the form is short enough to be ignorable until the user is convinced. A 15-field intake form on the right pushes the trust stack into the background; the page becomes a regular form.
- Don't add a CTA inside the dark column (e.g. "Try free / Book demo"). The dark column is non-interactive — it's a credibility surface. Adding a CTA there competes with the form's submit button on the right and breaks the "trust pre-frame, then form" cadence.
- Don't combine this with the [two-card decision pivot](./refero-376ca585-two-card-decision-pivot.md) — the pivot routes intent BEFORE a form; this pattern skips routing because the audience is already self-selected (they came from a "Contact Sales" link). For dealers who need both intent routing AND credibility pre-frame, use the pivot first then link to dedicated forms.
- Don't import the literal ElevenLabs capability bullets ("Unlimited characters, voices and concurrency", "Enterprise-grade security including SOC II, GDPR and HIPAA compliance") — those are AI-product specific. The transferable move is "3 short capability bullets that name the dealer's actual differentiation" — for a Terraplex dealer this is "[Part 137-certified](../../../content/compliance-program.md) application services / Same-day fleet diagnostics / On-site [R-32](../../../products/r-32.md) and [I-19](../../../products/i-19.md) demo flights" — claims the dealer can defend.

## Adaptation notes (cross-archetype)
- TACTICAL: native fit. Near-black left column, precision-sans heading and bullets, monochrome-with-cyan logo wall (cyan accents on the active state of any logo or on the star rating only). Sharper rectangle CTAs (~6px corner radius) on the right form. Add a thin 1px corner-bracket detail to the dark column's top-right and bottom-right corners (the corners that meet the white column) — consistent with the archetype's bracket motif. See [TACTICAL archetype](../../../archetypes/tactical.md). Pairs with the [hardware-lineup dark hero](../../hero/research/refero-0945a36c-hardware-lineup-dark-hero.md) on a parent page.
- CLEAN: lighten the contrast — left column becomes brand-navy (~#1F2C44) instead of near-black, white text and bullets, single brand-accent star color in the review row. Form on the right is plain white with neutral-gray field borders and a brand-navy "Submit" pill. The split-screen contrast still exists; it's just navy-vs-white instead of black-vs-white. See [CLEAN archetype](../../../archetypes/clean.md).
- TERRAIN: the dark left column reads as the page's "dark band" anchor; the white form column is the "light band." Add a thin topographic divider as the dark column's bottom edge (a contour motif tracing the hard split line). Capability bullets in the dark column use clay or sage accent colors on the icons. The hard vertical split IS the alternating-band rhythm at single-section scale. See [TERRAIN archetype](../../../archetypes/terrain.md).
- FORGE: native fit. Near-black left column, ember-orange capability-bullet icons, ember-orange star rating, ember-orange "Submit" pill on the form. Optional ember-warm rim light along the dark column's right edge (where it meets the white form) — gives the panel a forge-glow feel. The logo wall stays monochrome white; only the icons + stars + CTA carry ember. See [FORGE archetype](../../../archetypes/forge.md). Pairs with the [topographic layered-bg hero](../../hero/research/refero-ae84b62a-topographic-layered-bg.md).
- PATRIOT: replace the dark-charcoal left column with deep navy (~#1F2C44), cream text and bullets, cream-on-navy logo wall. Star rating in cream. The single barn-red moment is the "Submit" pill on the form (cream label on barn-red pill). Capability bullets reference heritage credibility ("Built in the Americas, for the American Farmer" — see [content/positioning.md](../../../content/positioning.md) — adapted into the bullet voice: "Built and serviced in [region]"). See [PATRIOT archetype](../../../archetypes/patriot.md). Pairs with the [manifesto-creed hero](../../hero/research/refero-b86571f2-manifesto-creed-hero.md).
- FIELD: warm-charcoal left column (~#3A2B1F instead of pure black) so the column reads as warm-dark rather than corporate-dark. Cream text and bullets, golden-amber capability-bullet icons. Star rating in amber. The logo wall is muted-cream on warm-charcoal. The right form column uses cream background instead of pure white, with warm-amber field focus rings and an amber "Submit" pill. The pattern works in FIELD when the dealer wants to surface warm credibility (real-farmer testimonials, regional logos) without abandoning the warm palette to a hard black panel. See [FIELD archetype](../../../archetypes/field.md). Pairs with the [manifesto-creed hero](../../hero/research/refero-b86571f2-manifesto-creed-hero.md).

## Source brief
ElevenLabs's [/contact-sales](https://elevenlabs.io/contact-sales) page — a B2B AI-product Contact Sales page with a hard split-screen credibility-curtain layout. The library-worthy move is the structural cadence (50/50 dark-left / white-right split, dark column carries heading + capability bullets + customer-trust caption + logo wall + star-rating row, white column carries the contact form + paired CTAs), reusable for a Terraplex dealer who needs the contact form to overcome buyer hesitation — heritage dealers, dealers introducing premium [Part 137 service tiers](../../../content/compliance-program.md), or dealers competing against more established regional distributors. The dark column's job is to address the skepticism the customer arrived with; the form's job is to collect data once the skepticism has been addressed.
