## Summary

Two regulatory developments in the past three weeks materially affect Terraplex dealers heading into the summer spray season. **(1) FAA — FIFA World Cup 2026 No Drone Zones**: the FAA news release dated **May 29, 2026** establishes a layered TFR architecture — stadium rings (3-nautical-mile / 3,000 ft AGL, match days only, in all 11 US host cities), fan-festival rings (1-nautical-mile / 1,000 ft AGL), and **100+ team-base-camp rings (1-nautical-mile / surface-to-400 ft AGL)** that activate continuously from **June 1, 2026 through July 21, 2026** at training facilities in cities **including Boise ID, Louisville KY, and Sandy UT that are NOT on the 11 US host-city list**. The base-camp TFRs follow the FIFA Team Base Camp map for all 48 qualified national teams and can sit up to 500 miles from any match venue; Part 107 / Part 137 authorizations stop at the stadium ring (no recreational or low-altitude commercial access without specific ATC authorization); civil penalties up to **$75,000 per violation**, criminal fines up to **$100,000**; the FAA's **DETER initiative** (captured in the [2026-05-04 digest](2026-05-04.md)) is the enforcement vehicle. **(2) FCC — second wave of Covered List Conditional Approvals**: the FCC Public Safety and Homeland Security Bureau on **May 18, 2026** granted Conditional Approvals to **Elevon Aerial Ag's Z30, Z50, and Z80 agricultural UAS** (US-assembled outside St. Louis, EFT-derived airframes aggressively transitioning toward >65% USA-manufactured components) and **Air6 Systems** platforms — exemptions valid through **December 31, 2026**, coordinated through DoD/DHS national-security review. This is the **second batch** of Conditional Approvals after the first wave on March 17–18, 2026, and is the materialization of the Wiley Rein "first Conditional Approvals" watch-item flagged in the [2026-05-13 digest's](2026-05-13.md) monitoring follow-up.

## Why this matters for Terraplex dealers

**The World Cup TFRs are immediately operational for Part 137 spray crews.** The headline restriction is the stadium ring (3 nm radius / 3,000 ft AGL on match days), but the **operationally important** TFR for ag operators is the **base-camp ring** — 1 nautical mile from the surface up to 400 feet AGL, **continuously active from June 1 through July 21, 2026** — and the FAA placed these around 48 national-team training facilities, **many of which sit in cities that are not hosting matches**. Boise, Louisville, and Sandy UT carry continuous restrictions despite no match-day footprint. The 400-foot ceiling is precisely where ag-spray drones operate, and the 1-nautical-mile radius covers roughly a 1.3-square-mile area each — meaning any [Hylio](../manufacturers/hylio.md), [GTEEX Revolution](../manufacturers/terraplex.md), or [DJI Agras](../manufacturers/dji-agras.md) airframe flying within that footprint without specific ATC authorization is exposed to the **$75K civil / $100K criminal** penalty schedule, with the [FAA DETER expedited-enforcement program effective 2026-04-17](2026-05-04.md) as the active enforcement vehicle. Dealers servicing Part 137 operators in or near any of the affected counties should be (a) checking the FAA published TFR venue list (the [2026-05-31 dronexl piece](https://dronexl.co/2026/05/31/faa-publishes-world-cup-tfr-venue-list/) covers the host-city list; the [June 3 dronexl follow-up](https://dronexl.co/2026/06/03/world-cup-drone-tfr-base-camps-far-from-host-cities/) covers the base-camp expansion), (b) coordinating with operators on field-by-field flight planning around any covered training facility, and (c) reminding customers that **Part 137 transit access through Standard UAFRs (preserved in the [Section 2209 NPRM proposed § 74.250](2026-05-06.md))** is **not** the same thing as authorization through a World Cup TFR — these are distinct regulatory carve-outs and the World Cup restrictions override Part 137 transit privileges for the duration.

**The FCC Conditional Approval expansion materially widens the dealer-pitch landscape.** The structural anchor of the past month of digests — that the [December 22, 2025 FCC Covered List ruling](../manufacturers/dji-agras.md) creates a durable "Built in the Americas" advantage for domestic OEMs — gets refined by the May 18 Conditional Approval grant. **Elevon Aerial Ag** is a US-assembly story: their Z30 (entry-level, ~400 acres/day capability), Z50 (50-ft swath, ~570 acres/day at 45 ft/s), and Z80 (heavier-payload) platforms are **assembled in the United States outside St. Louis**, derived from EFT (Chinese-origin) airframes, with the company publicly stating it is "aggressively transitioning towards > 65% USA-manufactured components." The Conditional Approval through December 31, 2026 means these are now sales-eligible US-assembled platforms with explicit FCC sign-off — a **third domestic-assembly option** for dealers alongside [Hylio](../manufacturers/hylio.md) (US-designed-and-built, with the [KULR battery collaboration](https://www.globenewswire.com/news-release/2026/02/18/3240208/0/en/KULR-and-Hylio-Announce-Strategic-Collaboration-to-Produce-Texas-Manufactured-Battery-Systems-for-U-S-Built-Unmanned-Agricultural-Drones.html) addressing the long-flagged battery-supply caveat — context: KULR-Hylio JDC announced 2026-02-18) and [GTEEX Revolution](../manufacturers/terraplex.md) (US-assembled, Revolution Drones-built; see [2026-05-03 digest](2026-05-03.md)). The honest dealer framing is that Elevon Aerial Ag occupies a **different point on the compliance gradient**: it is conditionally approved (expires year-end 2026, must be reauthorized) rather than NDAA-natively-compliant by design, and the airframe lineage is foreign-origin even though the assembly is domestic. For dealers in territories where customers value FCC sign-off but cannot wait for Hylio production capacity or GTEEX Revolution lead times, Elevon is now a legitimate third option in the conversation. For Sam Welton's [compliance program](../../content/compliance-program.md), the existence of the Conditional Approval pathway — and its expiration timeline — is curriculum-relevant: prep-course students need to understand that "FCC-authorized" can mean either Covered-List-exempt-by-design or Conditional-Approval-and-must-be-renewed, and these are not the same status.

## Key facts

### FAA FIFA World Cup 2026 No Drone Zones — May 29 news release; layered TFR architecture; June 1 – July 21 active window

- **FAA news release dated May 29, 2026**: "FAA Establishes 'No Drone Zones' for FIFA World Cup 2026 Stadiums, Fan Events and Base Camps" [source: https://www.faa.gov/newsroom/faa-establishes-no-drone-zones-fifa-world-cup-2026-stadiums-fan-events-and-base-camps] [source: https://dronelife.com/2026/05/29/faa-creates-world-cup-no-drone-zones-across-u-s-host-cities/]
- **Tournament window**: FIFA World Cup 2026 runs **June 11 through July 19, 2026** in the United States as primary host; FAA security restrictions span an envelope from **June 1 through July 21, 2026** to cover pre-tournament arrival and post-tournament departure logistics [source: https://dronelife.com/2026/05/29/faa-creates-world-cup-no-drone-zones-across-u-s-host-cities/] [source: https://www.uasvision.com/2026/06/04/faa-establishes-no-drone-zones-for-fifa-world-cup-2026-stadiums-fan-events-and-base-camps/]
- **Stadium-ring TFR specs (match days only, all 11 US host cities)**: prohibits all aircraft operations — including drones — within a **3-nautical-mile radius up to 3,000 feet AGL** around the stadium unless specifically authorized by air traffic control [source: https://www.faa.gov/newsroom/faa-establishes-no-drone-zones-fifa-world-cup-2026-stadiums-fan-events-and-base-camps] [source: https://dronelife.com/2026/05/29/faa-creates-world-cup-no-drone-zones-across-u-s-host-cities/]
- **Fan-event-ring TFR specs**: drone flights prohibited within a **1-nautical-mile radius up to 1,000 feet AGL** at fan festival and team-event locations [source: https://www.faa.gov/newsroom/faa-establishes-no-drone-zones-fifa-world-cup-2026-stadiums-fan-events-and-base-camps] [source: https://dronelife.com/2026/05/29/faa-creates-world-cup-no-drone-zones-across-u-s-host-cities/]
- **Base-camp-ring TFR specs (most operationally relevant for Part 137 ag-spray)**: more than **100 UAS-security TFRs** covering team training facilities; each TFR is **1 nautical mile in radius from the surface up to 400 feet AGL**; restrictions are **continuously active June 1 – July 21, 2026** (not toggled match-by-match like stadium rings) [source: https://www.flyingmag.com/faa-issues-drone-tfrs-for-world-cup-base-camps/] [source: https://dronexl.co/2026/06/03/world-cup-drone-tfr-base-camps-far-from-host-cities/]
- **Base camps placed in non-host cities**: the base-camp TFR list follows FIFA's published Team Base Camp map for all **48 qualified national teams**; restricted locations include **Boise ID, Louisville KY, and Sandy UT** — none of which appear on the 11-US-host-city list, and some sit up to **500 miles from any match venue** [source: https://dronexl.co/2026/06/03/world-cup-drone-tfr-base-camps-far-from-host-cities/] [source: https://www.flyingmag.com/faa-issues-drone-tfrs-for-world-cup-base-camps/]
- **Penalty schedule**: civil penalties up to **$75,000 per violation**; criminal fines up to **$100,000**; FBI authorized to use specialized mitigation tools to intercept and seize drones; possible criminal prosecution and immediate arrest [source: https://www.faa.gov/newsroom/faa-establishes-no-drone-zones-fifa-world-cup-2026-stadiums-fan-events-and-base-camps] [source: https://dronelife.com/2026/05/29/faa-creates-world-cup-no-drone-zones-across-u-s-host-cities/]
- **Active enforcement vehicle**: the FAA's **DETER (Drone Expedited and Targeted Enforcement Response) initiative** — effective **2026-04-17** per the [2026-05-04 digest](2026-05-04.md) — is the named enforcement mechanism for accelerated identification and prosecution of drone violations during the tournament [source: https://dronelife.com/2026/05/29/faa-creates-world-cup-no-drone-zones-across-u-s-host-cities/] [source: https://www.uasvision.com/2026/06/04/faa-establishes-no-drone-zones-for-fifa-world-cup-2026-stadiums-fan-events-and-base-camps/]
- **FAA Administrator Bryan Bedford on-record framing**: "the FAA is using every available tool to protect the airspace, including stronger drone-enforcement efforts" [source: https://dronelife.com/2026/05/29/faa-creates-world-cup-no-drone-zones-across-u-s-host-cities/]
- **Part 107 authorization status**: per the [dronexl May 31 venue-list piece](https://dronexl.co/2026/05/31/faa-publishes-world-cup-tfr-venue-list/), "Part 107 Authorizations Stop At The Stadium Ring" — meaning a standing Part 107 waiver does **not** carry through a World Cup TFR; specific ATC authorization is required for any covered operation [source: https://dronexl.co/2026/05/31/faa-publishes-world-cup-tfr-venue-list/]

### FCC second-wave Covered List Conditional Approvals — May 18, 2026; Elevon Aerial Ag (Z30/Z50/Z80) and Air6 Systems through December 31, 2026

- **FCC Public Safety and Homeland Security Bureau action dated May 18, 2026** granted Conditional Approval — expiration **December 31, 2026** — to **Elevon Aerial Ag's Z30, Z50, and Z80 uncrewed aircraft systems** and to **Air6 Systems** platforms (also Nokia Wi-Fi 8 routers in the same approval batch, on the broader Covered List) [source: https://dronelife.com/2026/05/18/cc-covered-list-drone-exemptions-elevon-air6/] [source: https://broadbandbreakfast.com/fcc-expands-conditional-drone-approvals-under-covered-list/] [source: https://convergedigest.com/fcc-exempts-nokia-wi-fi-8-router-elevon-uas/]
- **Conditional Approval pathway** (statutory mechanism): companies submit materials to the FCC; the FCC coordinates with the **Department of Defense and the Department of Homeland Security** to determine whether the system presents an unacceptable national security risk; if approved, the system is **exempted from Covered List restrictions for a defined period** — currently set to expire end of 2026 for the May 18 batch [source: https://broadbandbreakfast.com/fcc-expands-conditional-drone-approvals-under-covered-list/] [source: https://convergedigest.com/fcc-exempts-nokia-wi-fi-8-router-elevon-uas/]
- **Elevon Aerial Ag corporate profile**: US-based; **every Z50P is assembled outside St. Louis**; the company publicly states it is "aggressively transitioning towards > 65% USA-manufactured components"; airframe lineage is **EFT-derived** (Chinese-origin frame design); positions itself as Pacific Northwest distributor for the broader Elevon line [source: https://www.elevonaerialag.com/]
- **Elevon platform specs (from company materials, relevant to dealer comparison)**:
  - **Z30**: entry-level cost, pro-grade performance; "small enough to ride on an ATV"; rated capability **~400 acres per day** [source: https://www.elevonaerialag.com/]
  - **Z50**: **50-ft swath**, **45 ft/s flight speed**; rated capability **~570 acres per day** [source: https://www.elevonaerialag.com/]
  - **Z80**: heaviest-payload Z-series; specific spec not surfaced in search summaries; load capacities of the Z-series fall in the 30 kg / 50 kg range with quick-switching spray and spreading modules and IP67 waterproofing [source: https://www.elevonaerialag.com/]
- **Part 137 compliance**: per company materials, all three of the Z30/Z50/Z80 "have received conditional approvals by the FCC and are fully compliant for Part 137 operations in the US" — i.e., the FCC Conditional Approval is the operational unlock for US ag-spray use [source: https://www.elevonaerialag.com/]
- **Wave sequencing relative to prior FCC action**: the [Wiley Rein "first Conditional Approvals" watch-item](2026-05-13.md) referenced the FCC's first Conditional Approvals from **March 17–18, 2026**; the May 18 batch is the **second wave** under the same pathway, indicating the Conditional Approval process is now an operational, repeating mechanism — not a one-off March accommodation [source: https://dronelife.com/2026/03/20/fcc-updates-covered-list-introduces-first-conditional-approvals-for-drone-systems/] [source: https://dronelife.com/2026/05/18/cc-covered-list-drone-exemptions-elevon-air6/]
- **Caveat — Conditional Approval is not blanket approval**: per the FCC's own materials referenced in the trade coverage, Conditional Approval does NOT represent a blanket approval for all products from a manufacturer, and **all conditional approvals are temporary and set to expire at the end of 2026** — Elevon's Z-series and Air6's platforms will need to be re-approved (or the regime modified) to continue past December 31, 2026 [source: https://broadbandbreakfast.com/fcc-expands-conditional-drone-approvals-under-covered-list/]

## Implications

- **For dealer pre-tournament conversations with Part 137 customers** (immediate, before June 11 first-match day): pull the FAA published TFR venue list (referenced in [dronexl May 31](https://dronexl.co/2026/05/31/faa-publishes-world-cup-tfr-venue-list/) and [dronexl June 3](https://dronexl.co/2026/06/03/world-cup-drone-tfr-base-camps-far-from-host-cities/)) and cross-reference against customer field maps. Any customer whose acres fall within a 1-nautical-mile ring of a team base camp — including operators in Boise ID, Louisville KY, Sandy UT, and other non-host-city locations — needs to know they cannot fly under Part 137 transit authority alone during **June 1 – July 21, 2026**. The honest message is: "if your fields are within ~1.3 square miles of any listed training facility, you need either specific ATC authorization or to schedule the work outside the seven-week window."
- **For dealer-customer "what platform should I buy?" conversations** (Q3/Q4 2026 onboarding cycle): the May 18 Elevon Conditional Approval expands the **US-assembled-and-FCC-cleared** option set to three named platforms — [Hylio](../manufacturers/hylio.md), [GTEEX Revolution](../manufacturers/terraplex.md) (distributed by Terraplex), and Elevon Aerial Ag. Dealers should be precise about the **compliance gradient**: Hylio is NDAA-natively-compliant by design with US-domestic battery supply now in JDC with KULR (context: KULR-Hylio JDC announced 2026-02-18); GTEEX Revolution is US-assembled in NC/KS/NE with Revolution Drones as the named assembler (see [2026-05-03 digest](2026-05-03.md)); Elevon Aerial Ag is US-assembled outside St. Louis with EFT-lineage airframes operating under FCC Conditional Approval that **expires December 31, 2026** and must be re-approved to continue. These are three different points on the same gradient, not interchangeable equivalents.
- **For Sam Welton's [compliance program](../../content/compliance-program.md) curriculum** (medium-term, summer 2026 cohort): add a module distinguishing **Covered-List-exempt-by-design** (Hylio, GTEEX Revolution category) from **Conditional-Approval-and-renewable** (Elevon Aerial Ag, Air6 Systems category). Prep-course students need to understand that "FCC-authorized" is not a single status and that the renewal cadence matters operationally — Conditional Approvals expire end-of-2026, so a fleet purchase decision tied to that pathway carries renewal-risk that an NDAA-natively-compliant platform does not. The World Cup TFR rollout is also curriculum-relevant as a **fresh case study** in how Part 137 transit authority and event-driven TFRs interact (they don't override each other; both regimes apply simultaneously).
- **For onboarding scripts** when prospects ask "are there US-assembled options I'm not aware of besides Hylio and the GTEEX Revolution?": the accurate post-May-18 answer can include Elevon Aerial Ag's Z30/Z50/Z80 (US-assembled outside St. Louis, EFT-derived airframes, FCC Conditional Approval through 2026-12-31, Part 137 compliant per company materials). The qualifier is that this is a **conditional** authorization rather than a structural one — dealers should not represent Elevon as having the same long-term compliance posture as Hylio or GTEEX Revolution.
- **For monitoring follow-up** (next 30–60 days):
  - Watch for the **next Conditional Approval batch** at the FCC — the March 17–18 first wave and May 18 second wave are now establishing a cadence; the next batch will tell us whether the pathway is becoming an industry-wide mechanism or staying narrow
  - Watch for **first FAA enforcement action** under the World Cup TFR regime — DETER's first reported ag-adjacent prosecution (if any) will set the tone for how strictly the 1-nautical-mile / 400-foot base-camp rings are enforced against legitimate Part 137 transit operations
  - Watch for **FCC Commission-level disposition** of the DJI / Autel Petitions for Reconsideration (ET Dockets 26-22 / 26-23) — reply-comment window closed May 11, 2026 per the [2026-05-09 digest](2026-05-09.md); the Trusty defense ([2026-05-11 digest](2026-05-11.md)) and Robbins XPONENTIAL defense ([2026-05-14 digest](2026-05-14.md)) signal reconsideration unlikely to succeed but no vote date is published
  - Watch for any **DJI Agras / Autel** Conditional Approval submission — neither has yet been reported in the trade press as applying through the Conditional Approval pathway; if either does, that would meaningfully reshape the dealer-pitch landscape
