## Summary

DJI Agriculture debuted the **Agras T55** and the **Agras T100 Dual Battery Spraying System** at **AGRITECHNICA Asia 2026** (held at BITEC, Bangkok, Thailand) on **May 20, 2026**, and both are now listed for sale in Thailand. The T55 is a 50-litre-class spray platform with DJI's latest safety systems; the T100 pairs a 100-litre spray tank with coaxial dual-rotor power and a dual-battery lift configuration. The launch matters less for what it puts in front of US dealers — neither new SKU is FCC-authorized for the US market — than for the contrast it draws: DJI's heavy-payload line keeps advancing on the global calendar while its newest airframes remain gated out of the United States under the December 22, 2025 FCC Covered List ruling (context: ruling occurred 2025-12-22).

## Why this matters for Terraplex dealers

This is a competitor product-line event, not a US availability change. The [DJI Agras profile](../manufacturers/dji-agras.md) already records the key constraint: under the FCC's December 22, 2025 Covered List action, **new** foreign-made SKUs cannot obtain FCC authorization, and DJI's still-newer platforms (T100, T70P, T25P) are precisely the products inside DJI's stated **$860M new-SKU US revenue exposure**. The T55 was flagged as **still blocked** for the US in the [2026-05-13 digest](2026-05-13.md). So a Bangkok debut of the T55 and T100 is a clean illustration of the structural gap Terraplex dealers are selling into: DJI can ship a 100-litre dual-battery flagship in Thailand in May 2026, but a US farmer cannot buy that same airframe through normal channels. The honest dealer framing stays consistent with the [DJI profile's](../manufacturers/dji-agras.md) "SKU-by-SKU availability check" guidance — only now there are two more globally-launched models that reinforce, rather than reopen, the US availability gap.

The second reason this matters is competitive spec-anchoring. The T55 (50 L spray) and T100 (100 L spray tank) bracket the heavy-payload class where Terraplex's lineup competes — the [I-19](../../products/i-19.md) (20-gal liquid sell-sheet class) sits between them, and the Revolution-series [R-32](../../products/r-32.md) (≥30-gal spray per the [2026-05-03 digest](2026-05-03.md)) plays at the top of that class. When a prospect benchmarks against "the new DJI flagship," dealers can now point to a concrete, current competitor data point and steer the conversation to what the DJI airframe cannot offer a US buyer: a domestically-supportable, FCC-clear, dealer-serviced platform (see [content/positioning.md](../../content/positioning.md) — "the dealer IS the competitive advantage" and "Built in the Americas").

## Implications

- **For dealer positioning copy**: this is a "DJI keeps moving, but not here" data point. Use it to keep the [DJI profile's](../manufacturers/dji-agras.md) SKU-by-SKU availability discipline current — when a prospect references a "new DJI" (T55 or T100), the accurate response is that these are globally-launched 2026 platforms that are **not FCC-authorized for new US sale** under the Covered List, not new options for a US fleet. Pair this with the [content/positioning.md](../../content/positioning.md) "Built in the Americas" beat rather than with any claim that DJI has exited — its installed base and global momentum are real.
- **For pricing conversations**: no US pricing applies (the SKUs aren't sold here), and **no price was disclosed** in the launch materials even for Thailand. Do not quote or imply a US price for the T55/T100. The comparison to make is capability-class, not dollar-for-dollar.
- **For onboarding scripts**: when a prospect asks "isn't DJI still the leader / shouldn't I just wait for the next DJI?", the grounded answer is that DJI's newest heavy-payload airframes (T55, T100) launched abroad in May 2026 and remain gated from new US sale — so "waiting for the next DJI" is, under current FCC rules, waiting for a product that cannot be sold here, whereas the [I-19](../../products/i-19.md) and [R-32](../../products/r-32.md) are available and dealer-supported today.
- **For the [DJI Agras profile](../manufacturers/dji-agras.md)** (monthly intelligence-pass follow-up): the next revision should append the T55 and T100 Dual Battery system to the lineup with the May 20, 2026 AGRITECHNICA Asia debut and the 50 L / 100 L payload specs, noting both remain outside US FCC authorization.
- **For monitoring**: watch whether any of these newly-launched DJI SKUs appear in a future FCC Conditional Approval list (the Wiley Rein "first Conditional Approvals" watch-item from the [2026-05-13 digest](2026-05-13.md) remains the structural anchor) — that, not a global product debut, is the event that would change US availability.
