## Summary

**KULR Technology Group** (Webster, TX; NASDAQ: KULR) and **Factorial Inc.** (Woburn, MA; solid-state and lithium-metal cell developer; SPAC-merging with Cartesian Growth Corp III) announced on **May 21, 2026** a partnership to integrate Factorial's next-generation solid-state and lithium-metal cells into KULR's drone-battery pack architectures, with initial demonstrations shown at **XPONENTIAL 2026 in Detroit** (May 12–14). The deal is part of a three-continent Factorial integrator program — KULR (US), **Tulip Tech** (Netherlands), and **JRES** (South Korea) — explicitly targeting the commercial, industrial, and defense drone markets. KULR is, since the **February 18, 2026** joint-development collaboration announcement, **[Hylio's](../manufacturers/hylio.md) named partner for designing, prototyping, qualifying, and domestically manufacturing NDAA-compliant battery systems in Texas** for Hylio's unmanned agricultural drones (context: KULR–Hylio collaboration announced 2026-02-18). The May 21 KULR–Factorial announcement therefore points at a plausible cell-chemistry pipeline into Hylio's NDAA-compliant Texas battery line — closing in on the exact gap that the [Hylio profile's](../manufacturers/hylio.md) revision-2026-05-01 caveat flagged as "in work with partners but not yet shipping."

## Why this matters for Terraplex dealers

This is the **cell-chemistry-side** counterpart to the May 14 [ePropelled launch](2026-05-16.md) (motors + ESCs + intelligent power management). Where ePropelled addressed three of the five component classes named by the Pentagon's Cadenazzi at his May 12 XPONENTIAL Detroit keynote (motors, ESCs, intelligent power), KULR–Factorial addresses the **batteries** class — the largest of the five-component-class gap and, in the Hylio case, the single named caveat in the existing [manufacturer profile](../manufacturers/hylio.md). The honest dealer reframe is now that **two of the five Cadenazzi-flagged component classes have publicly visible domestic-allied supply-chain responses on the same calendar as the May 12 Pentagon keynote**: ePropelled on May 14 for motors/ESCs/power management, and KULR–Factorial on May 21 for next-generation cell chemistry plus an existing KULR–Hylio joint-development collaboration for Texas-manufactured NDAA-compliant pack assemblies (context: KULR–Hylio collaboration announced 2026-02-18). The supply-side response to Section 805 / 1260H enforcement is no longer a one-off data point; it is now a recognizable pattern landing within the same week as the regulatory pressure.

The important caveat is the same as the May 16 digest: **no Hylio design-win on Factorial cells has been announced**. Factorial's May 21 partner list names KULR as the US integration partner, not Hylio. The line of sight is **Factorial cells → KULR pack architecture → KULR–Hylio Texas-made NDAA-compliant batteries → Hylio drones** — but only the first two links are confirmed, and the second link's downstream output (Hylio-bound Texas-made packs) does not have a published cell-chemistry source yet. Dealers should treat this as **"the supply chain is now coherent in writing across two sourceable inputs"** rather than as a confirmed Hylio BOM change. The honest dealer-customer message: "the structural NDAA-compliance gap that the Hylio profile flagged at the cell-chemistry layer now has a named domestic-allied solid-state-cell pipeline upstream of Hylio's stated battery partner — but Hylio has not publicly confirmed they will ship Factorial-cell packs."

For the [DJI Agras](../manufacturers/dji-agras.md) installed base, this changes nothing on the near-term horizon — the [2026-05-13 digest's](2026-05-13.md) FCC DA 26-454 firmware-waiver extension to at least January 1, 2029 keeps existing T-series fleets supported. Where it does change the conversation is on the **next-fleet** question: a domestic-OEM Hylio (or GTEEX Revolution via [Terraplex](../manufacturers/terraplex.md), or Ceres Air, Exedy Drones, or Pyka) whose motors+ESCs+power-management could plausibly come from ePropelled and whose pack cells could plausibly come from a Factorial-supplied KULR line is now a defensibly NDAA-compliant target for 2027-and-later replacement decisions.

## Key facts

### KULR–Factorial drone-battery integration partnership (May 21, 2026)

- **KULR Technology Group** announced May 21, 2026 it is advancing next-generation drone battery pack integration using **Factorial Inc.'s solid-state and lithium-metal battery cell technologies**; the announcement is part of a broader Factorial multi-partner initiative spanning the United States, Europe (Netherlands), and Asia-Pacific (South Korea) [source: https://www.manilatimes.net/2026/05/21/tmt-newswire/globenewswire/kulr-technology-group-advances-next-generation-drone-battery-integration-with-factorial-cells/2349319] [source: https://www.stocktitan.net/news/KULR/kulr-technology-group-advances-next-generation-drone-battery-nv35u67m0p8c.html]
- **Factorial's three named integrator partners**: **KULR Technology Group** (US, leads US integration); **Tulip Tech** (Netherlands, developing battery packs using Factorial cells for advanced unmanned aircraft systems); **JRES** (South Korea, supporting commercial and industrial drone integration in Asia-Pacific) [source: https://www.manilatimes.net/2026/05/21/tmt-newswire/globenewswire/factorial-partners-with-top-integrators-across-three-continents-to-advance-next-generation-battery-integration-for-drone-systems/2349276] [source: https://www.benzinga.com/markets/tech/26/05/52715352/cartesian-spac-factorial-forges-global-agreements-with-drone-integrators]
- **Executive on-record quote — Michael Mo, Chief Executive Officer of KULR Technology Group**: *"Factorial is pushing the frontier of solid-state and lithium-metal battery innovation, and its next-generation cell technologies have the potential to meaningfully expand what is possible across electrified flight applications."* [source: https://www.manilatimes.net/2026/05/21/tmt-newswire/globenewswire/kulr-technology-group-advances-next-generation-drone-battery-integration-with-factorial-cells/2349319]
- **Executive on-record quote — Siyu Huang, CEO of Factorial**: *"We couldn't be more excited to have KULR as a strategic partner. They bring best-in-class battery platform and pack integration capabilities to a U.S. drone market that is scaling faster than ever."* [source: https://www.manilatimes.net/2026/05/21/tmt-newswire/globenewswire/factorial-partners-with-top-integrators-across-three-continents-to-advance-next-generation-battery-integration-for-drone-systems/2349276]
- **Demonstration venue**: KULR and Factorial showcased initial battery pack demonstrations at **XPONENTIAL 2026 in Detroit** (May 12–14, 2026 — same conference where Cadenazzi delivered his Section 805 / 1260H component-supply-chain keynote on May 12, captured in the [2026-05-14 digest](2026-05-14.md)) [source: https://www.stocktitan.net/news/KULR/kulr-technology-group-advances-next-generation-drone-battery-nv35u67m0p8c.html] [source: https://finance.yahoo.com/sectors/technology/articles/kulr-technology-group-advances-next-130000859.html]
- **Application targets**: longer-range, more autonomous UAS missions across **commercial, industrial, and defense** uses; KULR is contributing expertise in battery safety, thermal management, pack design, and mission-critical energy systems [source: https://www.manilatimes.net/2026/05/21/tmt-newswire/globenewswire/kulr-technology-group-advances-next-generation-drone-battery-integration-with-factorial-cells/2349319]
- **Factorial cell-chemistry claims** (as reported in DRONELIFE coverage of the announcement): Factorial's **FEST platform** is reported to deliver up to **50% more energy density** than conventional lithium-ion (i.e., higher Wh/kg, the critical metric for UAV endurance and payload), with longer flight duration, stronger takeoff and thrust performance, and reliable operation across a wide temperature range [source: https://dronelife.com/2026/05/21/factorial-solid-state-drone-batteries/]

### Pre-existing KULR–Hylio collaboration (context — announced February 18, 2026)

- (context: collaboration announced 2026-02-18) **KULR Technology Group** and **Hylio, Inc.** signed a Joint Development Collaboration in February 2026 to design, prototype, qualify, and domestically manufacture **NDAA-compliant battery systems in Texas** for integration into Hylio's unmanned aerial systems platforms, with the engineering, integration, and manufacturing activities centered in Texas and an emphasis on secure US-based supply chains [source: https://www.globenewswire.com/news-release/2026/02/18/3240208/0/en/KULR-and-Hylio-Announce-Strategic-Collaboration-to-Produce-Texas-Manufactured-Battery-Systems-for-U-S-Built-Unmanned-Agricultural-Drones.html] [source: https://dronelife.com/2026/02/18/new-partnership-building-farming-drone-batteries-in-texas/]
- (context: same announcement) The KULR–Hylio collaboration is framed as producing battery architectures **tailored to [Hylio's](../manufacturers/hylio.md) agricultural and defense-adjacent applications**, with the explicit goal of meeting federal procurement requirements for agricultural and defense drone applications [source: https://www.globenewswire.com/news-release/2026/02/18/3240208/0/en/KULR-and-Hylio-Announce-Strategic-Collaboration-to-Produce-Texas-Manufactured-Battery-Systems-for-U-S-Built-Unmanned-Agricultural-Drones.html]
- The combined picture: a documented Hylio → KULR (Texas-made packs) → Factorial (solid-state and lithium-metal cells) supply chain is now stated publicly across two separate announcements three months apart. The **Hylio → KULR link is a confirmed Joint Development Collaboration**; the **KULR → Factorial link is a confirmed integration partnership with public demo at XPONENTIAL Detroit**. What remains **unconfirmed** is whether any specific Hylio-shipping battery pack will source Factorial cells, or whether KULR's Hylio-bound packs will use different cell chemistry. No party has named the cell supplier for the Hylio packs.

### Alignment with May 12 Cadenazzi component-supply-chain keynote

- **Pentagon Assistant Secretary of War for Industrial Base Policy Michael Cadenazzi** named the focus of Section 805 / 1260H supply-chain scrutiny in his **May 12, 2026 XPONENTIAL Detroit** keynote as: **flight controllers, motors, batteries, electronic speed controllers, and rare-earth inputs** (context: keynote captured in the [2026-05-14 digest](2026-05-14.md))
- Two of those five component classes now have publicly visible domestic-allied supply-chain responses landing within ~10 days of the May 12 keynote: **motors / ESCs / intelligent power management** via ePropelled's May 2026 ag-UAV launch (captured in the [2026-05-16 digest](2026-05-16.md)) and **batteries (cell chemistry layer)** via the May 21 KULR–Factorial announcement
- **Section 805 statutory milestones** (context: enacted in FY24 NDAA, referenced in [2026-05-14 digest](2026-05-14.md)): direct-procurement ban against 1260H-listed entities **June 30, 2026**; indirect-supplier ban **June 30, 2027**; Cadenazzi confirmed contractor-notification pilots begin during 2026

### Caveats — what the announcement does NOT establish

- **No Hylio design-win on Factorial cells is announced**. Factorial's May 21 partner list names KULR (US), Tulip Tech (Netherlands), and JRES (South Korea) — not [Hylio](../manufacturers/hylio.md) [source: https://www.manilatimes.net/2026/05/21/tmt-newswire/globenewswire/factorial-partners-with-top-integrators-across-three-continents-to-advance-next-generation-battery-integration-for-drone-systems/2349276]
- **No specific Hylio-bound pack BOM is published**. Neither the May 21 KULR–Factorial announcement nor the February 18 KULR–Hylio announcement names the cell supplier for the Hylio-bound NDAA-compliant Texas-made battery packs [source: https://www.manilatimes.net/2026/05/21/tmt-newswire/globenewswire/kulr-technology-group-advances-next-generation-drone-battery-integration-with-factorial-cells/2349319] [source: https://www.globenewswire.com/news-release/2026/02/18/3240208/0/en/KULR-and-Hylio-Announce-Strategic-Collaboration-to-Produce-Texas-Manufactured-Battery-Systems-for-U-S-Built-Unmanned-Agricultural-Drones.html]
- **No solid-state cell production at scale yet**. The Factorial integrator program is positioned as forward-looking — the announcement describes "initial battery pack demonstrations" at XPONENTIAL Detroit, not commercial deliveries. **Factorial itself is mid-SPAC-merger with Cartesian Growth Corp III** (NASDAQ-listed shell) — i.e., not yet operating as a public company [source: https://www.benzinga.com/markets/tech/26/05/52715352/cartesian-spac-factorial-forges-global-agreements-with-drone-integrators]
- **No pricing has been disclosed** for either Factorial cells, KULR pack assemblies, or any prospective Hylio-bound NDAA-compliant pack

## Implications

- **For dealer-customer "Built in the Americas" conversations**: the script can now be sharpened from May 16's "ePropelled closes the propulsion-side gap in the Made in America pitch" to **"the propulsion-side AND the cell-chemistry-side gaps both now have named domestic-allied suppliers, landing within ~10 days of the May 12 Pentagon keynote that flagged exactly these component classes."** Do **not** claim that [Hylio](../manufacturers/hylio.md) has confirmed Factorial cells in its battery packs — that is not stated. Do claim that the domestic-allied component supply chain across motors, ESCs, intelligent power management, and now solid-state cell chemistry is filling in on the same calendar as Section 805 enforcement.
- **For the [Hylio manufacturer profile](../manufacturers/hylio.md)** — when the profile next refreshes (monthly intelligence pass cadence), the revision-2026-05-01 caveat "batteries are still China-sourced, with new battery chemistry 'in work with partners' but not yet shipping" should be augmented with: (a) the named partner is **KULR Technology Group** (joint-development collaboration announced 2026-02-18); (b) KULR has, as of 2026-05-21, a separate integration partnership with **Factorial Inc.** for solid-state and lithium-metal cells; (c) the **specific Hylio-pack cell supplier remains unnamed**, so the "in work with partners but not yet shipping" claim from the prior profile remains literally accurate, but the partner pipeline is now publicly traceable in writing across two announcements. **Suggested revision**: split the battery caveat into a "domestic-pack-assembly partner (named: KULR, Texas)" sub-bullet and a "cell-chemistry source (unnamed; KULR has a separate integration partnership with Factorial for solid-state cells)" sub-bullet.
- **For [Terraplex](../manufacturers/terraplex.md) / [GTEEX Revolution](../manufacturers/terraplex.md) airframe positioning**: this is **a watch item, not an action item**. Revolution Drones (the assembler of the GTEEX Revolution lineup that Terraplex distributes) has not publicly named a battery-pack partner — Hylio appears to be ahead on this dimension. If Terraplex wants to match the "Made in America BOM-coherence" pitch that Hylio's KULR-then-Factorial chain implies, the question to surface internally is whether Revolution Drones has a comparable named domestic-allied battery-pack partner, and whether that partner has a comparable named domestic-allied cell-chemistry pipeline. The honest current state: Terraplex's airframe-and-assembly story is on par with Hylio's; the battery-supply-chain story is harder to evaluate because Revolution Drones has not been as publicly explicit about its pack-and-cell sourcing.
