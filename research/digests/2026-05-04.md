## Summary
The FAA's Drone Expedited and Targeted Enforcement Response (DETER) Program took effect April 17, 2026 and was published in the Federal Register the same day. It creates a fast-track settlement path for individual first-time small-UAS violators — admit liability, waive appeal, accept a reduced civil penalty or shortened certificate suspension, and complete the steps within 10 days — and is being staged ahead of the FIFA World Cup window (June 12 – July 19) but applies year-round to commercial Part 107 operators including farmers and ag service providers. Separately, AgFunder News this week framed Santa Clara-based SiFly's NDAA-compliant Q250 — a 30-gallon-payload heavy-lift airframe with quoted endurance of about 100 minutes and roughly 2× the cruise speed of incumbent Chinese platforms — as a credible new entrant in the ag-spray segment that dealers should expect to field customer questions about.

## Why this matters for Terraplex dealers
DETER does not add a new rule, but it changes how quickly an FAA enforcement action lands and removes the appeals-heavy timeline operators previously assumed. For the small-UAS (sub-55-lb) Part 107 layer that surrounds an ag-drone operation — scouting flights, the farmer flying their own utility drone alongside the contracted spray drone, the operator-helper logging hours in the wrong airspace — first-offense violations now resolve in ~10 days at a reduced penalty if the operator opts in, or proceed through the slower standard track if they don't. The dealer-actionable change is the talking point: "you'll usually just get a warning" is no longer accurate even informally; the FAA has formalized a settlement-or-litigation choice and the program is structured to be used. Onboarding scripts and Part 107 prep materials (see [content/compliance-program.md](../../content/compliance-program.md)) should reflect the new posture rather than continue with discretionary-warning framing carried over from prior years.

The SiFly framing is a different kind of dealer signal — not a regulatory shift but a competitive-landscape one. AgFunder choosing to feature SiFly's ag-spray applicability in the same editorial cluster as the post-FCC realignment pieces means dealers should expect a "what about SiFly?" prospect question alongside the existing "what about Hylio?" and "what about Pyka?" questions. The honest competitive frame: SiFly's Q250 quotes a 30-gal payload (≈113 L) in a category between the [I-19](../../products/i-19.md) and the largest multirotor airframes; the company makes its own batteries and is NDAA-compliant out of the box (a real differentiator vs [Hylio](../manufacturers/hylio.md), which still sources batteries from China); and the company is post-stealth but pre-volume, so allocation risk is high. SiFly is not yet shipping at the cadence that would let a dealer write it into a 2026 fleet quote, but it deserves a row in the internal competitive-tracking grid.

## Key facts

### Item 1: FAA DETER program effective April 17, 2026 — fast-track enforcement for small-UAS first-time violators
- The Drone Expedited and Targeted Enforcement Response (DETER) Program, announced by the FAA and published in the Federal Register on April 17, 2026 as a Notice of Settlement Policy for Small Unmanned Aircraft System (UAS) Cases (Document 2026-07585), expedites enforcement against small-UAS operators who violate FAA regulations [source: https://www.faa.gov/newsroom/faa-launches-new-program-accelerate-enforcement-drone-violations] [source: https://www.federalregister.gov/documents/2026/04/17/2026-07585/settlement-policy-for-small-unmanned-aircraft-system-uas-cases]
- Eligibility: individual first-time offenders only, on operational violations of small-UAS rules; a pilot may use the program one time [source: https://www.suasnews.com/2026/04/new-faa-deter-program-offers-fast-track-penalties-for-first-time-drone-offenders/]
- Settlement structure: the operator admits liability and waives the right to appeal; in exchange, the FAA offers a reduced civil penalty, a shortened certificate suspension, or required corrective actions, with a 10-day window to complete the agreed steps [source: https://www.suasnews.com/2026/04/new-faa-deter-program-offers-fast-track-penalties-for-first-time-drone-offenders/] [source: https://www.faa.gov/newsroom/faa-launches-new-program-accelerate-enforcement-drone-violations]
- Exclusions: cases involving alcohol or drugs, weaponized drones, violations of temporary flight restrictions, operations in restricted airspace, or violations posing significant safety risk continue through standard FAA enforcement procedures, where civil penalties can reach $75,000 per violation [source: https://www.suasnews.com/2026/04/new-faa-deter-program-offers-fast-track-penalties-for-first-time-drone-offenders/]
- Scope statement applies to commercial Part 107 users including farmers and ag service providers; sUAS News explicitly notes the policy "applies to small unmanned aircraft system (UAS) operators, including commercial users such as farmers and ag service providers" [source: https://www.suasnews.com/2026/04/new-faa-deter-program-offers-fast-track-penalties-for-first-time-drone-offenders/]
- Program context: tied to the executive order on "Restoring American Airspace Sovereignty" and is being staged to support enforcement around the FIFA World Cup matches running June 12 – July 19, 2026, but is not time-limited to that window [source: https://www.faa.gov/newsroom/faa-launches-new-program-accelerate-enforcement-drone-violations] [source: https://www.suasnews.com/2026/04/new-faa-deter-program-offers-fast-track-penalties-for-first-time-drone-offenders/]
- Builds on the FAA's 2025 enforcement-posture shift previously documented in the [April 2026 regulatory roundup](../regulatory/2026-04-update.md) (legal action now required, not discretionary, when operations endanger the public, violate airspace, or further another crime) (context: that posture shift formalized through 2025–2026; DETER is the procedural mechanism that operationalizes it for first-time small-UAS cases)

### Item 2: SiFly framed by AgFunder as credible NDAA-compliant ag-spray entrant — Q250 quotes 30-gal payload, ~100-min endurance
- SiFly (Santa Clara, CA; founded 2021; led by Brian Hinman, founder of Polycom and Mimosa) emerged from stealth on May 1, 2025 and was reframed in an AgFunder ag-spray feature this week as a potentially disruptive entrant alongside [Hylio](../manufacturers/hylio.md), Revolution Drones, Exedy Drones, and Ceres Air in the post-FCC US landscape [source: https://agfundernews.com/faster-further-longer-can-sifly-upend-the-ag-spray-drone-market]
- Product line cited: the Q12 (mapping/scouting) and the larger Q250 (heavy-lift, ag-spray candidate); the Q250 is quoted at 30 gallons (≈113 L) of capacity, "more than double" DJI's core ag-spraying payload at the time of the article [source: https://agfundernews.com/faster-further-longer-can-sifly-upend-the-ag-spray-drone-market]
- Performance claims: SiFly says its drones cruise at roughly 2× the speed of comparable systems and an investor cited 3-hour endurance vs ~30-minute endurance for Chinese peers — design rationale: battery weight is 60–62% of total weight (target ~66%), enabled by a low-disc-loading design that admits energy-dense (rather than high-power) cells [source: https://agfundernews.com/faster-further-longer-can-sifly-upend-the-ag-spray-drone-market]
- NDAA compliance and supply chain: SiFly manufactures its own batteries and complies with the recent NDAA restrictions on certain Chinese-origin communications components — a clean structural differentiator versus Hylio, which still sources batteries from China and is publicly working to "de-Asia" its supply chain (cf. [Hylio profile](../manufacturers/hylio.md) for the China-battery caveat) [source: https://agfundernews.com/faster-further-longer-can-sifly-upend-the-ag-spray-drone-market]
- Funding: backed by Venture capital firm The First Thirty, with the investor cited in the AgFunder piece pointing to ag disruption potential as the primary thesis [source: https://agfundernews.com/faster-further-longer-can-sifly-upend-the-ag-spray-drone-market]
- Caveat for dealer use: SiFly's ag-spray credibility is built on stated specs and a single AgFunder feature; the company is post-stealth but has not yet been profiled in a recurring trade-press cadence the way DJI Agras and Hylio have. No US dealer channel is documented at this stage and allocation cadence is unknown — see "Items considered but rejected" in the PR body for sourcing notes.

## Implications

- **For dealer onboarding scripts and Part 107 prep**: update the enforcement-posture section to reflect DETER specifically. The legacy "if you mess up, you'll probably just get a warning the first time" framing should be replaced with "the FAA offers a fast-track settlement on a first offense — reduced penalty in exchange for waiving appeal, 10 days to complete — but that is structured as a *settlement*, not a warning, and it goes on your record." Pair this update to [content/compliance-program.md](../../content/compliance-program.md)'s Sam Welton Part 107 prep course materials so the same language flows into the prep curriculum.
- **For dealer-customer conversations on operator hand-off**: explicitly call out that DETER eligibility is single-use per pilot. A farmer-operator who has already used DETER once (e.g., on a sub-55-lb scouting drone) cannot use it again on a separate Part 137 violation later. This is a real reason to invest in proper training upfront rather than treat the first violation as a cheap learning experience.
- **For dealer competitive-tracking**: add SiFly to the internal grid alongside DJI Agras / Hylio / Pyka. The two SiFly facts that dealers should be ready to address in customer conversations are (a) NDAA compliance is structurally cleaner than Hylio's "de-Asia in progress" position, and (b) the Q250's 30-gal payload sits between the I-19 and the largest fixed-wing/multirotor platforms — it is a competitor in payload class but not yet in shipping volume.
- **For positioning copy**: do not yet write SiFly into customer-facing comparison decks. The AgFunder framing is a single-domain ag feature on top of widely corroborated stealth-exit product specs from May 2025; the company is real but not yet at the cadence where dealer copy can credibly say "available now" or "compare us to SiFly." Watch for a second trade-press piece (AgWeb, Successful Farming, or sUAS News) before promoting it from internal-tracking to customer-facing material.
- **For territory and channel thinking**: NDAA-compliant US-built capacity is now a multi-vendor story (Hylio scaling to 5,000+/yr in Texas, Revolution Drones in NC, Exedy Drones in MI, SiFly in CA). The dealer-channel competition for the next 12 months is less about which vendor "wins" and more about which vendor lands a *distribution* relationship in each territory before the FCC appeal resolves — see [research/dealer-economics/competitor-distributors.md](../dealer-economics/competitor-distributors.md) for the structural-comparison anchor and [Terraplex profile](../manufacturers/terraplex.md) for the 49-location network context.
