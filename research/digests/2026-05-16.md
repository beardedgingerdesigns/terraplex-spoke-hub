## Summary

**ePropelled** (Laconia, New Hampshire — global facilities US/UK/India, founded 2018, 40+ patents across 13 categories) launched in May 2026 a **fully integrated propulsion and power system designed specifically for agricultural UAVs**, combining high-thrust brushless motors, electronic speed controllers (ESCs), and intelligent power management into a single optimized architecture for heavy-payload spraying and spreading missions. The announcement, datelined Laconia NH and carried by **sUAS News**, **Vertical Mag**, **Global Ag Tech Initiative**, and **Comunicaffe** in the past 24–48 hours, names Chief Commercial Officer Dean Marcarelli as the company spokesperson and frames the product as a turnkey reference design for OEMs building ag-spray UAVs. The launch lands four days after **Assistant Secretary of War for Industrial Base Policy Michael Cadenazzi** named **flight controllers, motors, batteries, electronic speed controllers, and rare-earth inputs** — i.e., precisely this stack — as the focus of federal Section 805 / 1260H supply-chain scrutiny in his May 12 XPONENTIAL Detroit keynote (context: keynote captured in the [2026-05-14 digest](2026-05-14.md)). ePropelled's prior 2026 milestones (Sparrow motor-line expansion for defense UAVs, December 9, 2025; hybrid-propulsion partnership with Hirth Engines, January 13, 2026) establish the defense-credibility runway for the ag-UAV pivot.

## Why this matters for Terraplex dealers

This is a **supply-chain-side response** to the regulatory architecture captured across the past two weeks of digests — and it lands on the exact component stack that the Pentagon publicly flagged on May 12. The [Hylio profile's](../manufacturers/hylio.md) revision-2026-05-01 baseline explicitly qualifies the "Made in America" pitch with the caveat that **batteries are still China-sourced** and **new battery chemistry is "in work with partners" but not yet shipping** — meaning Hylio's domestic-OEM narrative has known holes in the propulsion-and-power layer. An integrated motor + ESC + intelligent-power-management system from a US/UK/India-footprint supplier with existing defense-UAV credentials (the Sparrow line ships into mission-critical DoD applications) is the kind of product that, if it gets OEM design wins inside the domestic ag-drone supply chain, **closes the propulsion-side gap in the "Made in America" pitch** that the [2026-05-14 digest's](2026-05-14.md) Cadenazzi-pillar enforcement timeline will start exposing on June 30, 2026 (direct-procurement cutoff) and June 30, 2027 (indirect-supplier cutoff). It is too early to know whether [Hylio](../manufacturers/hylio.md), Revolution Drones (the assembler of [GTEEX Revolution](../manufacturers/terraplex.md) airframes that Terraplex distributes), or other US-based ag-drone OEMs will adopt ePropelled's reference design — neither party has confirmed a customer announcement — but the product's existence is the data point that matters: **the domestic-allied component supply chain is filling in on the same calendar as the regulatory pressure**. Dealers should treat this as supporting infrastructure for the "Built in the Americas" story rather than as a confirmed BOM change.

The complementary dealer-conversation reframe is on the **time-to-replacement** question for existing [DJI Agras](../manufacturers/dji-agras.md) fleets. The [2026-05-13 digest's](2026-05-13.md) FCC DA 26-454 firmware-waiver extension to at least January 1, 2029 means existing T30/T40/T50 customers do not need to harvest their fleets early. But the ePropelled launch — combined with the Cadenazzi May 12 supply-chain timeline — points at where the **next-fleet** decision should land: a domestic-OEM airframe (Hylio, GTEEX Revolution) whose motor/ESC/power stack will, plausibly within 12–24 months, be sourceable from a US/UK/India-footprint supplier rather than from China. The honest dealer message is "your existing DJI fleet has firmware support through 2028; your next fleet should plan for a domestic OEM whose component supply chain will be NDAA-compliant on the same calendar as Section 805 enforcement."

## Key facts

### ePropelled launches integrated propulsion + power system for agricultural UAVs (May 2026, Laconia NH)

- ePropelled, a **global technology provider specializing in smart propulsion solutions and energy management systems for uncrewed vehicle operations**, announced in May 2026 the launch of a fully integrated propulsion and power solution **designed specifically for agricultural UAVs** — described as targeting "one of the fastest growing and most commercially mature segments of the global drone industry" [source: https://www.suasnews.com/2026/05/epropelled-launches-integrated-propulsion-and-power-system-to-accelerate-next-generation-of-agricultural-uavs/] [source: https://verticalmag.com/press-releases/epropelled-launches-integrated-propulsion-and-power-system-for-agricultural-uavs/]
- **Press release dateline**: Laconia, New Hampshire — ePropelled Group corporate location, with additional facilities in the UK and India [source: https://www.globalagtechinitiative.com/market-watch/epropelled-launches-integrated-propulsion-and-power-system-to-accelerate-next-generation-of-agricultural-uavs/] [source: https://www.comunicaffe.com/epropelled-launches-integrated-propulsion-and-power-system-to-accelerate-next-generation-of-agricultural-uavs/]
- **System architecture** — integrates: (a) high-efficiency brushless motors with high thrust density to support heavy-payload spraying and spreading missions; (b) advanced electronic speed controllers (ESCs); (c) intelligent power management for sustained performance and extended flight time; (d) advanced thermal management for performance in harsh environments; (e) integrated motor-and-ESC control for precise and consistent spray application [source: https://www.suasnews.com/2026/05/epropelled-launches-integrated-propulsion-and-power-system-to-accelerate-next-generation-of-agricultural-uavs/] [source: https://verticalmag.com/press-releases/epropelled-launches-integrated-propulsion-and-power-system-for-agricultural-uavs/]
- **Executive on-record quote** — Dean Marcarelli, Chief Commercial Officer: *"With agricultural drones becoming a core part of farm operations globally, propulsion is no longer just a component decision, it is a mission critical enabler. By integrating propulsion, control, and intelligent power into a single optimized system, we are enabling a new generation of UAVs that can operate longer, carry more, and perform more reliably in real world conditions."* [source: https://www.suasnews.com/2026/05/epropelled-launches-integrated-propulsion-and-power-system-to-accelerate-next-generation-of-agricultural-uavs/] [source: https://www.comunicaffe.com/epropelled-launches-integrated-propulsion-and-power-system-to-accelerate-next-generation-of-agricultural-uavs/]
- **Market sizing referenced in press release**: the global agricultural drone market is projected to **exceed $4 billion in 2026** and is expanding at annual growth rates of **20–30%**, driven by precision agriculture, labour efficiency, and reduced chemical inputs [source: https://www.suasnews.com/2026/05/epropelled-launches-integrated-propulsion-and-power-system-to-accelerate-next-generation-of-agricultural-uavs/] [source: https://www.globalagtechinitiative.com/market-watch/epropelled-launches-integrated-propulsion-and-power-system-to-accelerate-next-generation-of-agricultural-uavs/]

### ePropelled corporate profile — US/UK/India footprint, defense-UAV credentials

- **Founded 2018** with over **40 patents across 13 categories**; facilities in the **USA (Laconia NH), UK, and India** [source: https://www.globalagtechinitiative.com/market-watch/epropelled-launches-integrated-propulsion-and-power-system-to-accelerate-next-generation-of-agricultural-uavs/] [source: https://www.comunicaffe.com/epropelled-launches-integrated-propulsion-and-power-system-to-accelerate-next-generation-of-agricultural-uavs/]
- **Prior milestone — Sparrow motor line for defense UAVs**: ePropelled announced an expansion of its **Sparrow motor line for mission-critical defense UAVs** on December 9, 2025 — the defense-credibility predicate for the ag-UAV launch (context: defense-UAV product line precedes ag-UAV launch by ~5 months) [source: https://dronelife.com/2025/12/09/epropelled-expands-sparrow-motor-line-for-mission-critical-defense-uavs/]
- **Prior milestone — Hirth Engines hybrid-propulsion partnership**: ePropelled and Hirth Engines announced a **strategic collaboration on hybrid UAV power systems on January 13, 2026** — establishes hybrid-electric range/endurance capability for heavy-payload missions, plausibly relevant to longer-duration ag operations [source: https://dronelife.com/2026/01/13/hirth-engines-epropelled-partner-for-hybrid-uav-power-systems/]

### Alignment with May 12 XPONENTIAL Cadenazzi keynote on Section 805 component scrutiny

- At his **May 12, 2026 XPONENTIAL Detroit** keynote, **Assistant Secretary of War for Industrial Base Policy Michael Cadenazzi** named the specific component classes that are the focus of federal Section 805 / 1260H supply-chain scrutiny: **flight controllers, motors, batteries, electronic speed controllers, and the rare-earth inputs they depend on** (context: keynote captured in full in the [2026-05-14 digest](2026-05-14.md))
- ePropelled's integrated propulsion-and-power system covers three of the five flagged classes (motors, ESCs, intelligent power management) and is being marketed by a US/UK/India-footprint supplier — i.e., outside the 1260H supply chain [source: https://www.suasnews.com/2026/05/epropelled-launches-integrated-propulsion-and-power-system-to-accelerate-next-generation-of-agricultural-uavs/]
- **Section 805 statutory milestones** (context: enacted in FY24 NDAA, milestones referenced in [2026-05-14 digest](2026-05-14.md)): direct-procurement ban against 1260H-listed entities **June 30, 2026**; indirect-supplier ban **June 30, 2027**. Cadenazzi confirmed contractor-notification pilots begin **during 2026**

### Caveats — what the announcement does NOT establish

- **No US ag-drone OEM design win has been announced** as of this digest. The press release frames the product as available for OEM adoption; it does **not** name [Hylio](../manufacturers/hylio.md), Revolution Drones, Exedy Drones, Pyka, SiFly, Ceres Air, or any other named US ag-drone OEM as a confirmed customer [source: https://www.suasnews.com/2026/05/epropelled-launches-integrated-propulsion-and-power-system-to-accelerate-next-generation-of-agricultural-uavs/] [source: https://verticalmag.com/press-releases/epropelled-launches-integrated-propulsion-and-power-system-for-agricultural-uavs/]
- **No pricing has been disclosed** in the press materials; system is positioned as an OEM/reference-design offering rather than as an end-user retrofit kit
- **The product does not address the battery side of the [Hylio profile's](../manufacturers/hylio.md) "in work with partners" caveat** — ePropelled's role is intelligent power management (ESCs, motor control, thermal), not cell chemistry; Hylio's still-pending domestic battery chemistry remains a separate open question
