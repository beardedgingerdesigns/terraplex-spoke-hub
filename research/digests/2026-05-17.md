## Summary

**Natrion** (Buffalo, New York; battery-materials company; prior production-facility opening in Western NY supported by New York Power Authority funding) announced **May 14, 2026** the launch of two new defense-optimized battery cell product lines — **Cirrus** (anode-free architecture) and **Stratus** (lithium-metal architecture) — both built on the company's proprietary **Active Separator** material, both **certifiable to NDAA Section 842 supply-chain requirements**, and both explicitly designed for uncrewed systems including **drones**, surface and underwater vessels, ground vehicles, and humanoid systems. The cells deliver up to **~80% greater energy density** than conventional lithium-ion alternatives — anode-free cells exceeding **450 Wh/kg** vs the conventional Li-ion baseline of ~250–280 Wh/kg — in a **pouch format dimensionally equivalent to packs built from standard 21700-type cylindrical Li-ion cells**, so existing battery packs can in principle be re-engineered without weight or volume penalty. Manufacturing leverages Natrion's existing high-volume Active Separator line in Buffalo; Cirrus is designed for **low-cost, open-air assembly with standard equipment and less than two days of aging** (vs significantly longer for conventional Li-ion). Natrion is currently **shipping samples for user testing** — no production volume or named OEM design wins disclosed. The announcement lands **two days after the [2026-05-16 digest's](2026-05-16.md) ePropelled propulsion-and-power-system launch** for ag UAVs and **two days after the Pentagon's Cadenazzi May 12 XPONENTIAL keynote** that named batteries (alongside flight controllers, motors, ESCs, and rare-earth inputs) as the focus of Section 805 / 1260H supply-chain scrutiny (context: keynote captured in the [2026-05-14 digest](2026-05-14.md)).

## Why this matters for Terraplex dealers

This is the **battery-chemistry-side** complement to yesterday's ePropelled motor-and-ESC announcement — and it lands on the specific gap the [Hylio profile's](../manufacturers/hylio.md) revision-2026-05-01 explicitly flagged. Hylio's own characterization on May 1 was that "batteries are still made in China," that the company has been "trying to de-Asia our supply chain from day one," and that it is "working on new battery chemistry with some partners," but that **no domestic battery is shipping yet**. Natrion is now a publicly visible domestic supplier shipping samples of NDAA-Section-842-certifiable cells at a chemistry tier (anode-free / lithium-metal, 450+ Wh/kg) substantially above conventional Li-ion. Combined with the separately announced **KULR + Hylio joint development collaboration to design, prototype, qualify, and domestically manufacture NDAA-compliant battery systems in Texas for Hylio ag UAVs** (announced February 18, 2026; not within the 24-hour window but directly relevant — *context: Feb 18 announcement, captured here because it changes the read on today's news*), the picture is that **the US-domestic ag-drone battery stack is filling in at both the cell-chemistry layer (Natrion) and the pack-integration layer (KULR/Hylio) on the same calendar as Section 805 enforcement**. Whether or not Hylio specifically adopts Natrion cells, the existence of a domestic anode-free / lithium-metal cell supplier with a Buffalo-NY manufacturing line that is certifiable under NDAA Section 842 is the data point that closes the "but where do the cells come from?" objection to the "Made in America" pitch.

The dealer-conversation framing is now: **(a) the propulsion-and-power side of the Section 805 component stack is being addressed by [ePropelled](../digests/2026-05-16.md) (motors / ESCs / intelligent power management) at the OEM-reference-architecture layer; (b) the battery-cell-chemistry side is being addressed by Natrion (anode-free Cirrus / lithium-metal Stratus) at the cell layer**, with both announcements landing in the same calendar week as Cadenazzi's XPONENTIAL keynote naming both component classes. Two weeks ago, the most accurate dealer answer to "is the domestic supply chain actually going to be NDAA-compliant or is this paper compliance?" was "the regulatory architecture is in place but the supply chain is still partly TBD." After May 12–15, the more accurate answer is "the regulatory architecture and the domestic-supplier announcements are landing on the same calendar — motors and ESCs from a US/UK/India supplier with defense credentials on May 14, and anode-free / lithium-metal cells from a Buffalo NY supplier certifiable to NDAA Section 842 on May 14." Dealers should still NOT claim that [Hylio](../manufacturers/hylio.md) or Revolution Drones have adopted either supplier — neither has been announced as a design win — but the supporting-infrastructure narrative is now visibly underwritten on both component axes.

A second-order point worth tracking: Natrion's pouch-format-equivalent-to-21700-cylindrical claim means the **upgrade path is realistic for existing US-assembly ag UAVs** that today use Li-ion packs built from 21700 cells. If the form factor really is drop-in compatible — and the company is saying it is — then domestic-OEM platforms that today ship with imported cells can in principle be re-engineered without redesigning the airframe's battery bay. The qualification path to a real Hylio (or Revolution Drones / Exedy Drones / Pyka / Ceres Air) BOM change is still gated on flight-test qualification, thermal characterization in ag operating conditions, and cell-pricing-at-volume — none of which is disclosed in the announcement — but the **mechanical compatibility claim removes one of the bigger structural barriers**.

## Key facts

### Natrion launches Cirrus + Stratus battery cell lines for uncrewed systems (May 14, 2026, Buffalo NY)

- **Natrion**, a Buffalo, New York-based American battery materials company, announced on **May 14, 2026** that it has launched defense-optimized battery cells for **uncrewed and battery-powered systems**, with target platforms listed as **drones, surface and underwater vessels, ground vehicles, and humanoid systems** [source: https://dronelife.com/2026/05/15/buffalos-natrion-rolls-out-ndaa-compliant-drone-battery-cells/] [source: https://www.unmannedsystemstechnology.com/2026/05/natrion-introduces-new-battery-product-lines-for-uncrewed-systems/]
- **Two new cell product lines**: **Cirrus** (uses an anode-free architecture) and **Stratus** (uses lithium-metal architecture). Both rely on Natrion's proprietary **Active Separator** material [source: https://dronelife.com/2026/05/15/buffalos-natrion-rolls-out-ndaa-compliant-drone-battery-cells/] [source: https://www.unmannedsystemstechnology.com/2026/05/natrion-introduces-new-battery-product-lines-for-uncrewed-systems/]
- **Energy density**: the new cells deliver **nearly 80% greater energy density** than conventional lithium-ion alternatives. Anode-free cells exceed **450 Wh/kg** vs the conventional Li-ion baseline of approximately **250–280 Wh/kg** [source: https://www.unmannedsystemstechnology.com/2026/05/natrion-introduces-new-battery-product-lines-for-uncrewed-systems/]
- **NDAA compliance**: cells are **certifiable to NDAA Section 842 supply-chain requirements** (Section 842 is the existing NDAA provision restricting DoD procurement of specific Chinese-origin battery cells; certifiability to Section 842 is the recognized domestic-supplier standard) [source: https://dronelife.com/2026/05/15/buffalos-natrion-rolls-out-ndaa-compliant-drone-battery-cells/] [source: https://www.unmannedsystemstechnology.com/2026/05/natrion-introduces-new-battery-product-lines-for-uncrewed-systems/]
- **Form-factor compatibility**: pouch-format cells are **dimensionally equivalent to battery packs built using standard 21700-type cylindrical lithium-ion cells**, enabling Cirrus- and Stratus-based battery packs to be **integrated into existing systems while providing increased energy capacity with virtually no additional weight** [source: https://www.unmannedsystemstechnology.com/2026/05/natrion-introduces-new-battery-product-lines-for-uncrewed-systems/]
- **Manufacturing**: Natrion's existing **high-volume Active Separator manufacturing line in Buffalo, NY** accelerates the rollout. **Cirrus** is designed for **low-cost, open-air assembly with standard equipment** and requires **less than two days of aging** following assembly (vs significantly longer processing periods typical of conventional lithium-ion batteries) [source: https://dronelife.com/2026/05/15/buffalos-natrion-rolls-out-ndaa-compliant-drone-battery-cells/] [source: https://www.unmannedsystemstechnology.com/2026/05/natrion-introduces-new-battery-product-lines-for-uncrewed-systems/]
- **Availability**: Natrion is **shipping samples for user testing**; no production volume targets, named OEM design wins, or cell pricing disclosed in the announcement [source: https://www.unmannedsystemstechnology.com/2026/05/natrion-introduces-new-battery-product-lines-for-uncrewed-systems/]
- **Prior defense-work credibility**: per DroneLife's coverage, the new product lines "build on Natrion's longstanding work with the **U.S. Air Force and Navy**" — i.e., established defense-customer relationships predate the ag-UAV-relevant launch [source: https://dronelife.com/2026/05/15/buffalos-natrion-rolls-out-ndaa-compliant-drone-battery-cells/]

### Natrion corporate context — Buffalo NY manufacturing footprint

- Natrion describes itself as an **American battery materials company** and operates a production facility in **Western New York**, opened with support from **New York Power Authority funding** (date of facility opening referenced on Natrion's news page; predates the May 14 cell-line launch) [source: https://www.natrion.co/news/natrion-opens-new-production-facility-in-western-new-york-supported-by-new-york-power-authority-funding] [source: https://esd.ny.gov/esd-media-center/press-releases/empire-state-development-announces-lithium-battery-company-natrion]
- **Existing Active Separator manufacturing line** in Buffalo is the production substrate that the May 14 cell-line launch builds on, per the announcement [source: https://www.unmannedsystemstechnology.com/2026/05/natrion-introduces-new-battery-product-lines-for-uncrewed-systems/]

### Alignment with the May 12 Cadenazzi keynote and the May 14 ePropelled launch

- At his **May 12, 2026 XPONENTIAL Detroit** keynote, Assistant Secretary of War for Industrial Base Policy **Michael Cadenazzi** named the specific component classes that are the focus of federal Section 805 / 1260H supply-chain scrutiny: **flight controllers, motors, batteries, electronic speed controllers, and the rare-earth inputs they depend on** (context: keynote captured in full in the [2026-05-14 digest](2026-05-14.md))
- **Section 805 statutory milestones**: direct-procurement ban against 1260H-listed entities **June 30, 2026**; indirect-supplier ban **June 30, 2027** (context: from the [2026-05-14 digest](2026-05-14.md)). Cadenazzi confirmed contractor-notification pilots begin **during 2026**
- The **ePropelled integrated propulsion-and-power-system launch for ag UAVs** (motors + ESCs + intelligent power management) was announced in **May 2026** and captured in the [2026-05-16 digest](2026-05-16.md). Between **May 12 (Cadenazzi names motors/ESCs/batteries)**, **May 14 (Natrion launches anode-free + lithium-metal cells)**, and **May 14–15 (ePropelled launches motor + ESC + intelligent-power system)**, four of the five Cadenazzi-named component classes now have publicly announced domestic-allied supplier responses on the same calendar week
- The remaining unaddressed component class from the Cadenazzi list is **flight controllers** (and the **rare-earth inputs** the whole stack depends on); no equivalent domestic-supplier launch has surfaced in this digest window

### Caveats — what the Natrion announcement does NOT establish

- **No named US ag-drone OEM design win** has been announced as of this digest. The press materials do not name [Hylio](../manufacturers/hylio.md), Revolution Drones, Exedy Drones, Pyka, SiFly, Ceres Air, or any other named US ag-drone OEM as a confirmed customer [source: https://dronelife.com/2026/05/15/buffalos-natrion-rolls-out-ndaa-compliant-drone-battery-cells/]
- **No production volume targets, cell pricing, or shipping timeline** disclosed beyond "shipping samples for user testing" [source: https://www.unmannedsystemstechnology.com/2026/05/natrion-introduces-new-battery-product-lines-for-uncrewed-systems/]
- **Section 842 vs Section 805**: the Natrion announcement specifically cites **certifiability to NDAA Section 842** (the existing DoD-procurement battery-supplier provision), not Section 805 (the newer broader supplier-clearance regime captured in the [2026-05-14 digest](2026-05-14.md)). These are related-but-distinct provisions; Section 842 certifiability is the established "domestic / allied battery supplier" credential, and is the relevant qualification standard for the NDAA-compliance pitch that domestic ag-drone OEMs already use
- **KULR + Hylio JDC is separately announced**: a domestic-battery-system collaboration was announced **February 18, 2026** between **KULR** and [Hylio](../manufacturers/hylio.md), with the scope being to "design, prototype, qualify, and domestically manufacture NDAA-compliant battery systems in Texas for integration into Hylio unmanned agricultural drones" (*context: announced 2026-02-18, outside the 24-hour window; cited here because it directly affects how today's Natrion news is read*). Natrion (cells, Buffalo NY) and KULR/Hylio (battery systems / packs, Texas) operate at different points in the battery supply chain — they are not direct competitors and could in principle be complementary [source: https://www.natrion.co/news/]

## Implications

- **For dealer-customer "Built in the Americas" conversations**: after May 12–15, the propulsion-and-power side and the battery-cell-chemistry side of the Section 805 component stack have both received publicly announced domestic-allied supplier launches in the same calendar week as the Pentagon's keynote naming both component classes. The honest framing is "the supply chain is filling in on the regulatory calendar, not lagging it — but no named ag-drone OEM has yet announced adoption of either supplier." Do **not** claim Hylio uses Natrion cells. Do claim that the cell-chemistry-side gap in [Hylio's](../manufacturers/hylio.md) revision-2026-05-01 caveat ("no domestic battery is shipping yet") now has a publicly visible domestic-supplier-side candidate.
