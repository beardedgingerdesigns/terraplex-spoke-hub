## Summary
At the **AUVSI XPONENTIAL 2026 conference in Detroit on Tuesday, May 12, 2026**, three policy actors aligned in public for the first time on the "Made in America" architecture for US drones. (1) **AUVSI President & CEO Michael Robbins**, in his community-address keynote, publicly defended the FCC's broader-than-AUVSI-asked-for December 22, 2025 Covered List sweep — the trade association no longer argues for narrower implementation. (2) **Assistant Secretary of War for Industrial Base Policy Michael Cadenazzi** confirmed the Pentagon will begin notifying contractors of potential Section 1260H supply-chain links **during 2026**, ahead of Section 805's direct-procurement ban taking effect June 30, 2026 and the indirect-supplier ban June 30, 2027 — DJI is on the 1260H list. (3) **FAA Air Traffic Organization COO Franklin J. McIntosh** and **Office of Advanced Aviation Technologies Executive Director Jessica Jones** told the same audience the agency now views drones and drone operators as aircraft and pilots: "We don't see drones as drones, we see drones as aircraft. We don't see drone operators as drone operators. We see them as pilots." McIntosh also previewed a radar-data-sharing test program to be announced within two weeks.

## Why this matters for Terraplex dealers
The [2026-05-11 digest](2026-05-11.md) captured FCC Commissioner Trusty's May 7 SCSP speech as the first sitting-Commissioner public defense of the Covered List rationale and read it as a directional signal that reconsideration was unlikely to succeed at the Commission level. Tuesday's XPONENTIAL programming converts that single-Commissioner signal into a **three-pillar inter-agency-plus-industry consensus**: the FCC controls the equipment-authorization layer, the Department of War controls the supply-chain layer through Section 805 / 1260H, and the FAA controls the airspace-integration layer. The largest industry lobby that originally pushed for narrower FCC implementation has now publicly affirmed the broader sweep. For dealers this hardens the [2026-05-11 digest's](2026-05-11.md) "structurally durable advantage" framing for [GTEEX Revolution](../manufacturers/terraplex.md) and [Hylio](../manufacturers/hylio.md) hardware one more notch: the political-economic environment around foreign-made drones is no longer contested between FCC and industry — it is the operating environment all three actors are building on.

The FAA's "drones as aircraft, operators as pilots" framing is the most immediately operational piece for Sam Welton's [compliance program](../../content/compliance-program.md): Part 137 holders are now framed by the senior FAA air-traffic official as pilots with equal NAS rights, not as a separate regulatory class. This is curriculum-relevant — prep-course students should hear it directly, and the framing supports the airspace-integration argument that ag-drone operators need the same fluency with sectional charts, NOTAMs, and ADS-B as crewed pilots. The Cadenazzi Section 805 timeline is dealer-relevant on a longer horizon: any dealer servicing operators with DoD or DoD-supplier customers (university extension trial work, USDA cost-share research operators, state-government cooperative spray programs that touch federal funding) needs to start tracking which platforms in their fleet trace components back to [DJI Agras](../manufacturers/dji-agras.md) or other 1260H-listed entities, because June 30, 2026 direct enforcement is six weeks away and June 30, 2027 indirect enforcement is fourteen months away.

## Key facts

### XPONENTIAL Detroit 2026 conference convened May 11–14, 2026 at Huntington Place; Tuesday May 12 was the policy-keynote day

- AUVSI's annual XPONENTIAL conference ran **May 11–14, 2026** at Huntington Place in downtown Detroit, co-located with the Michigan Defense Expo (MDEX) [source: https://dronelife.com/2026/05/13/xponential-2026-auvsi-new-phase-drone-industry/]
- The Tuesday May 12 morning programming opened with **AUVSI President & CEO Michael Robbins'** Community Address keynote, followed by **Assistant Secretary of War for Industrial Base Policy Michael Cadenazzi's** keynote and a fireside chat with Robbins, then a panel pairing **FAA Air Traffic Organization COO Franklin J. McIntosh** with **Office of Advanced Aviation Technologies Executive Director Jessica Jones**, moderated by Robbins [source: https://dronexl.co/2026/05/13/auvsi-robbins-defends-fcc-drone-ban-cadenazzi-section-805-pilots/] [source: https://dronexl.co/2026/05/13/faa-mcintosh-drones-aircraft-xponential/]

### Pillar 1 — AUVSI Robbins publicly defended the FCC's broader-than-AUVSI-asked-for Covered List sweep

- Robbins reframed how AUVSI talks about the **FCC's December 22, 2025 Covered List action** (see [DJI Agras profile](../manufacturers/dji-agras.md) for the underlying ruling and DJI's Ninth Circuit Case 26-1029 appeal): "AUVSI pushed for narrower implementation. The White House went broader. Robbins now argues the broader approach was the right call" [source: https://dronexl.co/2026/05/13/auvsi-robbins-defends-fcc-drone-ban-cadenazzi-section-805-pilots/]
- Robbins anchored the address in Detroit's WWII "Arsenal of Democracy" history and described today's autonomy sector as a moment when "industrial scale and production capacity become national priorities"; the keynote's theme line: *"The center of gravity is shifting — from invention alone to integration, manufacturing, trust, and execution"* [source: https://dronelife.com/2026/05/13/xponential-2026-auvsi-new-phase-drone-industry/] [source: https://www.auvsi.org/xponential-2026-michael-robbins-community-address/]
- This is the **first time** the largest US drone-industry trade association has publicly defended the broader sweep rather than continuing to ask for narrower implementation; AUVSI's earlier December 22, 2025 response had qualified support [source: https://dronexl.co/2026/05/13/auvsi-robbins-defends-fcc-drone-ban-cadenazzi-section-805-pilots/]

### Pillar 2 — Pentagon's Cadenazzi confirmed Section 805 pilot notification timeline

- **The Honorable Michael Cadenazzi, Assistant Secretary of War for Industrial Base Policy**, used his keynote and fireside chat with Robbins to set the Department of War's enforcement timeline for **Section 805** of the FY24 NDAA: contractor notifications of potential **Section 1260H** Chinese-military-company supply-chain links will begin **during 2026** [source: https://dronexl.co/2026/05/13/auvsi-robbins-defends-fcc-drone-ban-cadenazzi-section-805-pilots/] [source: https://breakingdefense.com/2026/05/working-outside-the-lines-to-scale-production-isnt-working-cadenazzi/]
- Section 805 statutory milestones (context: enacted in FY24 NDAA): **direct procurement ban** against 1260H-listed entities effective **June 30, 2026**; **indirect-supplier ban** effective **June 30, 2027** [source: https://dronexl.co/2026/05/13/auvsi-robbins-defends-fcc-drone-ban-cadenazzi-section-805-pilots/]
- The components Cadenazzi flagged as the focus of federal scrutiny on drone supply chains: **flight controllers, motors, batteries, electronic speed controllers, and the rare-earth inputs they depend on** [source: https://dronexl.co/2026/05/13/auvsi-robbins-defends-fcc-drone-ban-cadenazzi-section-805-pilots/]
- DJI is on the Section 1260H list — meaning any US defense contractor whose drone subassemblies trace back to DJI or affiliated entities faces the 2027 indirect-procurement cutoff (relevant to the [DJI Agras profile](../manufacturers/dji-agras.md) supply-chain risk discussion) [source: https://dronexl.co/2026/05/13/auvsi-robbins-defends-fcc-drone-ban-cadenazzi-section-805-pilots/]

### Pillar 3 — FAA's McIntosh + Jones reframed drones and drone operators as aircraft and pilots; radar-data-sharing test in two weeks

- **Franklin J. McIntosh**, named permanent FAA ATO COO on **January 26, 2026** after eight months as acting COO, stated onstage: *"We don't see drones as drones, we see drones as aircraft. We don't see drone operators as drone operators. We see them as pilots. Everyone has equal rights and usage to the National Airspace System"* [source: https://dronexl.co/2026/05/13/faa-mcintosh-drones-aircraft-xponential/]
- McIntosh announced that a **new FAA radar-data-sharing test program** will be unveiled "within two weeks" — i.e., by approximately **end of May 2026** — as a step toward integrating uncrewed traffic into the NAS [source: https://dronexl.co/2026/05/13/faa-mcintosh-drones-aircraft-xponential/]
- McIntosh confirmed funding architecture for NAS modernization: **$12.5 billion appropriated for NextGen Phase 1** (radars, voice switches, communications — "foundational infrastructure"); the next request will be **$18 billion** for "common automation platforms — the digital, data-driven traffic management layer where shared situational awareness and integration of new entrants actually happen" [source: https://dronexl.co/2026/05/13/faa-mcintosh-drones-aircraft-xponential/]
- McIntosh and Jones were paired on the panel specifically because Jones, as **Executive Director of the new FAA Office of Advanced Aviation Technologies**, owns the regulatory pathway for the FAA's proposed **Part 108 BVLOS rule** — the rule AUVSI's Robbins identified as the key policy unlock for routine commercial drone operations at scale, including agriculture [source: https://dronelife.com/2026/05/13/xponential-2026-auvsi-new-phase-drone-industry/]

## Implications

- **For dealer-customer conversations**: post-XPONENTIAL the prior digests' "Built in the Americas" framing for [GTEEX Revolution](../manufacturers/terraplex.md) and [Hylio](../manufacturers/hylio.md) hardware can be defended on three independent grounds, not one: FCC equipment authorization (Pillar 1, established December 22, 2025; defended by Trusty May 7 per the [2026-05-11 digest](2026-05-11.md) and now by Robbins May 12), DoD supply-chain enforcement (Pillar 2, Section 805 timeline confirmed by Cadenazzi May 12), and FAA airspace integration (Pillar 3, McIntosh/Jones framing May 12). The "is this just a temporary FCC thing that might evaporate?" objection is meaningfully weaker today than it was a week ago.
- **For Sam Welton's [compliance program](../../content/compliance-program.md) curriculum**: add a curriculum module on the FAA's "drones as aircraft, operators as pilots" reframe. The framing supports the prep-course's existing emphasis on sectional-chart fluency, NOTAM awareness, and airspace etiquette — and gives instructors a senior-FAA-official quote to anchor the lesson. Watch fcc.gov and faa.gov for the radar-data-sharing test program announcement expected by approximately **end of May 2026**; that announcement, when it lands, is the next teachable moment.
- **For onboarding scripts**: when prospects ask "isn't all this DJI ban stuff going to get reversed?", the accurate post-XPONENTIAL answer now includes: "no — AUVSI, the largest drone-industry trade association, publicly defended the broader FCC ruling at their flagship conference on May 12, and the Pentagon confirmed Section 805 supply-chain enforcement starts June 30, 2026 with indirect-supplier enforcement following June 30, 2027. The Ninth Circuit appeal in Case 26-1029 (see [DJI Agras profile](../manufacturers/dji-agras.md)) is on a separate, longer track."
- **For Section 805 supply-chain monitoring** — relevant for dealers servicing operators with any DoD-adjacent customer base (university extension, USDA-funded research operators, state cooperative-extension cost-share programs that touch federal money): the Pentagon's contractor-notification pilot runs **during 2026**; June 30, 2026 is the direct-procurement cutoff and June 30, 2027 is the indirect-supplier cutoff. Dealers should know which platforms in their fleet trace components (flight controllers, motors, batteries, ESCs) back to [DJI Agras](../manufacturers/dji-agras.md) or other 1260H-listed entities, because operators serving DoD-adjacent customers will need to know.
- **For territory-level outreach** in DJI-heavy territories (Iowa, Nebraska, Kansas, Missouri, Illinois — see the [competitor distributors profile](../dealer-economics/competitor-distributors.md)): the May 12 XPONENTIAL programming is the strongest external signal yet to firm up "your installed-base Agras fleet is the fleet you'll be operating for several years" conversations. Three independent policy levers are now publicly aligned — not just the FCC ruling.
- **For monitoring follow-up**: watch (a) the FAA radar-data-sharing test program announcement expected within two weeks of May 12, 2026; (b) the FCC's procedural response to the May 11 reply-comment window closure on ET Dockets 26-22 (DJI) and 26-23 (Autel) — no Commission vote date is published yet; (c) Department of War notifications to specific contractors when those begin in 2026 under the Cadenazzi-confirmed Section 805 pilot timeline. The [April 2026 regulatory roundup](../regulatory/2026-04-update.md) is the structural anchor to update if and when any of these milestones lands.
