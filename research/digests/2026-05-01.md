## Summary
Hylio raised $2M on StartEngine to stand up a Texas facility capable of 5,000+ drones per year, leaning into "Made in America" positioning while disclosing 2025 revenue just under $13M. Separately, DJI quantified the impact of the FCC's December 22, 2025 covered-list ruling at roughly $1.5B in 2026 US sales — $700M tied to 14 already-authorized products and $860M tied to 25 new models DJI had planned to launch this year — as its Ninth Circuit appeal (Case 26-1029) advances.

## Why this matters for Terraplex dealers
The FCC ruling and DJI's own loss projections are reshaping the supplier set US ag-drone dealers can credibly stock and quote on. Dealers selling DJI inventory should expect tighter availability on new SKUs through 2026 even as the 14 already-authorized models remain legal to sell, and should prepare customer talking points for the "what happens to support if DJI loses the appeal?" question that is now unavoidable on the demo floor.

Hylio's capacity build-out is the clearest near-term domestic alternative to track: 5,000-drones-per-year is enough to be a real second source for many dealer territories, but the company has confirmed batteries are still China-sourced, which limits how cleanly the "Made in America" pitch can be made to a customer who interrogates the BOM. Dealers who want to lead with a domestic story should script around airframe/assembly, not full supply chain.

## Key facts

### Item 1: Hylio raises $2M to scale Texas production to 5,000+ drones/year
- Hylio raised $2M on equity crowdfunding platform StartEngine to expand production at a new facility capable of producing 5,000-plus drones per year at full capacity [source: https://agfundernews.com/ag-spray-drone-co-hylio-raises-2m-to-expand-production-capacity-builds-on-made-in-america-positioning]
- CEO Arthur Erickson disclosed: "In 2025 our revenue was just under $13 million and 2026 is looking good" [source: https://agfundernews.com/ag-spray-drone-co-hylio-raises-2m-to-expand-production-capacity-builds-on-made-in-america-positioning]
- Hylio manufactures spray drones in Texas and is positioning as a "Made in America" option, but some components — notably batteries — are still made in China; the company says it has been "trying to de-Asia our supply chain from day one" and is "working on new battery chemistry with some partners" [source: https://agfundernews.com/ag-spray-drone-co-hylio-raises-2m-to-expand-production-capacity-builds-on-made-in-america-positioning]
- Other US entrants moving into the same vacuum include Revolution Drones (founded by North Carolina farmer Russell Hedrick) and Exedy Drones (a unit of EXEDY Globalparts repurposing automotive engineering) [source: https://agfundernews.com/made-in-america-fcc-decision-sparks-scramble-to-localize-ag-spray-drone-production]

### Item 2: DJI puts $1.5B figure on FCC ruling impact as Ninth Circuit appeal proceeds
- DJI projects the FCC's December 22, 2025 covered-list ruling could cost it ~$1.5B in 2026 US sales [source: https://agfundernews.com/dji-fcc-action-could-wipe-out-1-5bn-in-us-drone-sales-in-2026-with-staggering-real-world-consequences]
- Breakdown: $700M from FCC setting aside authorizations for 14 existing DJI products (5 UAS, 9 non-UAS); $860M from inability to sell 25 new UAS and non-UAS models DJI planned to launch in 2026 [source: https://agfundernews.com/dji-fcc-action-could-wipe-out-1-5bn-in-us-drone-sales-in-2026-with-staggering-real-world-consequences]
- DJI filed its appeal at the Ninth Circuit Court of Appeals (Case 26-1029) on February 20, 2026, arguing the ruling is "procedurally and substantively flawed" and that the FCC has never identified a national-security threat associated with DJI products [source: https://agfundernews.com/dji-fcc-action-could-wipe-out-1-5bn-in-us-drone-sales-in-2026-with-staggering-real-world-consequences]
- Existing DJI ag models that were already authorized before the ruling can still be legally bought and deployed in the US under the current FCC framework [source: https://agfundernews.com/made-in-america-fcc-decision-sparks-scramble-to-localize-ag-spray-drone-production]

## Implications
- For dealer positioning copy: lead with "domestic assembly" or "Texas-built airframe" rather than full "Made in America" when discussing Hylio — the China-sourced battery undermines an unqualified claim and a sharp customer will catch it.
- For pricing conversations: do not assume DJI new-SKU availability when quoting 2026 fleet additions; price quotes should specify the model and explicitly note whether it is among the 14 already-authorized SKUs that remain legally sellable, versus a 2026 launch model that may not arrive at all in the US.
- For onboarding scripts: add a "What happens if DJI loses the appeal?" objection handler — the honest answer is that already-purchased and already-authorized DJI ag drones remain legal to operate, support burden shifts to the dealer/distributor channel, and customers concerned about long-term parts/support continuity should be steered toward dual-vendor fleet planning (e.g., DJI plus Hylio) rather than single-vendor commitment.
- For territory planning: Hylio's stated 5,000+ drones/year capacity is the first credible domestic figure to plug into 2027 fleet-addition models — dealers in territories where DJI was the only realistic supplier should reach out to Hylio early to understand allocation policy before the capacity is spoken for.
