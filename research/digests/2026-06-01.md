## Summary

On May 28, 2026, [DJI](../manufacturers/dji-agras.md) released results of an independent security assessment conducted by Florida-based U.S. cybersecurity firm OnDefend, covering the **DJI Air 3S** (with RC 2 controller) and the **DJI Matrice 4E** (with RC Plus 2 Enterprise controller), plus the DJI Fly and DJI Pilot 2 mobile apps. OnDefend ran a five-month adversarial engagement from October 21, 2025 through March 13, 2026, on retail-purchased consumer units and dealer-stock enterprise units; the assessment found **zero critical, zero high, and zero medium-risk findings**, with 10 low-risk findings (weak TLS configurations in the companion app, authentication tokens in URLs) that DJI says it is patching via firmware. DJI commissioned and paid for the engagement — that funding model is the headline caveat — but the retail-procurement methodology was designed to defeat unit-tampering risk by buying off-the-shelf units without notifying DJI.

## Why this matters for Terraplex dealers

The audit doesn't change the FCC regulatory picture — the December 22, 2025 Covered List ruling, the still-pending DJI/Autel Petitions for Reconsideration (ET Dockets 26-22 / 26-23, reply window closed May 11), the DA 26-454 firmware-only patch waiver out to January 1, 2029 (see the [2026-05-13 digest](2026-05-13.md)), and the Section 805 / 1260H supply-chain enforcement timeline that Pentagon's Cadenazzi confirmed at AUVSI XPONENTIAL Detroit (see the [2026-05-14 digest](2026-05-14.md)) — none of that moves. New DJI drones still cannot get FCC authorization; the Covered List ruling does not rest on a specific OnDefend-style technical finding the agency must now revisit. But the audit hands customers a clean talking point ("DJI passed an independent security audit, so why can't I buy a new T70P?") that dealers will encounter immediately, and the answer is *not* "because DJI drones are unsafe." The answer is "because the FCC ruling is a procurement-eligibility decision, not a security-on-the-merits decision, and it stands regardless of vendor-funded audit outcomes."

For Terraplex dealers selling the GTEEX Revolution lineup, this means the [positioning](../../content/positioning.md) needs to anchor on **regulatory durability** (the FCC ruling, the Commissioner Trusty defense of it captured in the [2026-05-11 digest](2026-05-11.md), the three-pillar AUVSI/DoD/FAA consensus in the [2026-05-14 digest](2026-05-14.md), the Pentagon's classified-intelligence opposition memo) rather than on contested security-on-the-merits debate, which OnDefend's report just made harder to win on the technical layer alone. The pitch is "Built in the Americas, locked-in supply chain, no FCC-ruling exposure" — not "DJI drones are dangerous." That language was already correct; the audit reinforces why.

## Key facts

- **Audited products and configurations**: DJI Air 3S with RC 2 controller (consumer); DJI Matrice 4E with RC Plus 2 Enterprise controller (enterprise); DJI Fly and DJI Pilot 2 mobile applications [source: https://www.dji.com/media-center/announcements/dji-drone-independent-security-assessment] [source: https://dronexl.co/2026/05/28/dji-ondefend-security-audit-air-3s-matrice-4e/].
- **Auditor**: OnDefend, a U.S.-based cybersecurity firm (Florida). Engagement ran October 21, 2025 through March 13, 2026 — five months of adversarial testing [source: https://dronelife.com/2026/05/28/dji-releases-independent-security-assessment-as-fcc-covered-list-debate-continues/] [source: https://www.tomshardware.com/tech-industry/dji-commissioned-security-audit-finds-no-major-vulnerabilities-in-two-drone-models-as-fcc-lawsuit-continues].
- **Headline finding**: Zero critical, zero high, zero medium-risk findings across the engagement [source: https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/dji-releases-findings-of-the-most-comprehensive-independent-security-assessment-of-its-drone-systems-to-date-302784340.html] [source: https://dronedj.com/2026/05/28/dji-drone-ban-security-report/].
- **Specific negative findings**: No evidence of data transmission outside the United States; no backdoors or unauthorized remote-access mechanisms; no unexplained radio-frequency emissions; no supply-chain tampering or unauthorized hardware modifications [source: https://dronexl.co/2026/05/28/dji-ondefend-security-audit-air-3s-matrice-4e/] [source: https://petapixel.com/2026/05/28/independent-audit-finds-no-security-basis-for-restricting-dji-in-the-usa/].
- **Low-risk findings (10 total)**: Weak TLS protocol configurations in the companion app and authentication tokens in URLs, described by OnDefend as consistent with standard practices for complex embedded systems; DJI is addressing them via firmware updates [source: https://dronelife.com/2026/05/28/dji-releases-independent-security-assessment-as-fcc-covered-list-debate-continues/] [source: https://www.thedronegirl.com/2026/05/28/dji-security-audit/].
- **Procurement methodology**: Consumer Air 3S units purchased directly from retail without pre-notifying DJI; enterprise Matrice 4E units pulled from existing dealer stock — designed so units under test would match what any US customer would actually receive [source: https://dronexl.co/2026/05/28/dji-ondefend-security-audit-air-3s-matrice-4e/].
- **Funding caveat**: DJI itself commissioned and paid for the assessment — OnDefend describes its testing as independent, but the funder-of-audit dynamic is the dominant methodological critique cited across coverage [source: https://www.thedronegirl.com/2026/05/28/dji-security-audit/] [source: https://www.tomshardware.com/tech-industry/dji-commissioned-security-audit-finds-no-major-vulnerabilities-in-two-drone-models-as-fcc-lawsuit-continues].
- **Scope caveat**: The audit covered the Air 3S and Matrice 4E — not the ag-spray T-series ([T50, T25, T30, T20, T10, T100, T70P, T25P](../manufacturers/dji-agras.md)). Ag-drone dealers should not represent the OnDefend results as covering Agras platforms specifically [source: https://www.dji.com/media-center/announcements/dji-drone-independent-security-assessment].
- **Timing relative to FCC proceeding**: Released May 28, 2026 — 17 days after the May 11 close of the FCC Petition for Reconsideration reply window (ET Dockets 26-22 / 26-23), but the Commission has set no decision timeline; the China Unicom precedent is roughly a three-year reconsideration cycle [source: https://dronelife.com/2026/05/28/dji-releases-independent-security-assessment-as-fcc-covered-list-debate-continues/] [source: https://www.tomshardware.com/tech-industry/dji-commissioned-security-audit-finds-no-major-vulnerabilities-in-two-drone-models-as-fcc-lawsuit-continues].
- **Parallel filing context** (context: filed at the May 11 deadline): Autel's reply at ET Docket 26-23, reported May 19, 2026, separately argued the FCC ruling rests on "secret evidence" and "borrowed allegations aimed at DJI," noting AES-128/256 encryption and local-by-default flight-data storage — different filing, same theme of contesting the technical justification rather than the procurement-eligibility framework [source: https://dronexl.co/2026/05/19/autel-fcc-reply-covered-list-secret-evidence-dji/].

## Implications

- **For dealer positioning copy**: Do *not* drop the "Built in the Americas" narrative in response to this audit, and do *not* pivot to attacking the methodology — the funder-funded-audit critique is widely cited and dealers don't need to litigate it. Reframe instead around regulatory durability: the FCC Covered List ruling is a procurement-eligibility decision binding regardless of vendor-funded audit outcomes. Existing FCC-authorized DJI ag SKUs remain legal to operate (DA 26-454 keeps them patchable through January 1, 2029), but new DJI ag SKUs cannot get FCC authorization — and the OnDefend audit doesn't change either fact.
- **For pricing conversations**: No direct pricing effect. The audit may produce a short-term wave of "wait and see if DJI gets reinstated" customer hesitation; counter by anchoring on the multi-year FCC reconsideration timeline (China Unicom precedent ≈3 years) and DJI's own Ninth Circuit appeal still in abeyance briefing — i.e., the regulatory horizon is multi-year, not multi-quarter, so deferring a 2026 fleet decision means deferring a 2026 spray season.
- **For onboarding scripts**: Add a Q&A item: "What about the DJI security audit from late May 2026?" Answer template: "OnDefend's audit covered the Air 3S and Matrice 4E consumer/enterprise drones — not the Agras ag-spray T-series. The audit is technically interesting but legally moot: the FCC ruling is a national-security supply-chain decision, not a per-drone vulnerability assessment, and it does not require the FCC to find a specific technical flaw to be valid. The DJI Petition for Reconsideration filed January 21, 2026 is still pending with no decision timeline. We sell US-built airframes today because we cannot quote a multi-year support horizon on any new DJI ag SKU under the current FCC framework."
- **For competitor-comparison talking points**: When a customer cites OnDefend, acknowledge the audit existed, note its scope (Air 3S + Matrice 4E, not Agras), and pivot to the structural point: the [DJI Agras profile's](../manufacturers/dji-agras.md) ~$1.5B stated 2026 US revenue exposure across 14 already-authorized + 25 planned-for-2026 SKUs comes from the FCC ruling, not from a security finding the audit could rebut.
