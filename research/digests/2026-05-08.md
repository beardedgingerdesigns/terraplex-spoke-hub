## Summary
Two parallel FCC drone-policy comment windows are open right now and both are dealer-actionable. Reply comments on DJI's January 21, 2026 Petition for Reconsideration (ET Docket 26-22) and Autel's parallel Application for Review (ET Docket 26-23) of the December 22, 2025 Covered List ruling are due **Monday, May 11, 2026** under FCC Public Notice DA 26-223 — three days from this digest. Separately, reply comments on the FCC's "Unleashing American Drone Dominance" proceeding (Public Notice DA 26-314, jointly issued by the Wireless Telecommunications Bureau and Office of Engineering and Technology and published in the Federal Register on April 16, 2026 as Document 2026-07381) are due **Monday, May 18, 2026** — that proceeding asks for input on broader drone-friendly regulatory reforms including spectrum, device certification, and siting rules. The two windows are complementary: the first is the agency reconsidering its own ban on foreign-made UAS (the gate that captures DJI / Autel); the second is the agency soliciting input on what *positive* domestic-drone-industry reforms it should make. Agricultural spray drones are explicitly within scope of both.

## Why this matters for Terraplex dealers
This is the last administrative leverage point on the Covered List ruling before the case proceeds further on the judicial track at the Ninth Circuit (Case 26-1029, captured in the [April 2026 regulatory roundup](../regulatory/2026-04-update.md) and the [DJI Agras profile](../manufacturers/dji-agras.md)). The FCC's reconsideration mechanism is the agency's own internal review — if a critical mass of substantive comments lands by May 11, the full Commission can act on the petitions during the period the Ninth Circuit case is still in briefing. That is a real, narrow window where dealer-coordinated operator-customer commentary on specific operational impacts (the Mavic 3 Multispectral with no domestic equivalent at the same price point; the spray-drone fleet that depends on hardware now frozen at its current generation; the inability of trained Part 137 operators to refresh their fleets when DJI Care covered units retire) has direct procedural weight. The American Spray Drone Coalition (Eric Ringer, president), AUVSI, the Commercial Drone Alliance, and the Drone Service Providers Alliance have all publicly telegraphed reply filings, and the operational-impact testimony the FCC is asking for is exactly the kind of grounded specificity that comes naturally from a dealer's [49-location dealer network](../manufacturers/terraplex.md) interfacing daily with farmer operators.

The DA 26-314 "Unleashing American Drone Dominance" proceeding is structurally different and arguably more important for dealers than the reconsideration petition. Where the Covered List action is *defensive* (don't take away what the channel currently has), DA 26-314 is *offensive* — the FCC is asking, in essence, "what spectrum, device certification, and siting rules should we modernize so the domestic drone industry can grow?" The May 18 reply deadline is the dealer's chance to file specific operational-realism comments on, e.g., how spectrum allocation around 5GHz affects spray-drone telemetry over wide-area row-crop fields, or how device-certification rules apply to GTEEX Revolution swarming under one operator. The two proceedings together represent the regulatory baseline that will shape US ag-drone economics for the rest of the decade — comments filed this month land in the record those rules will be built on. The implication for the Sam Welton compliance program (see [content/compliance-program.md](../../content/compliance-program.md)) is straightforward: prep students should leave the course knowing not just *that* these proceedings exist but *how* their own field experience can be filed as substantive testimony.

## Key facts

### FCC Covered List reconsideration — reply comments due May 11, 2026

- **ET Docket 26-22** is the FCC docket designated for DJI's January 21, 2026 Petition for Reconsideration of the December 22, 2025 Covered List action; **ET Docket 26-23** is the parallel docket for Autel Robotics's Application for Review filed the same day [source: https://docs.fcc.gov/public/attachments/DA-26-223A1.pdf]
- The FCC opened the formal comment process under **Public Notice DA 26-223** released March 6, 2026; oppositions to the DJI petition and the Autel application were due **April 6, 2026**, and the corrected deadline for replies to those oppositions is **Monday, May 11, 2026** [source: https://docs.fcc.gov/public/attachments/DA-26-223A1.pdf] [source: https://dronelife.com/2026/05/05/public-comment-window-closing-fcc-weighs-dji-challenge-to-covered-list-ruling/]
- Reply comments may be filed at the FCC ECFS portal — express filings at `fcc.gov/ecfs/filings/express` for short text comments, standard filings at `fcc.gov/ecfs/filings/standard` for attached documents — under either ET Docket 26-22 (DJI) or 26-23 (Autel) [source: https://www.thedronegirl.com/2026/05/06/fcc-drone-ban-public-comment/]
- DJI's own filings before the Ninth Circuit say the December 22, 2025 Covered List action will block 25 planned 2026 product launches and cost roughly **$1.56 billion in lost US revenue** in calendar year 2026 — quantification the FCC has on the record as it weighs reconsideration [source: https://dronexl.co/2026/05/07/dji-autel-fcc-may-11-comment-deadline-covered-list/]
- Procedural posture (context: the April 2026 [DJI Agras profile](../manufacturers/dji-agras.md) entries logged the parallel Ninth Circuit case): on April 15, 2026 DJI filed an opposition brief in the Ninth Circuit (Case 26-1029) requesting the court hold the case in abeyance for six months while the FCC's reconsideration process plays out, with a status report due in November 2026 [source: https://dronexl.co/2026/05/07/dji-autel-fcc-may-11-comment-deadline-covered-list/]
- **Agricultural-specific impact framing in the public record**: agricultural drone applications are explicitly named among the operations affected; commentary submitted before the May 11 deadline is encouraged to include "the specific capabilities you depend on and what the real-world consequences of hardware stagnation look like in your field" — particularly relevant to ag-spray operators flying Mavic 3 Multispectral platforms with no domestically-built equivalent at the same price point [source: https://www.thedronegirl.com/2026/05/06/fcc-drone-ban-public-comment/] [source: https://dronedj.com/2026/05/06/dji-drone-ban-fcc-comments/]
- **Operational scope of the existing Covered List rule**: the FCC's December 22, 2025 action is prospective — it bars new authorization of foreign-made UAS, but does not ground previously-authorized units; agricultural drone programs can largely continue using any drones they already own [source: https://dronedj.com/2026/05/06/dji-drone-ban-fcc-comments/]

### FCC "Unleashing American Drone Dominance" proceeding — reply comments due May 18, 2026

- **Public Notice DA 26-314** was released April 1, 2026 by the FCC's **Wireless Telecommunications Bureau (WTB)** and **Office of Engineering and Technology (OET)**, asking for input on regulatory reforms that would support the domestic drone industry in light of the President's June 2025 Executive Orders on "Restoring American Airspace Sovereignty" and "Unleashing American Drone Dominance" [source: https://docs.fcc.gov/public/attachments/DA-26-314A1.pdf]
- The notice was published in the Federal Register on **April 16, 2026** as Document 2026-07381, opening a comment period with **comments due May 1, 2026** and **reply comments due May 18, 2026** [source: https://www.federalregister.gov/documents/2026/04/16/2026-07381/unleashing-american-drone-dominance]
- **Scope of input requested**: the WTB/OET seek input on "alleviating unnecessary regulatory burdens that frustrate drone deployment, such as siting or device certification rules that may create friction for the growth of a competitive, secure, and innovative domestic drone ecosystem" — explicitly broader than the Covered List question, including spectrum allocation, equipment authorization, and licensing [source: https://www.federalregister.gov/documents/2026/04/16/2026-07381/unleashing-american-drone-dominance]
- **Agricultural angle**: the parallel Bureau of Industry and Security action under the same Executive Order package allows certain long-range cargo delivery and agricultural spraying drones to be exported to specified U.S. partners and allies under License Exception STA — agricultural spray drones are explicitly named in the broader regulatory program the FCC is asking input on [source: https://www.federalregister.gov/documents/2026/04/16/2026-07381/unleashing-american-drone-dominance]

## Implications

- **For dealer outreach and Sam Welton's Part 107 prep curriculum**: brief operator-customers on the May 11 reply window with a one-page how-to (ECFS portal links, ET Docket 26-22 / 26-23, what kinds of operational specifics carry weight: "I have N acres I sprayed with the Mavic 3 Multispectral last season, the alternative platforms at this price point are X, the substitution cost to me is Y"). The [compliance program](../../content/compliance-program.md) is the natural delivery channel — it already has the operator-customer relationship and the credibility to translate procedural mechanics. Even three to four substantive ag-spray-specific filings from the dealer network is a measurable contribution to the record.
- **For dealer positioning copy**: do *not* lead positioning copy with the Covered List ruling status pending the May 11 reply window — the procedural posture genuinely could change. Lead instead with the GTEEX Revolution fleet's [domestic supply chain](../../research/manufacturers/terraplex.md) (NC/NE/IN/IL components, NC + KS/NE assembly), which is the *answer* to the regulatory question prospects are increasingly asking. The [May 3 digest's Revolution Drones / Exedy entry](2026-05-03.md) is the structural anchor.
- **For dealer-customer conversations**: when an operator-customer asks "what happens to my DJI fleet on May 12?", the accurate answer is "nothing changes for what you already own — the rule is prospective, not retroactive — but the fleet you own today is the fleet you may have to operate for the next several years if the FCC and Ninth Circuit both go against DJI; this is a buy-now-or-wait moment, and the GTEEX path is the answer for buy-now without the regulatory tail risk." Both the [Hylio profile](../manufacturers/hylio.md) and the [DJI Agras profile](../manufacturers/dji-agras.md) bear the same structural pattern.
- **For the May 18 reply window (DA 26-314)**: this is the more strategically valuable filing for dealers because the questions the FCC is asking — spectrum, device certification, siting, equipment authorization — are exactly the regulatory chokepoints that will determine how the next generation of domestic ag-spray fleets (GTEEX Revolution, Hylio AG-230, Exedy, Ceres Air) can scale. A dealer-network coordinated comment focused on, e.g., swarm-control spectrum or one-pilot-multiple-drones equipment-authorization questions, has a structural shot at shaping the operating envelope for the lineup Terraplex distributes. Lead-time consideration: comments on May 18 land while the Covered List reconsideration is still potentially open.
- **For territory thinking**: dealers in territories with high concentrations of ag-spray operators currently locked into DJI fleets (Iowa, Nebraska, Kansas, Missouri, Illinois — see the [competitor distributors profile](../dealer-economics/competitor-distributors.md)) face the highest comment-density opportunity, because their operator-customers are the testimony class the FCC's reconsideration record most needs. Dealer-network coordination is the leverage; the [49-location dealer network](../manufacturers/terraplex.md) is structurally well-suited to producing a coordinated multi-dealer comment.
