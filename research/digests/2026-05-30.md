## Summary

**DJI** on **May 28, 2026** released the findings of an independent cybersecurity assessment commissioned from US-based firm **OnDefend** covering the **DJI Air 3S** (with RC 2 controller) and the **DJI Matrice 4E** (with RC Plus 2 Enterprise controller); the assessment ran **October 2025 through March 2026** and produced **zero critical, high, or medium-risk findings**, **ten low-risk findings**, and **thirteen observations** — with no evidence of hidden backdoors, no data transmissions outside the United States, no unexplained RF emissions, and no supply-chain tampering. **sUAS News** (allowlisted) carried the result under the headline *"Independent assessment clears DJI drones of critical national security risks,"* and DJI distributed the underlying press release via PRNewswire on the same day. The release lands **17 days after** the May 11 close of the FCC reply-comment window on **DJI's Petition for Reconsideration (ET Docket 26-22)** and **Autel's Application for Review (ET Docket 26-23)** of the December 22, 2025 Covered List ruling (context: window-closing posture captured in the [2026-05-09 digest](2026-05-09.md), [2026-05-11 digest](2026-05-11.md), and [2026-05-13 digest](2026-05-13.md)), and **9 days after** Autel's May 19 reply-comment filing arguing the Covered List rests on classified material and on allegations originally aimed at DJI.

## Why this matters for Terraplex dealers

This is the **technical-evidence salvo** in the FCC Covered List proceeding — and it lands at the exact procedural moment where one is most needed. The prior month's digests track the regulatory architecture **hardening from contingent to consensus**: FCC Commissioner Olivia Trusty publicly defended the Covered List rationale four days before the comment deadline (see [2026-05-11 digest](2026-05-11.md)), AUVSI President Robbins endorsed the broader-than-AUVSI-asked-for sweep at XPONENTIAL Detroit (see [2026-05-14 digest](2026-05-14.md)), and the FCC OET extended the firmware-update waiver to at least January 1, 2029 (see [2026-05-12 digest](2026-05-12.md) and [2026-05-13 digest](2026-05-13.md)). That trajectory pointed at "Built in the Americas" being a **structurally durable** dealer advantage, not a contingent one. The OnDefend audit does not directly reverse any of that trajectory — but it is the **first independent, point-in-time technical attestation** that the underlying national-security premise lacks evidentiary support in the two tested SKUs. For [DJI Agras](../manufacturers/dji-agras.md) customers in particular, the audit is a **morale and continuity signal** rather than a regulatory one: it does not change the FCC Covered List, does not reopen new-equipment authorization (T55 remains blocked), does not affect the still-pending DJI Ninth Circuit appeal (Case 26-1029), and does not directly cover any [DJI Agras](../manufacturers/dji-agras.md) ag-spray model — but it does materially strengthen DJI's case that existing fleets can continue to be maintained, serviced, and operated in good faith through the firmware-waiver window.

The dealer-conversation reframe is on the **two-track message** Terraplex dealers have been running since the May 13 firmware-waiver extension: **(1) existing DJI fleets are operationally supported through at least January 1, 2029** (firmware/software updates permitted under FCC DA 26-454 — see [2026-05-13 digest](2026-05-13.md)); **(2) next-fleet purchases should target domestic OEMs** ([Hylio](../manufacturers/hylio.md), GTEEX Revolution via [Terraplex](../manufacturers/terraplex.md), Exedy Drones) whose component supply chains can be NDAA-compliant on the Section 805 enforcement calendar. The OnDefend audit **reinforces track 1** (existing-fleet customers should not panic-replace) while leaving **track 2 intact** (the structural advantage of a domestic supplier is regulatory and supply-chain durability, not a technical-security claim). Important: the audit did **not** test any [DJI Agras](../manufacturers/dji-agras.md) ag-spray model — the tested platforms (Air 3S consumer / Matrice 4E enterprise) are different airframes with different radios, flight controllers, and firmware stacks from the T30/T40/T50/T100/T70P/T25P agricultural lineup. Dealers should **not** generalize the OnDefend findings to claim that DJI Agras platforms have been independently cleared — they have not. The honest statement is "DJI commissioned a third-party security audit of two non-ag SKUs; the audit found no critical, high, or medium-risk vulnerabilities; the FCC Covered List is procedurally unchanged."

## Key facts

### DJI commissions OnDefend independent security audit; releases findings May 28, 2026

- **DJI** released findings of an independent security assessment conducted by **OnDefend**, a US-based cybersecurity firm, on **May 28, 2026**; the assessment is described by DJI as "the most comprehensive independent security assessment ever undertaken on our products" [source: https://www.suasnews.com/2026/05/independent-assessment-clears-dji-drones-of-critical-national-security-risks/] [source: https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/dji-releases-findings-of-the-most-comprehensive-independent-security-assessment-of-its-drone-systems-to-date-302784397.html]
- **Scope** — two DJI drone systems tested: the **DJI Air 3S** (consumer) with **RC 2 controller**, and the **DJI Matrice 4E** (enterprise) with **RC Plus 2 Enterprise controller**. Subjected to "advanced adversarial testing across software, hardware, and radio frequency domains" [source: https://www.suasnews.com/2026/05/independent-assessment-clears-dji-drones-of-critical-national-security-risks/] [source: https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/dji-releases-findings-of-the-most-comprehensive-independent-security-assessment-of-its-drone-systems-to-date-302784397.html]
- **Assessment period**: **October 2025 through March 2026** — five months of testing concluded two months before the May 28 publication date [source: https://www.suasnews.com/2026/05/independent-assessment-clears-dji-drones-of-critical-national-security-risks/] [source: https://dronelife.com/2026/05/28/dji-releases-independent-security-assessment-as-fcc-covered-list-debate-continues/]
- **Findings summary**: **zero critical, zero high, zero medium-risk findings**; **ten low-risk findings**; **thirteen observations**. OnDefend reports: "no clear evidence of hidden backdoors, no data transmissions outside the United States, and no viable pathways for hijacking or weaponization" [source: https://www.suasnews.com/2026/05/independent-assessment-clears-dji-drones-of-critical-national-security-risks/] [source: https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/dji-releases-findings-of-the-most-comprehensive-independent-security-assessment-of-its-drone-systems-to-date-302784397.html]
- **Methodology — software/network**: static and dynamic analysis of the **DJI Fly** and **Pilot 2** mobile applications; full network-traffic analysis across standard and local-data-mode operation; packet-capture across pre-flight, in-flight, and post-flight states; adversary simulations including meddler-in-the-middle attacks, certificate bypass, privilege escalation, and jailbreak attempts. All network connections from the flight-control applications **resolved to US-based IP addresses** [source: https://dronelife.com/2026/05/28/dji-releases-independent-security-assessment-as-fcc-covered-list-debate-continues/] [source: https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/dji-releases-findings-of-the-most-comprehensive-independent-security-assessment-of-its-drone-systems-to-date-302784397.html]
- **Methodology — hardware/RF**: full-spectrum RF scanning from **1 MHz to 6 GHz**; PCB-level teardown; component analysis; RF exploitation testing including replay, jamming, and injection attacks [source: https://www.suasnews.com/2026/05/independent-assessment-clears-dji-drones-of-critical-national-security-risks/] [source: https://www.tomshardware.com/tech-industry/dji-commissioned-security-audit-finds-no-major-vulnerabilities-in-two-drone-models-as-fcc-lawsuit-continues]
- **Sample-procurement integrity**: "consumer units were procured directly from retail outlets without pre-notification to DJI, and enterprise units were sourced from existing dealer stock" — i.e., DJI was not given the opportunity to provide cherry-picked test units [source: https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/dji-releases-findings-of-the-most-comprehensive-independent-security-assessment-of-its-drone-systems-to-date-302784397.html] [source: https://www.thedronegirl.com/2026/05/28/dji-security-audit/]
- **Low-risk findings detail** (the ten findings rated low-risk): persistent cross-site scripting; weak transport-layer-security protocols; exposed authentication tokens in URLs within mobile applications; a default shared password that DJI patched via firmware update during the testing period. All characterized as "consistent with industry norms for complex mobile and embedded systems" and as not presenting "a realistic risk to safe drone operation" [source: https://www.suasnews.com/2026/05/independent-assessment-clears-dji-drones-of-critical-national-security-risks/] [source: https://dronelife.com/2026/05/28/dji-releases-independent-security-assessment-as-fcc-covered-list-debate-continues/]
- **DJI executive on-record quote** — **Adam Welsh, Head of Global Policy at DJI**: *"This is the most comprehensive independent security assessment ever undertaken on our products. These findings confirm what DJI has consistently maintained: our products are secure, our data practices are transparent, and the concerns underlying our FCC Covered List designation are not supported by technical evidence."* [source: https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/dji-releases-findings-of-the-most-comprehensive-independent-security-assessment-of-its-drone-systems-to-date-302784397.html] [source: https://www.tomshardware.com/tech-industry/dji-commissioned-security-audit-finds-no-major-vulnerabilities-in-two-drone-models-as-fcc-lawsuit-continues]
- **OnDefend point-in-time caveat**: OnDefend explicitly acknowledged the audit is a "point-in-time assessment, meaning future software or firmware changes would still require ongoing review and validation" [source: https://dronelife.com/2026/05/28/dji-releases-independent-security-assessment-as-fcc-covered-list-debate-continues/]

### Procedural context — what the audit does and does NOT change

- **Procedural status of the FCC Covered List ruling**: **unchanged** by the audit. The FCC Covered List remains in force. New foreign-made UAS and critical-component authorizations remain blocked (T55 specifically remains blocked — context: see [2026-05-13 digest](2026-05-13.md)). The audit is **evidence in support of** DJI's [Petition for Reconsideration](../regulatory/2026-04-update.md) (ET Docket 26-22, filed January 21, 2026), not a reconsideration ruling — the FCC has not committed to a timeline and the China Unicom precedent shows the agency taking nearly three years to resolve a comparable reconsideration petition [source: https://www.suasnews.com/2026/05/independent-assessment-clears-dji-drones-of-critical-national-security-risks/] [source: https://dronelife.com/2026/05/28/dji-releases-independent-security-assessment-as-fcc-covered-list-debate-continues/]
- **Procedural status of FCC firmware/software-update waiver (DA 26-454)**: **unchanged**. The May 8, 2026 OET delegated-authority waiver extending firmware-update permission to at least January 1, 2029 is independent of the reconsideration proceeding (context: see [2026-05-12 digest](2026-05-12.md) and [2026-05-13 digest](2026-05-13.md)). Existing [DJI Agras](../manufacturers/dji-agras.md) fleets continue to be patchable through 2028+ regardless of how the FCC weighs the OnDefend findings.
- **Procedural status of DJI Ninth Circuit appeal (Case 26-1029)**: **unchanged**. The appeal is currently in abeyance briefing (context: see [2026-05-09 digest](2026-05-09.md)); the OnDefend audit is evidence DJI may cite in subsequent filings but does not by itself alter the appeal's procedural posture.
- **Tested SKUs vs ag-spray fleet** — important dealer-positioning constraint: the **Air 3S** and **Matrice 4E** are **not [DJI Agras](../manufacturers/dji-agras.md) ag-spray platforms**. The Air 3S is a consumer photography quadcopter; the Matrice 4E is an enterprise inspection/mapping platform. The audit therefore **does not directly attest** to the security posture of the T30, T40, T50, T100, T70P, or T25P agricultural lineup. Generalizing the OnDefend findings to claim DJI's ag-spray fleet has been "independently cleared" would be **factually overreaching** [source: https://www.suasnews.com/2026/05/independent-assessment-clears-dji-drones-of-critical-national-security-risks/]
- **Audit-funding caveat**: DJI **commissioned and paid for** the OnDefend assessment. Multiple commentaries note that commissioned audits introduce a methodological caveat — "companies don't typically commission audits they expect to fail" — even when (as here) procurement integrity was maintained via independent retail sourcing of test units [source: https://www.thedronegirl.com/2026/05/28/dji-security-audit/] [source: https://dronelife.com/2026/05/28/dji-releases-independent-security-assessment-as-fcc-covered-list-debate-continues/]

## Implications

- **For dealer-customer continuity conversations with existing [DJI Agras](../manufacturers/dji-agras.md) fleet owners**: the accurate two-sentence read is **"DJI commissioned an independent US cybersecurity firm to audit two of its non-agricultural drone systems; the audit found no critical, high, or medium-risk vulnerabilities and no data leaving the US. The FCC Covered List is procedurally unchanged, your existing fleet's firmware support continues through at least January 1, 2029 under FCC DA 26-454, and your next-fleet decision should still target a domestic OEM."** Do **not** claim DJI Agras platforms have been independently cleared — they were not tested.
- **For "Built in the Americas" positioning**: the structural-advantage thesis is **unchanged**. The advantage was never that DJI Agras was technically insecure — the [content/positioning.md](../../content/positioning.md) language has always rested on **supply-chain durability, dealer relationships, and US-built availability**, not on a technical-security claim. The OnDefend findings remove a *partisan-rhetorical* talking point ("the FCC banned DJI because it's a security risk") that some dealers may have leaned on, but it does **not** remove the structural rationale (Section 805 / 1260H enforcement calendar, FCC Covered List durability, the [Hylio profile's](../manufacturers/hylio.md) and [Terraplex profile's](../manufacturers/terraplex.md) domestic-OEM positioning).
- **For onboarding scripts** when prospects bring up the OnDefend report ("doesn't this prove DJI is fine?"): the script-ready answer is **"The OnDefend audit is real and the technical findings are real, but the FCC Covered List ruling and the Section 805 enforcement timeline are independent of the technical findings — they are about supply-chain country-of-origin and procurement policy, not about whether any individual airframe has a backdoor. The audit also covered two consumer/enterprise non-ag platforms, not the ag-spray Agras line. Your next-fleet decision should still target a domestic OEM."**
- **For Sam Welton's [compliance program](../../content/compliance-program.md) curriculum**: this is a **curriculum-context-relevant item**, not a curriculum-change item. The prep-course module on FCC Covered List and "Made in America" positioning should note that DJI has produced independent technical attestation, that the attestation does not change the procedural status of the Covered List, and that the procedural posture matters for purchasing decisions independent of any technical-security claim. The teaching point is **"regulatory posture is what determines availability, not technical-security findings"** — useful corrective against students who conflate the two.
- **For the next [DJI Agras profile](../manufacturers/dji-agras.md) revision** (monthly intelligence pass cadence): append a `### Revision 2026-06` entry capturing the OnDefend audit as a defensive-positioning data point. Flag the tested-SKU-vs-ag-fleet distinction in the manufacturer profile so future query callers don't read the audit as ag-fleet attestation.
- **For monitoring follow-up**: watch (a) any FCC Commissioner statement or Office of Engineering and Technology response to the OnDefend report — Commissioner Trusty's May 7 SCSP keynote (see [2026-05-11 digest](2026-05-11.md)) is the most recent benchmark of FCC public posture; (b) any DJI filing of the OnDefend report into the ET Docket 26-22 or ET Docket 26-23 record — the audit becomes formally adjudicable only when entered into the proceeding; (c) whether DJI commissions a follow-up audit covering [DJI Agras](../manufacturers/dji-agras.md) T-series platforms specifically — that would close the consumer/enterprise-vs-ag-spray gap that limits the present audit's applicability to dealer-positioning conversations.
