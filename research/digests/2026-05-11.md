## Summary
FCC Commissioner **Olivia Trusty** delivered a keynote address titled *"Commanding the Skies: Drone Dominance in the Age of AI"* at the **SCSP AI+ Expo in Washington, D.C. on Wednesday, May 7, 2026** — four days before today's reply-comment deadline on the DJI Petition for Reconsideration (ET Docket 26-22) and Autel Application for Review (ET Docket 26-23). The speech, released by the FCC as document DOC-421529A1, restated and defended the rationale for the December 22, 2025 Covered List action that added foreign-manufactured UAS and critical drone components to the agency's list of prohibited equipment — the action whose reply window closes end-of-day **today, Monday, May 11, 2026** under Public Notice DA 26-223. Trusty framed drones and AI as "the next major technological battleground" and characterized the Covered List as a tool for "preventing insecure or untrusted equipment from being incorporated into critical infrastructure." She offered no signal that the agency is leaning toward reversal on reconsideration; the speech is the clearest public posture from a sitting Commissioner since the petition was filed January 21, 2026.

## Why this matters for Terraplex dealers
The prior two digests ([2026-05-08](2026-05-08.md), [2026-05-09](2026-05-09.md)) intentionally kept dealer-facing messaging neutral on reconsideration outcome — "do not promise customers a DJI re-authorization timeline in either direction until the Commission acts." Trusty's May 7 speech is the first sitting-Commissioner statement since January that meaningfully shifts the probability distribution: a member of the same Commission that issued the December 22, 2025 ruling publicly defended the rationale **four days before voting on whether to reconsider it**, using the same national-security supply-chain frame the original ruling rested on. The honest dealer-facing read is that the reconsideration petition is unlikely to succeed at the Commission level — though dealers should still avoid promising specific outcomes, because the full Commission has not yet acted, the petitioners' reply filings landing today are not yet on the public record, and DJI's parallel [Ninth Circuit appeal (Case 26-1029)](../regulatory/2026-04-update.md) remains in jurisdictional briefing on a separate track.

The operational consequence for Terraplex dealers is straightforward: the "Built in the Americas, for the American Farmer" positioning around the [GTEEX Revolution lineup](../manufacturers/terraplex.md) and [Hylio](../manufacturers/hylio.md) hardware can be treated as a structurally durable advantage through the rest of 2026, not a temporary policy artifact that might evaporate on a Commission vote. Dealers in territories with high DJI installed-base concentration (Iowa, Nebraska, Kansas, Missouri, Illinois — see the [competitor distributors profile](../dealer-economics/competitor-distributors.md)) can now firm up the "your existing Agras fleet is the fleet you'll be operating for several years" framing without hedging it as speculation; the most senior public signal from inside the agency aligns with that read. The Sam Welton [compliance program](../../content/compliance-program.md) curriculum — which has been delivering the May 11 ECFS filing how-to per the [2026-05-08 digest](2026-05-08.md) — should pivot post-deadline to a substantive briefing on Trusty's speech and what it implies for the post-petition regulatory baseline, so prep-course students understand that the FCC's signal-from-the-top is "the Covered List frame is the operating environment we are building on, not the one we are walking back."

## Key facts

### FCC Commissioner Trusty's May 7 SCSP keynote defends the Covered List four days before reply-comment deadline

- FCC Commissioner **Olivia Trusty** delivered the keynote *"Commanding the Skies: Drone Dominance in the Age of AI"* at the **SCSP AI+ Expo in Washington, D.C., on Wednesday, May 7, 2026**; the speech was released by the FCC as document **DOC-421529A1** [source: https://docs.fcc.gov/public/attachments/DOC-421529A1.pdf] [source: https://dronedj.com/2026/05/08/fcc-dji-drone-ban-trusty/]
- The speech directly cites the FCC's **December 22, 2025 Covered List action**: "Through tools such as the Covered List, the FCC is working to prevent insecure or untrusted equipment from being incorporated into critical infrastructure," and "Last year, the FCC expanded that approach to drones, adding foreign-manufactured unmanned aircraft systems and critical components to the Covered List based on national security determinations" [source: https://dronedj.com/2026/05/08/fcc-dji-drone-ban-trusty/]
- Trusty framed the broader policy as: "At its core, this effort is about ensuring that the United States' drone market is secure, trusted, and that principles of market fairness will determine who designs, builds, and deploys the intelligent systems that will define the future of our economic and national security" [source: https://docs.fcc.gov/public/attachments/DOC-421529A1.pdf]
- Strategic-competition frame: "The decisions we make today will determine whether the United States leads or follows in these intelligent systems. If the United States hesitates, other nations will set global standards, build dominant supply chains, and shape the future of open, democratic societies" [source: https://docs.fcc.gov/public/attachments/DOC-421529A1.pdf]
- Action prescription: "Build American, test faster, share data across agency lines, and treat the connected and intelligent airspace as the strategic domain it has already become" [source: https://docs.fcc.gov/public/attachments/DOC-421529A1.pdf]
- The speech offered **no signal that the agency is leaning toward reversal** on the DJI Petition for Reconsideration ([ET Docket 26-22](../manufacturers/dji-agras.md)) or Autel Application for Review (ET Docket 26-23); the framing is consistent with the original December 22, 2025 ruling and with the DoD's April 10, 2026 opposition memorandum captured in the [2026-05-09 digest](2026-05-09.md) [source: https://dronedj.com/2026/05/08/fcc-dji-drone-ban-trusty/]
- **Timing context**: the speech was delivered **four days before** today's end-of-day Monday, **May 11, 2026** reply-comment deadline under **FCC Public Notice DA 26-223** for both ET Docket 26-22 (DJI) and ET Docket 26-23 (Autel) (context: deadline established March 6, 2026) [source: https://docs.fcc.gov/public/attachments/DA-26-223A1.pdf]
- SCSP AI+ Expo programming venue: the keynote sat in the "Drone Dominance" track of the Special Competitive Studies Project's annual Washington gathering, the largest AI-policy event of 2026 — placing the FCC's restated position in front of the federal-policy audience most likely to influence subsequent Commission deliberation [source: https://expo.scsp.ai/schedule-at-a-glance/]

## Implications

- **For dealer-customer conversations post-May 11**: the prior digests' "do not promise re-authorization timing in either direction" guidance can be evolved, not abandoned. Updated framing: "the Commissioner who voted for the original Covered List ruling publicly defended that rationale four days before the petitioners' reply-comment deadline, so the most honest read of the public signal is that reconsideration is unlikely to succeed at the Commission level — but the full Commission has not yet acted, and the parallel Ninth Circuit appeal is on a separate track that could take 18+ months." Avoid stating outcome with certainty; do allow the directional confidence the public signal supports.
- **For positioning copy**: the "Built in the Americas, for the American Farmer" message around the [GTEEX Revolution](../manufacturers/terraplex.md) and [Hylio](../manufacturers/hylio.md) hardware can now be treated as a structurally durable advantage rather than a contingent one. Do **not** rewrite copy to claim the DJI exclusion is permanent — the Ninth Circuit case is still open and reverses on multi-year timeframes — but the *operating assumption for the rest of 2026 and into 2027* is that no new DJI Agras model will be FCC-authorized for US sale or import.

Watch ECFS for DJI's and Autel's reply filings landing today and tomorrow, and watch fcc.gov/news for any procedural order responding to the closed comment window.
