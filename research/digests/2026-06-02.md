## Summary

Two ag-drone developments accumulated during the 2026-05-17 → 2026-06-02 gap since the last digest. **(1) DJI Agriculture publicly launched the Agras T55 and the T100 Dual Battery Spraying System into a sales channel on May 20, 2026** at AGRITECHNICA Asia 2026 in Bangkok (BITEC venue), making both models commercially available in Thailand and exhibiting the T70P, T25P, and Dock 3 alongside; the T70P took the AGRITECHNICA Asia 2026 Sustainable Practices Award. The T55 was specifically flagged in the [2026-05-13 digest](2026-05-13.md) and the [DJI Agras profile's](../manufacturers/dji-agras.md) revision-2026-05-01 as an "unreleased model" that "remains blocked from US authorization" — it is now released, just not into the US. **(2) The Council of the European Union adopted on May 27, 2026** (Brussels, Council document **9737/26**) the Member-State negotiating mandate on the "Omnibus X" simplification package, which modifies the Sustainable Use Regulation to **open a new pathway for certain types of drones to apply plant-protection products from the air** — material softening of the decades-old EU aerial-spray prohibition. EFSA must issue risk-assessment guidance defining qualifying drone characteristics; a delegated act has a **30-month deadline**. Council mandate now goes to trilogue with the European Parliament before final adoption.

## Why this matters for Terraplex dealers

**On the DJI launch**: the T55 has been the headline "unreleased / future" DJI ag SKU referenced in dealer-customer conversations all year — the [2026-05-13 digest](2026-05-13.md) explicitly described it as "the 2026 flagship [that] cannot benefit from this [FCC firmware] waiver and remains blocked from US authorization under the Covered List ruling." The Thailand launch resets that conversation in one specific way: the T55 is no longer a paper SKU — it is a shipping product in another major ag market. The honest dealer line for prospects asking "when can I get the T55?" is now: "it shipped in Thailand on May 20, 2026; it remains blocked from US authorization under the December 22, 2025 FCC Covered List ruling; the DJI Petition for Reconsideration (ET Docket 26-22) is pending after the May 11 reply-comment deadline closed, with no FCC decision date set." This is a small but useful update to the [DJI Agras profile](../manufacturers/dji-agras.md), which until now has been able to refer to the T55 as "unreleased." Separately, the T100 Dual Battery variant — with the Dual Battery system specifically called out as new — and the T70P Sustainable Practices Award at AGRITECHNICA Asia represent product-line iteration that is happening on the AsiaPac calendar while the US channel sits frozen behind the FCC ruling. None of these SKUs are currently available to US dealers or their customers. The [Hylio profile](../manufacturers/hylio.md) and the [GTEEX Revolution](../manufacturers/terraplex.md) lineup that Terraplex distributes remain the operative domestic-OEM alternatives for any prospect who walks in asking for a high-payload airframe today.

**On the EU Council Omnibus X**: this is not directly dealer-actionable for US Terraplex dealers — the EU pesticide-drone pathway lives entirely outside the FAA Part 137 / FCC Covered List architecture that governs US operations. But it is dealer-conversation-relevant in two ways. **First**, when a prospect asks "is aerial drone spraying actually growing globally, or is this a US-only fad," the post-May-27-2026 answer can now include the structural data point that the EU has just adopted (at Council level) a softening of its decades-old aerial-spray prohibition specifically to accommodate drones — complementing the [2026-05-05 digest's](2026-05-05.md) global DJI 600,000-unit fleet figure as evidence of secular global adoption. **Second**, for the multi-year prospect ("what does the regulatory environment look like in 3–5 years"), the EU pathway is a directional signal: jurisdictions that have historically been more restrictive than the US on aerial pesticide application are moving toward drone-specific accommodation. Sam Welton's [compliance program](../../content/compliance-program.md) curriculum already covers FAA Part 137 / Part 107 as the US framework; the EU development is not curriculum material but is useful context if a Part 107 prep-course attendee asks "where is global drone regulation headed."

### EU Council adopts Omnibus X negotiating mandate, opens drone pesticide pathway (May 27, 2026, Brussels — Council doc 9737/26)

- **Member States adopted their negotiating position on the "Omnibus X" simplification package at the Council of the European Union on May 27, 2026** in Brussels (Council document reference **ST-9737-2026-INIT**) [source: https://www.eunews.it/en/2026/05/27/the-eu-council-approves-simplifying-pesticide-regulations-and-facilitates-drone-usage/] [source: https://data.consilium.europa.eu/doc/document/ST-9737-2026-INIT/en/pdf]
- **Substance of the change** — the current **Sustainable Use of Pesticides Directive (SUD)** prohibits aerial spraying with derogations only available at high administrative cost to Member States and professional users. The Council mandate **maintains the existing derogation regime but introduces a new pathway for certain types of drones** to apply pesticides where ground-based spraying is not feasible [source: https://www.eunews.it/en/2026/05/27/the-eu-council-approves-simplifying-pesticide-regulations-and-facilitates-drone-usage/] [source: https://euperspectives.eu/2026/05/drones-cleared-to-spray-pesticides-eu-lifts-decades-old-ban/]
- **Implementation mechanics** — **European Food Safety Authority (EFSA)** must issue **guidance on risk assessment, drone characteristics, and conditions of use**; a **delegated act** specifying which drone types qualify has a **30-month deadline** from final adoption [source: https://www.eunews.it/en/2026/05/27/the-eu-council-approves-simplifying-pesticide-regulations-and-facilitates-drone-usage/]
- **Procedural status** — adoption is a **Council negotiating mandate**, not final EU law; **trilogue negotiations with the European Parliament can now begin**, after which the package must clear both institutions before entering into force [source: https://euperspectives.eu/2026/05/drones-cleared-to-spray-pesticides-eu-lifts-decades-old-ban/]
- **Scope of broader Omnibus X package** — beyond plant-protection products, the package covers biocides, animal feed, official controls, animal health and welfare; the drone-pesticide pathway is one element among several simplification measures [source: https://www.eunews.it/en/2026/05/27/the-eu-council-approves-simplifying-pesticide-regulations-and-facilitates-drone-usage/]

### Caveats — what the EU Council mandate does NOT establish

- **No drone type has been authorized yet** — the Council position only sets the framework for EFSA to define qualifying drone characteristics via a future delegated act, with a 30-month deadline. Operational drone-spraying under EU law remains gated on (a) trilogue and final Parliament/Council adoption of the SUR amendment, then (b) EFSA risk-assessment guidance, then (c) the delegated act itself [source: https://euperspectives.eu/2026/05/drones-cleared-to-spray-pesticides-eu-lifts-decades-old-ban/]
- **Member-State decree work continues separately** — e.g., Italy's MASAF implementing decree under Article 13-bis (referenced as targeting "end of May 2026" per Senator De Carlo) sits on a separate national-law track; the Council Omnibus X action does not resolve that Member-State-level work
- **No US application** — the FCC Covered List ruling, FAA Part 137 framework, and Section 2209 NPRM (covered across the 2026-05-06/07 and 2026-05-08-through-13 digest sequences) are unaffected by the EU action

## Implications

- **For dealer-customer "when can I get the T55" conversations**: the post-May-20-2026 honest answer is now "it shipped in Thailand on May 20, 2026 alongside the T100 Dual Battery System and T70P Sustainable Practices Award winner; none of those SKUs is available in the US, because the FCC Covered List ruling from December 22, 2025 bars new equipment authorizations for foreign-made UAS; DJI's Petition for Reconsideration (ET Docket 26-22) is pending with no FCC decision date set." Do **not** speculate on US availability timelines — the Petition outcome is not announced.
- **For the [DJI Agras profile](../manufacturers/dji-agras.md)**: when the profile next refreshes (monthly intelligence pass cadence), the revision-2026-05-01 description of the T55 as a paper SKU should be augmented with the May 20, 2026 Thailand commercial launch; the T100 entry should add the **Dual Battery System** variant as now formally available in the Thai market; the T70P entry should note the AGRITECHNICA Asia 2026 Sustainable Practices Award. These are factual line-item updates, not framing changes — the FCC-blocked-from-US framing remains unchanged.
- **For dealer-customer "is global ag-drone spraying actually growing" conversations**: the post-May-27-2026 EU Council adoption — combined with the [2026-05-05 digest's](2026-05-05.md) DJI Agrishow 600,000-unit global fleet figure and the May 20 DJI Thailand launch — supports the structural reading that aerial drone spraying is a secular global growth story, with the US specifically frozen by FCC-Covered-List supply-side constraints rather than by softness in agronomic demand. The fresh datapoint that **even the EU, historically the most restrictive jurisdiction on aerial pesticide application, is opening a drone-specific pathway** is a useful "drone spraying is the global default, not a fad" anchor.
- **For multi-year prospect conversations** ("what does the regulatory environment look like in 3–5 years"): the EU 30-month delegated-act timeline puts a usable EU drone-spraying framework on a **late-2028 / 2029 horizon**, comparable in scale to the FAA Section 2209 NPRM rulemaking window covered in the [2026-05-06 digest](2026-05-06.md) and to the OET DA 26-454 firmware-waiver "**at least January 1, 2029**" cutoff covered in the [2026-05-12 digest](2026-05-12.md) and [2026-05-13 digest](2026-05-13.md). The cross-jurisdictional alignment on "2028–2029 as the next major drone-regulatory horizon" is the structural framing for any prospect doing fleet-planning out beyond the current Part 137 + Covered List status quo.
- **For monitoring follow-up**: watch (a) any DJI official-newsroom announcement on the **status of the Petition for Reconsideration** at the FCC (ET Docket 26-22); the May 11 reply-comment window closed, but no decision date has been published — Commissioner Trusty's May 7 SCSP AI+ Expo keynote (covered in the [2026-05-11 digest](2026-05-11.md)) signals reconsideration is unlikely to succeed; (b) trilogue progress on the EU Omnibus X package between the Council mandate (adopted 2026-05-27) and the European Parliament; (c) any Member-State implementing decree (especially Italy's MASAF Article 13-bis decree) that converts EU framework into operational drone-spray rights inside individual EU markets; (d) any announcement from [Hylio](../manufacturers/hylio.md), Revolution Drones (the assembler of [GTEEX Revolution](../manufacturers/terraplex.md) airframes that Terraplex distributes), Ceres Air, Exedy Drones, or Pyka of a US-domestic high-payload SKU positioned as the functional alternative to the now-shipping-elsewhere DJI T55 / T100 Dual Battery.
