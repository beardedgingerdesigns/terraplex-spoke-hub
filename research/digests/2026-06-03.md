## Summary

The **National Agricultural Aviation Association (NAAA)** opened the 2026 growing season with a reinforced public-safety campaign urging UAS operators to **yield right-of-way to manned agricultural aircraft** and adopt specific airspace-coordination practices — anchored on the NAAA late-2025 end-of-season survey finding that **20% of manned aerial-application operators reported at least one unsafe encounter with a drone in 2025, up from 16% in 2024 and 11% in 2023** (i.e., the unsafe-encounter rate nearly doubled over three seasons). The campaign first rolled out via NAAA's April 15, 2026 release and was carried by Brownfield Ag News, CropLife, Southeast AgNET (April 24), American Ag Network, Market Talk, Agriculture of America, and State Aviation Journal (all April 20) — and is now being reinforced **at the front edge of the 2026 spray-application season** by AgAir Update's **June 1, 2026 "Fly Safe: Managing Risk in the 2026 Ag Aviation Season"** column, which adds a fresh hard-fact data point: **three mid-air collisions occurred in agricultural aviation in 2024**, and the column frames cockpit distraction and airspace coordination as the dominant 2026-season risk drivers. The NAAA recommendations now read as a near-checklist for any Part 137 drone operator working in the same low-altitude airspace as crewed ag aviators.

## Why this matters for Terraplex dealers

This is the first in-season ag-aviation safety push of 2026, and it lands on the [compliance-program](../../content/compliance-program.md) curriculum directly. Sam Welton's [Part 107 prep course](../../content/compliance-program.md) — which most recently ran May 27–28, 2026 — already covers airspace fluency and Part 137 obligations; the NAAA 20%-encounter statistic plus the AgAir Update three-mid-airs-in-2024 datum gives the course a fresh, citable "why this matters" anchor for the airspace-coordination module that the [2026-05-14 digest's](2026-05-14.md) FAA-McIntosh "drones as aircraft, drone operators as pilots" reframe already pulled into curriculum scope. The specific recommendations NAAA has put in writing — **monitor 122.925 MHz (the ag-aviation common frequency)**, **equip drones with visible strobe lights and ADS-B In**, **consult NAAA's "Find An Aerial Applicator" database before flying**, and **land immediately when a low-flying manned aircraft is nearby** — are operational instructions a dealer can hand a new customer on day one and a prep-course instructor can teach as a hard requirement, not soft guidance.

The dealer-conversation reframe is on **the implicit liability story** behind the 11% → 16% → 20% encounter trend. The honest message to a prospect operator entering the [Part 137](../../content/compliance-program.md) certification track in 2026: the encounter rate is rising fast enough that crewed ag-aviation's largest trade association is now putting concrete equipment-and-procedure expectations on UAS operators — strobes, ADS-B In, monitored frequency, named lookup-database — and a UAS operator without those is the operator most exposed when an encounter does happen. This is not yet a regulatory mandate (FAA Part 137 doesn't require ADS-B In on the UAS side); it's NAAA-published industry expectation. But it converges with the FAA's [2026-05-14 digest](2026-05-14.md) "drones as aircraft, operators as pilots" framing toward a near-term posture where ADS-B In on a heavy-payload spray drone is the dealer-recommended default for any operator working corn-belt territory where crewed Air Tractors / AT-802s are working the same fields. Dealers in Iowa, Nebraska, Kansas, Missouri, and the Mississippi Delta should plan to mention this in onboarding regardless of whether the operator asks.

## Key facts

### NAAA end-of-2025-season survey — unsafe drone encounters climbed to 20% of manned ag aviators

- The **National Agricultural Aviation Association (NAAA)** survey, conducted near the end of the 2025 agricultural aviation season, found **20% of manned aerial-application operators reported at least one unsafe encounter with a drone in 2025**, up from **16% in 2024** and **11% in 2023** — i.e., the unsafe-encounter rate has nearly doubled in three seasons [source: https://www.americanagnetwork.com/2026/04/20/ag-aviation-industry-urges-drone-operators-to-give-right-of-way-to-manned-agricultural-aircraft-this-growing-season/] [source: https://avweb.com/flight-safety/ag-aircraft-drone-encounters-increase-2025/] [source: https://stateaviationjournal.com/index.php/national-news/ag-aviation-industry-urges-drone-operators-to-give-right-of-way-to-manned-agricultural-aircraft-this-growing-season]
- NAAA framing of the visibility asymmetry: *"it is extremely difficult, if not impossible, for manned aircraft to see a drone while conducting aerial applications 10 feet off the ground at speeds of up to 140 mph"* — i.e., the crewed-aircraft pilot cannot reliably acquire the UAS visually, so the obligation to deconflict sits on the UAS operator [source: https://www.croplife.com/management/naaa-urges-drone-operators-to-operate-safely-near-low-altitude-manned-agricultural-aircraft-this-growing-season/] [source: https://southeastagnet.com/2026/04/24/agricultural-aviation-drone-safety-awareness/]
- **Right-of-way rule (FAA-standing)**: UAS must always yield right-of-way to manned aircraft; this is not new in 2026 but is the legal predicate NAAA is anchoring its 2026 campaign on [source: https://www.americanagnetwork.com/2026/04/20/ag-aviation-industry-urges-drone-operators-to-give-right-of-way-to-manned-agricultural-aircraft-this-growing-season/] [source: https://stateaviationjournal.com/index.php/national-news/ag-aviation-industry-urges-drone-operators-to-give-right-of-way-to-manned-agricultural-aircraft-this-growing-season]

### NAAA's 2026 published recommendations for UAS operators

- **Equip the drone with visible strobe lights, highly visible markings, and tracking technology such as an ADS-B In system** — first time NAAA has put ADS-B In on the equipment list as a named recommendation [source: https://www.americanagnetwork.com/2026/04/20/ag-aviation-industry-urges-drone-operators-to-give-right-of-way-to-manned-agricultural-aircraft-this-growing-season/] [source: https://www.croplife.com/management/naaa-urges-drone-operators-to-operate-safely-near-low-altitude-manned-agricultural-aircraft-this-growing-season/]
- **Monitor aviation radio frequency 122.925 MHz** for crewed agricultural aircraft activity (the established ag-aviation common frequency) [source: https://www.americanagnetwork.com/2026/04/20/ag-aviation-industry-urges-drone-operators-to-give-right-of-way-to-manned-agricultural-aircraft-this-growing-season/] [source: https://agairupdate.com/2026/06/01/fly-safe-managing-risk-in-the-2026-ag-aviation-season/]
- **Contact local agricultural aviation operators before flying** by consulting NAAA's **"Find An Aerial Applicator" database at AgAviation.org** — operational lookup tool, not an FAA system [source: https://stateaviationjournal.com/index.php/national-news/ag-aviation-industry-urges-drone-operators-to-give-right-of-way-to-manned-agricultural-aircraft-this-growing-season] [source: https://www.croplife.com/management/naaa-urges-drone-operators-to-operate-safely-near-low-altitude-manned-agricultural-aircraft-this-growing-season/]
- **Land the UAS immediately when a low-flying aircraft is nearby** — NAAA's primary tactical instruction [source: https://www.americanagnetwork.com/2026/04/20/ag-aviation-industry-urges-drone-operators-to-give-right-of-way-to-manned-agricultural-aircraft-this-growing-season/] [source: https://southeastagnet.com/2026/04/24/agricultural-aviation-drone-safety-awareness/]
- **Carry UAS-operator liability insurance** [source: https://www.americanagnetwork.com/2026/04/20/ag-aviation-industry-urges-drone-operators-to-give-right-of-way-to-manned-agricultural-aircraft-this-growing-season/]
- Procedural recommendation **to crewed ag aviators**: circle the application site (ideally in opposite-direction orbits) before entering, so any UAS operators have time to spot the manned aircraft and land — this is the symmetric piece, not aimed at UAS operators but explaining what the crewed pilot will be doing [source: https://agairupdate.com/2026/06/01/fly-safe-managing-risk-in-the-2026-ag-aviation-season/] [source: https://www.americanagnetwork.com/2026/04/20/ag-aviation-industry-urges-drone-operators-to-give-right-of-way-to-manned-agricultural-aircraft-this-growing-season/]

### AgAir Update June 1, 2026 — "Fly Safe: Managing Risk in the 2026 Ag Aviation Season"

- **Three mid-air collisions occurred in 2024 in agricultural aviation** — AgAir Update's June 1 column flags this as the worst-case manifestation of the unsafe-encounter trend NAAA's survey is tracking [source: https://agairupdate.com/2026/06/01/fly-safe-managing-risk-in-the-2026-ag-aviation-season/]
- AgAir Update frames the 2026-season risk model as **cockpit distraction** as the leading contributor to accidents, with many wire strikes involving hazards pilots already knew were present but momentarily forgot due to mental overload or competing tasks — frames distraction as a human-factors problem comparable across crewed and UAS operations [source: https://agairupdate.com/2026/06/01/fly-safe-managing-risk-in-the-2026-ag-aviation-season/]
- AgAir Update reinforces NAAA's **122.925 MHz** monitoring recommendation for all aircraft (crewed and UAS) sharing the same low-altitude application airspace [source: https://agairupdate.com/2026/06/01/fly-safe-managing-risk-in-the-2026-ag-aviation-season/]

### Boundary conditions — what the campaign does NOT change

- **No new FAA rulemaking has been triggered** by the NAAA campaign as of this digest; the FAA's separate [Section 2209 NPRM](../regulatory/2026-04-update.md) and Part 108 BVLOS proposal (context: NPRM published August 7, 2025; comment period closed October 6, 2025; final rule still pending as of mid-2026 per NAAA tracking) remain on their own dockets and are unaffected
- **Part 137 operating-certificate requirements are unchanged** — the NAAA recommendations are industry expectations, not new federal mandates. ADS-B In on a UAS is **recommended**, not required by FAA Part 137 [source: https://www.americanagnetwork.com/2026/04/20/ag-aviation-industry-urges-drone-operators-to-give-right-of-way-to-manned-agricultural-aircraft-this-growing-season/]
- The campaign does **not** name any specific UAS manufacturer or model as exposed/preferred — it is platform-agnostic; the recommendations apply equally to operators flying [DJI Agras](../manufacturers/dji-agras.md), [Hylio](../manufacturers/hylio.md) HYL-series, GTEEX Revolution [Terraplex profile](../manufacturers/terraplex.md), Pyka, or any other Part 137 airframe

## Implications

- **For Sam Welton's [Part 107 / Part 137 prep course](../../content/compliance-program.md) curriculum** — add a 2026-season-specific airspace-coordination module anchored on the NAAA 20%-encounter datum and the AgAir Update three-mid-airs-in-2024 datum. The module should teach: (1) the 122.925 MHz monitoring expectation as standard operating procedure during application missions; (2) the NAAA "Find An Aerial Applicator" lookup as a pre-flight step in operator-served territories; (3) immediate-landing as the default response to a low-flying crewed aircraft; (4) the rising-trend visualization (11% → 16% → 20%) as the "why this matters" framing. This is the second consecutive curriculum addition (after the [2026-05-14 digest's](2026-05-14.md) FAA "drones as aircraft" reframe) drawn from a hard, citable 2026 data source — the prep course's airspace-fluency block is now meaningfully fresher than the textbook.
- **For dealer onboarding scripts** when a prospect asks "what's the safety story on these drones around crewed crop dusters?" — the accurate 2026 answer can now include: "the NAAA-published unsafe-encounter rate has roughly doubled in three seasons — 11% in 2023 to 20% in 2025 — and NAAA's published 2026 recommendations include strobes, ADS-B In, monitoring 122.925 MHz, and using NAAA's 'Find An Aerial Applicator' database to know what crewed operators are working your county. We coach our customers on every one of those as part of standard onboarding." This converts an objection into evidence of operational rigor.
- **For [Hylio](../manufacturers/hylio.md), [DJI Agras](../manufacturers/dji-agras.md), and GTEEX Revolution ([Terraplex profile](../manufacturers/terraplex.md)) accessory recommendations**: dealers should review whether their preferred ADS-B In add-on path is documented in the customer onboarding packet for each platform. The NAAA recommendation puts ADS-B In on a customer-facing question; dealers should not be discovering the per-platform answer at the point of sale.
- **For territory-level outreach** in heavy aerial-application states (Iowa, Nebraska, Kansas, Missouri, Mississippi Delta, plus Texas where [Hylio](../manufacturers/hylio.md) is built) — the AgAir Update June 1 reinforcement timing matters: aerial-application crews are mobilizing for the early summer fungicide and post-emerge insecticide work in the next 2–6 weeks. Dealers servicing operators in those counties should put the NAAA "Find An Aerial Applicator" reference into outbound communications **now**, before the season's first 122.925 MHz radio call goes out.
- **For monitoring follow-up**: (a) watch for an NAAA mid-2026-season interim survey (NAAA traditionally re-surveys at the end of the season — the 11% → 16% → 20% series will get its 2026 data point in spring 2027); (b) watch for any FAA enforcement action under the [DETER program (effective 2026-04-17; see the 2026-05-04 digest)](2026-05-04.md) against a UAS operator following a manned-aircraft encounter — that's when the NAAA campaign moves from industry guidance into the enforcement-precedent layer; (c) watch for any state ag-department adoption (Iowa, Texas, Nebraska, Kansas, Missouri) of the NAAA recommendations as a Part 137 operator-conduct expectation tied to state-issued pesticide-applicator licenses.
