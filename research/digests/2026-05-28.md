## Summary

On May 19, 2026 **Autel Robotics filed its reply** in the FCC Covered List proceeding (Autel's Application for Review, **ET Docket 26-23** — the parallel track to DJI's Petition for Reconsideration in **ET Docket 26-22**), making two arguments: (1) the FCC added Autel to the December 22, 2025 Covered List on the basis of **classified material Autel was never permitted to see**, plus technical allegations **originally aimed at DJI**; and (2) the Commission relied on **broad national-security assumptions rather than an individualized review of Autel itself**. The filing notably **distances Autel from DJI** rather than arguing the two companies' cases jointly. The reply was filed into the reply-comment window that closed May 11, 2026 (context: window captured in the [2026-05-08](2026-05-08.md) and [2026-05-09](2026-05-09.md) digests); the FCC has **not committed to a decision timeline**, and reporting notes the China Unicom reconsideration precedent took nearly three years to resolve. All three covering outlets are drone-trade publications outside the `research/README.md` allowlist (see flag below).

## Why this matters for Terraplex dealers

The dominant regulatory storyline this wiki has tracked daily through early-to-mid May — the FCC's December 22, 2025 Covered List sweep and the DJI/Autel challenges to it — was last advanced by Commissioner Trusty's May 7 defense of the ruling (see [2026-05-11 digest](2026-05-11.md)) and the AUVSI/Pentagon/FAA three-pillar alignment at XPONENTIAL on May 12 (see [2026-05-14 digest](2026-05-14.md)). Autel's May 19 reply is the first substantive new content in the proceeding since the May 11 window closed, and it matters for dealer positioning in two ways. First, the **"distancing from DJI"** posture is itself the news: Autel is arguing it should be treated as a distinct company that received DJI's allegations by association, which is a tacit concession that the **DJI-specific national-security case is the strong one** and that being lumped with DJI is a liability. That hardens — it does not weaken — the [2026-05-11 digest's](2026-05-11.md) "structurally durable advantage" reading of the [GTEEX Revolution](../manufacturers/terraplex.md) / Hylio "Built in the Americas" pitch: even a co-listed foreign manufacturer is now trying to get out from under the DJI shadow rather than overturn the listing framework.

Second, the **timeline signal is the dealer-actionable takeaway**. The FCC has set no decision date, and the cited China Unicom precedent (~3 years to resolve a comparable reconsideration) means dealers should plan as if the Covered List stands for the foreseeable horizon — neither a near-term reversal that would reopen [DJI Agras](../manufacturers/dji-agras.md) / Autel new-equipment authorizations, nor a fast adverse ruling that would force existing-fleet decisions. Combined with the DA 26-454 firmware-waiver extension to at least January 1, 2029 (see [2026-05-13 digest](2026-05-13.md)), the honest dealer message is unchanged and now better-supported: *existing foreign-made fleets keep getting firmware support for years; the petitions are a multi-year process unlikely to reopen the new-equipment channel soon; plan the next fleet around a domestic OEM.*

## Implications

- **For dealer positioning copy**: the "Built in the Americas" advantage is now reinforced from an unexpected direction — a co-listed foreign manufacturer (Autel) is publicly trying to separate itself from DJI rather than defend the foreign-made category as a whole. Do **not** overstate this as an FCC ruling or a win; it is a litigant's filing. The accurate framing is: "the foreign-made-drone manufacturers are now arguing against each other's national-security baggage, not presenting a united front to overturn the listing."
- **For pricing / fleet-planning conversations**: the no-timeline + ~3-year-precedent signal means there is **no near-term path to reopening new DJI/Autel equipment authorizations** in the US channel. Dealers can commit to multi-year planning horizons (existing fleets supported via the DA 26-454 firmware waiver through at least January 1, 2029 per the [2026-05-13 digest](2026-05-13.md); next-fleet decisions pointed at domestic OEMs).
- **For Sam Welton's [compliance program](../../content/compliance-program.md) curriculum**: a teaching point on the *durability* of the Covered List — the proceeding is a slow administrative-review process, not a switch that could flip back at any moment. Students asking "won't the ban just get reversed?" can be told the petitions exist, are being actively litigated, and have no decision date, with comparable proceedings running multiple years.
- **For monitoring follow-up**: watch for (a) any FCC procedural action or decision date on ET Dockets 26-22 / 26-23 — none published yet; (b) DJI's separate Ninth Circuit Case 26-1029 (in abeyance briefing per prior digests); (c) an **allowlisted-source** account of the Autel reply (AgWeb, sUAS News, AgFunder, or the FCC docket itself) to corroborate the drone-trade-press coverage this entry relies on.
