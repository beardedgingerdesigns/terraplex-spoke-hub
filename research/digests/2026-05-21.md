## Summary

**Autel Robotics** filed its substantive **reply at the FCC in ET Docket 26-23** — the Application for Review proceeding contesting its placement on the December 22, 2025 Covered List — and three drone-industry outlets (**DroneXL**, **DroneLife**, **DroneDJ**) ran simultaneous May 19, 2026 stories on the filing. The reply makes two arguments that have not previously been on the public docket as a single coherent record: (1) the FCC's underlying evidentiary basis for putting Autel on the Covered List relies on **classified material Autel was never permitted to see**, and (2) the FCC's opposition brief leans on **technical complaints originally aimed at DJI** (AeroScope behavior, mobile-app telemetry, alleged data collection) and uses them by category-association to justify keeping Autel on the list rather than conducting an individualized review of Autel's own products. Autel's reply includes sworn declarations describing its data-handling architecture in technical detail: **flight data is stored locally by default and not automatically uploaded to company servers**, **drone communications and stored data use AES-128 or AES-256 encryption**, and **no third party has been authorized to access drone operation software or customer accounts**. This is the first time Autel's substantive technical record has appeared in the docket as a single document; the reply-comment window in ET Docket 26-23 closed May 11, 2026 per FCC Public Notice DA 26-223 (context: window-close captured in [2026-05-09 digest](2026-05-09.md) and [2026-05-13 digest](2026-05-13.md)), and the May 19 filing reaches the docket through the established ex parte procedures the FCC announced for both DJI and Autel proceedings.

## Why this matters for Terraplex dealers

The Autel reply does not change the operative dealer reality — the December 22, 2025 Covered List ruling stands, the **DJI Agras** line remains blocked from new authorizations, and the OET DA 26-454 firmware waiver (see [2026-05-13 digest](2026-05-13.md)) extends installed-base support to at least January 1, 2029 — but it does sharpen one specific dealer-conversation point. The [2026-05-11 digest's](2026-05-11.md) read was that Commissioner Trusty's May 7 public defense of the Covered List rationale signaled reconsideration was unlikely to succeed at the Commission level, and the [2026-05-14 digest's](2026-05-14.md) AUVSI-Robbins / DoW-Cadenazzi / FAA-McIntosh three-pillar XPONENTIAL consensus hardened that read into "structurally durable advantage." Autel's reply is a procedural-defect argument that, even if it does not succeed, **shifts where the FCC has to do its work**: the Commission will now have to either (a) explain on the record why category-wide treatment based on DJI-specific allegations satisfies APA individualized-review requirements, or (b) order limited additional process. Either outcome takes time — meaning **the practical horizon for new DJI Agras or new Autel airframe authorizations in the US remains "not before late 2026 at the earliest, and more likely 2027+"** regardless of the underlying merits. The "Built in the Americas" dealer pitch for [GTEEX Revolution](../manufacturers/terraplex.md) and [Hylio](../manufacturers/hylio.md) hardware sits comfortably inside that horizon.

The secondary dealer-relevance point is on the **AES-encryption and local-by-default data-handling claims in Autel's sworn declarations**. These claims are now on the public docket and are likely to surface in customer conversations from operators considering whether to wait out the Covered List ruling. The honest dealer answer is: regardless of whether Autel's technical claims are accurate (and the FCC has not conceded them), **the Covered List status is the operative legal-procurement constraint, not the technical-data-handling debate** — and DoW Section 805 / 1260H enforcement (June 30, 2026 direct procurement, June 30, 2027 indirect; see [2026-05-14 digest](2026-05-14.md)) does not turn on encryption-strength rebuttals. Operators servicing DoD or DoD-supplier customers face the same June 30, 2027 indirect-supplier cutoff whether the underlying technical concerns turn out to be DJI-specific or category-general. The cleanest dealer line: "Autel's filing is procedural — it doesn't change what you can buy or operate today, and it doesn't change Section 805 enforcement on the dates already in statute."

## Key facts

### Autel files substantive reply in ET Docket 26-23 — May 19, 2026 coverage

- **Three drone-industry outlets ran simultaneous May 19, 2026 stories** on Autel's reply filing in ET Docket 26-23: DroneXL ("Autel Tells FCC The Drone Ban Rests On Secret Evidence And Borrowed Allegations Aimed At DJI"), DroneLife ("Autel Challenges FCC Covered List Decision, Distances Itself from DJI"), and DroneDJ ("Autel fights FCC drone ban, denies China data access claims") — three-domain corroboration on the same filing [source: https://dronexl.co/2026/05/19/autel-fcc-reply-covered-list-secret-evidence-dji/] [source: https://dronelife.com/2026/05/19/autel-fcc-covered-list-challenge/] [source: https://dronedj.com/2026/05/19/autel-drone-ban-us-fcc/]
- **Docket and deadline**: the reply lands in **ET Docket 26-23** — Autel's Application for Review of the December 22, 2025 Covered List ruling — with the reply-comment window having closed **May 11, 2026** under FCC Public Notice **DA 26-223** (March 6, 2026) [source: https://docs.fcc.gov/public/attachments/DA-26-223A1.pdf]
- **Procedural posture**: per the FCC's ex parte procedures announced for both proceedings, post-deadline material can still reach the docket through structured ex parte filings; the May 19 coverage describes Autel's reply as the substantive record-development the company has been waiting to put on the docket as a single coherent filing [source: https://www.fcc.gov/document/pn-announcing-dji-and-autel-ex-parte-administrative-procedures]

### Autel's two core arguments

- **Argument 1 — Secret-evidence challenge**: Autel argues the FCC added Autel to the Covered List **on the basis of classified material Autel was never permitted to see**, which constitutes a procedural defect under Administrative Procedure Act individualized-review requirements [source: https://dronexl.co/2026/05/19/autel-fcc-reply-covered-list-secret-evidence-dji/] [source: https://dronedj.com/2026/05/19/autel-drone-ban-us-fcc/]
- **Argument 2 — Borrowed-allegations challenge**: Autel argues the FCC's opposition brief leans heavily on **DJI-specific controversies** — AeroScope behavior, mobile-app telemetry, alleged data collection — and uses them by category-association to justify keeping Autel on the list, rather than conducting individualized review of Autel's own products; Autel's response is that **evidence against one company cannot automatically justify restrictions on another** [source: https://dronexl.co/2026/05/19/autel-fcc-reply-covered-list-secret-evidence-dji/] [source: https://dronelife.com/2026/05/19/autel-fcc-covered-list-challenge/]
- **Distancing strategy**: across all three outlets, Autel is reported as explicitly trying to **avoid being treated as interchangeable with [DJI](../manufacturers/dji-agras.md)** in the Covered List proceeding — a strategic departure from the unified DJI+Autel response posture that defined the early-2026 petition phase [source: https://dronelife.com/2026/05/19/autel-fcc-covered-list-challenge/]

### Autel's sworn data-handling declarations (newly on the public docket)

- **Local-by-default storage**: per Autel's sworn declarations in the filing, **flight data is stored locally by default and is not automatically uploaded to company servers** [source: https://dronedj.com/2026/05/19/autel-drone-ban-us-fcc/] [source: https://dronexl.co/2026/05/19/autel-fcc-reply-covered-list-secret-evidence-dji/]
- **Encryption strength**: Autel says its drone communications and stored data use **AES-128 or AES-256 encryption** [source: https://dronelife.com/2026/05/19/autel-fcc-covered-list-challenge/] [source: https://dronedj.com/2026/05/19/autel-drone-ban-us-fcc/]
- **Third-party access**: Autel says **no third party has been authorized to access drone operation software or customer accounts** [source: https://dronelife.com/2026/05/19/autel-fcc-covered-list-challenge/]
- **None of this operational detail appeared in the December 22, 2025 Public Notice** that put Autel on the Covered List — the May 19 reply is the first time Autel's substantive technical record has reached the public docket in a single document [source: https://dronexl.co/2026/05/19/autel-fcc-reply-covered-list-secret-evidence-dji/]

### What the Autel reply does NOT change

- **Covered List status unchanged**: Autel and [DJI](../manufacturers/dji-agras.md) remain on the December 22, 2025 Covered List; the filing is a procedural challenge, not a Commission ruling [source: https://dronelife.com/2026/05/19/autel-fcc-covered-list-challenge/]
- **No new authorizations**: the filing does not reopen new equipment authorizations for Autel, DJI, or any other Covered List entity — the DJI Agras T55 remains blocked, the existing [DJI Agras profile](../manufacturers/dji-agras.md) installed-base economics described in the revision-2026-05-01 baseline remain operative [source: https://dronexl.co/2026/05/19/autel-fcc-reply-covered-list-secret-evidence-dji/]
- **DA 26-454 firmware waiver unchanged**: the May 8, 2026 OET firmware waiver (see [2026-05-13 digest](2026-05-13.md) and the [2026-04 regulatory update](../regulatory/2026-04-update.md)) extending installed-base support to at least January 1, 2029 remains the operative installed-base policy; the Autel reply does not touch it
- **Section 805 / 1260H enforcement unchanged**: the DoW Section 805 direct-procurement ban (June 30, 2026) and indirect-supplier ban (June 30, 2027) named at Cadenazzi's May 12 XPONENTIAL Detroit keynote (see [2026-05-14 digest](2026-05-14.md)) does not turn on the FCC reply-filing process; the enforcement calendar runs independent of the FCC reconsideration proceedings

## Implications

- **For dealer-customer "wait it out" conversations**: when prospects ask whether they should wait for the FCC reconsideration to play out before committing to a domestic-OEM purchase, the Autel May 19 filing is **not a softening signal**. It is a procedural-defect argument that, even if it succeeds in extracting limited additional process from the Commission, takes time the prospects do not have if they need to fly the 2026 season. The honest dealer line is "the FCC fight is real, the procedural arguments have substance, but on the dealer-purchase horizon — 30 to 120 days — nothing about today's filing changes what you can buy and what you can fly." Reinforces the [2026-05-14 digest's](2026-05-14.md) "structurally durable advantage" framing for [GTEEX Revolution](../manufacturers/terraplex.md) and [Hylio](../manufacturers/hylio.md).
- **For dealer responses to encryption-strength talking points**: if a prospect cites Autel's AES-128/AES-256 sworn declarations as evidence that the underlying security concerns are misplaced, the accurate dealer answer is that **the Covered List status and Section 805 enforcement do not turn on encryption-strength rebuttals** — they turn on national-security findings the FCC and DoW have already made and on the supply-chain provenance of the components inside the airframe (see Cadenazzi's flagged components in the [2026-05-14 digest](2026-05-14.md): flight controllers, motors, batteries, ESCs, rare-earth inputs). Encryption strength is not the cited risk surface; Autel's filing is a procedural-defect argument, not a merits-overturn argument.
- **For the [DJI Agras profile](../manufacturers/dji-agras.md) revision history**: when the profile next refreshes (monthly intelligence pass cadence), the revision should note that **Autel filed its substantive reply distancing itself from DJI** on May 19, 2026 — a strategic departure from the unified petition posture that defined the January–February 2026 phase. The DJI side of the Covered List proceeding (ET Docket 26-22, Petition for Reconsideration) is now procedurally independent from Autel's Application for Review (ET Docket 26-23) in a more pointed way than the earlier filings suggested.
- **For monitoring follow-up**: watch (a) any **DJI ex parte reply** in ET Docket 26-22 that mirrors Autel's filing strategy — DJI has not yet put its substantive technical record on the public docket as a single document, and the Autel filing is a template; (b) any Commission-level vote on either proceeding (no date set as of this digest); (c) the FAA radar-data-sharing test program announcement that McIntosh teed up at May 12 XPONENTIAL — the two-week window expires approximately May 26, 2026, so this should land within ~5 days of this digest; (d) any USDA, DoW, or DHS Conditional Approval program that references domestic component-supplier qualification standards (Wiley Rein "first Conditional Approvals" remains the structural watch-item from [2026-05-13 digest](2026-05-13.md)).
