## Summary
DJI Agriculture used Agrishow 2026 in Ribeirão Preto, Brazil (April 30, 2026) to announce that its global agricultural-drone fleet has crossed 600,000 units — a 50% jump from the 400,000 figure DJI reported at Agrishow 2025 — operated by more than 600,000 trained pilots across 100+ countries, supported by 3,500 service-and-repair centers and over 7,000 certified instructors. DJI's accompanying impact claims: roughly 410 million tonnes of water saved (roughly the annual drinking-water consumption of 740 million people) and ~51 million tonnes of carbon emissions cut versus conventional spraying. The announcement was rolled out via DJI's own press release on April 30 and continues to surface in trade-press coverage through May 4.

## Why this matters for Terraplex dealers
The 600,000-unit number is the freshest authoritative global benchmark on ag-drone adoption available, and it materially updates the 400,000-unit figure quoted in the [2026-05-02 digest](2026-05-02.md) (which reflected DJI's 2024 white paper rather than the Agrishow 2026 update). The +50% step-up in roughly 18 months is the strongest single signal that ag-drone adoption globally is still in the steep part of its growth curve, and it pairs cleanly with the American Spray Drone Coalition's domestic +58.7% YoY treated-acreage figure (also covered in the [2026-05-02 digest](2026-05-02.md)) to anchor a "this category is real and accelerating" frame for fence-sitting customers.

The dealer-actionable nuance: DJI's number is a *global* operator-base story at the same time the [DJI Agras profile](../manufacturers/dji-agras.md) shows the US channel is structurally locked out of new-SKU imports under the December 22, 2025 FCC covered-list ruling (context: ruling effective 2025-12-22; appeal Case 26-1029 active). Customers who hear "DJI is the standard" need to be redirected to "DJI is the global standard; the US 2026 channel is on a different supply track because of the FCC ruling" — without conceding that the GTEEX Revolution lineup the [Terraplex parent profile](../manufacturers/terraplex.md) distributes is somehow second-tier. The right framing is that the global maturity numbers (3,500 service centers, 7,000 instructors, 600K pilots) prove the operator/training infrastructure for ag-drone is post-experimental everywhere, including the US — which strengthens, not weakens, the dealer's pitch on operator confidence and post-sale support.

## Implications

- **For dealer onboarding scripts and "is ag-drone real?" customer conversations**: update the global-context stat from 400,000 (DJI 2024 white paper) to 600,000 (DJI Agrishow 2026 announcement). The 600K figure is the freshest authoritative reference and the +50%-in-~18-months delta makes the "this is still accelerating" narrative sharper than the prior +33% framing. Pair with the American Spray Drone Coalition's domestic +58.7% YoY acreage figure from the [2026-05-02 digest](2026-05-02.md) for the US-specific complement.

- **For competitive objection handling**: when a prospect says "DJI is the standard," the honest answer is "globally yes — and that proves the technology and operator-training infrastructure are mature" rather than a defensive deflection. The dealer can then pivot to the US channel reality: existing FCC-authorized DJI SKUs remain legal to operate, but the new-SKU pipeline is gated by the appeal (Case 26-1029) at the Ninth Circuit, while [Terraplex's GTEEX Revolution distribution](../manufacturers/terraplex.md) sits on a structurally separate, NDAA-aligned supply track. See [DJI Agras profile](../manufacturers/dji-agras.md) for the SKU-by-SKU availability framing dealers should use on quotes.

- **For positioning copy referencing global scale**: 100+ countries, 600K operators, 3,500 service centers, and 7,000 instructors collectively constitute a "post-experimental" reference point for ag-drone in customer-facing copy. Dealers describing the operator and service ecosystem should not understate global maturity — the maturity is the dealer's friend (it normalizes the technology for hesitant customers) even though it sits behind a Chinese-supply-chain that the US channel is moving away from.

- **For ESG-adjacent customer conversations** (sustainability talks, corporate-buyer due diligence, or grant-funded acreage programs): DJI's water-saved (410 Mt) and carbon-saved (51 Mt) numbers are vendor-reported and not third-party audited, so they are not the right primary citation for a dealer's own claims. The right use is as a directional industry signal — "the global category claims X-scale environmental impact, and our [GTEEX product line](../../products/r-32.md) operates on the same per-acre water-and-spray-volume reduction physics" — rather than as a number a dealer should quote as their own.

- **For territory / channel thinking and the deeper dealer question of "what's the install base going to look like in 5 years?"**: a global fleet that grew from 400K → 600K in 18 months at +50% with no apparent slowdown suggests US 2026's −59% unit-sales drop (per the [2026-05-02 digest](2026-05-02.md)'s Coalition data) is structurally about supply, not demand. When the FCC appeal resolves — in either direction — the underlying demand curve is the global one, not the constrained-supply US one. Dealers planning fleet additions and territory expansion should size against the global trajectory, not the 2026 US shipment dip.
