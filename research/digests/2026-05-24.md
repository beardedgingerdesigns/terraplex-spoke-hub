## Summary

The FCC's Public Safety and Homeland Security Bureau released **Public Notice DA 26-489 on Friday, May 15, 2026**, granting **Conditional Approval** — based on a Department of War (DoW) trust determination — to two additional foreign-component drone manufacturers, exempting their listed systems from Covered List restrictions through **December 31, 2026**. The newly cleared platforms are **Elevon Aerial, LLC (d/b/a Elevon Aerial AG)** — a small-town Illinois assembler near St. Louis whose EFT-branded **Z30, Z50, and Z80** spray drones are aimed squarely at the US precision-agriculture market — and **Air6 Systems GmbH** of Austria, whose industrial-inspection **AIR8 Medium Lifter** (10 kg payload, 60 min endurance) and smaller **AIR4** platforms are non-ag. The DA 26-489 grant is the **first time the DoW-cleared Conditional Approval channel has been used by an ag-spray-focused assembler**, opening a third "Built in the Americas" market lane that sits between fully native engineering (Hylio's Richmond TX line) and US-final-assembly of imported airframes (Elevon's EFT-frame model).

## Why this matters for Terraplex dealers

DA 26-489 reshapes the "Built in America" dealer pitch in a specific way: it is no longer a two-channel landscape. Through 2026-05-15 the cleanly-domestic ag-spray narrative had two recognized lanes — the [GTEEX Revolution lineup that Terraplex distributes](../manufacturers/terraplex.md) (engineered and assembled across NC / KS / NE with US component supply, see the [2026-05-03 digest](2026-05-03.md)) and [Hylio's fully native engineering](../manufacturers/hylio.md) (Richmond TX HQ, $2M StartEngine raise in late April per the [2026-05-01 digest](2026-05-01.md); HYL-150 Ares billed as "NDAA-compliant" with "US and global parts"). DA 26-489 now formally adds a **third lane** — US final-assembly of EFT-branded airframes under DoW Conditional Approval — into the channel-permitted set. Elevon's value proposition is explicitly Midwest (small-town Illinois, just outside St. Louis), explicitly "with farmers in mind, rugged, simple to operate," and explicitly cleared through the FCC equipment-authorization pipeline that the December 22, 2025 Covered List ruling otherwise closed off. For dealers in Iowa, Missouri, and Illinois geographic territories — which sit directly in Elevon's assembly-and-service catchment — the competitive map now contains a domestic-assembly, foreign-frame entrant whose pricing structure (imported airframe + US labor) is likely different from both the GTEEX (US-component) and Hylio (US-engineered) cost stacks.

Two caveats temper the read. **First**, Conditional Approval is **time-limited** — all current DoW conditional clearances under the Covered List exemption framework terminate **December 31, 2026** unless renewed, so a dealer or customer treating Elevon's authorization as a multi-year operational guarantee is overreading the order; renewal is at FCC + DoW discretion. **Second**, the FCC public notice **does not publish the technical conditions** attached to each approval — it confirms only that DoW determined the listed platforms do not pose unacceptable national-security risks. Customers comparing Elevon to a GTEEX or Hylio spec sheet should be told (a) the regulatory equivalence-of-channel-access exists through year-end 2026; (b) the underlying supply-chain composition is meaningfully different (foreign frame + US assembly vs. US-component sourcing); and (c) the Section 805 / 1260H Department of War contractor-notification pilots referenced by Cadenazzi at XPONENTIAL (see the [2026-05-14 digest](2026-05-14.md)) apply to DoW-procurement linkages, not to commercial-farmer purchases, so the Conditional Approval doesn't carry over to defense-related buyers.

This is a regulatory-channel story, not a head-to-head product story. Elevon's EFT Z50 specs (50 L payload, swappable spreader, Control System 2.0) sit between the GTEEX Revolution platform and the Hylio HYL-150 Ares (110 lb / 13 gal liquid; up to 50+ ac/hr liquid, 120 ac/hr spreading) on payload class. The DA 26-489 grant doesn't change those specs — it changes which of those specs can be sold through FCC-authorized channels in the US.

## Key facts

### FCC Public Notice DA 26-489 (May 15, 2026) — DoW Conditional Approval added for Elevon Aerial AG and Air6 Systems GmbH

- The FCC's **Public Safety and Homeland Security Bureau released Public Notice DA 26-489 on Friday, May 15, 2026** announcing additional Conditional Approval grants under the Covered List exemption framework, based on a Department of War trust determination [source: https://docs.fcc.gov/public/attachments/DA-26-489A1.pdf] [source: https://dronelife.com/2026/05/18/cc-covered-list-drone-exemptions-elevon-air6/]
- **Elevon Aerial, LLC, doing business as Elevon Aerial AG**, received Conditional Approval for its **Z30, Z50, and Z80 Uncrewed Aircraft Systems** — approvals valid **May 15, 2026 – December 31, 2026** [source: https://dronelife.com/2026/05/18/cc-covered-list-drone-exemptions-elevon-air6/] [source: https://broadbandbreakfast.com/fcc-expands-conditional-drone-approvals-under-covered-list/]
- **Air6 Systems GmbH** (Austria) — separately approved on the same notice — covers the **AIR8 Medium Lifter** (industrial missions including LiDAR mapping, inspection, logistics, specialized sensors; up to **10 kg payload**, up to **60 min flight time**, optional 4G/5G/satellite communications) plus several smaller **AIR4** platforms [source: https://dronelife.com/2026/05/18/cc-covered-list-drone-exemptions-elevon-air6/] [source: https://www.air6systems.com/applications/]
- **All current Conditional Approvals listed by the FCC are time-limited and currently terminate at the end of 2026**; the public notice does not provide technical details about the aircraft or explain what conditions were attached to the approval process [source: https://dronelife.com/2026/05/18/cc-covered-list-drone-exemptions-elevon-air6/] [source: https://broadbandbreakfast.com/fcc-expands-conditional-drone-approvals-under-covered-list/]
- **Procedural mechanism**: the Conditional Approval channel was first introduced in March 2026 (see [DRONELIFE coverage](https://dronelife.com/2026/03/20/fcc-updates-covered-list-introduces-first-conditional-approvals-for-drone-systems/) of the first-batch announcement); DA 26-489 is the latest addition to that running list. Conditional Approval **does not modify Covered List status**, **does not affect Blue UAS**, and **does not reopen DJI / Autel new-equipment authorizations** — it is a manufacturer-specific carve-out at the equipment-authorization layer, distinct from the [DA 26-454 firmware-update waiver](2026-05-13.md) covered in the 2026-05-13 digest

### Elevon Aerial AG — Midwest US assembler of EFT-frame ag drones

- **Company location and assembly model**: Elevon Aerial Ag is described on its own site as "rooted in the Midwest"; the EFT Z50P is **"assembled in small-town Illinois,"** "just outside St. Louis" [source: https://www.elevonaerialag.com/] [source: https://www.elevonaerialag.com/about]
- **Operating history**: company states it has been operating **EFT drones in its own spraying operations, servicing thousands of acres every year, for the past 3 years** before field-testing and selling them — putting the in-house operating base back to roughly 2023 [source: https://www.elevonaerialag.com/about]
- **EFT Z-series payload classes**: Z30 (~30 L liquid / 30 kg payload class), Z50 (~50 L tank with exchangeable spreader, upgraded Control System 2.0); the **Z80 model named in DA 26-489 is not yet detailed on Elevon's public product pages** at access time, so the larger-payload SKU appears to be a forthcoming or newly added platform [source: https://www.elevonaerialag.com/] [source: https://dronelife.com/2026/05/18/cc-covered-list-drone-exemptions-elevon-air6/]
- **Positioning language** from elevonaerialag.com: "drones that are dependable, simple to operate, and assembled in the U.S."; explicit anti-pitch is "we won't sell it" if "it's not field-ready" — a service-led, in-house-operator framing rather than a spec-sheet pitch [source: https://www.elevonaerialag.com/about]

## Implications

### For dealer positioning copy

- **Update the "Built in the Americas" framing** from a two-lane pitch (US-component-sourced GTEEX vs. US-engineered Hylio) to a **three-lane pitch** that explicitly distinguishes (a) US-component sourcing — the strongest supply-chain claim — from (b) US-engineering — strong product-IP claim — from (c) **US-assembly-of-imported-frames under DoW Conditional Approval** — a regulatory-channel claim that is **time-limited to December 31, 2026 absent renewal**. Don't conflate them; customers who hear "American-made" applied to all three will be confused if a renewal lapse changes Elevon's status.
- The [Hylio profile's](../manufacturers/hylio.md) revision-2026-05-01 caveat that batteries are still China-sourced is now a useful **contrast point** rather than a vulnerability: Hylio's frame and engineering remain native; Elevon's regulatory channel is conditional and renews annually.

### For pricing conversations

- A foreign-frame + US-assembly cost stack is likely to **undercut** both the GTEEX (US-component) and Hylio (US-engineered) cost stacks on listed price, because imported EFT airframes are at high commodity volume in the global ag-drone market. Dealers should anticipate price comparisons surfacing in customer conversations within the next 30–60 days, particularly in IL / MO / IA where Elevon's catchment overlaps the Terraplex dealer footprint. The honest answer is: lower frame cost does not equal lower total cost of ownership when the regulatory authorization expires Dec 31, 2026 and any 2027-onward service depends on FCC + DoW renewal — that uncertainty has a real dollar value.
- For customers who explicitly prioritize **Department of War / Blue UAS / federal-procurement** eligibility (county sheriff partnerships, USDA pilots, university research projects), **Conditional Approval is not equivalent** to Blue UAS — it does not flow through to those federal-procurement pathways. The GTEEX lineup and other US-engineered platforms remain the cleaner answer for those customers; the DA 26-489 grant is a commercial-channel mechanism only.

### For onboarding scripts

- Add Elevon Aerial AG to the **competitor watch list** in dealer onboarding scripts, alongside Hylio, Revolution Drones, and Exedy Drones (see the [2026-05-03 digest](2026-05-03.md) for the prior watch-list update). Note the **Conditional Approval expiration date (Dec 31, 2026)** in the onboarding script so new dealers can track renewal as it approaches Q4 2026.
- Flag for new dealers that the **Conditional Approval channel is a moving target** — the [March 2026 first batch](https://dronelife.com/2026/03/20/fcc-updates-covered-list-introduces-first-conditional-approvals-for-drone-systems/), this **May 15 DA 26-489 addition**, and any further batches form a running list that should be re-checked monthly; the regulatory update file ([2026-04-update.md](../regulatory/2026-04-update.md)) should be the next routine refresh point to consolidate the Conditional Approval channel as a standing topic.
