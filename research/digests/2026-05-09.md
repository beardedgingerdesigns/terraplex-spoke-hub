## Summary
The FCC's public-comment window on DJI and Autel's Petitions for Reconsideration of the December 23, 2025 Covered List ruling closes end-of-day Monday, **May 11, 2026** — two days from now. DJI's petition sits in **ET Docket 26-22** and Autel's in **ET Docket 26-23**; reply comments are accepted via the FCC Electronic Comment Filing System through the Express filing path (short text) or the Standard path (attached documents). More than 460 comments had been logged in ECFS as of May 7, 2026, including filings from US farmers, FAA Part 107 license holders, search-and-rescue operators, and industry advocacy groups; the Department of Defense filed an opposition memorandum on April 10, 2026 citing both classified and unclassified intelligence. This administrative-petition track is procedurally distinct from DJI's parallel Ninth Circuit appeal (Case 26-1029, filed February 20, 2026), which remains in jurisdictional briefing with DJI requesting a six-month abeyance to November 2026.

## Why this matters for Terraplex dealers
The May 11 deadline is the last on-ramp for dealers and their farmer customers to put operational facts in front of FCC decision-makers before the Commission acts on the petitions. Filing is open to anyone via [fcc.gov/ecfs](https://www.fcc.gov/ecfs) — a dealer who has lost prospective DJI Agras T100 / T70P / T50 customers since the December ruling, or who has redirected those customers to the [GTEEX Revolution](../manufacturers/terraplex.md) lineup or [Hylio](../manufacturers/hylio.md) ARES / AG-230 hardware, has direct first-person facts that public-comment filings need (and that the broader trade-press coverage tends to abstract). This is materially different from the [Section 2209 NPRM comment window covered in the 2026-05-07 digest](2026-05-07.md) — that one closes July 6, 2026 and concerns FAA airspace; this one closes Monday and concerns FCC equipment authorization, which is what actually gates whether new DJI/Autel hardware can be sold or imported into the US at all.

The substantive outcome also matters for dealer-customer conversations through the rest of the 2026 spray season. A reversal of the Covered List decision would re-open authorization pathways for new DJI Agras models — meaning the "domestic-only fleet" pitch dealers have been making since January loses some of its permanence. A reaffirmation locks in the current regime: no new DJI Agras model can be FCC-authorized for sale or import regardless of how the [Ninth Circuit appeal](../regulatory/2026-04-update.md) ultimately resolves, and the [Hylio US-manufactured runway](../manufacturers/hylio.md) plus the [GTEEX Revolution distribution program through Terraplex](../manufacturers/terraplex.md) keep their structural advantage through at least 2026. Dealers should not be promising customers DJI re-authorization timing in either direction until the Commission acts.

## Key facts

### FCC public-comment window on DJI / Autel Covered List petitions closes May 11, 2026
- Reply-comment deadline is end-of-day Monday, **May 11, 2026** for both **ET Docket 26-22 (DJI)** and **ET Docket 26-23 (Autel)**; submissions accepted via the FCC Electronic Comment Filing System, with the Express path at fcc.gov/ecfs/filings/express for short text comments and the Standard path at fcc.gov/ecfs/filings/standard for attached documents [source: https://dronexl.co/2026/05/07/dji-autel-fcc-may-11-comment-deadline-covered-list/] [source: https://dronelife.com/2026/05/05/public-comment-window-closing-fcc-weighs-dji-challenge-to-covered-list-ruling/]
- DJI filed its Petition for Reconsideration on **January 21, 2026**, arguing the FCC's December 23, 2025 Covered List action exceeded statutory authority and bypassed required procedures; the petition was published in the Federal Register on **February 5, 2026** as Document 2026-02285 [source: https://www.federalregister.gov/documents/2026/02/05/2026-02285/petition-for-reconsideration-of-action-in-rulemaking-proceeding-application-for-review-of-action-in]
- The FCC's Public Safety and Homeland Security Bureau released **DA 26-223** on **March 6, 2026**, the public-notice order that established the comment cycle and docket numbering for the petitions [source: https://docs.fcc.gov/public/attachments/DA-26-223A1.pdf]
- More than **460 comments** had been logged in ECFS as of May 7, 2026 — submitters include US farmers describing direct operational impact on Part 137 spray operations, FAA Part 107 license holders, public-safety agencies, search-and-rescue operators (citing thermal-imaging dependency), and industry advocacy groups [source: https://dronexl.co/2026/05/07/dji-autel-fcc-may-11-comment-deadline-covered-list/] [source: https://dronedj.com/2026/05/06/dji-drone-ban-fcc-comments/]
- The **Department of Defense filed an opposition memorandum on April 10, 2026** urging the FCC to reject DJI's petition, citing both classified and unclassified intelligence (context: Pentagon brief filed in advance of the comment window) [source: https://dronelife.com/2026/04/10/pentagon-memo-dji-fcc-petition/]
- This administrative-petition proceeding is **procedurally distinct from DJI's Ninth Circuit appeal** (Case 26-1029, filed February 20, 2026), which is currently in jurisdictional briefing — DJI has proposed a six-month abeyance to November 2026 and the FCC has filed a motion to dismiss; oral arguments have not been scheduled (context: appeal filed 2026-02-20) [source: https://dronedj.com/2026/05/06/dji-drone-ban-fcc-comments/]
- Coverage of the deadline appeared in the same-week DroneXL May 8 weekly roundup pairing the FCC May 11 deadline with the [FAA Section 2209 NPRM](2026-05-07.md) and 2025 wildfire-incursion data — confirming the two regulatory tracks (FCC equipment authorization, FAA airspace) are reaching consequential moments simultaneously [source: https://dronexl.co/2026/05/08/faa-drops-section-2209-nprm-dji-fcc-deadline/]
- The petitioned action is the FCC's December 23, 2025 ruling adding foreign-made UAS and key components to the Covered List, blocking new equipment authorizations for [DJI](../manufacturers/dji-agras.md), Autel, and XAG — the underlying ruling whose $1.5B 2026 sales-impact estimate was captured in the [2026-05-01 digest](2026-05-01.md) (context: ruling occurred 2025-12-23)

## Implications

- **For dealer advocacy**: the May 11 deadline is the practical close of the FCC's window for first-person operator/dealer facts. A dealer who has lost specific prospective DJI Agras customers since December — with named-territory or named-acreage detail — has standing the trade press cannot supply. Filing via fcc.gov/ecfs/filings/express takes minutes; the [49-location dealer network](../manufacturers/terraplex.md) can coordinate a multi-dealer set of submissions that carries more weight than individual filings, particularly on the operational-realism questions (parts availability, replacement-airframe lead times, customer redirect to domestic OEMs).
- **For dealer-customer conversations**: do not promise a DJI re-authorization timeline either direction until the Commission acts. The honest framing is "the petition outcome is pending; presently no new DJI Agras model can be FCC-authorized for US sale or import; we recommend the [GTEEX Revolution](../manufacturers/terraplex.md) and [Hylio](../manufacturers/hylio.md) options that have a clear regulatory path." Avoid speculating on reversal odds.
- **For positioning copy**: the "Built in the Americas, for the American Farmer" line is structurally safe under either outcome — a Covered-List reversal still leaves DJI Agras hardware with the lead-time and parts-availability handicap of the current 2026 regulatory environment, and the GTEEX / Hylio domestic supply chain remains the lower-friction path for any customer closing through Q3. **Do not** add language that bakes in a permanent DJI exclusion; the petition could reverse that.
- **For onboarding scripts**: when prospects compare against DJI Agras product specs in their decision process, route through the comparison data Sam Welton's [compliance program](../../content/compliance-program.md) already covers (operational availability, regulatory path) rather than getting drawn into petition-outcome speculation.
- **For product mix planning**: a reaffirmation by the FCC locks in the current channel structure for at least the rest of 2026; a reversal opens an unknown lead time for new DJI Agras authorizations to clear and for distribution to rebuild. Either way, the [R-32](../../products/r-32.md) and [I-19](../../products/i-19.md) inventory plan for the 2026 spray season remains the right commitment.
