## Summary
AgFunder and AgWeb both report that Revolution Drones — the US-side partner brand of GTEEX, whose lineup Terraplex exclusively distributes — is scaling American manufacturing under founder Russell Hedrick (Hickory, NC), with components built across NC / NE / IN / IL and final assembly in NC plus KS or NE. Hedrick is launching a 100% American-made mapping drone alongside an "Independent" series (≤29-gallon spray) and a "Revolution" series (≥30-gallon spray); cumulative units shipped or built to date are reported in the low hundreds. In a companion piece, AgFunder names Japanese-owned Exedy Drones as a second new domestic entrant — Michigan manufacturing, 50–70-liter payload tier, launching this year — while flagging that existing US-made airframes from Hylio and Ceres Air still cost three to five times more than the Chinese hardware they replace, with limited production volume.

## Why this matters for Terraplex dealers
This is the most directly hub-relevant news in any digest to date: Revolution Drones is the OEM-partner brand behind the [R-32](../../products/r-32.md) and [I-19](../../products/i-19.md) products that Terraplex's 49-location dealer network sells (see the [Terraplex profile](../manufacturers/terraplex.md) for the GTEEX ↔ Revolution ↔ Terraplex chain). External coverage of Hedrick's US production ramp validates the [content/positioning.md](../../content/positioning.md) "Built in the Americas, for the American farmer" brand language with cite-able third-party reporting — dealers can now point to AgFunder and AgWeb rather than only the corporate site. The "components from NC / NE / IN / IL, assembly in NC + KS or NE" supply-chain map is the kind of concrete provenance detail that prospects evaluating made-in-America claims will ask about, and it survives the FCC covered-list scrutiny that tripped up DJI inventories (context: FCC ruling occurred 2025-12-22; see [April 2026 regulatory roundup](../regulatory/2026-04-update.md)).

The companion Made-in-America piece is also dealer-relevant defensively: AgFunder's "three-to-five-times-more-expensive" framing for Hylio/Ceres Air is a price objection dealers should expect from prospects who have read the article and assume all American-made airframes carry the same premium. Terraplex dealers can counter with the actual GTEEX Revolution price point ([I-19 listed at ~$51,500 starting](../manufacturers/terraplex.md)) and the fact that the Revolution lineup is built by a working farmer, not a SaaS-style domestic-manufacturing startup. Exedy's 50–70 L (≈13–18 gal) payload tier slots between the I-19 and R-32 ranges and is worth tracking as a future competitor — though "launching this year" with timing dependent on regulatory factors means commercial impact is unlikely before late 2026.

## Key facts

### Item 1: Revolution Drones scales US manufacturing; Exedy enters US ag-drone market
- AgFunder reports Revolution Drones is the brainchild of [Russell Hedrick](https://x.com/covercropnc), a first-generation farmer growing corn and soybeans in Hickory, North Carolina; the company is launching a 100% American-made mapping drone, an "Independent" series of spray drones at 29 gallons or under, and a "Revolution" series at 30 gallons or more [source: https://agfundernews.com/revolution-drones-and-exedy-drones-target-us-ag-spray-drone-market-as-fcc-rewrites-the-rules]
- AgWeb reports Hedrick launched his first drone at Husker Harvest Days in 2025; he had sold approximately 250 drones, with another 100 built and 200 more projected to be built by end of February 2026 [source: https://www.agweb.com/news/business/technology/farmer-builds-drone-company-eyes-historic-breakthrough-agriculture-tech]
- Per AgWeb, after the FCC ruling, Revolution Drones production shifted to the US: components built in North Carolina, Nebraska, Indiana, and Illinois, with final assembly in North Carolina and either Kansas or Nebraska [source: https://www.agweb.com/news/business/technology/farmer-builds-drone-company-eyes-historic-breakthrough-agriculture-tech]
- Hedrick (per AgWeb): "you literally won't find a farm with a planter that doesn't also own a drone, because the savings and functions are incredible" [source: https://www.agweb.com/news/business/technology/farmer-builds-drone-company-eyes-historic-breakthrough-agriculture-tech]
- AgFunder describes Exedy Drones as a division of Exedy Global Parts (parent: Japanese EXEDY Corporation), building drone manufacturing capabilities in Michigan; first products in the 50–70-liter payload range, targeted to launch this year, with timing dependent on regulatory factors [source: https://agfundernews.com/revolution-drones-and-exedy-drones-target-us-ag-spray-drone-market-as-fcc-rewrites-the-rules]
- Companion AgFunder piece names [Hylio](../manufacturers/hylio.md) (Texas) and Vermont-based Ceres Air as the established US-made-drone players picking up reallocated business; quotes that "American-made alternatives from Hylio and Ceres Air exist but cost three to five times more than the Chinese hardware they replace," with production volume remaining limited [source: https://agfundernews.com/made-in-america-fcc-decision-sparks-scramble-to-localize-ag-spray-drone-production]
- Triggering regulatory backdrop (context: ruling occurred 2025-12-22): the FCC added all foreign-made drones and critical components — motors, flight controllers, navigation systems, batteries — to its covered list, blocking new equipment authorizations for [DJI](../manufacturers/dji-agras.md), Autel, XAG, and any platform built outside the US [source: https://agfundernews.com/revolution-drones-and-exedy-drones-target-us-ag-spray-drone-market-as-fcc-rewrites-the-rules]

## Implications
- **For dealer positioning copy**: anchor the "Built in the Americas, for the American farmer" claim ([content/positioning.md](../../content/positioning.md)) to the AgFunder + AgWeb reporting on Revolution Drones rather than only the corporate site. The "components from NC / NE / IN / IL, assembly in NC + KS or NE" supply-chain map is the most concrete cite-able provenance fact yet available for the Revolution series — useful in dealer site copy and in dealer-chat answers when a prospect asks "where is this actually made?"
- **For pricing conversations**: when prospects raise the AgFunder "3–5× more expensive than Chinese hardware" framing, the rebuttal is that the cited multiplier applies to Hylio/Ceres Air, not to the GTEEX Revolution lineup that Terraplex distributes. The [I-19 lists at ~$51,500 starting](../manufacturers/terraplex.md) — that is the data point dealers should bring to the conversation rather than letting the article's price framing stand uncontested.
- **For onboarding scripts**: add a short "what does it mean that Revolution Drones is American-made now?" answer to the dealer-chat playbook. The honest version: Hedrick is a working corn-and-soybean farmer in NC who founded the brand; the partnership with GTEEX (Brazil) supplies the airframe lineage; final assembly is on US soil with a US supply chain after the FCC ruling forced the shift. This is a stronger story than a generic "made in America" claim because the founder and supply-chain map are specific.
- **For competitive watch**: Exedy Drones (Japanese parent, Michigan manufacturing, 50–70 L payload) slots between the I-19 (≈20 gal liquid / 76 L) and a smaller-spray tier — the lower end of Exedy's payload range overlaps the Independent-series space Hedrick is also targeting. Track Exedy's actual launch date, dealer signup model, and pricing when the product appears; flag for the [competitor-distributors](../dealer-economics/competitor-distributors.md) profile if Exedy stands up an independent dealer channel that overlaps Terraplex territories.
- **For territory and channel thinking**: cumulative shipments in the low hundreds of units (per AgWeb) suggests Revolution Drones output is still in early-rampscale — dealers should manage customer expectations on lead times accordingly, and the supply-constrained 2026 environment flagged in the [2026-05-02 digest](2026-05-02.md) (Coalition survey: +58.7% acreage / −59% units) continues to support holding MAP firm through this season.
