## Summary

Backfill of a February 18, 2026 industry development missed by prior daily digests: **KULR Technology Group (NYSE American: KULR) and Hylio entered a Joint Development Collaboration to design, prototype, qualify, and domestically manufacture NDAA-compliant battery systems in Texas** for integration into Hylio's unmanned agricultural drone platforms — explicitly naming the partner behind the unattributed phrase "working on new battery chemistry with some partners" in the [Hylio profile's](../manufacturers/hylio.md) revision-2026-05-01 supply-chain caveat. The collaboration targets high-performance battery architectures for Hylio's agricultural and "defense-adjacent" applications, on a U.S.-based supply chain footing. Separately, **today (May 18, 2026) is the reply-comment deadline for FCC GN Docket No. 26-74 "Unleashing American Drone Dominance"** (the proceeding rooted in Executive Orders 14307 and 14305 signed June 2025) — the proceeding that 90+ initial comments addressed by the May 1 first-round deadline (context: existence of this docket noted in the [2026-05-08 digest](2026-05-08.md) under the FCC public-notice DA 26-314 reference). The KULR/Hylio item is the substantive ingest; the GN Docket 26-74 deadline is a regulatory-calendar checkpoint.

## Why this matters for Terraplex dealers

The KULR/Hylio JDA is the **named-partner update** to the [Hylio profile's](../manufacturers/hylio.md) revision-2026-05-01 supply-chain caveat. That profile (and the [2026-05-16 digest's](2026-05-16.md) closing paragraph cross-referencing it) currently reads "batteries are still China-sourced, with new battery chemistry 'in work with partners' but not yet shipping — so dealers should script around domestic airframe/assembly rather than full BOM." The Hylio profile's language is anonymous ("some partners"); the February 18, 2026 KULR press release names KULR Technology Group — a NYSE American-listed energy-and-thermal-management company — as the JDA partner. The product is **not shipping yet** (the agreement covers "design, prototype, qualify, and domestically manufacture" — i.e., a development pipeline, not a 2026 shipping product), so the profile's "no domestic battery is shipping yet" caveat remains accurate. The change is that dealers now have a **specific, public, NYSE-listed partner name** to reference when customers ask "who is actually building the domestic batteries?" rather than the anonymous "some partners" formulation. This is also the cleanest direct continuation of the [2026-05-16 ePropelled digest](2026-05-16.md)'s observation that the propulsion-and-power layer of the "Made in America" supply chain is being filled in on the same calendar as Section 805 enforcement: KULR-Hylio (batteries) and ePropelled (motors + ESCs + power management) are the two named domestic-allied supplier partnerships landing on the same component stack that Cadenazzi flagged at his May 12 XPONENTIAL Detroit keynote (context: see [2026-05-14 digest](2026-05-14.md)).

The GN Docket 26-74 reply-comment deadline today is a **procedural milestone**, not a substantive ruling. The proceeding is the FCC's spectrum-and-licensing companion track to the December 22, 2025 Covered List action — i.e., the affirmative "build up the domestic drone industry" side, distinct from the negative "block the foreign-made drones" side. Dealers won't see operational impact today; the value of flagging the deadline is so that when the FCC issues a Report and Order out of this proceeding in the coming months, the [American Spray Drone Coalition](../regulatory/2026-04-update.md) and the broader ag-drone trade-press cycle will already have framed it as a sequel to today's filing window. Dealers should not editorialize on what the FCC will do; they should track that this is the regulatory channel the "Built in the Americas" structural advantage (see [2026-05-11](2026-05-11.md) and [2026-05-14](2026-05-14.md) digests for the consensus-positioning context) gets formally codified through.

## Key facts

### Item 1: KULR/Hylio Joint Development Collaboration for Texas-manufactured NDAA-compliant battery systems

- **Announcement date**: February 18, 2026. KULR Technology Group, Inc. (NYSE American: KULR) announced it has entered into a **Joint Development Collaboration with [Hylio, Inc.](../manufacturers/hylio.md)**, the Texas-based designer and manufacturer of advanced unmanned agricultural drones [source: https://kulr.ai/kulr-and-hylio-announce-strategic-collaboration-to-produce-texas-manufactured-battery-systems-for-u-s-built-unmanned-agricultural-drones/] [source: https://www.globenewswire.com/news-release/2026/02/18/3240208/0/en/KULR-and-Hylio-Announce-Strategic-Collaboration-to-Produce-Texas-Manufactured-Battery-Systems-for-U-S-Built-Unmanned-Agricultural-Drones.html]
- **Scope of the JDA**: The collaboration aims to **design, prototype, qualify, and domestically manufacture NDAA-compliant (National Defense Authorization Act) battery systems in Texas** for integration into Hylio's unmanned aerial systems (UAS) platforms — explicitly emphasizing **secure, U.S.-based supply chains** [source: https://dronelife.com/2026/02/18/new-partnership-building-farming-drone-batteries-in-texas/] [source: https://chargedevs.com/newswire/kulr-hylio-to-develop-ndaa-compliant-battery-systems-for-us-built-agricultural-drones/]
- **Application targeting**: The companies state they will focus on **high-performance, mission-critical battery architectures tailored to Hylio's agricultural and defense-adjacent applications** — framing batteries as energy storage for both commercial ag spraying and federal/defense use cases (context: Hylio also markets defense-adjacent customers per its own positioning, separate from the agricultural channel that Terraplex dealers focus on) [source: https://kulr.ai/kulr-and-hylio-announce-strategic-collaboration-to-produce-texas-manufactured-battery-systems-for-u-s-built-unmanned-agricultural-drones/] [source: https://www.nasdaq.com/press-release/kulr-and-hylio-announce-strategic-collaboration-produce-texas-manufactured-battery]
- **Stated expected outcomes**: jointly developed products are expected to **support customers requiring trusted, certifiable energy storage solutions for government, defense, and critical-infrastructure use cases**, while also enhancing performance and reliability across commercial agricultural operations [source: https://www.globenewswire.com/news-release/2026/02/18/3240208/0/en/KULR-and-Hylio-Announce-Strategic-Collaboration-to-Produce-Texas-Manufactured-Battery-Systems-for-U-S-Built-Unmanned-Agricultural-Drones.html] [source: https://chargedevs.com/newswire/kulr-hylio-to-develop-ndaa-compliant-battery-systems-for-us-built-agricultural-drones/]
- **Market context cited in the release**: KULR/Hylio reference a Research and Markets projection that the global agricultural drone market grows from **$4.08 billion in 2026 to $8.65 billion by 2032**, a CAGR of **13.21%** — directionally consistent with the $4B-in-2026 figure cited in the [2026-05-16 ePropelled press materials](2026-05-16.md) [source: https://kulr.ai/kulr-and-hylio-announce-strategic-collaboration-to-produce-texas-manufactured-battery-systems-for-u-s-built-unmanned-agricultural-drones/]
- **Pipeline stage** (CRITICAL caveat): the JDA covers **design, prototype, qualify, and manufacture** — i.e., a development pipeline. **No shipping product is named**, no delivery date is committed, and the Hylio profile's standing language that "no domestic battery is shipping yet" remains accurate as of this digest. The change is that the **partner is now named** (KULR), where the Hylio profile previously cited "some partners" [source: https://kulr.ai/kulr-and-hylio-announce-strategic-collaboration-to-produce-texas-manufactured-battery-systems-for-u-s-built-unmanned-agricultural-drones/]
- **Why this matters in the post-2026-05-14 regulatory architecture**: combined with the [2026-05-16 ePropelled digest](2026-05-16.md) (motors/ESCs/power management for ag UAVs, US/UK/India footprint) and Cadenazzi's May 12 XPONENTIAL keynote naming **flight controllers, motors, batteries, ESCs, and rare-earth inputs** as the focus of Section 805 / 1260H scrutiny (context: see [2026-05-14 digest](2026-05-14.md)), the **batteries** prong of that list is now publicly addressed at the domestic-supplier layer for Hylio specifically. This is two of the five Cadenazzi-flagged component classes (motors+ESCs via ePropelled in the broader market, batteries via KULR specifically for Hylio) covered by named US-supplier partnerships within a four-month window

### Item 2: FCC GN Docket No. 26-74 "Unleashing American Drone Dominance" reply-comment deadline today

- **Reply-comment deadline**: Monday, **May 18, 2026** (today) — closes the second-round filing window for the FCC's "Unleashing American Drone Dominance" proceeding under Public Notice **DA 26-314** [source: https://docs.fcc.gov/public/attachments/DA-26-314A1.pdf] [source: https://www.federalregister.gov/documents/2026/04/16/2026-07381/unleashing-american-drone-dominance]
- **Initial comment window closed May 1, 2026**: more than **90 comments** were filed by the May 1 first-round deadline, including filings from the [American Public Power Association](https://www.publicpower.org/periodical/article/appa-files-comments-response-fcc-notice-unleashing-american-drone-dominance) and other industry associations [source: https://www.hklaw.com/en/insights/publications/2026/04/unleashing-drone-dominance-the-fcc-perspective-and-request]
- **Docket scope**: The proceeding spans **GN Docket No. 26-74, WT Docket No. 22-323, and WT Docket No. 24-629**, asking for input on: alleviating unnecessary regulatory burdens; ensuring American drone manufacturers and users have sufficient spectrum for drone testing and operations; facilitating and encouraging American firms' investment in drone capabilities; ensuring regulatory clarity and technical access for U.S.-based manufacturers; coordinating more effectively with other federal agencies; streamlining experimental licensing rules; and establishing additional dedicated drone innovation zones or testbeds [source: https://www.federalregister.gov/documents/2026/04/16/2026-07381/unleashing-american-drone-dominance] [source: https://docs.fcc.gov/public/attachments/DA-26-314A1.pdf]
- **Statutory grounding**: The proceeding is rooted in two executive orders signed in **June 2025** — **EO 14307 "Unleashing American Drone Dominance"** and **EO 14305 "Restoring American Airspace Sovereignty"** — which direct federal agencies including the FCC to **prioritize spectrum access, streamline certification, and favor domestically manufactured UAS in federal procurement to the maximum extent permitted by law** [source: https://www.federalregister.gov/documents/2026/04/16/2026-07381/unleashing-american-drone-dominance] [source: https://www.hklaw.com/en/insights/publications/2026/04/unleashing-drone-dominance-the-fcc-perspective-and-request]
- **Procedural relationship to the December 22, 2025 Covered List action**: GN Docket 26-74 is the **affirmative spectrum-and-licensing companion track** to the December 22, 2025 Covered List action (the negative "block foreign-made drones" rulemaking that the still-pending [DJI Petition for Reconsideration (ET Docket 26-22) and Autel Application for Review (ET Docket 26-23)](2026-05-09.md) challenge). The two tracks are procedurally distinct; today's deadline does not affect the DJI/Autel petition timeline [source: https://docs.fcc.gov/public/attachments/DA-26-314A1.pdf]
- **Next milestone**: after the reply-comment window closes today, the FCC will review the record before issuing a Report and Order or further notice; no statutory deadline is set for the next FCC action under DA 26-314

### Caveats — what the items do NOT establish

- **KULR/Hylio JDA does not name a shipping product**. The Hylio profile's standing language "no domestic battery is shipping yet" is unchanged. The dealer-facing update is the named partner, not a shipping timeline
- **The KULR partnership does not address the chemistry-side detail in the Hylio profile** (cell chemistry development is unspecified in the public press materials — "NDAA-compliant" describes provenance and procurement compliance, not chemistry)
- **GN Docket 26-74 reply-comment closure is a procedural milestone, not an FCC ruling**. No new dealer-facing rule, exemption, or restriction takes effect today as a direct consequence of the deadline passing
- **Neither item is dated to the prior 24-hour news window**. The KULR/Hylio JDA dates to February 18, 2026, captured here as a **prior-digest backfill** because it is the named-partner update to the Hylio profile that no prior digest captured. The GN Docket 26-74 reply-comment deadline is today (regulatory-calendar item) but the proceeding itself was noted as existing in the [2026-05-08 digest](2026-05-08.md)
