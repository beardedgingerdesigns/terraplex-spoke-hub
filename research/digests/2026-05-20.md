## Summary

On **Tuesday, May 19, 2026**, three drone trade publications (**dronexl.co**, **dronedj.com**, **dronelife.com**) independently surfaced the substantive contents of **Autel Robotics' reply filing in ET Docket 26-23** — the Application for Review of the FCC's December 22, 2025 Covered List action — submitted into the FCC record by the **Monday, May 11, 2026 reply-comment deadline** established under Public Notice DA 26-223 (context: deadline established March 6, 2026, captured in the [2026-05-08 digest](2026-05-08.md) and [2026-05-09 digest](2026-05-09.md)). Autel's reply does three things that none of its prior public statements had done: (1) it argues the FCC's Covered List decision against Autel rests on **classified material Autel was never permitted to see** and on **technical complaints originally aimed at DJI** rather than at Autel — citing the 2014 D.C. Circuit ruling in *Ralls Corp. v. CFIUS* as the governing due-process precedent; (2) it submits **sworn declarations** detailing Autel's data-handling architecture (AES-128 / AES-256 encryption on drone communications and stored data; flight data stored locally by default rather than auto-uploaded to company servers; US drone-operator data stored on US-based servers or US-based cloud infrastructure rather than on servers in China); (3) it categorically denies ever having received data-access requests from the Chinese government — or from Russia, Iran, North Korea, Cuba, or Venezuela — and denies ever having transferred US customer data to those governments. The reply is procedurally distinct from DJI's Petition for Reconsideration in **ET Docket 26-22**, on which the [2026-05-11 digest's](2026-05-11.md) Commissioner Trusty signal and the [2026-05-14 digest's](2026-05-14.md) XPONENTIAL three-pillar consensus continue to read as directional. No Commission vote has been scheduled on either docket.

## Why this matters for Terraplex dealers

The Autel filing **separates Autel's case from DJI's** at the legal-substance level — and that separation runs in only one direction. Autel's procedural-due-process argument (Ralls Corp.) plus its specific factual rebuttals (AES encryption disclosure, local-by-default flight-data storage, US-data-residency claim, sworn no-China-access declaration) is the strongest contestation of the Covered List anyone has put on the record so far, and trade-press coverage frames it as plausibly viable on its own terms. But the same filing **concedes by silence** that the AeroScope / mobile-app-telemetry / data-collection allegations the FCC cited were *DJI-specific* — Autel's argument is "those concerns don't apply to us," not "those concerns don't apply to anyone." That asymmetry **strengthens** the structural position of the FCC's case against [DJI](../manufacturers/dji-agras.md), because the Autel defense implicitly stipulates that DJI's situation is materially different and more vulnerable. For dealers, this is a useful sharpening of the public-record picture: even if Autel ultimately prevails at the Commission or in court, DJI's parallel ET Docket 26-22 petition does not get pulled across by the same legal theory.

The operational read for dealer-customer conversations is **unchanged from the [2026-05-13 digest](2026-05-13.md) and [2026-05-14 digest](2026-05-14.md) baseline** — the firmware waiver under DA 26-454 still runs to January 1, 2029 for already-authorized Agras platforms, the [GTEEX Revolution](../manufacturers/terraplex.md) and [Hylio](../manufacturers/hylio.md) "Built in the Americas" structural advantage still holds, and the Section 805 / 1260H supply-chain enforcement timeline (June 30, 2026 direct, June 30, 2027 indirect) still sits on top of the equipment-authorization layer. What changes is the **honesty boundary** for dealers fielding the "is the Covered List going to get reversed?" question. The accurate post-May-19 answer is: "Autel's reply filing on May 11 is the most substantive challenge anyone has filed — sworn declarations on data handling, a Ralls Corp. due-process argument, and a public separation from DJI — but Autel's argument structurally helps Autel, not DJI. DJI's separate Petition for Reconsideration sits in worse procedural shape, with the Department of War's Section 805 timeline (per Cadenazzi's [May 12 XPONENTIAL keynote](2026-05-14.md)) operating in parallel. There is no near-term path on the public record by which new DJI Agras hardware re-enters the US market." Dealers should not over-read the trade-press coverage as a softening signal on the underlying ban — it is not.

## Key facts

### Autel's reply filing in ET Docket 26-23 — substantive contents disclosed May 19, 2026

- **Three independent trade-press outlets** (dronexl.co, dronedj.com, dronelife.com) published substantive coverage of Autel's reply filing on **Tuesday, May 19, 2026**; the underlying filing was submitted into the FCC record on or before the **Monday, May 11, 2026 reply-comment deadline** in **ET Docket 26-23**, the Autel **Application for Review** of the FCC's December 22, 2025 Covered List addition [source: https://dronexl.co/2026/05/19/autel-fcc-reply-covered-list-secret-evidence-dji/] [source: https://dronedj.com/2026/05/19/autel-drone-ban-us-fcc/] [source: https://dronelife.com/2026/05/19/autel-fcc-covered-list-challenge/]
- The May 11 deadline was established by the FCC's Public Safety and Homeland Security Bureau under **Public Notice DA 26-223** released March 6, 2026 (context: docket-cycle scaffolding captured in the [2026-05-08 digest](2026-05-08.md) and [2026-05-09 digest](2026-05-09.md)) [source: https://dronexl.co/2026/05/19/autel-fcc-reply-covered-list-secret-evidence-dji/]

### Autel's procedural-due-process argument — Ralls Corp. v. CFIUS

- The reply **repeatedly cites** *Ralls Corp. v. CFIUS*, the **2014 D.C. Circuit ruling** that held national security review cannot rest on opaque, category-wide assumptions without giving the affected company access to at least the **unclassified portion** of the record against it [source: https://dronexl.co/2026/05/19/autel-fcc-reply-covered-list-secret-evidence-dji/] [source: https://dronedj.com/2026/05/19/autel-drone-ban-us-fcc/]
- Autel's argument: the FCC's December 22, 2025 Public Notice that added Autel to the Covered List rests on **classified material Autel was never allowed to see** and gives Autel **no opportunity to rebut** the underlying evidence — the same procedural defect the D.C. Circuit found unconstitutional in Ralls Corp. [source: https://dronexl.co/2026/05/19/autel-fcc-reply-covered-list-secret-evidence-dji/] [source: https://dronelife.com/2026/05/19/autel-fcc-covered-list-challenge/]

### Autel distances itself from DJI — "guilt by association" argument

- The reply argues the FCC's brief leaned heavily on **DJI-specific controversies** — specifically **AeroScope**, **mobile app behavior**, and **alleged telemetry collection** — and used those to justify keeping Autel on the Covered List alongside DJI; Autel's position is that **evidence against one company cannot automatically justify restrictions on another** [source: https://dronexl.co/2026/05/19/autel-fcc-reply-covered-list-secret-evidence-dji/] [source: https://dronelife.com/2026/05/19/autel-fcc-covered-list-challenge/]
- Trade-press framing: this is the first time Autel has publicly drawn a substantive legal distinction between its situation and DJI's, rather than presenting a unified "foreign drone manufacturer" defense [source: https://dronedj.com/2026/05/19/autel-drone-ban-us-fcc/] [source: https://dronelife.com/2026/05/19/autel-fcc-covered-list-challenge/]

### Sworn declarations on data handling — first public technical disclosure

- The reply attaches **sworn declarations** describing how Autel handles flight data and what encryption protects its communications — material that did **not appear in the December 22, 2025 Public Notice** that put Autel on the Covered List [source: https://dronexl.co/2026/05/19/autel-fcc-reply-covered-list-secret-evidence-dji/] [source: https://dronedj.com/2026/05/19/autel-drone-ban-us-fcc/]
- **Encryption**: drone communications and stored data use **AES-128 or AES-256 encryption**; **no third party** has been authorized to access drone-operation software or customer accounts [source: https://dronedj.com/2026/05/19/autel-drone-ban-us-fcc/] [source: https://dronexl.co/2026/05/19/autel-fcc-reply-covered-list-secret-evidence-dji/]
- **Flight-data residency**: flight data is **stored locally by default** and is **not automatically uploaded** to Autel company servers [source: https://dronedj.com/2026/05/19/autel-drone-ban-us-fcc/] [source: https://dronexl.co/2026/05/19/autel-fcc-reply-covered-list-secret-evidence-dji/]
- **US-data residency**: US drone-operator data is **stored within the United States** using local servers or US-based cloud infrastructure rather than on servers in China [source: https://dronedj.com/2026/05/19/autel-drone-ban-us-fcc/] [source: https://dronelife.com/2026/05/19/autel-fcc-covered-list-challenge/]

### Sworn denial of foreign-government data-access requests

- Autel states it has **never received requests** from the Chinese government — or from **Russia, Iran, North Korea, Cuba, or Venezuela** — seeking access to US customer data; and has **never transferred** US customer data to those governments [source: https://dronedj.com/2026/05/19/autel-drone-ban-us-fcc/] [source: https://dronexl.co/2026/05/19/autel-fcc-reply-covered-list-secret-evidence-dji/]
- Coverage frames this as a notable on-the-record commitment because submitting it via **sworn declaration** (rather than a public-relations statement) exposes Autel to perjury and false-statement liability if subsequently contradicted by evidence [source: https://dronelife.com/2026/05/19/autel-fcc-covered-list-challenge/]

### Procedural posture — what the filing does NOT establish

- **No Commission vote has been scheduled** on either ET Docket 26-22 (DJI Petition for Reconsideration) or ET Docket 26-23 (Autel Application for Review) as of this digest; the reply-comment window closed May 11 and the matter is now in front of the full Commission for action (timing unset) [source: https://dronexl.co/2026/05/19/autel-fcc-reply-covered-list-secret-evidence-dji/]
- The filing is **specific to Autel** — it does **not** make legal arguments that would carry across to DJI's parallel petition; DJI's case (ET Docket 26-22) continues to face the [Department of War's classified-intelligence opposition](2026-05-09.md) (filed April 10, 2026) and the [Section 805 / 1260H supply-chain enforcement timeline](2026-05-14.md) (Cadenazzi confirmed Pentagon contractor-notification pilots begin during 2026, ahead of June 30, 2026 direct-procurement ban and June 30, 2027 indirect-supplier ban) — neither of which the Autel filing addresses [source: https://dronelife.com/2026/05/19/autel-fcc-covered-list-challenge/]
- The Autel filing **does not modify or affect** the FCC OET's separate firmware-waiver Public Notice **DA 26-454** (released May 8, 2026) that extends software/firmware updates for already-authorized Autel and DJI Agras platforms through at least January 1, 2029 — see the [2026-05-13 digest](2026-05-13.md) for the installed-base maintenance read [source: https://dronexl.co/2026/05/19/autel-fcc-reply-covered-list-secret-evidence-dji/]

## Implications

- **For dealer-customer conversations about the Covered List trajectory**: the post-May-19 honest framing is that Autel has filed the most substantive challenge yet — sworn declarations, a Ralls Corp. due-process argument, and a public legal separation from [DJI](../manufacturers/dji-agras.md) — but that the structure of Autel's argument helps Autel only. Even a full Autel win at the Commission does not pull DJI across the line, and the Department of War's Section 805 supply-chain enforcement timeline is a separate enforcement track that operates regardless of how the FCC reconsideration proceedings resolve. Dealers should not be promising customers DJI re-authorization timing in either direction.
- **For "Built in the Americas" positioning** around the [GTEEX Revolution lineup](../manufacturers/terraplex.md) and [Hylio](../manufacturers/hylio.md) hardware: **nothing material changes**. The structural advantage is unchanged by Autel's filing, because (a) Autel's argument is Autel-specific, not industry-wide; (b) DJI remains on the Section 1260H list and faces the FY24 NDAA Section 805 enforcement calendar regardless of FCC outcomes; (c) the [2026-05-14 digest's](2026-05-14.md) three-pillar inter-agency-plus-industry consensus (FCC + DoW + FAA + AUVSI) on the "Made in America" architecture is not contested by any party in the Autel filing. Do **not** rewrite dealer copy to suggest the Covered List is wavering — it is not.
- **For onboarding scripts** when prospects ask "what's the latest on the FCC reconsideration?": the accurate post-May-19 answer is "On May 11, 2026, Autel and DJI both filed reply comments in their separate FCC reconsideration dockets (ET 26-23 and ET 26-22 respectively); trade-press coverage on May 19 surfaced the substantive contents of Autel's filing, which argues the FCC relied on classified material Autel was never allowed to see and on technical complaints originally aimed at DJI. Autel's filing explicitly distinguishes its case from DJI's. The FCC has not scheduled a Commission vote on either docket, and the Department of War's Section 805 supply-chain enforcement timeline operates regardless of how the FCC matters resolve."
- **For Sam Welton's [compliance program](../../content/compliance-program.md) curriculum**: this is a **legal-procedure module point**, not a curriculum-altering item. Prep-course students who ask "could the FCC reverse the ban?" should hear the bifurcated answer: Autel's case is structurally stronger than DJI's at the legal-substance level, but DJI's case is what determines whether the [DJI Agras](../manufacturers/dji-agras.md) installed base ever sees re-authorization for new SKUs — and DJI faces both the FCC track and the DoW Section 805 / 1260H track. The teaching point is that **the multi-agency architecture** captured in the [2026-05-14 digest](2026-05-14.md) means any single-agency win does not unwind the broader ban.
- **For monitoring follow-up**: watch (a) any **Commission-level scheduling** notice on ET Docket 26-22 or ET Docket 26-23 — the reply-comment window has been closed eight days as of this digest with no further procedural activity reported; (b) any **government response** in either docket addressing Autel's Ralls Corp. argument specifically — this is the most procedurally distinct argument now on the record; (c) any **DoD / DHS Conditional Approval** determinations that could create narrow new-equipment exceptions (the Wiley Rein "first Conditional Approvals" watch-item from the [2026-05-13 digest](2026-05-13.md) remains the structural anchor); (d) the **FAA radar-data-sharing test program announcement** that ATO COO Franklin J. McIntosh teed up within ~2 weeks of his May 12 XPONENTIAL Detroit keynote (see [2026-05-14 digest](2026-05-14.md)) — if the announcement slips past Memorial Day weekend it signals a longer policy-review path.
