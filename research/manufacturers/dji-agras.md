## Current state (as of 2026-05-01)

DJI Agras remains the dominant ag spray drone supplier in the US by installed base, anchored on the T50 (40 kg spray / 50 kg spread payload) and T25 introduced in 2024, alongside still-shipping T30, T20, and T10 platforms. The defining 2026 fact for the line is regulatory: the FCC's December 22, 2025 covered-list ruling cut off FCC authorization for any new foreign-made drones and key components, and DJI now estimates a ~$1.5B 2026 US sales loss across both already-authorized and planned-for-2026 SKUs. Existing FCC-authorized DJI ag models (a 14-product set covering current US-shipping Agras platforms) remain legal to sell and operate while DJI's Ninth Circuit appeal (Case 26-1029, filed February 20, 2026) proceeds. For dealers, this turns DJI from an "always available, just pick a model" supplier into one that requires a SKU-by-SKU availability check before any new-fleet quote.

## Revision history

### Revision 2026-05-01

- First profile created.
- Lineup in market: T50, T25, T30, T20, T10. T50 is the current flagship — coaxial dual-rotor design with 54-inch propellers, 40 kg spray / 50 kg spread payload, and dual-atomization spraying at up to 16 L/min (24 L/min with optional centrifugal sprinklers); spreader mode flows up to 108 kg/min (≈1.5 t/hr); throughput cited at up to 50 acres/hr [source: https://www.suasnews.com/2024/04/dji-agras-t50-and-t25-expand-aerial-crop-protection-capabilities/].
- T50 sensing/comms: dual active phased-array radars plus binocular vision for obstacle sensing; remote-controller link to 2 km; DB1560 intelligent flight battery, 30 Ah, rated 1,500 charge cycles [source: https://www.suasnews.com/2024/04/dji-agras-t50-and-t25-expand-aerial-crop-protection-capabilities/].
- FCC covered-list ruling (Dec 22, 2025) added all new foreign-made drones and critical components (motors, flight controllers, navigation systems, batteries) to the covered list, cutting off new FCC authorizations for drones manufactured outside the US [source: https://www.suasnews.com/2025/12/fcc-updates-covered-list-to-add-certain-uas-and-uas-components-grounds-foreign-made-drones-in-sweeping-national-security-crackdown/].
- DJI's stated 2026 US revenue exposure: ~$1.5B total — $700M tied to 14 already-authorized DJI products (5 UAS, 9 non-UAS) for which the FCC set aside authorizations, plus $860M tied to 25 new UAS and non-UAS models DJI had planned to launch in 2026 [source: https://agfundernews.com/dji-fcc-action-could-wipe-out-1-5bn-in-us-drone-sales-in-2026-with-staggering-real-world-consequences].
- Legal status: DJI filed appeal at Ninth Circuit Court of Appeals on February 20, 2026 (Case 26-1029), arguing the FCC ruling is "procedurally and substantively flawed," that the agency exceeded its statutory authority, failed required procedures, never identified an actual national-security threat from DJI products, and violated the Fifth Amendment [source: https://agfundernews.com/dji-fcc-action-could-wipe-out-1-5bn-in-us-drone-sales-in-2026-with-staggering-real-world-consequences] [source: https://www.suasnews.com/2026/02/dji-mounts-legal-challenge-against-us-regulator-over-arbitrary-security-ban/].
- Key dealer-facing nuance: existing DJI ag models that were authorized before the December 22, 2025 ruling can still be legally bought and deployed in the US under the current FCC framework; the constraint is on new SKUs and re-authorizations, not on operation of already-authorized airframes [source: https://agfundernews.com/made-in-america-fcc-decision-sparks-scramble-to-localize-ag-spray-drone-production].
