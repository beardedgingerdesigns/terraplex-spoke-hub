## Current state (as of 2026-06-01)

Yamaha's ag-drone story is a heritage-aerial-application franchise (the **R-MAX** unmanned helicopter, in commercial service since 1997 in Japan) plus a 2025 US strategic reset under the new **Yamaha Agriculture** subsidiary headquartered in the San Francisco Bay Area, with field offices in Napa CA and Wenatchee WA and ~175 employees as of early 2025. The product center of gravity is no longer the aerial spray helicopter — combined R-MAX + FAZER global installed base sits at ~2,800 units across customer farms worldwide, primarily in Japan and Australia — but rather autonomous **ground** equipment via the **Prospr** modular spraying / weed-control vehicle (acquired via Robotics Plus) plus AI-powered digital agronomy from **The Yield**. For US row-crop spray markets, Yamaha is **not** a current competitor to the [DJI Agras](dji-agras.md) / [Hylio](hylio.md) / [GTEEX Revolution](terraplex.md) multi-rotor category; the FAZER-R / R-MAX heritage line is unlikely to re-enter the US spray-helicopter space at scale, and Yamaha Agriculture's near-term US rollout is ground-autonomy, not aerial. For Terraplex dealers, Yamaha is mostly a "what about Yamaha?" prospect question with a specialty-crop / vineyard answer (their historical US footprint), not a head-to-head ag-spray competitor.

## Revision history

### Revision 2026-06-01

- First profile created.
- **Corporate structure (US, 2025-present)**: in late January 2025, Yamaha Motor Co. announced the launch of **Yamaha Agriculture**, a new US-based subsidiary formed via strategic acquisitions of robotics-and-AI startups Robotics Plus and The Yield. HQ is in the San Francisco Bay Area (positioned for easy access to Tokyo, Sydney, and Auckland); local offices in Napa, California and Wenatchee, Washington; approximately 175 employees [source: https://www.agweb.com/news/business/technology/yamaha-ag-qa-when-can-growers-buy-farm-robots-north-america-rollout-expl] [source: https://agfundernews.com/ufraction8-closes-4-3m].
- **Aerial product line (heritage)**:
  - **R-MAX** — unmanned single-rotor helicopter, ~28 kg payload, 16-liter spray tank capacity; first commercially deployed in Japan in 1997 [source: https://www.suasnews.com/2015/10/super-drone-sprayer-comes-with-risks/]
  - **FAZER** / **FAZER R** — next-generation spray helicopter, deployed alongside R-MAX in the global installed base
  - Combined R-MAX + FAZER installed base: ~2,800 units across customer farms worldwide [source: https://www.agweb.com/news/business/technology/yamaha-ag-qa-when-can-growers-buy-farm-robots-north-america-rollout-expl]
- **US aerial-application regulatory history**:
  - December 2015: R-MAX received FAA **Part 137 Agricultural Aircraft Operations** certification — the **first** for any unmanned aerial system [source: https://www.agweb.com/news/business/technology/yamaha-ag-qa-when-can-growers-buy-farm-robots-north-america-rollout-expl]
  - May 2016: first US commercial R-MAX spray operation at Silverado Farming Company, Napa Valley, California — a vineyard service deployment rather than row-crop [source: https://www.suasnews.com/2016/05/43723/]
  - April 2017: discussed industrial-drone alliance with enRoute (subsequently did not materialize into a US row-crop multi-rotor product) [source: https://www.suasnews.com/2017/04/yamaha-motor-enroute-discussing-industrial-drone-alliance/]
- **Ground autonomy product (current US push)**:
  - **Prospr** — modular autonomous ground vehicle from Robotics Plus, currently configured for spraying and weed control; deployed with customers and distributors in the US, Australia, and New Zealand. Roadmap includes implements for mowing and under-row cultivation [source: https://www.agweb.com/news/business/technology/yamaha-ag-qa-when-can-growers-buy-farm-robots-north-america-rollout-expl]
  - Initial US deployment focus: permanent and specialty crops (vineyards, orchards, berries) — not row-crop herbicide / fungicide application
- **Adjacent ag-tech investments**:
  - Yamaha Motor Ventures led a $7.5M Series A in a strawberry-harvesting robotics startup, signaling continued investment in specialty-crop ag-robotics beyond the Robotics Plus / The Yield acquisitions [source: https://agfundernews.com/yamaha-leads-7-5m-series-a-in-strawberry-harvesting-robot]
- **FCC Covered List exposure**: not directly impacted by the December 22, 2025 FCC ruling — Yamaha's manufacturing footprint is Japan (R-MAX, FAZER) and US/Australia/NZ (Prospr via Robotics Plus); not a covered foreign-China-OEM. The R-MAX heritage installed base in the US is small and concentrated in vineyards, so the practical regulatory question is moot, but the structural fact is worth knowing: Yamaha is not a Covered List entity (see [`../regulatory/2026-05-update.md`](../regulatory/2026-05-update.md) for the broader FCC framing).
- **What Yamaha is NOT doing in the US**: no announced plan to bring R-MAX / FAZER spray helicopters back into US row-crop markets at scale; no announced multi-rotor ag-spray drone competing with [DJI Agras T-series](dji-agras.md), [Hylio AG-230](hylio.md), or the [GTEEX R-32](../../products/r-32.md). The Yamaha Agriculture rollout pivots toward ground autonomy and digital agronomy, leaving the aerial-spray category to multi-rotor OEMs.

## Implications

- **For dealer prospect conversations**: the "what about Yamaha?" question typically comes from specialty-crop or vineyard prospects who know the R-MAX from West Coast trade press. The honest answer is "Yamaha is a heritage Japanese spray-helicopter franchise that became the first FAA Part 137-certified unmanned aerial system in 2015 and has done US vineyard spraying since 2016 — but it's not a row-crop multi-rotor competitor to the [GTEEX R-32](../../products/r-32.md). For 2026 row-crop ag spraying, the choice set is DJI Agras (FCC-gated), Hylio (US-built), and the GTEEX Revolution lineup we distribute."
- **For territory positioning**: Yamaha Agriculture's HQ is the Bay Area with field offices in Napa CA and Wenatchee WA — Terraplex's [Pacific Northwest dealer](terraplex.md) (509-287-2900) is the geography most likely to overlap with Yamaha's specialty-crop / vineyard footprint. Other Terraplex dealer territories (IA / NE / WI / ID / OK) are unlikely to encounter Yamaha as a serious head-to-head competitor in 2026.
- **For onboarding scripts**: Sam Welton's [compliance program](../../content/compliance-program.md) curriculum can flag the Yamaha R-MAX 2015 Part 137 certification as a **historical milestone** when teaching the Part 137 framework — it's the first unmanned-aerial-system Part 137 certificate and useful context for explaining why ag drones are governed by Part 137 in the first place.
- **For monitoring follow-up**: watch for any 2026 Yamaha Agriculture announcement of a US multi-rotor ag-spray product — none indicated through May 2026 in the allowlisted sources. The Prospr ground-autonomy rollout is the active signal; if and when Prospr enters Midwest row-crop application via Yamaha's US channels, it becomes a structurally different competitor (ground autonomy vs aerial spray) and warrants its own profile revision.
