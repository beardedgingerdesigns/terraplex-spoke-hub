## Current state (as of 2026-05-01)

Hylio is the most credible US-domestic alternative to DJI Agras in the wake of the FCC's December 22, 2025 covered-list ruling. The company manufactures spray drones in Texas, holds an FAA exemption to swarm up to three drones under one operator and to fly spray missions at night, and reports 2025 revenue just under $13M. In April 2026 it closed a $2M StartEngine equity-crowdfunding raise to scale a new Texas facility to 5,000+ drones per year at full capacity. The "Made in America" pitch is qualified — batteries are still China-sourced, with new battery chemistry "in work with partners" but not yet shipping — so dealers should script around domestic airframe/assembly rather than full BOM. Pricing has historically run roughly 35% above DJI; that gap has narrowed as tariffs and the FCC ruling raise effective DJI cost and constrain availability.

## Revision history

### Revision 2026-05-01

- First profile created.
- Lineup referenced in trade press: AG-230 spray drone (also applies dry dispersibles and cover-crop seed); HYL-150 ARES, cited at 40–60 acres/hr [source: https://www.agweb.com/news/crops/crop-production/whats-new-agriculture-drones].
- Throughput: a single Hylio spray drone covers up to ~50 acres/hr; a three-drone swarm of AG-230s under one operator covers up to ~150 acres/hr, comparable to a large pull-behind sprayer in some conditions [source: https://www.agweb.com/news/business/technology/threes-crowd-hylio-secures-faa-drone-swarm-night-flight-exemptions].
- FAA exemption status: Hylio holds an FAA exemption permitting a single operator to swarm up to three application drones in concert, plus an exemption to conduct spray missions after sundown. Ag drones are jointly governed by Part 107 (pilot) and 14 CFR Part 137 (service provider) [source: https://www.agweb.com/news/business/technology/threes-crowd-hylio-secures-faa-drone-swarm-night-flight-exemptions].
- Capacity build-out: Hylio raised $2M on StartEngine to expand production at a new Texas facility capable of 5,000+ drones per year at full capacity; CEO Arthur Erickson disclosed 2025 revenue "just under $13 million" with "2026 looking good" [source: https://agfundernews.com/ag-spray-drone-co-hylio-raises-2m-to-expand-production-capacity-builds-on-made-in-america-positioning]. (Captured in the 2026-05-01 daily digest.)
- Supply-chain caveat: batteries are still made in China; Hylio says it has been "trying to de-Asia our supply chain from day one" and is "working on new battery chemistry with some partners," but no domestic battery is shipping yet [source: https://agfundernews.com/ag-spray-drone-co-hylio-raises-2m-to-expand-production-capacity-builds-on-made-in-america-positioning].
- Pricing posture: Hylio pricing has historically run ~35% above DJI; that gap has narrowed somewhat with recent tariffs on Chinese imports, though Hylio remains a price-premium choice on a sticker basis [source: https://www.agweb.com/news/crops/crop-production/whats-new-agriculture-drones].
- Competitive context: other US-assembly entrants moving into the same vacuum include Revolution Drones (founded by NC farmer Russell Hedrick) and Exedy Drones (a unit of EXEDY Globalparts repurposing automotive engineering); Iowa-based Rantizo also holds FAA swarm authorization [source: https://agfundernews.com/made-in-america-fcc-decision-sparks-scramble-to-localize-ag-spray-drone-production].
