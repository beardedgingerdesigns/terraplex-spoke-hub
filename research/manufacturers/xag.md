## Current state (as of 2026-06-01)

XAG (Guangzhou, China) is the second-largest Chinese agricultural drone OEM after [DJI Agras](dji-agras.md) and the only credible Chinese-OEM alternative to DJI for US ag-spray buyers — though that role is now constrained by the same FCC December 22, 2025 Covered List ruling that gates DJI. XAG's US-relevant lineup is anchored on the **V40** (twin-rotor, ~10 m spray width), **P40** / **P100** (50 kg payload quadcopters with RevoSpray 3), and the **P150** that Rantizo brought to the US market in 2025 — pitched at launch as "the largest and fastest spray drone on the market in the U.S." The US channel runs through [Pegasus Robotics](https://www.suasnews.com/2024/03/xag-attends-us-2nd-spray-drone-user-conference-sponsored-by-pegasus-robotics/) (CEO Arthur Chen) and through Rantizo's hardware-agnostic service network across 35 states. Like every foreign-manufactured UAS, XAG is on the FCC Covered List for new equipment authorizations, but its already-authorized airframes benefit from the May 8, 2026 firmware-waiver extension to January 1, 2029 (see [`../regulatory/2026-05-update.md`](../regulatory/2026-05-update.md)). For a Terraplex dealer, XAG is the *secondary* China-OEM that customers occasionally bring up — most often when comparing single-airframe throughput against the [R-32](../../products/r-32.md) (32-gallon US flagship) and the [I-19](../../products/i-19.md) (multi-role spray / spread / scout platform).

## Revision history

### Revision 2026-06-01

- First profile created.
- **Product lineup** (global, US availability varies by SKU):
  - **V40** — twin-rotor design with effective spray width up to 10 m; XAG markets spray penetration "over twice that of traditional multi-rotor drones" via aerodynamic optimization for dense canopies [source: https://www.suasnews.com/2021/11/xag-launches-v40-and-p40-agricultural-drone-globally-to-benefit-more-farmers/]
  - **P40** — quadcopter, launched globally alongside V40 in November 2021; positioned as the entry-grade autonomous mapping/spraying/broadcast platform [source: https://www.suasnews.com/2021/11/xag-launches-v40-and-p40-agricultural-drone-globally-to-benefit-more-farmers/]
  - **P100** — 50 kg payload, RevoSpray 3 system, 50-liter smart liquid tank, max flow rate 22 L/min [source: https://www.suasnews.com/2022/09/xag-goes-global-with-its-p100-agricultural-drone-helping-farmers-to-reap-more/]
  - **P100 Pro** — bigger, foldable, cost-reduced refresh of the P100; launched in Vietnam July 2023 as the broader global P-line refresh [source: https://www.suasnews.com/2023/07/xag-p100-pro-agricultural-drone-launches-in-vietnam-bigger-foldable-and-cost-saving/]
  - **P150** — current top-of-line in the US channel; Rantizo announced availability in March 2025, calling it "the largest and fastest spray drone on the market in the U.S." [source: https://www.agweb.com/news/business/technology/soaring-yields-and-lower-costs-7-expert-tips-maximize-spray-drone-effici]
- **US distribution and service footprint**:
  - **Pegasus Robotics** is XAG's US partner — sponsored the US 2nd Spray Drone User Conference in 2024; Arthur Chen is CEO [source: https://www.suasnews.com/2024/03/xag-attends-us-2nd-spray-drone-user-conference-sponsored-by-pegasus-robotics/]
  - **Rantizo** services 35 states deploying both DJI and XAG airframes; spun off its spray-services business and created **American Autonomy Inc.** focused on software for the US spray-drone industry [source: https://agfundernews.com/dji-faces-uncertain-future-in-us-as-ag-spray-drone-industry-readies-for-realignment]
- **International regulatory milestones** (positions XAG as the most "regulator-engaged" Chinese OEM after DJI):
  - UK Civil Aviation Authority granted XAG P40 and V40 Operational Authorization for agricultural spraying in January 2023 — the UK's first-ever such authorization for ag drones [source: https://www.suasnews.com/2023/01/xag-agricultural-drone-granted-caa-operational-authorization-to-spray-in-the-uk/]
  - XAG has prior commercial deployments in Vietnam, Ukraine, Panama, and EU/UK vineyards [source: https://www.suasnews.com/2022/02/xag-delivered-the-largest-drone-fleet-for-agriculture-in-ukraine/]
- **FCC Covered List exposure**: XAG is captured by the December 22, 2025 FCC ruling that added all foreign-manufactured UAS and critical components to the Covered List; new XAG models and components cannot receive FCC authorization. Trade-press analysis post-ruling identifies XAG and EAVision as the Chinese OEMs that **most stood to benefit** from DJI's US import restriction prior to the Covered List sweep, with several distributors reporting that XAG supplies "have generally been good" — that channel-availability advantage is now eroded by the same ruling that gates DJI [source: https://agfundernews.com/dji-faces-uncertain-future-in-us-as-ag-spray-drone-industry-readies-for-realignment] [source: https://agfundernews.com/made-in-america-fcc-decision-sparks-scramble-to-localize-ag-spray-drone-production]
- **Firmware/software runway**: previously-authorized XAG airframes benefit from FCC OET Public Notice DA 26-454 (May 8, 2026) which extends the firmware-and-software-update waiver for foreign-manufactured UAS and critical components from January 1, 2027 to at least January 1, 2029, and broadens scope to Class II permissive changes — same waiver that protects the installed [DJI Agras](dji-agras.md) fleet (see [`../regulatory/2026-05-update.md`](../regulatory/2026-05-update.md) for the full DA 26-454 entry).
- **Partnership history**: XAG has prior commercial tie-ups with Bayer (agronomy data and crop-protection product integration) and Huawei (connectivity / IoT stack) [source: https://agfundernews.com/xag-taps-on-bayer-and-huawei-to-help-its-drones-take-off]. The Bayer relationship is notable when discussing R-32 / I-19 customers who run Bayer crop-protection programs — XAG markets a tighter agronomy stack than DJI through that partnership.

## Implications

- **For dealer competitive positioning**: when a prospect mentions XAG, the structurally honest answer is "XAG is in the same FCC Covered List bucket as DJI — already-authorized airframes are legal to operate and now have a firmware-patch runway through January 2029, but new XAG SKUs cannot get US authorization. The domestic alternative is the [GTEEX Revolution](terraplex.md) lineup that we distribute, plus [Hylio](hylio.md) as the other US-built option." This frames XAG as a parallel-to-DJI risk profile, not a workaround.
- **For pricing conversations**: XAG has no published US pricing in the allowlisted sources. The P150 is the current US flagship via Rantizo; Pegasus Robotics handles dealer-network sales. If a customer cites a specific XAG quote, the dealer should ask whether it's an FCC-authorized SKU before treating the comparison as apples-to-apples with the [R-32](../../products/r-32.md).
- **For onboarding scripts**: Sam Welton's [compliance program](../../content/compliance-program.md) curriculum should treat XAG identically to DJI for the FCC-status question — both Covered List, both protected for firmware updates through 2029 via DA 26-454, both blocked from new-SKU authorization. The distinction between "DJI" and "XAG" matters less than the distinction between "already-authorized airframe" and "post-December-2025 SKU."
- **For dealer territory awareness**: Rantizo's footprint (35 states, hardware-agnostic services model post-spinoff) overlaps Terraplex's 49-location dealer network in IA / NE / WI / WA / ID / OK. A dealer fielding a "should I use Rantizo's XAG service or buy a [GTEEX Revolution](terraplex.md) airframe?" question should reframe as service-versus-ownership economics rather than DJI-versus-XAG specs (see [`../dealer-economics/competitor-distributors.md`](../dealer-economics/competitor-distributors.md) for the structural breakdown).
