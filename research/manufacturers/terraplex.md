## Current state (as of 2026-05-01)

Terraplex Ag is a **farmer-owned agricultural drone reseller** headquartered in Odebolt, Iowa, and the **exclusive distributor of the GTEEX Revolution Drone series** in the US — built in the Americas, for the American farmer. Two product platforms: [R-32](../../products/r-32.md) (32-gallon flagship, 1,200–1,600 acres/day) and [I-19](../../products/i-19.md) (20 gal liquid + 26 gal solids, 600 acres/day, ±4" RTK). Operates a 49-location dealer network across IA / NE / WI / WA / ID / OK and runs in-house FAA Part 137 / Part 107 compliance services led by [Director of Compliance Sam Welton](../../content/compliance-program.md). The company's stated differentiator vs DJI is *local* — real dealer faces, real phone numbers, parts in stock, software updates responsive to American farmer feedback (see [content/positioning.md](../../content/positioning.md)). BGD's manager-app builds and maintains the dealer-tier sites under this network.

## Implications

- **For dealer positioning copy**: dealer sites should reinforce — not contradict — the parent brand's "farmer-owned reseller" framing. A dealer is an extension of the Terraplex network, not an alternative to it. When mentioning Terraplex by name, link the dealer's customer to the compliance program (Sam Welton) rather than burying it in the about page.
- **For pricing conversations**: the parent brand publishes no pricing. Dealers handle quotes per the [pricing-policy](../../content/pricing-policy.md) MAP rules. Sell-sheet language is ©2024 — confirm any spec is still current before quoting hard numbers in customer conversations.
- **For onboarding scripts**: every Terraplex dealer should know Sam Welton's direct line (641-691-6277) and the Part 107 course schedule. New dealer reps should sit through one Part 107 session within their first 6 months.
- **For brand consistency**: use the canonical phrases above verbatim where appropriate. Specifically, "Built in the Americas, for the American Farmer" is the brand's primary geographic claim — note "Americas" (plural), not "America." This matches the GTEEX manufacturing footprint claim more accurately than "Made in USA."
