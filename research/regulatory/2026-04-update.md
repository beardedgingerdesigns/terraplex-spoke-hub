## Summary

April 2026 was a quiet month for new federal rulemaking but an active one for ongoing proceedings: the FCC covered-list ruling continues to dominate dealer-facing conversations as DJI's Ninth Circuit appeal (Case 26-1029) progresses, and the FAA's Part 108 BVLOS NPRM unveiled in August 2025 remains in post-comment review with no final rule yet. USDA Research, Education, and Economics announced on April 7 the launch of the National Proving Grounds Network for AgTech (NPG-Ag), a real-conditions evaluation network that is relevant to ag-drone dealers as a future independent benchmarking channel. State-level activity in Iowa, Texas, Nebraska, Kansas, and Missouri produced no notable new ag-drone-specific regulation in April — the substantive state signal continues to be where domestic manufacturers and service providers are clustering (TX, IA, MO, NC) rather than new statute.

## Why this matters for Terraplex dealers

The practical regulatory picture for a dealer quoting a 2026 fleet has not changed since March: already-FCC-authorized DJI Agras SKUs are legally sellable and operable; new DJI 2026-launch SKUs are not authorized for the US market and will not be unless the Ninth Circuit reverses the FCC; domestic manufacturers (Hylio, Revolution, Exedy) are scaling but not yet at allocation parity. The Part 108 BVLOS NPRM is still the most consequential pending rule for ag-drone economics — it would normalize beyond-visual-line-of-sight ops that today require Part 137 exemptions — but no April 2026 update materially advanced the timeline.

The April-specific dealer-actionable item is the FAA enforcement posture (formalized in 2025 and reported on in late March 2026): operations that endanger the public, violate airspace restrictions, or are conducted in furtherance of another crime now trigger required legal action rather than discretionary warnings. Onboarding scripts and operator-handoff materials should explicitly call this out so new Part 137 operators do not assume informal warnings are still on the table.

## Key facts

### Federal (FAA, USDA, FCC)

- USDA's Research, Education, and Economics Under Secretary Dr. Scott Hutchins announced on April 7, 2026 the launch of the USDA National Proving Grounds Network for AgTech (NPG-Ag), a nationwide initiative to evaluate agricultural technologies under real conditions [source: https://www.usda.gov/about-usda/news/press-releases].
- FAA Part 108 BVLOS NPRM (announced by Transportation Secretary Sean Duffy on August 6, 2025) remains pending; the 60-day public comment window closed in October 2025 and no final rule has been issued as of April 30, 2026 [source: https://www.faa.gov/newsroom/us-transportation-secretary-sean-p-duffy-unveils-proposed-rule-unleash-american-drone] [source: https://www.faa.gov/newsroom/beyond-visual-line-sight-bvlos].
- FAA enforcement posture (in effect through 2025–2026): the agency now requires legal action — not discretionary warnings — when drone operations endanger the public, violate airspace restrictions, or are conducted in furtherance of another crime [source: https://www.suasnews.com/2026/03/faa-steps-up-drone-enforcement-in-2025/].
- DJI Ninth Circuit appeal (Case 26-1029, filed February 20, 2026 against the December 22, 2025 FCC covered-list ruling) remained active through April with no oral-argument date yet set publicly; existing FCC-authorized DJI ag models continue to be legally sellable in the US [source: https://agfundernews.com/dji-appeal-sets-stage-for-legal-showdown-over-future-of-us-ag-spray-drone-market] [source: https://agfundernews.com/made-in-america-fcc-decision-sparks-scramble-to-localize-ag-spray-drone-production].
- 14 CFR Part 137 (aerial application) and Part 107 (pilot) framework unchanged in April; ag-drone operators with payloads under 55 lb continue to operate under Part 107 with Part 137 exemptions for chemical dispensing and Section 107.36 (hazardous-material carriage) [source: https://www.faa.gov/newsroom/beyond-visual-line-sight-bvlos].

### State (IA, TX, NE, KS, MO)

- No new state ag-drone-specific statute or rule identified for IA, TX, NE, KS, or MO in April 2026 within the allowlisted sources.
- Industry-cluster signal (not a regulatory change, but state-relevant context): Texas continues to host Hylio's manufacturing scale-up to 5,000+ drones/year; Missouri-based Agri Spray Drones has signaled a move into US production; Iowa-based Rantizo continues to hold FAA swarm authorization [source: https://agfundernews.com/made-in-america-fcc-decision-sparks-scramble-to-localize-ag-spray-drone-production].

## Implications

- For dealer onboarding scripts: add an explicit note that FAA enforcement is now mandatory (not discretionary) for the three triggering categories above; new Part 137 operators should not be told "you'll usually just get a warning."
- For application support: continue planning around Part 137 exemptions (swarm, night, BVLOS) for the foreseeable 2026 season — Part 108 BVLOS final rule did not arrive in April and no public timeline indicates it will arrive before peak spray season.
- For pricing and availability conversations: the April status quo on DJI is unchanged — already-authorized SKUs only, no 2026-launch DJI ag drones available in the US, Ninth Circuit case ongoing. Quotes should still specify "is this an FCC-authorized SKU?" before promising delivery.
- For territory planning: the USDA NPG-Ag network is worth tracking — if any of its proving-ground sites land in IA/TX/NE/KS/MO, dealers should pursue early relationships, since independent benchmark data on Hylio vs. DJI throughput would directly support customer-facing comparison decks.
