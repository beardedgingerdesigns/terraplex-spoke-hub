## Summary

May 2026 was the most active month for ag-drone-relevant regulatory action since the December 22, 2025 FCC Covered List ruling. Three federal items dominate: the FCC Office of Engineering and Technology released **Public Notice DA 26-454** on May 8 extending the firmware-and-software-update waiver for previously-authorized foreign-manufactured UAS to at least January 1, 2029 and broadening it to Class II permissive changes; the FAA published its long-delayed **Section 2209 NPRM** in the Federal Register on May 6 (Docket FAA-2026-4558) with proposed § 74.250 explicitly preserving Part 137 transit access through fixed-site-facility flight restrictions; and the FCC's parallel **reply-comment window** on the DJI Petition for Reconsideration (ET Docket 26-22) and Autel Application for Review (ET Docket 26-23) closed on May 11, 2026. State-level activity was concentrated in Texas, where AG Ken Paxton on May 5 issued a Civil Investigative Demand to drone distributor Drone Nerds, LLC under the Texas Deceptive Trade Practices Act — escalating the February 2026 Anzu Robotics lawsuit from manufacturer to distribution channel and establishing precedent for state-level country-of-origin enforcement against drone distributors. AUVSI XPONENTIAL 2026 in Detroit (May 11–14) produced the first publicly-aligned three-way industry-FCC-FAA-DoD policy consensus around the "Made in America" drone architecture.

## Why this matters for Terraplex dealers

The May 2026 regulatory picture splits cleanly into two operationally distinct stories: the **installed-base patch question** for DJI Agras / [XAG](../manufacturers/xag.md) / Autel fleets is now resolved through January 1, 2029 via DA 26-454 (and OET has signaled it intends to codify the waiver via rulemaking rather than leave it as a revocable administrative decision), while the **new-equipment authorization question** for the same OEMs remains closed — neither the DJI Petition for Reconsideration nor the Ninth Circuit appeal (Case 26-1029) produced a reversal in May, and the AUVSI XPONENTIAL keynotes from AUVSI's Robbins, the Department of War's Cadenazzi, and the FAA's McIntosh + Jones aligned publicly behind the broader Covered List framework. The honest dealer-customer framing for the rest of the 2026 spray season is therefore the two-part frame the May 12 digest already crystallized: "your existing DJI / XAG fleet keeps flying and keeps receiving security patches and substantive firmware updates through at least the 2028 spray season, and your next airframe comes from a domestic OEM with a clean regulatory path — the [GTEEX Revolution lineup](../manufacturers/terraplex.md) ([R-32](../../products/r-32.md), [I-19](../../products/i-19.md)) or [Hylio](../manufacturers/hylio.md)."

The Section 2209 NPRM is procedurally large but operationally narrow for ag-spray: proposed § 74.250 explicitly preserves Part 137 transit access through Standard Unmanned Aircraft Flight Restrictions (UAFRs) so long as the operator broadcasts Remote ID, transits in the shortest practicable time, and notifies the facility per § 74.255. The comment period closes July 6, 2026 — that is the leverage window for the dealer network if a fixed-site-facility lobby pushes to narrow the Part 137 carve-out during comment. The Texas AG's escalation against Drone Nerds is mostly *not* an ag-drone story (Anzu Raptor is a small inspection-class drone, not a spray platform) but does establish that state AGs are now willing to litigate **distributor-level** country-of-origin claims — which raises the substantiation bar for Terraplex's "Built in the Americas, for the American Farmer" language even though Terraplex's claims are factually grounded.

## Key facts

### Federal (FCC)

- **FCC OET Public Notice DA 26-454 (released May 8, 2026)** extends the existing firmware-and-software-update waiver for previously-authorized foreign-manufactured UAS, UAS critical components, and consumer-grade routers from January 1, 2027 to **at least January 1, 2029**; broadens scope from Class I to **Class I + Class II permissive changes** (substantive software/firmware modifications that mitigate consumer harm, patch critical vulnerabilities, and maintain OS/network compatibility); OET signaled it intends to recommend the full Commission codify the waiver via rulemaking [source: https://docs.fcc.gov/public/attachments/DA-26-454A1.pdf] [source: https://www.suasnews.com/2026/05/fcc-grants-two-year-software-lifeline-for-dji-drone-and-tp-link-router-owners/]. **Scope limitation**: the waiver does NOT remove [DJI Agras](../manufacturers/dji-agras.md), [XAG](../manufacturers/xag.md), Autel, or other foreign-manufactured UAS from the Covered List, and does NOT reopen new-equipment authorizations — it only protects software/firmware support for airframes authorized before December 22, 2025. Full breakdown captured in the [2026-05-12 digest](../digests/2026-05-12.md) and [2026-05-13 digest](../digests/2026-05-13.md).
- **Reply-comment window closed May 11, 2026** on the DJI Petition for Reconsideration (ET Docket 26-22) and Autel Application for Review (ET Docket 26-23) of the December 22, 2025 Covered List ruling. The week before the deadline, FCC Commissioner Olivia Trusty's May 7, 2026 SCSP AI+ Expo keynote (*"Commanding the Skies: Drone Dominance in the Age of AI"*, FCC document DOC-421529A1) publicly defended the original Covered List rationale — the first sitting-Commissioner statement since the petitions were filed January 21, 2026, signaling reconsideration is unlikely to succeed at Commission level. Captured in detail in the [2026-05-09 digest](../digests/2026-05-09.md) and [2026-05-11 digest](../digests/2026-05-11.md).
- **DJI Ninth Circuit appeal** (Case 26-1029) remains active with no oral-argument date publicly set as of May 31, 2026; DJI has asked the court to pause the case for six months to allow the FCC to act on its petition for reconsideration [source: https://agfundernews.com/dji-appeal-sets-stage-for-legal-showdown-over-future-of-us-ag-spray-drone-market].

### Federal (FAA)

- **Section 2209 NPRM published May 6, 2026** in the Federal Register as Document 2026-08943, Notice No. 26-03, under Docket FAA-2026-4558 — implements section 2209 of the FAA Extension, Safety, and Security Act of 2016 a decade after the statute directed FAA to create the petition process. Creates new **14 CFR Part 74** with a two-tier framework: fixed-site-facility owners (qualifying critical-infrastructure or fixed-site categories) can petition the FAA to designate a **Standard Unmanned Aircraft Flight Restriction (UAFR)** around their facility [source: https://www.federalregister.gov/documents/2026/05/06/2026-08943/designation-restrict-the-operation-of-unmanned-aircraft-in-close-proximity-to-a-fixed-site-facility] [source: https://www.unmannedairspace.info/uncategorized/faa-publishes-proposed-rule-for-protecting-critical-infrastructure-from-rogue-drones/].
- **Part 137 transit access preserved**: proposed § 74.250 explicitly allows drones operating under Parts 91, 107, 108, 135, **and 137** to transit a Standard UAFR provided they (a) broadcast Remote ID under Part 89, (b) transit in the shortest practicable time, and (c) notify the facility per the procedures in proposed § 74.255 [source: https://dronexl.co/2026/05/05/faa-section-2209-nprm-drops/]. "Food and agriculture" is one of the 16 NSM-22 sectors so grain elevators, ethanol plants, and food-processing sites can themselves petition.
- **NPRM comment period closes July 6, 2026** (60 days after Federal Register publication), filed via regulations.gov to Docket FAA-2026-4558, by mail to Docket Operations at 1200 New Jersey Avenue SE, by hand delivery, or by fax to (202) 493-2251 [source: https://www.federalregister.gov/documents/2026/05/06/2026-08943/designation-restrict-the-operation-of-unmanned-aircraft-in-close-proximity-to-a-fixed-site-facility]. Captured in the [2026-05-06 digest](../digests/2026-05-06.md) and [2026-05-07 digest](../digests/2026-05-07.md).
- **Part 108 BVLOS NPRM** (unveiled by Transportation Secretary Sean Duffy August 6, 2025) remains in post-comment review with no final rule issued as of May 31, 2026; 60-day public comment window closed October 2025 [source: https://www.faa.gov/newsroom/beyond-visual-line-sight-bvlos]. No material May 2026 advance.
- **Part 137 + Part 107 framework unchanged**: ag-drone operators with payloads under 55 lb continue to operate under Part 107 with Part 137 exemptions for chemical dispensing and Section 107.36 (hazardous-material carriage); the joint-governance pattern continues to be the regulatory baseline for both the [GTEEX R-32](../../products/r-32.md) and [Hylio](../manufacturers/hylio.md) fleets dealers sell.
- **FAA DETER program** (fast-track settlement for first-time small-UAS violators, including farmer / ag-service operators) — effective April 17, 2026; no May 2026 enforcement-pattern data published yet but the program is now operationally available. Documented in the [2026-05-04 digest](../digests/2026-05-04.md).

### Federal (USDA)

- **USDA National Proving Grounds Network for AgTech (NPG-Ag)** — launched April 7, 2026 by USDA Research, Education, and Economics Under Secretary Dr. Scott Hutchins; designed to evaluate agricultural technologies under real-world conditions through the Agricultural Research Service and Land-Grant Universities [source: https://www.usda.gov/agtech]. No May 2026 update on which proving-ground sites have been designated; tracking continues from the [April 2026 regulatory roundup](2026-04-update.md).
- **No new USDA program specifically targeting ag-drone funding or precision-spray adoption** identified in May 2026 within the allowlisted sources. Secretary Rollins' May 2026 announcements focused on the Assistance for Specialty Crops Farmers (ASCF) program (~$1.625B) and the Great American Cotton Plan — neither is a direct ag-drone program but the specialty-crops payment touches some dealer customers.

### Federal (cross-agency policy alignment)

- **AUVSI XPONENTIAL 2026 Detroit (May 11–14, policy-keynote day Tuesday May 12)** produced three concurrent public alignments behind the "Made in America" architecture: (1) AUVSI President & CEO Michael Robbins publicly defended the broader-than-AUVSI-asked-for FCC December 22, 2025 Covered List sweep; (2) Assistant Secretary of War for Industrial Base Policy Michael Cadenazzi confirmed Department of War contractor-notification pilots for Section 1260H supply-chain links begin during 2026, ahead of the Section 805 direct-procurement ban June 30, 2026 and indirect-supplier ban June 30, 2027; (3) FAA ATO COO Franklin J. McIntosh + Office of Advanced Aviation Technologies Executive Director Jessica Jones framed drones-as-aircraft and drone-operators-as-pilots, with a radar-data-sharing test program announcement teed up. Full keynote breakdown in the [2026-05-14 digest](../digests/2026-05-14.md).

### State (IA, TX, NE, KS, MO)

- **Texas AG / Drone Nerds escalation (May 5, 2026)** — AG Ken Paxton issued a Civil Investigative Demand to Florida-based drone retailer Drone Nerds, LLC under the **Texas Deceptive Trade Practices Act**, escalating the February 18, 2026 lawsuit against Anzu Robotics from manufacturer to distribution channel. Specific allegation: Drone Nerds' marketing line that the Anzu Raptor is "headquartered and operated within the United States, giving you the peace of mind that your solution is delivered by your neighbors" is alleged to be misleading because the Raptor is a DJI Mavic 3 Enterprise rebrand. Corporate context: in November 2025, XTI Aerospace acquired both Drone Nerds and Anzu Robotics, making them corporate siblings [source: https://www.texasattorneygeneral.gov/news/releases/attorney-general-ken-paxton-investigates-drone-company-threatening-texans-security-and-privacy]. **First publicly-noticed state-level enforcement specifically targeting a drone *distributor* for misrepresenting product origin** — establishes precedent that state AGs can use deceptive-trade-practices law to enforce country-of-origin claims against distributors, not just manufacturers. Full breakdown in the [2026-05-06 digest](../digests/2026-05-06.md). Direct dealer-product overlap with the [GTEEX R-32](../../products/r-32.md) / [I-19](../../products/i-19.md) is minimal (Anzu Raptor is an inspection-class small UAS, not Part 137 ag-spray), but the legal-pattern read-through to [Terraplex](../manufacturers/terraplex.md)'s "Built in the Americas, for the American Farmer" provenance language is significant.
- **No new ag-drone-specific statute or rule** identified for Iowa, Nebraska, Kansas, or Missouri in May 2026 within the allowlisted sources.
- **Industry-cluster signal (not a regulatory change, but state-relevant context)**: Texas continues to host Hylio's manufacturing scale-up to 5,000+ drones/year (see [Hylio profile](../manufacturers/hylio.md)); the May 2026 Revolution Drones (NC + KS/NE assembly, GTEEX Revolution lineup) and Exedy Drones (Japanese-owned, Michigan, 50–70 L payload) scale-up captured in the [2026-05-03 digest](../digests/2026-05-03.md) anchors the Made-in-Americas competitive map across IA / TX / NE / KS / MO / NC / MI.

## Implications

- **For dealer onboarding scripts**: the post-May-12 honest framing on installed-base longevity is "your existing DJI Agras / XAG / Autel airframes will keep flying and will receive security patches plus substantive firmware updates through at least January 1, 2029, and the FCC has signaled it intends to codify that via rulemaking rather than leave it as a revocable waiver — what the FCC has *not* done is reopen the door to importing or selling new foreign-manufactured ag-drone models." Sam Welton's [compliance program](../../content/compliance-program.md) Part 107 / Part 137 prep curriculum should now include a DA 26-454 segment so prep-course students can answer the farmer question "will my T40 / T50 firmware updates stop in 2027?" with the concrete fact pattern above.
- **For application support and FAA exemption planning**: continue planning around Part 137 exemptions (swarm, night, BVLOS) for the foreseeable 2026 season — the Part 108 BVLOS final rule did not arrive in May and no public timeline suggests it will arrive before the second half of the 2026 spray season. The Section 2209 NPRM does **not** add a Part 137 compliance burden — proposed § 74.250 preserves transit access. Add a one-line entry to the compliance curriculum: "If the FAA finalizes the proposed § 74.250 from the May 6, 2026 NPRM, Part 137 operators will need to (a) broadcast Remote ID, (b) take the shortest practicable transit, and (c) notify the facility before transiting a Standard UAFR. The rule does not yet exist; comments close July 6, 2026."
- **For network-level advocacy**: the July 6, 2026 Section 2209 NPRM comment deadline is the leverage window. If Terraplex wants to file a comment defending the Part 137 transit allowance — which is structurally favorable to the [49-location dealer network](../manufacturers/terraplex.md) — that needs to be queued in Sam Welton's work plan. Simplest unified position: "preserve § 74.250 as drafted; do not narrow Part 137 access in response to fixed-site-facility comments."
- **For positioning copy and country-of-origin claims**: the Anzu / Drone Nerds escalation specifically validates "headquartered and operated within the United States" as a category of distributor claim state AGs are willing to litigate. Audit [content/positioning.md](../../content/positioning.md)'s "Built in the Americas, for the American Farmer" language and the [Terraplex profile](../manufacturers/terraplex.md) manufacturer-relationship description against the substantiation bar Paxton is establishing — every provenance claim should be paired with a documentable supply-chain fact (assembly location, primary component sourcing, OEM relationship). Terraplex's claims are factually grounded; the bar moved, not the answer.
- **For pricing and availability conversations**: the May status quo on DJI / [XAG](../manufacturers/xag.md) is unchanged — already-authorized SKUs only, no 2026-launch foreign-OEM ag drones available in the US, FCC reconsideration unlikely to succeed at Commission level, Ninth Circuit case ongoing. Quotes should continue to specify "is this an FCC-authorized SKU?" before promising delivery on any foreign-manufactured airframe.
- **For monitoring follow-up**: watch for (1) the OET-to-Commission codification recommendation referenced in DA 26-454's final paragraph — when that lands as a Notice of Proposed Rulemaking, the firmware permission becomes structurally durable; (2) the Commission's eventual ruling on the DJI / Autel petitions now that the May 11 reply-comment window has closed; (3) the July 6 Section 2209 NPRM comment close and whether any fixed-site-facility lobby successfully narrows § 74.250; (4) any USDA NPG-Ag proving-ground designations landing in IA / TX / NE / KS / MO — early dealer relationships would directly support customer-facing comparison decks for the [R-32](../../products/r-32.md) vs DJI / Hylio.
