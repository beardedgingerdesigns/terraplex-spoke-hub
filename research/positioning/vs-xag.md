## TL;DR

XAG is China-based, which means it faces the same FCC Covered List exposure as DJI. Existing authorized XAG airframes can still operate with firmware support through January 2029. New XAG SKUs cannot get US authorization. For US farmers buying new equipment in 2026, XAG is not a forward path. In Terraplex territory (IA/NE/WI/WA/ID/OK), XAG appears mostly through Rantizo's service network — which frames the conversation as service-versus-ownership, not a head-to-head drone comparison.

## Dealer Talking Points

- "XAG is in the same regulatory situation as DJI. Both are Chinese-manufactured, both are on the FCC Covered List, and both are blocked from new US authorization. That's not a debate — it's the FCC ruling from December 2025."
- "If someone already has XAG equipment authorized before December 2025, they can keep flying it. Firmware support runs through January 2029. But their next machine should be domestic."
- "Rantizo runs XAG hardware in their service network. If a farmer is comparing our equipment sale to a Rantizo service contract, that's a different conversation — buy versus rent, not drone versus drone."
- "Revolution parts come from your dealer. XAG parts travel an international supply chain with tariff exposure. That's a different support experience when something breaks during a spray window."

## Objection Handlers

### "XAG has larger payload specs — the P150 is bigger"

The XAG P150 (Rantizo's US flagship) has a high payload and strong throughput numbers. But throughput only matters if the platform is available for new purchase and serviceable long-term. The P150 was authorized before December 2025 — it can still be sold — but its future is constrained. No new XAG models can get US authorization. The R-32 (1,200-1,600 acres/day, 32-gallon tank) competes directly with any XAG model currently in the US market, with a supply chain that has a forward path.

### "Rantizo has a reliable nationwide service model"

Rantizo covers 35 states and has spun off its spray-services business into American Autonomy Inc. Their model is service-based, not equipment ownership. If a farmer wants to hire custom spray services without owning equipment, Rantizo is worth considering. If they want to own their operation, fly under their own Part 137 certificate, and build a custom-application business — the Revolution lineup and Terraplex's compliance program give them that path. Service and ownership are different businesses. Rantizo is not a dealer in the traditional sense.

### "XAG had good supply when DJI was constrained last year"

That was 2024. The December 2025 ruling swept every foreign-manufactured UAS into the Covered List simultaneously — DJI and XAG in the same order. XAG's pre-ban channel advantage is gone. They are now in the same regulatory position as DJI.

### "What about XAG's Bayer integration — I run Bayer programs"

XAG's partnership with Bayer (crop-protection data integration) is a genuine differentiator in their agronomy stack. If a farmer is deep in Bayer programs and that integration matters, it's worth acknowledging. The honest answer is: XAG's Bayer integration is specific to their platform. If the farmer's primary concern is the agronomy data layer, that's a real conversation. If the primary concern is buying equipment they can legally purchase new, service locally, and own for 5+ years — XAG is not that answer.

## What XAG Does Better

- **Agronomy data integration.** XAG's Bayer partnership gives XAG-using operations a tighter crop-protection data stack than most alternatives. Meaningful for farms already in Bayer's precision agronomy programs.
- **International regulatory experience.** XAG earned the UK CAA's first operational authorization for ag drone spraying in January 2023. Strong regulator-engagement track record outside the US.
- **P150 throughput.** The XAG P150 (Rantizo US channel) is one of the highest-capacity spray platforms available to US operators on currently-authorized pre-ban inventory.

## Recent Developments

- **DA 26-454 firmware waiver (May 8, 2026)** extends software update permissions for previously-authorized XAG airframes (P40, P100, P150) to at least January 1, 2029. Same waiver that protects the DJI Agras installed base. No change to Covered List status or new-authorization block. See [digest 2026-05-13](../digests/2026-05-13.md).
- **Rantizo service footprint overlap.** Rantizo's hardware-agnostic services model (post-American Autonomy spinoff) continues to deploy XAG airframes across 35 states, including IA/NE where Terraplex's dealer network operates. Frame this as service-versus-ownership, not XAG-versus-Revolution. See [research/dealer-economics/competitor-distributors.md](../dealer-economics/competitor-distributors.md).
- **Elevon Aerial Ag conditional approval (May 2026)** introduces a new competitor in the "US-assembled" lane — distinct from XAG's foreign-manufactured status. Elevon's Z30/Z50/Z80 (EFT-derived, St. Louis assembly) received DoW Conditional Approval through December 31, 2026. This creates a clearer "Revolution vs XAG" framing: Revolution has full, permanent authorization; XAG has firmware-only runway; Elevon has a time-limited exception. See [digest 2026-06-06](../digests/2026-06-06.md).
