## TL;DR

DJI is the biggest name in ag spraying worldwide, but the FCC's December 22, 2025 ruling cut off new DJI equipment authorizations in the US. Revolution/GTEEX is not on the Covered List. For farmers buying new equipment in 2026, that is the single most consequential fact. On top of that, Revolution's dealer model puts a local person behind every sale, Revolution's software responds to American farmer feedback in weeks, and the platform is designed to be owner-maintained — not locked down like DJI's ecosystem.

## Objection Handlers

### "But DJI is cheaper"

DJI's sticker price on currently-available SKUs is lower. But factor in total cost of ownership: DJI parts require authorized repair centers, downtime during spray windows is expensive, and DJI's supply chain risk is real. If DJI's Ninth Circuit appeal fails or parts supply tightens, replacement costs go up with no domestic alternative in the DJI ecosystem. Revolution is built to be owner-maintained with local dealer parts in stock. One extended downtime event during a narrow spray window can cost more than the price difference.

Also: DJI's new flagship models — T100, T70P, T25P — cannot currently be sold new in the US under the FCC ruling. The available DJI ag inventory is 2024-era SKUs. Revolution is building forward.

### "But DJI has proven technology — it's been around longer"

DJI has global scale, and it shows. It also means DJI's priorities are global, not American. The T-series ag software serves 600,000+ drones across 100+ countries. Revolution's software is built for American application practices. When USDA compliance, corn-belt variable rate maps, or narrow spray-window logistics matter, Revolution's team responds in weeks. The track record that matters most is the one for your geography.

### "But everyone uses DJI — parts will be available"

Existing DJI fleets have a firmware support runway through January 2029 (FCC DA 26-454). That's firmware, not parts. Revolution's dealer network stocks parts locally. A dealer who runs the equipment he sells is a different supply chain than shipping a controller to an OEM service center and waiting. The goal is not to bash DJI's installed base. It's to point out that the next machine you buy should have a local support structure.

### "DJI passed an independent cybersecurity audit"

The OnDefend audit covered the Air 3S and Matrice 4E — two consumer/enterprise mapping platforms. It did not cover any Agras T-series drone. The ag sprayers your operation would actually use were not tested. DJI's own communications confirm the scope. Beyond the security question, the FCC Covered List ruling stands regardless of vendor-funded audit outcomes. (See [digest 2026-06-04](../digests/2026-06-04.md) for the full audit scope caveat.)

## What DJI Does Better

- **Global scale and data.** DJI's 600,000+ drone fleet and 500M+ hectare treatment history is the deepest operational dataset in ag aviation. Their product development has years of iterative refinement.
- **Ecosystem breadth.** DJI offers more SKUs across payload sizes, a wider accessory ecosystem, and established training programs with 7,000+ certified instructors globally.
- **Specialist crop positioning.** DJI segments their product line between grain crops and commercial crops (citrus, orchards, tea). Revolution's lineup is primarily row-crop focused.
- **Available pre-ban authorized inventory.** Dealers who stocked DJI T50/T25/T30 units before December 2025 can still legally sell them. In the short term, some buyers will find DJI units on dealer shelves.
- **Established service record.** DJI Care / DJI Care Refresh has years of US customer history. Revolution's dealer service network is newer and distributed across the Terraplex network.

## Recent Developments

- **DJI Agras T55 and T100 launched commercially in Thailand** at AGRITECHNICA Asia 2026 (May 20, 2026). Both remain blocked from new US FCC authorization. DJI continues expanding globally while the US channel stays gated. See [digest 2026-05-29](../digests/2026-05-29.md).
- **OnDefend audit released May 28, 2026.** Zero critical/high/medium findings. Covered Air 3S and Matrice 4E only. DJI Agras T-series not in scope. FCC Covered List status unchanged. See [digest 2026-06-04](../digests/2026-06-04.md).
- **FCC firmware waiver extended to January 2029** (DA 26-454, May 8, 2026). Existing authorized DJI Agras fleets can receive firmware updates. Does not reopen new authorization or remove DJI from the Covered List. See [digest 2026-05-13](../digests/2026-05-13.md).
- **Section 805 direct procurement ban takes effect June 30, 2026.** Federal contractors cannot directly procure from Covered List companies. Dealers serving USDA programs or government accounts should brief customers now.
- **NAAA 2026 safety data.** 20% of US manned aerial applicators reported an unsafe drone encounter in 2025, up from 11% in 2023. Certified operators with Part 137 credentials and proper airspace awareness are the defensible standard. See [digest 2026-06-03](../digests/2026-06-03.md).
- **Caution — conflicting off-allowlist claim.** One non-allowlisted source (dronefly.com) asserts DJI received pre-ban FCC authorization for the Agras T55. This conflicts with allowlisted sources (digests 2026-05-29, 2026-06-02) that clearly state T55 remains blocked from new US sale. Do not treat T55 as US-available until confirmed via allowlisted sources. Flag for human review.
